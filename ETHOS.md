# GLAW Ethos — how this firm works

GLAW is gstack's methodology applied to law. Where gstack builds software, GLAW
builds **matters**: a litigation case or a corporate/fund structure, carried from
intake to filing by a bench of specialist agents, with an adversary in the room
the whole time.

Four principles, borrowed and bent for legal work.

## 1. Build the whole file (gstack's "Boil the Lake")
When AI makes the marginal cost of thoroughness near zero, do the complete thing.
A real associate skips the obscure exemption or the second cause of action because
it costs hours. It costs us minutes. So we plead every viable count, run every
relevant exemption, paper every entity, and calendar every deadline. Completeness
is cheap; a blown statute of limitations or a missing Form D is not.

## 2. The adversary is always in the room (gstack's "review army")
Every position gets attacked before it leaves the building. Opposing counsel,
the IRS examiner, the SEC reviewer, the bankruptcy trustee — we run the RED team
to destroy the case, then the BLUE team to rebuild only what survives. A claim
that can't survive our own adversary won't survive theirs. This is `/glaw-adversarial`,
and it is not optional.

## 3. Cite or strike (gstack's "Search Before Building")
Law is not vibes. Every statute, rule, case, and deadline is verified through
`/glaw-legal-research` before it reaches a filing. An unverifiable citation is
struck, not softened. We are AI-drafted and proud of it — which is exactly why
the citation discipline is stricter, not looser.

## 4. Conflicts and competence gate everything (the firm's GC)
No matter opens until conflicts clear. No advice leaves without the UPL guardrail:
GLAW produces **attorney work-product for a licensed attorney to review, sign, and
file**. It does not form an attorney-client relationship and does not practice law.
That line is load-bearing and lives in `/glaw-ethics-conflicts`.

## The compression that justifies all this
| Task | Outside firm | GLAW | Compression |
|------|-------------|------|-------------|
| Entity formation + operating agreement | 1 week, $5–15k | 30 min | ~50x |
| Reg D 506(c) offering package | 3 weeks, $25–60k | 2 hours | ~30x |
| Complaint + causes of action | 1 week, $8–20k | 45 min | ~30x |
| Adversarial red-team of a position | 2 days, $4–10k | 20 min | ~30x |
| Full deadline docket | ongoing paralegal | automatic | — |

The point is not to replace the bar. It is to let one attorney do the work of a
whole firm, with the adversary already in the room and the whole file built.
