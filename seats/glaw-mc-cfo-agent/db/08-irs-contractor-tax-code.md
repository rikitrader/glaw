# IRS Tax Code Reference for Roofing Contractors

**Source:** Synthesized from Peterson Ch. 5, 13, 18 (Construction Accounting), Accounting Policies Manual, and current IRS publications applicable to US roofing, solar, and contractor operations.

## Section 179 Deduction (IRC §179)

### Rules
- Expense qualifying property in the year placed in service
- 2026 limit: $1,220,000 (adjusted annually for inflation)
- Phase-out begins at $3,050,000 in total property placed in service
- Must be used >50% for business
- Applies to: equipment, machinery, vehicles, off-the-shelf software, certain improvements

### Roofing-Specific Applications
| Asset | §179 Eligible? | Notes |
|-------|---------------|-------|
| Pickup trucks (<6,000 lbs GVWR) | Yes, with limit | Passenger auto limits (~$12,400 first year) |
| Trucks >6,000 lbs GVWR | Yes | Full §179 up to SUV limit ($28,900) |
| Trucks >14,000 lbs GVWR | Yes | No SUV limit — full §179 |
| Trailers | Yes | 7-year MACRS property |
| Generators, compressors | Yes | 7-year MACRS property |
| Nail guns, roofing tools | Yes or de minimis | If <$2,500, use de minimis safe harbor |
| Office computers | Yes | 5-year MACRS property |
| Xactimate/software licenses | Yes | Off-the-shelf qualifies |
| Building improvements (HVAC, roof, alarm) | Yes | Qualified improvement property |

## Bonus Depreciation (IRC §168(k))

### Phase-Down Schedule
| Year | Bonus Depreciation % |
|------|---------------------|
| 2022 | 100% |
| 2023 | 80% |
| 2024 | 60% |
| 2025 | 40% |
| 2026 | 20% |

### Strategy
- Front-load asset purchases in years with higher bonus %
- Combine §179 + bonus depreciation for maximum first-year write-off
- Consider if creates NOL (net operating loss) and whether carryforward is beneficial

## S-Corp Salary Optimization (IRC §3121, §1402)

### Principle
S-Corp shareholders who are also employees must take a "reasonable salary." Amounts above reasonable salary taken as distributions avoid:
- Employee FICA (6.2% SS + 1.45% Medicare)
- Employer FICA (matching 6.2% SS + 1.45% Medicare)
- Total SE tax savings: 15.3% up to SS wage base ($168,600 in 2026), 2.9% above

### Reasonable Salary Factors
- Industry and geographic pay benchmarks
- Role responsibilities (CEO vs. estimator vs. sales)
- Company revenue and profitability
- Time devoted to business
- Compensation paid to non-shareholder employees in similar roles
- **IRS scrutiny:** too-low salary = audit risk, too-high = wasted tax benefit

### Example (Roofing Company Owner)
| Scenario | Salary | Distribution | Total | SE Tax Saved |
|----------|--------|-------------|-------|-------------|
| No optimization | $300K | $0 | $300K | $0 |
| Optimized | $150K | $150K | $300K | ~$23K |
| Aggressive (risky) | $80K | $220K | $300K | ~$34K |

## Worker Classification (IRC §3509, IRS 20-Factor Test)

### 1099 vs. W-2 Determination
The IRS uses three categories of evidence:

**Behavioral Control:**
- Does the company control HOW work is done?
- Does it provide training, tools, specific instructions?
- If yes → employee

**Financial Control:**
- Does the worker have unreimbursed expenses?
- Does the worker invest in own tools/equipment?
- Does the worker have opportunity for profit/loss?
- If worker bears financial risk → independent contractor

**Relationship:**
- Written contract terms
- Benefits provided?
- Permanency of relationship?
- Is the work a key aspect of business?

### Penalties for Misclassification
| Situation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Unintentional misclassification | 1.5% of wages + 20% of employee FICA share |
| Intentional misclassification | 20% of wages + 100% of employee FICA + penalties |
| No 1099 filed | Additional penalties |
| State penalties | Vary — CA, TX, FL have aggressive enforcement |

### Safe Harbor for Roofing Subs
A subcontractor is likely properly classified as 1099 if ALL of these are true:
- Has own business license / contractor license
- Has own insurance (GL, WC if applicable)
- Sets own hours and methods
- Provides own tools and equipment
- Works for multiple companies
- Submits invoices (not timesheets)
- Has written subcontractor agreement
- Bears risk of profit/loss on the job

## Per Diem (IRS Rev. Proc. 2019-48, Pub. 1542)

### Rules for Tax-Free Per Diem
- Employee must travel **away from tax home** (sleep away required)
- Or travel to job site >50 miles from regular office
- Must be under an **accountable plan** (business connection, substantiation, excess returned)
- 2026 rates: $59-$79/day (varies by locality — check IRS per diem tables)
- Per diem covers meals and incidental expenses (M&IE)
- Lodging: must be substantiated with receipts (no standard per diem for lodging)

### Roofing Application
- Storm chasers traveling to disaster areas → per diem eligible
- Crews working >50 miles from office → per diem eligible
- Local crews (<50 miles) → NOT eligible for tax-free per diem
- **Savings:** $60/day × 200 working days × 10 crew = $120,000 tax-free vs. taxable

## Mileage Deduction (IRC §274)

### 2026 Standard Mileage Rate (estimated)
- Business: $0.70/mile
- Must choose: standard mileage OR actual expenses (not both for same vehicle)
- If using actual expenses: fuel, maintenance, insurance, depreciation, tolls, parking

### Vehicle Strategies for Roofers
| Strategy | Best For | Key Rule |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Standard mileage | Employees using personal vehicles | Track every business mile |
| Actual expense | Company-owned fleet | Track all costs + allocate by business % |
| §179 + bonus | New trucks >6,000 lbs GVWR | Expense in year 1 |
| Accountable plan | Reimbursing employee vehicles | Reimburse at IRS rate, tax-free to employee |

## Energy Tax Credits (IRC §48, §25D, §45)

### Residential Solar (§25D — Residential Clean Energy Credit)
- 30% of installed cost through 2032
- Applies when roofing company installs solar for homeowner
- Credit goes to homeowner (contractor can market this benefit)

### Commercial Solar (§48 — Investment Tax Credit)
- 30% base ITC + bonus adders (domestic content, energy community)
- If contractor installs on own property → claim ITC
- Prevailing wage + apprenticeship requirements for full credit

### Solar Installation Accounting for Roofers
| Revenue Stream | Accounting Treatment |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Roof + solar bundle | Allocate revenue proportionally |
| Solar-only installation | Separate service line P&L |
| Solar equipment sold | COGS = equipment cost |
| Tax credit value (marketing) | Not revenue to contractor — benefit to property owner |

## Qualified Business Income Deduction (IRC §199A)

### QBI Deduction
- 20% deduction on qualified business income from pass-through entities
- Effective top rate: 29.6% (vs. 37% without)
- Limitation for "specified service trades": does NOT include construction (good news)
- W-2 wage limitation: deduction capped at greater of 50% of W-2 wages OR 25% of wages + 2.5% of UBIA (unadjusted basis of qualified property)

### Strategy for Roofers
- Maximize QBI by properly classifying income as business income
- Ensure enough W-2 wages to support deduction (especially for S-Corps)
- Asset purchases (UBIA component) support higher deduction

## Fuel Tax Credit (IRC §34)

- Off-highway use of diesel/gas in generators, compressors, etc.
- Credit: $0.243/gallon (diesel), $0.184/gallon (gasoline)
- Track fuel usage by purpose (on-highway vs. off-highway)
- Claimed on Form 4136

## Home Office Deduction (IRC §280A)

- Regular and exclusive use for business
- Principal place of business OR place to meet clients
- Simplified: $5/sq ft × max 300 sq ft = $1,500 max
- Actual: proportion of home expenses (mortgage, utilities, insurance, repairs)
- Available to S-Corp shareholders if company reimburses under accountable plan

## Cost Segregation (IRC §168)

### When Applicable
- Roofing company purchases or builds a building (office, warehouse)
- Study reclassifies building components to shorter-lived assets
- Land improvements (15-year): parking lot, fencing, landscaping, signage
- Personal property (5/7-year): specialized electrical, plumbing, flooring
- Typically recovers 20-40% of building cost as shorter-lived property

### ROI
- Cost of study: $5,000-$15,000
- Typical tax savings: $50,000-$200,000+ (depending on building value)
- Worth it for buildings purchased for >$500,000
