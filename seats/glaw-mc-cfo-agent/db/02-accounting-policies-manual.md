# Accounting & Financial Policies and Procedures Manual

**Source:** City of Superior, Wisconsin — Accounting & Financial Policies and Procedures Manual. Effective January 1, 2018. Adapted for roofing contractor context.

## Applicable Frameworks

- **2 CFR Part 200** — Uniform Administrative Requirements (for federally funded projects)
- **Accrual basis** — record revenue when earned, expenses when incurred
- **Modified accrual** — for governmental fund reporting

## Chart of Accounts

- Organized by: Assets (1xxx), Liabilities (2xxx), Equity (3xxx), Revenue (4xxx), Expenses (5xxx-6xxx), Other (7xxx)
- Chart changes require finance director approval
- No unauthorized account creation

## Revenue Recognition

- **Contract revenue:** recognized as work is performed (%-of-completion preferred)
- **Service revenue:** recognized upon completion
- **Insurance claim revenue:** recognized when approved, not when filed
- **Supplement revenue:** recognized when carrier approves, not when submitted
- **Retainage:** recognized as earned but classified as retainage receivable until released

## Accounts Receivable

- Monthly aging review required
- AR >90 days requires write-off authorization
- Bad debt reserve: percentage-of-sales or aging method
- Collection escalation: 30 days → statement, 60 → call, 90 → demand letter, 120 → collections

## Purchasing & Disbursements

- **Competitive bidding thresholds** — adapt for roofing: material orders >$10K require 3 quotes
- **Purchase order system** — PO required for all material orders >$500
- **Invoice approval workflow:** 3-way match (PO → receipt → invoice)
- **Payment terms negotiation:** target Net 30, negotiate 2/10 Net 30 discounts
- **Check signing authority:** dual signatures required above threshold

## Payroll Policies

- **Worker classification:** IRS 20-factor test for 1099 vs. W-2
- **Payroll frequency:** weekly for field crews, bi-weekly for office
- **Overtime:** federal FLSA and state law compliance
- **Per diem:** substantiated under accountable plan (IRS Rev. Proc. 2019-48)
- **Timekeeping:** GPS-verified clock-in/out for field crews
- **Payroll tax deposits:** semi-weekly if >$50K annual liability

## Capital Assets

- **Capitalization threshold:** $2,500+ (align with IRS de minimis safe harbor)
- **Useful life estimates:**
  - Vehicles: 5-7 years
  - Heavy equipment: 7-10 years
  - Office equipment: 5 years
  - Buildings: 39 years (nonresidential)
- **Depreciation method:** straight-line for book, MACRS for tax
- **Asset listing:** maintained with serial numbers, purchase date, cost, location, assigned job/employee
- **Disposition:** requires written authorization, gain/loss recorded

## Cash Management

- **Bank reconciliation:** monthly, within 10 days of statement
- **Petty cash:** $500 max, receipts required, monthly replenishment
- **Wire transfers:** dual authorization required
- **Minimum cash balance:** maintain 2-3 months of overhead expenses

## Internal Controls

- Segregation of duties: person who approves ≠ person who pays
- Physical inventory counts: quarterly for materials yard
- Bank reconciliation performed by someone other than check preparer
- Credit card reconciliation: monthly, receipts attached, business purpose documented
- Access controls: role-based access to accounting software

## Insurance Requirements

- **General liability:** minimum $1M per occurrence, $2M aggregate
- **Commercial auto:** minimum $1M combined single limit
- **Workers' compensation:** statutory limits per state
- **Umbrella/excess:** $2M-$5M (scale with revenue)
- **Builder's risk:** for large commercial projects
- **Professional liability (E&O):** if providing design services
- **Bonding:** payment and performance bonds for commercial GC work

## Record Retention

| Record Type | Retention Period |
|-------------|----------------|
| Tax returns and supporting docs | 7 years |
| Financial statements | Permanent |
| Bank statements and reconciliations | 7 years |
| Accounts payable records | 7 years |
| Accounts receivable records | 7 years |
| Payroll records | 7 years |
| Employee records (W-4, I-9) | 4 years after termination |
| 1099 forms | 7 years |
| Contracts | 10 years after completion |
| Insurance policies | Permanent (claims can be long-tail) |
| Corporate records | Permanent |
| Job cost records | 7 years after project completion |
| Equipment records | Life of asset + 7 years |

## Annual Audit

- External CPA review recommended for companies >$3M revenue
- Full audit required for companies >$10M or seeking bank financing / PE investment
- Audit prep: reconcile all accounts, accrue all expenses, document estimates
- Management letter findings tracked and remediated
