# 10 — Civil Theft (Florida, §772.11 + §812.014)

⟦VERIFY all section numbers, the demand mechanics, and the burden of proof against current Fla. Stat. §§772.11 and 812.014 before filing.⟧

## Statutory basis
- **§772.11** — the civil cause of action and remedy (treble damages + fees) for a
  violation of the criminal-theft provisions.
- **§812.014** — the underlying **criminal theft** statute that supplies the conduct and
  intent elements. Civil theft borrows the criminal definition, so you must satisfy the
  criminal standard in a civil case.

## Elements (prove by CLEAR AND CONVINCING evidence)
The heightened burden — not mere preponderance — is itself a frequent dismissal point.
The defendant:
1. **Knowingly obtained or used, or endeavored to obtain or use**;
2. **The plaintiff's property** (must be *specifically identifiable* property or funds —
   see "money exception" below);
3. **With felonious / criminal intent** to either:
   - (a) **temporarily or permanently deprive** the plaintiff of a right to the property
     or a benefit from it; OR
   - (b) **appropriate** the property to the defendant's own use or the use of a person
     not entitled to it.

## THE critical issue — not a dressed-up contract claim
> **A mere breach of contract / failure to pay money owed is NOT civil theft.** Florida
> courts routinely dismiss civil-theft counts that are contract disputes wearing a tort
> costume. You must allege and prove **criminal/felonious intent** *independent of, and
> beyond,* the breach — typically by pointing to **specifically identifiable funds** the
> defendant was obligated to keep separate (escrow, trust, customer payments, investor
> funds) and instead diverted. Commingled general-account "money owed" usually fails;
> earmarked/segregated funds the defendant had no right to touch usually survives.

## Pre-suit demand — DO NOT SKIP (§772.11)
- Serve a **written demand** for the treble amount of the actual damages claimed and allow
  the statutory period (commonly **30 days**) for payment before filing the civil-theft
  count. ⟦VERIFY current period/text.⟧
- **Failure to comply jeopardizes (and can defeat) the entire civil-theft count** and the
  fee entitlement. Calendar it; document service; attach the demand as an exhibit.
- Practical sequencing: the §772.11 demand can ride alongside a FUFTA / alter-ego demand
  (cross-link → `references/06-fufta` and `references/09-alter-ego-veil-piercing.md`).

## Damages & remedies (§772.11)
- **Actual damages**, then **treble (3×)** damages;
- **Reasonable attorney's fees** and **court costs** to the prevailing party (note: a
  prevailing *defendant* may also recover fees — a risk to weigh before pleading it).

## Counts commonly pleaded alongside civil theft
Stack the substantive and equitable theories that fit the record:
- **Conversion** (the tort cousin — lower intent bar, no treble/fees, no demand; often
  pleaded as the fallback if felonious intent can't be proven).
- **Fraud / fraudulent misrepresentation / fraudulent concealment.**
- **Civil conspiracy** (needs an underlying tort + agreement).
- **Unjust enrichment; constructive trust; accounting** (equitable tracing of the funds).
- **Breach of fiduciary duty; aiding & abetting breach of fiduciary duty.**
- **Fraudulent transfer** (FUFTA, Ch. 726) → `references/06`.
- **Alter ego / piercing the veil; successor liability** → `references/09`.
- **RICO** (state/federal) where there's a *pattern* of theft/fraud → `references/08`.
- **Replevin** (recover the specific property), **declaratory** and **injunctive** relief.

## Typical fact patterns Florida courts have recognized
Embezzlement of company funds; diversion of **escrow** funds; misappropriation of **trust**
funds; theft of **customer payments**; **unauthorized wire transfers**; theft of
partnership/LLC assets; conversion of **insurance proceeds**; employee theft;
misappropriation of inventory; theft of equipment/vehicles; **diversion of investor
funds**. The common thread that makes these *theft* rather than *breach*: identifiable
funds/property the defendant was not entitled to take and took with intent.

## Sample count title
```
COUNT V – CIVIL THEFT
(Florida Statutes §§ 772.11 and 812.014)
```
followed by allegations that the defendant **knowingly and intentionally misappropriated
specifically identifiable property or funds** belonging to the plaintiff, with felonious
intent, and that the **§772.11 written demand was served and the statutory period elapsed
without payment.**

## Build order
1. **Identify the specific property/funds** and the duty to keep them separate (escrow,
   trust, earmarked). If you can't, civil theft is the wrong count — plead conversion.
2. **Plead felonious intent beyond breach** — facts showing the taking was wrongful, not
   just unpaid.
3. **Serve the §772.11 demand** (use `templates/civil-theft-demand-letter.md`); calendar
   the 30-day clock; preserve proof of service.
4. **Plead the count** with the dual citation; stack conversion as a fallback and the
   equitable tracing counts (constructive trust/accounting).
5. **Weigh the fee-shifting risk** — a losing plaintiff can owe the defendant's fees.
