# 06 — Fraudulent Transfer (Florida UFTA, Ch. 726)

⟦VERIFY all section numbers/thresholds against current Fla. Stat. Ch. 726.⟧

## The two core claims
- **§726.105 — actual or constructive fraud (present & future creditors):**
  - (1)(a) **Actual intent** to hinder, delay, or defraud any creditor; OR
  - (1)(b) **Constructive**: transfer without **reasonably equivalent value** AND
    (i) debtor was/became undercapitalized, or (ii) intended/believed it would incur
    debts beyond ability to pay.
- **§726.106 — present creditors:** transfer without reasonably equivalent value while
  insolvent (or rendering insolvent); plus insider-preference provisions.

## Badges of fraud (§726.105(2)) — assemble all that apply
- Transfer to an **insider**; debtor **retained control**; transfer **concealed**;
  debtor **sued or threatened** before transfer; transfer of **substantially all**
  assets; **absconding**; **removed/concealed** assets; **not reasonably equivalent
  value**; debtor **insolvent** or became so; transfer shortly **before/after a large
  debt**; transfer of essential assets to a lienor who transferred to an insider.

> A **collusive judgment + lien** can itself be the "transfer" or the mechanism that
> hinders other creditors — link the judgment module to FUFTA: the agreed judgment
> creates a lien for an insider-controlled "creditor," priming legitimate creditors
> without reasonably equivalent value.

## Remedies (§726.108–.109)
- **Avoidance** of the transfer/obligation to the extent necessary to satisfy the
  claim; **attachment**; **injunction** against further disposition; **receiver**;
  levy/execution on the asset. Money judgment against the first transferee or the
  person for whose benefit the transfer was made.
- **Good-faith transferee** for reasonably equivalent value has a defense (§726.109).

## Limitations (§726.110)
- Actual-intent claims: generally **4 years** after transfer, or **1 year** after it
  was or could reasonably have been discovered (discovery rule). Constructive claims:
  **4 years**. ⟦VERIFY current text.⟧

## Standing
- A "**creditor**" with a "**claim**" (broadly defined, incl. contingent/unmatured)
  may sue. Identify the claimant's claim and its accrual date; both present and future
  creditors are protected under §726.105.

## Build order
1. Identify the **transfer/obligation** and the **transferee/beneficiary**.
2. Show **no reasonably equivalent value** and/or **actual intent** (badges).
3. Show **insolvency** or undercapitalization (balance-sheet or equity test).
4. Pick remedy + forum; calendar the 4-yr / 1-yr discovery clock.
