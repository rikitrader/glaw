# 05 — Fraud on the Court & Collusive / Consent Judgments

⟦VERIFY citations against current Florida authority.⟧

## Two distinct theories — don't conflate
1. **Intrinsic fraud / misrepresentation** between the parties → **Rule 1.540(b)(3)**,
   **one-year** limit, by a *party*.
2. **Fraud UPON the court** → preserved by Rule 1.540(b)'s savings clause and the
   court's inherent power; **no fixed time limit**; reachable by **independent
   action**. Reserved for an "unconscionable scheme" that *defiles the court itself*
   or corrupts the judicial machinery (e.g., fabricated evidence, bribery, attorney
   complicity, a sham/collusive proceeding) — a high bar. (See *DeClaire v. Yohanan*;
   *Flemenbaum*; Cox-line cases ⟦VERIFY⟧.)

## Collusive / "friendly" / consent judgments
A judgment entered **by agreement among commonly-controlled parties**, fast, with no
real adversity, to manufacture a debt, lien, or priority is a classic target.
**Badges to assemble:**
- Same individual/entity controls **both** plaintiff-side and all defendant-side
  parties (signs for everyone; receives "service" at his own email).
- "**Agreed**"/consent or rapid **default** judgment; waivers of service; no answer,
  no discovery, no contest; weeks from filing to judgment.
- Judgment amount taken on a **bare face number** with no ledger/affidavit of amount.
- **"Lost note"** count used to avoid producing the original (cross-reference §673.3091
  and the forensics module).
- Express **preservation of liens / in rem rights** — the judgment is a tool to prime
  *other* creditors.
- Concealment from the court of the debt's true nature/origin (e.g., a defaulted MCA
  relabeled a "loan").

## Who can attack, and how
- A **party** defrauded: Rule 1.540(b)(3) within a year, or independent action for
  fraud on the court anytime.
- A **non-party creditor** harmed by a collusive judgment generally attacks via
  **independent action** and/or a **fraudulent-transfer** theory (see FUFTA module),
  and may seek to intervene/vacate; standing turns on concrete injury (e.g., the
  collusive lien primes their recovery).
- A **bankruptcy trustee**: §§ 544/548 avoidance, plus collusive-judgment attack.

## Defense side (anticipate)
- "Lost-note reestablishment is a statutory, routine remedy; attaching a copy is
  required." "Consent judgments are favored/efficient." "No false statement of fact
  was made to the court." "Movant is a stranger without standing / outside the
  one-year window / barred by laches." Neutralize each before filing.
