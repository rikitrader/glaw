# 01 — Method & Engagement

## Intake (answer before analyzing)
1. **Whose side?** Creditor / debtor / guarantor / successor-in-interest / bankruptcy
   trustee / third-party creditor / investor / regulator. The same facts produce
   opposite work product depending on this.
2. **Goal & posture.** Collect, defend, vacate, unwind a transfer, block a lien,
   negotiate, refer to authorities, preserve appeal.
3. **Jurisdiction & forum.** State vs. federal; which county/circuit; choice-of-law
   vs. forum-selection clauses (they differ); is the contractual forum even the one
   where suit was filed?
4. **Deadlines.** Statutes of limitation/repose, response deadlines, the 1-year
   limit on Rule 1.540(b)(1)-(3), notice-of-appeal windows. Calendar them first.
5. **Conflicts.** Same principal on both sides? Common control? That itself is a fact.

## How to read a record
- Build a **single dated chronology** spanning every instrument and docket event.
  Discrepancies (dates, amounts, parties, signatures) jump out only on a timeline.
- Tie each legal element to a **quoted** document location (page/¶/§).
- Track **money**: amount advanced vs. amount claimed vs. amount adjudged. Demand a
  ledger; face amount ≠ payoff.
- Track **entities**: who signed, in what capacity, for which entity, on what date.

## Standards of proof (orient every conclusion to one)
- Ordinary civil claim: **preponderance**.
- Fraud / fraudulent transfer (actual intent) / fraud on the court: many courts
  require **clear and convincing** ⟦VERIFY by jurisdiction⟧.
- Criminal: **beyond a reasonable doubt** (you only *flag* exposure; you don't charge).

## Output discipline
- Lead with a 3-5 line **bottom line**.
- Then **Findings** (fact → authority → conclusion), **Counter-arguments**,
  **Missing evidence**, **Recommended next steps with deadlines**.
- Mark unverified law `⟦VERIFY⟧`. One non-advice disclaimer per deliverable.
