# 08 — Criminal & Federal Exposure (spotting only)

## Florida state exposure
- **Criminal usury / loan-sharking** — Fla. Stat. §687.071: charging/collecting
  willfully at a **criminal rate** (>25%; >45% felony). Aggregate stated rate + default
  rate + per-diem + fees.
- **Organized fraud / scheme to defraud** — Fla. Stat. §817.034 (Florida Communications
  Fraud Act): a systematic, ongoing course of conduct to defraud, value-tiered felony.
- **False statements / perjury** in court filings — Fla. Stat. ch. 837 (perjury in
  official proceedings; false official statements); sworn pleadings/affidavits that are
  knowingly false.
- **Grand theft** — §812.014 — where money is obtained by deception.
- **Filing false documents / fraudulent court filings** — ⟦VERIFY specific statute⟧.

## Federal hooks (when an interstate wire, bank, or bankruptcy is involved)
- **Wire fraud** — 18 U.S.C. §1343: scheme to defraud + interstate wire (emails,
  e-filing, wire transfers, ACH). Each wire is a count.
- **Bank fraud** — 18 U.S.C. §1344: scheme to defraud a financial institution.
- **Bankruptcy fraud** — 18 U.S.C. §§152, 157: concealing assets, false oaths,
  fraudulent transfers in contemplation of bankruptcy; using a sham judgment to defeat
  creditors near a filing.
- **Money laundering** — 18 U.S.C. §§1956/1957: moving proceeds of specified unlawful
  activity.
- **RICO** — 18 U.S.C. §§1961-1968 (and Florida RICO, Ch. 895): an *enterprise* + a
  *pattern* of racketeering acts (wire fraud, usury/"collection of unlawful debt,"
  etc.). Usurious lending at >2x the enforceable rate is expressly "unlawful debt"
  under federal RICO ⟦VERIFY 18 U.S.C. §1961(6)⟧ — a powerful civil-RICO hook with
  treble damages for victims.

## Investigative build (for the panel)
1. **Predicate conduct** — identify the specific false statement / unlawful charge /
   concealed asset and the document that proves it.
2. **Interstate/federal nexus** — wires, banks, e-filing across state lines, a
   bankruptcy.
3. **Intent** — pattern, control of both sides, concealment, timing vs. other
   creditors.
4. **Victims & loss** — who was hindered; quantify.
5. **Referral** — state: State Attorney / OFR (lenders) / AG; federal: FBI / U.S.
   Attorney / IRS-CI; bankruptcy: U.S. Trustee. Civil-RICO suit by victims in parallel.

## Hard limit
Do not draft anything to *commit* fraud, conceal assets, or deceive a tribunal.
Advising how to *detect, report, attack, or defend* such conduct is proper.
