# 03 — Florida Civil Litigation

⟦VERIFY all rule/statute numbers against current Fla. R. Civ. P. and Fla. Stat.⟧

## Pleadings & exhibits
- **Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.130**: contracts/notes sued upon must be attached. The attached
  instrument controls over inconsistent allegations (a "blank exhibit" or one that
  contradicts the body is fatal/embarrassing).
- Counts must plead each element; "all conditions precedent performed" (Rule 1.120(c))
  is a general allegation that can be specifically denied.

## Service / appearance
- Personal service (Ch. 48) vs. **waiver of service**. A defendant who "waives
  service" and never answers, followed by a fast judgment, is a default/consent
  pattern — examine whether the waivers were genuine and who signed them.

## Judgments
- **Default** (Rule 1.500) vs. **summary judgment** (Rule 1.510, now federal-style
  standard) vs. **consent/"agreed" final judgment** (parties stipulate). An *agreed*
  judgment among commonly-controlled entities is a hallmark to scrutinize.
- A money judgment should rest on competent proof of amount (affidavit/ledger), not
  just a face number — especially where the note says the balance "is not necessarily
  the face amount."

## Reestablishing a lost instrument
- **Fla. Stat. §673.3091** (lost, destroyed, or stolen instrument): plaintiff must
  prove it (i) was *entitled to enforce* when loss occurred (or acquired from such a
  person), (ii) loss was *not* by transfer or lawful seizure, and (iii) *cannot
  reasonably obtain* possession. Court **must** provide the obligor *adequate
  protection* against another holder. Pleading "lost" while a fully executed copy
  exists, or where the proponent likely holds the original, is a vulnerability.

## Relief from judgment — Rule 1.540(b)
- Grounds: (1) mistake/excusable neglect; (2) newly discovered evidence; (3) **fraud,
  misrepresentation, or other misconduct** of an adverse party; (4) void judgment;
  (5) satisfied/no longer equitable.
- **One-year** limit for (1)-(3); "reasonable time" for (4)-(5).
- The rule's savings clause preserves the court's power to entertain an **independent
  action** and to **set aside a judgment for fraud upon the court** — *no fixed time
  limit*. (See fraud module.)

## Appeals
- Notice of appeal generally within **30 days** of rendition (Fla. R. App. P. 9.110).
  Don't let a vacatur strategy blow the appellate clock.
