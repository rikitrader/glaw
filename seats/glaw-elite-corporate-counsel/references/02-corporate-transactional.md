# 02 — Corporate & Transactional

## Entities & authority
- **LLC ≠ corporation.** LLCs have *members/managers*, *Articles of Organization*,
  an *Operating Agreement*; corporations have *shareholders/directors*, *Articles of
  Incorporation*, *Bylaws*. A "promissory note" that calls an LLC "the Corporation"
  and references "Directors," "Bylaws," "dividends," or "shareholders" is a template
  mismatch — flag it; it can signal an unconformed form and undermines "validly
  authorized" reps. (Fla. Rev. LLC Act, Ch. 605; Del. LLC Act, 6 Del. C. ch. 18.)
- **Authority to bind.** Confirm signer's capacity, that the entity authorized the
  deal (resolutions / written consent), and that required members/managers signed.
  A guaranty by an individual must be signed *in the individual capacity* — a
  signature only as "CEO"/"Managing Member" generally does **not** bind the person.
- **Good standing / existence.** Certificates of good standing and the actual
  Articles/Operating Agreement must *exist and be attached* where a closing
  certificate swears they are. A sworn "attached hereto as Exhibit __" with a blank
  exhibit is a false certification.

## Secured lending & UCC Article 9 (Fla. Stat. Ch. 679)
- **Attachment**: value + debtor rights in collateral + authenticated security
  agreement (or possession/control). **Perfection**: usually UCC-1 financing
  statement filed where the debtor is "located" (state of organization for
  registered entities). **Priority**: generally first-to-file-or-perfect; PMSI and
  control exceptions.
- Lien searches, UCC-1 filings, and termination of prior UCC-3s are standard closing
  conditions — verify they were actually done, not just promised.
- An unsigned master agreement, blank schedules (indebtedness, liens, ownership), and
  a missing security agreement undercut both enforceability and perfection.

## Financing red flags
- Face amount that includes undisclosed fees rolled into "principal."
- "Loan" that is actually a restructured purchase/advance (see MCA module).
- Cross-guarantees by many affiliates controlled by one person (collusion / FUFTA risk).
- Mixed governing law across related instruments tied to the same lender.
- "Most favored terms," confessions of judgment, jury/venue waivers, attorney-in-fact
  powers — note them and who they favor.

## M&A / corporate (quick frame)
- Stock vs. asset vs. merger; reps & warranties, indemnity, baskets/caps, MAC clause,
  successor liability, bulk-sale/fraudulent-transfer overlay, change-of-control
  consents, IP/employment/tax diligence. Match each rep to a diligence artifact.
