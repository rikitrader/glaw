# Governance Documents

What governs the entity after formation. Draft from `templates/`; have local counsel review.

## Corporation — the core set
- **Bylaws** (`templates/bylaws-skeleton.md`) — the operating manual: shareholder meetings
  (annual/special, notice, quorum, voting, written consent), directors (number, election, term,
  classified board?, removal, vacancies, meetings, committees), officers (titles, duties),
  stock (certificates/uncertificated, transfer), indemnification, amendments, fiscal year.
- **Shareholders' agreement** — among holders: transfer restrictions, **ROFR / co-sale (tag-
  along)**, **drag-along**, preemptive rights, board-composition/voting commitments, information
  rights, deadlock/buy-sell, confidentiality, non-compete (where enforceable).
- **Voting agreement** (`templates/voting-agreement-skeleton.md`) — how shares are voted
  (board seats, founder control); often paired with an **irrevocable proxy**.
- **Equity plan + award agreements** — stock-option plan (ISO/NSO), RSUs, with §409A pricing.
- **Board/committee charters** for larger companies (audit, comp, nominating).

## LLC — the operating agreement does almost everything
`templates/llc-operating-agreement-skeleton.md`:
- **Management:** member-managed vs **manager-managed**; officer titles; authority/limits.
- **Capital:** initial contributions, additional-capital mechanics, capital accounts.
- **Allocations & distributions:** profit/loss allocations (with **§704(b)** language if special
  allocations), tax distributions, waterfall.
- **Membership interests:** classes/units, **transfer restrictions**, ROFR, drag/tag, admission of
  new members, buy-sell on death/withdrawal/default.
- **Governance:** voting thresholds, reserved/major decisions, deadlock resolution, books/records,
  fiduciary-duty modification (permitted in DE/most states), dissolution.

## Partnership / LP
Partnership or LP agreement: capital, allocations, management authority (GP control), limited-
partner protections, transfer restrictions, dissolution. Use an **entity GP** to cap GP liability.

## Decisions are made by writing
- **Written consent** in lieu of a meeting (board or holders) — fast, but follow the statute's
  consent rules (unanimous vs majority; notice to non-consenters).
- **Board/shareholder minutes** for every material action (issuances, comp, big contracts, debt,
  related-party deals) — this is what preserves the **corporate veil** (cross-ref
  `elite-corporate-counsel`).

## Cross-class protective provisions (common to all forms)
Transfer restrictions, ROFR/co-sale, drag/tag, preemptive/anti-dilution, information rights,
confidentiality/IP, non-solicit, and dispute resolution (forum + arbitration). For **founder
control** structures (dual-class, voting trusts), see `founder-control-and-dual-class.md`.
