# Ongoing Compliance Calendar

Keep the entity in good standing and the veil intact. Build a per-entity calendar. Verify all
amounts/dates per SKILL.md Step 1a — they're state-specific and change.

## Annual / recurring state obligations

| Obligation | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Annual report / statement of information** | Most states; CA Statement of Information (every 1–2 yrs); missing it → loss of good standing |
| **Franchise / privilege tax** | **Delaware** franchise tax (corps; min + assumed-par-value method — can be large, calc carefully) + $300 LLC tax; **California** $800 minimum franchise tax (corps & LLCs); **Texas** margin (franchise) tax; verify each state |
| **Registered agent** | Maintain in formation state + every foreign-qualified state |
| **Foreign qualification** | Register in every state with **nexus** (employees, property, sales); failure → penalties + inability to sue in that state |

## Federal / tax compliance
- **Income/info returns** — 1120 (C), 1120-S (S), 1065 (partnership/LLC), Schedule C (SMLLC/sole
  prop). Coordinate with `tax-strategy` / `tax-compliance`.
- **S-corp reasonable compensation** — pay shareholder-employees a **defensible W-2 wage** before
  distributions (IRS FS-2008-25; under-paying salary to dodge payroll tax is the #1 S-corp audit
  issue). Document with a comp study.
- **Payroll** (941/940, W-2) and **1099-NEC** for contractors; **Form 5472** for foreign-owned
  single-member LLCs and 25%-foreign-owned corps.

## CTA / Beneficial Ownership (FinCEN) — CURRENT RULE
- Under the **March 26, 2025 interim final rule**, the definition of "reporting company" was
  narrowed: **all entities created in the US ("domestic reporting companies") and US persons are
  EXEMPT** from BOI reporting.
- **Only foreign reporting companies** (formed abroad, registered to do business in a US state)
  must file BOI — and they do **not** report US-person beneficial owners. Deadlines: pre-3/26/2025
  registrants by 4/25/2025; later registrants within 30 days of effective registration.
- FinCEN is finalizing the rule — **verify current status on fincen.gov before advising**; don't
  rely on the pre-2025 "all small companies must file" version.

## Corporate housekeeping (preserves the veil)
- **Minute book** — annual shareholder + board meetings (or written consents); document material
  decisions (issuances, comp, loans, big contracts, related-party deals).
- **Separate finances** — own bank accounts, no commingling, adequate capitalization, arm's-length
  intercompany terms. (Sloppiness here is what lets a creditor pierce — `elite-corporate-counsel`.)
- **Cap table + stock ledger** kept current; option-plan administration; 409A refreshes.

## Lifecycle events
- **Amendments** (charter/bylaws/OA) — board + holder approval per the documents + state filing.
- **Foreign qualification / withdrawal** as you enter/exit states.
- **Dissolution / wind-down** — formal dissolution filing, creditor notice, final returns, asset
  distribution; don't just "stop filing" (accruing penalties + personal exposure).
