# Statutes of Limitations & Taxpayer Rights

## The three clocks (quick reference)

| Clock | Limits | Starts | Length | Authority |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **RSED** | Getting a refund | Original due date | 3 yrs (or 2 yrs from payment) | IRC §6511 |
| **ASED** | IRS assessing tax | Return **filed** | 3 yrs (6 if >25% omitted; ∞ if fraud/no return) | IRC §6501 |
| **CSED** | IRS collecting assessed tax | Date of **assessment** | 10 yrs | IRC §6502 |

**Key consequence of not filing:** the ASED never starts, so an unfiled year stays open
**forever**. Filing starts the clock — which is one reason coming into compliance helps even
when there's a balance.

## CSED — the 10-year collection clock and tolling

The 10 years runs from assessment, but certain events **suspend (toll)** it, extending the end
date:
- A pending **OIC** (+ 30 days after).
- A pending **CDP hearing** request.
- **Bankruptcy** automatic stay (+ 6 months).
- Time the taxpayer is **out of the country** ≥ 6 months.
- Pending **innocent-spouse** request, certain **IA** requests.

Pull the **Account Transcript** to find the assessment date and estimate the CSED; flag if the
debt is near expiration (collection alternatives like CNC can run it out).

## Collection notice escalation (CP-series)

Typical sequence (numbers/letters vary):
1. **CP14** — first notice of balance due.
2. **CP501 / CP502 / CP503** — reminders.
3. **CP504** — Notice of Intent to Levy (state refunds / limited assets) — *not* yet full CDP.
4. **Letter 1058 / CP90 / CP1058** — **Final Notice of Intent to Levy and Notice of Your Right
   to a Hearing** → triggers **CDP rights** and a **30-day** clock. This is the urgent one.
5. **CP523** — IA default / intent to terminate.

## Lien vs. levy

- **Lien** = legal *claim* securing the debt (Notice of Federal Tax Lien is public).
- **Levy** = actual *seizure* (bank, wages, receivables). Wage garnishment is a continuous levy.

## Collection Due Process (CDP) — Form 12153

- A Final Notice of Intent to Levy or a Notice of Federal Tax Lien filing gives the right to a
  **CDP hearing** before the IRS Independent Office of Appeals.
- **File Form 12153 within 30 days** to preserve the right to Tax Court review and to suspend
  levy action. Past 30 days (within ~1 year) you can get an **Equivalent Hearing** — but
  without the levy-suspension and Tax Court guarantees.
- At the hearing you can raise collection alternatives (IA, OIC, CNC), spousal defenses, and—in
  limited cases—the underlying liability. **Deadline-sensitive; flag it immediately.**

## Taxpayer Bill of Rights (orient the user)

Ten rights including: to be informed, to quality service, to pay no more than the correct
amount, to challenge and be heard, to appeal, to finality, to privacy, to confidentiality, to
retain representation, and to a fair and just tax system.

## Taxpayer Advocate Service (TAS) — Form 911

An independent organization within the IRS. Use when:
- The taxpayer faces **economic harm / hardship** (e.g., imminent levy on essential funds).
- An IRS process has **broken down** or stalled past normal timeframes.
- Normal channels have failed.

File **Form 911** (Request for Taxpayer Advocate Service Assistance). TAS can issue a Taxpayer
Assistance Order.

## Representation

- **Form 2848** (Power of Attorney) — lets a CPA/EA/attorney *represent* and speak for the
  taxpayer.
- **Form 8821** (Tax Information Authorization) — lets a third party *receive* info only.
- Only an **attorney** carries privilege; a **§7525** limited practitioner privilege exists for
  CPAs/EAs in *non-criminal* federal tax matters but **not** for criminal matters or return
  preparation — another reason to route potential-criminal cases to a tax attorney first (see
  persona-and-guardrails.md).
