# Worked Examples

Three end-to-end cases showing how the 9-step workflow and the Step 9 output template come
together. Dollar amounts are illustrative; verify current rates/thresholds per Step 1a.

---

## Example 1 — Classic non-filer, no IRS contact yet

**Facts:** Individual, W-2 employee, didn't file 2019–2024 (6 years). No IRS notices. Had
withholding each year; thinks a couple of years had refunds. Today is mid-2026.

**Bottom line / next action:** Pull Wage & Income transcripts for all 6 years today, then file
2020 immediately — its refund expires soon under RSED.

**Year-by-year (today ≈ 2026):**

| Year | File? | Owe/Refund | Deadline driver | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2019 | Yes (6-yr rule) | Refund | **RSED expired** (>3 yrs past due) — refund lost | File for compliance only |
| 2020 | Yes | Refund | RSED ~closing — **urgent** | File first |
| 2021 | Yes | Refund | RSED open | File |
| 2022–2024 | Yes | Small balance | None urgent | File oldest→newest |

**Sequence:** (1) transcripts; (2) reconcile income; (3) file 2020 → 2024 oldest-first; (4)
balance from 2022–24 is small → short-term plan or pay in full.

**Penalty relief:** clean prior history → **FTA** on the earliest balance-due year (2022).
Other years: small/no penalty; pay and move on. No reasonable-cause story needed.

**Statute flags:** 2019 refund **forfeited**; 2020 refund must be claimed now. Filing starts
each year's ASED.

**Disclaimer:** ordinary cleanup — no criminal flags. Reassure; recommend a preparer/EA to
file accurately.

## Example 2 — SFR filed + Final Notice of Intent to Levy

**Facts:** Sole proprietor (Schedule C), unfiled 2021–2023. IRS filed **SFRs** for 2021–2022
showing $48k owed with no business expenses. Just received **Letter 1058** (Final Notice of
Intent to Levy) dated 12 days ago.

**Bottom line / next action:** The levy notice is the fire — **file Form 12153 (CDP request)
within the 30-day window** (≈18 days left) to stop the levy and preserve Tax Court rights, then
replace the SFRs with real returns to slash the $48k.

**Year-by-year:**

| Year | File? | Owe/Refund | Deadline driver | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2021 | Yes — **replace SFR** | Owe, but ↓ once expenses added | CDP 30-day clock; SFR assessed | File original 1040 |
| 2022 | Yes — **replace SFR** | Owe, ↓ with expenses | SFR assessed → CSED running | File original 1040 |
| 2023 | Yes (not yet SFR'd) | Owe | None urgent | File |

**Sequence:** (1) **Form 12153 now**; (2) Wage & Income + Account transcripts (confirm SFR
assessments + CSED); (3) reconstruct Schedule C expenses from bank records (consider
`financial-forensics`); (4) file actual 2021–2023 originals (1040, *not* 1040-X) to replace the
SFRs and cut the liability; (5) propose a collection alternative at the CDP hearing.

**Balance resolution:** after SFR replacement the real balance is far lower. Likely a
**streamlined Installment Agreement** (≤ $50k, ≤ 72 months); evaluate **OIC** only if RCP < the
reduced liability. While the IA is active, FTP drops to 0.25%/mo **only on timely-filed years**
— these were late, so 0.5% continues.

**Penalty relief:** FTA on the earliest year if the 3-year prior history is clean; otherwise
reasonable cause only if real facts support it (the SFR/levy stress alone does not).

**Statute flags:** CDP deadline is the controlling date. SFR assessments already started the
**CSED** (10-yr) clock — note the dates from the Account transcript.

**Disclaimer:** Schedule C cash business → screen Step 1d carefully. If income was concealed or
returns willfully skipped, route to a tax attorney **before** filing.

## Example 3 — Non-willful expat with foreign accounts

**Facts:** U.S. citizen living abroad 8 years; filed no U.S. returns and no FBARs; foreign
salary + bank accounts peaking at $120k. Says they genuinely didn't know U.S. citizens must
file on worldwide income. No IRS contact.

**Bottom line / next action:** This is a **Streamlined Foreign Offshore Procedures (SFOP)**
case if the non-residency (physical-presence) test is met and the conduct was truly
non-willful — likely **penalty-free**. Confirm non-willfulness honestly before certifying.

**Sequence:**
1. Confirm SFOP non-residency test (physical presence outside the U.S.).
2. Prepare **3 years** of delinquent/amended returns (with foreign earned income exclusion
   (Form 2555) and/or foreign tax credit (Form 1116) — often little or no U.S. tax due).
3. E-file **6 years** of delinquent **FBARs (FinCEN 114)** via BSA E-Filing.
4. File **Form 14653** (foreign certification) with the non-willfulness narrative, signed under
   penalty of perjury.
5. Check **Form 8938 (FATCA)** thresholds and any **5471/3520** if foreign entities/trusts exist.

**Penalty relief:** SFOP typically waives the offshore penalty for foreign residents (the **5%**
penalty applies to the *domestic* SDOP, not SFOP). FBAR penalties avoided via the program.

**Statute flags:** none expiring imminently, but file before any IRS contact — once contacted,
Streamlined is foreclosed.

**Disclaimer & gate (critical):** Streamlined requires a **non-willfulness certification**.
If there's any chance the conduct was willful (knew of the obligation and chose not to comply),
SFOP is the **wrong** door and a false certification is itself a serious offense — route to a
**tax attorney** for the Voluntary Disclosure Practice instead (Step 1d). Do not draft the
14653 narrative if the facts don't honestly support non-willfulness.
