# Copy-Ready Templates

Fill every `[BRACKET]`. Never invent facts to fit a relief category — if the facts don't
support the narrative, don't write it; route to FTA or a payment plan instead. Each draft
should close by reminding the user to review for accuracy and sign.

---

## 1. Reasonable-Cause Penalty Abatement Letter (written statement)

```
[Date]

Internal Revenue Service
[Address from the notice]

Re: Request for Penalty Abatement — Reasonable Cause
    Taxpayer: [Name]      SSN/EIN: [xxx-xx-1234]
    Tax Form: [1040]      Tax Period(s): [YYYY]
    Notice/Letter No.: [CPxxxx], dated [date]

To Whom It May Concern:

I am writing to request abatement of the [failure-to-file / failure-to-pay] penalt[y/ies]
assessed for the tax period(s) above, in the amount of $[amount], based on reasonable cause
under IRC § 6651 and IRM 20.1.1.3.2.

[FACTS — what happened, in plain chronological order. Tie directly to a recognized reasonable-
cause ground: serious illness, death in the family, natural disaster, records destroyed/
unobtainable, reliance on a tax professional for a matter of judgment, etc. Be specific about
dates.]

During this period I exercised ordinary business care and prudence but was nevertheless unable
to [file/pay] on time because [reason]. As soon as [the circumstance resolved], I took prompt
action to come into compliance by [filing the return on (date) / arranging payment].

I have enclosed supporting documentation: [medical records / death certificate / FEMA
declaration / insurance or fire report / correspondence, etc.].

I respectfully request that the penalt[y/ies] and associated interest be abated. Please contact
me at [phone/email] if you need any further information.

Sincerely,
[Signature]
[Printed name]
[Address / phone]
Enclosures: [list]
```

## 2. First-Time Abatement (FTA)

### 2a. Phone script
```
"I'd like to request first-time penalty abatement for the [failure-to-file / failure-to-pay]
penalty on my [Form 1040] for tax year [YYYY].

I believe I qualify: I have no penalties in the three years before [YYYY], all my required
returns are filed, and my balance is [paid / under an installment agreement]."

— Ask the agent to confirm the abatement, the amount, and get their ID badge number and a
reference/confirmation number. Note the date and time of the call.
```

### 2b. FTA letter (if you prefer paper / large balance)
```
Re: Request for First-Time Abate (FTA) — [Form], Tax Year [YYYY]
    Taxpayer / SSN-EIN / Notice No.

I request abatement of the [FTF/FTP] penalty for [YYYY] under the IRS First-Time Abate
administrative waiver (IRM 20.1.1.3.3.2.1). I meet the criteria:
  1. No penalties were assessed for the three prior tax years ([YYYY-3] through [YYYY-1]);
  2. All currently required returns are filed; and
  3. My balance is [paid in full / covered by an active installment agreement].

Please abate the penalty and any related interest, and confirm in writing.
```

## 3. Form 843 — Claim for Refund and Request for Abatement

Use when the penalty was **already paid** (claiming it back), or for statutory/written-advice
claims. Key lines:
- **Line 1**: tax period.  **Line 3**: check the penalty box; cite **IRC § 6651** (or the
  applicable section).  **Line 4**: the IRM/penalty reference.
- **Line 5a/5b**: reason — reasonable cause / FTA / IRS written advice.
- **Line 7**: the explanation — attach the narrative from Template 1, plus documentation.
- For incorrect IRS written advice: attach **your written request** and the **IRS written
  response**, citing **IRC § 6404(f)**.

## 4. Installment Agreement cover narrative (attach to Form 9465 or use with OPA)

```
I am requesting a monthly installment agreement for my outstanding balance of $[amount] for
tax year(s) [YYYY]. I propose monthly payments of $[amount] on the [Nth] of each month, by
direct debit from [bank]. This amount reflects my ability to pay after necessary living
expenses. All my required returns are filed and I will remain current on future obligations.
```

## 5. Offer in Compromise — doubt-as-to-collectibility narrative (attach to Form 656 / 433-A(OIC))

```
I submit this Offer in Compromise based on doubt as to collectibility. My Reasonable Collection
Potential, computed on the enclosed Form 433-A(OIC), is $[RCP], consisting of $[net realizable
equity in assets] plus $[future income component]. This is less than the assessed liability of
$[liability]. I offer $[offer amount], payable as a [lump sum within 5 months / periodic
payments over 6–24 months].

My monthly income is $[X] and my allowable living expenses under the IRS Collection Financial
Standards are $[Y], leaving $[Z] in monthly disposable income. [Brief facts: income source,
household size, health, age, employment stability.] All required returns are filed and my
current-year withholding/estimated payments are current.
```

> Reminder to the user: OIC acceptance is never guaranteed; the IRS accepts when the offer
> meets or exceeds RCP. If RCP exceeds what you can offer, an installment agreement or Currently
> Not Collectible status is the realistic path.

## 6. CDP hearing request — Form 12153

File **within 30 days** of a Final Notice of Intent to Levy / NFTL filing. On the form:
- Identify the tax/periods and the notice.
- Check the basis: **lien** and/or **levy**.
- State the **collection alternative** you want considered (installment agreement / OIC /
  currently not collectible) and any other issue (e.g., spousal defense, the penalty itself).
- Sign and send to the address on the notice. Keep proof of timely mailing.

---

## 7. Taxpayer Advocate — Form 911

Use for economic hardship or a stalled IRS process. State the hardship concretely (e.g.,
"a levy on my wages will leave me unable to pay rent and basic living expenses"), what relief
you need, and attach supporting proof. Submit to your local Taxpayer Advocate office.
