# Collection Resolution: Paying Down or Settling the Balance

Resolve the balance *after* all required returns are filed (most programs require filing
compliance). Verify current dollar thresholds per SKILL.md Step 1a.

## Pick the path by ability to pay

| Ability to pay | Best path | Form / channel |
|---|---|---|
| In full, now | Pay in full | Preserves clean record for FTA |
| In full within ~180 days | Short-term payment plan | Online Payment Agreement (no setup fee) |
| Monthly over time | **Installment Agreement** | Online OPA or **Form 9465** |
| Less than full, has assets/income | **Offer in Compromise** (doubt as to collectibility) | **Form 656** + **433-A(OIC)** (individual) / **433-B(OIC)** (business) |
| Can't pay anything without hardship | **Currently Not Collectible** | **Form 433-F** (or 433-A) |

## Installment Agreement (IA)

- **Streamlined IA** (Fresh Start): individuals owing **≤ $50,000** combined tax/penalty/
  interest can generally get up to **72 months** with minimal financial disclosure via the
  Online Payment Agreement. Higher thresholds exist for short-term plans.
- **Form 9465** for paper/by-mail or amounts/situations the online tool won't take.
- While an IA is active, the **FTP penalty drops to 0.25%/month** — but only if the return for
  that liability was **timely filed** (including extensions) (IRC §6651(h)). Late-filed years
  keep the 0.5% rate even under an IA.
- A direct-debit IA reduces default risk and may avoid/withdraw a lien at certain thresholds.
- IAs don't stop interest; the balance keeps accruing until paid.

## Offer in Compromise (OIC)

Settle for less than owed. Most offers are **doubt as to collectibility** — the IRS accepts
when your **Reasonable Collection Potential (RCP)** is less than the liability.

**RCP formula (doubt as to collectibility):**

```
RCP = Net realizable equity in assets
      + (monthly disposable income × multiplier)

  Net realizable equity = quick-sale value of assets − encumbrances − exemptions
  Monthly disposable income = monthly income − allowable living expenses
        (IRS Collection Financial Standards: housing, food, transport, etc.)
  Multiplier ≈ 12 (lump-sum offer) or 24 (periodic-payment offer)  ← verify current rules
```

The IRS will accept an offer **≥ RCP**. Build the 433-A(OIC) carefully — allowable-expense
standards, not actual spending, govern. Mechanics:
- Non-refundable **application fee** + initial payment due with Form 656 (low-income waiver
  exists).
- **All returns must be filed** and estimated payments/withholding current, or the OIC is
  returned unprocessed.
- During review the **CSED is suspended**; a rejected offer can be appealed (Form 13711).
- **Acceptance is never guaranteed** — set honest expectations. Many offers are rejected
  because RCP exceeds the offered amount; an IA or CNC is often the realistic outcome.
- After acceptance, the taxpayer must stay compliant for **5 years** or the offer defaults.

There are also **doubt as to liability** (you don't actually owe it — Form 656-L) and
**effective tax administration** (you can pay but it'd be inequitable) offers; rarer.

## Currently Not Collectible (CNC)

- For genuine hardship: paying anything would prevent meeting basic living expenses.
- File **Form 433-F** (or 433-A) showing income ≤ allowable expenses.
- Collection pauses, but **interest/penalties keep accruing** and the **CSED keeps running** —
  so CNC can run out the 10-year clock on the debt. Liens may still be filed.
- The IRS reviews periodically and can reactivate collection if income rises.

## Fresh Start framework (umbrella)

The Fresh Start initiative raised the streamlined-IA threshold (to $50k), raised the lien-
filing threshold, made lien withdrawal easier with a direct-debit IA, and expanded OIC
allowable expenses. It's not a separate program — it's the set of relaxed rules inside IA/OIC/
lien procedures above.

## Liens vs. levies (so the user knows the stakes)

- **Notice of Federal Tax Lien** — a public claim against property; hurts credit/sales. Can be
  **withdrawn** (after IA/payment), **discharged** (specific property), or **subordinated**.
- **Levy** — actual seizure (bank account, wages). Preceded by a **Final Notice of Intent to
  Levy (Letter 1058 / CP90)** granting **CDP** rights — see statutes-and-rights.md. A levy is
  the urgent fire to put out first.
