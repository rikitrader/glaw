> https://www.thorwealthmanagement.com/robs/ . Ingested 2026-06-20. Secondary/practitioner source —
> corroborates the seat's primary authority; verify all positions against the Code/ERISA + current IRS guidance.

## Core concept
ROBS = "a way to use your pre-tax account(s) to fund your business **penalty-free and tax-free**" — provided
strict compliance is maintained. It substitutes for bank loans / SBA / angel / personal capital.

## The 5 steps (mechanics)
1. **Establish a new C corporation** — must be a *new* C-corp (not S-corp, not LLC) capable of selling
   Qualifying Employer Securities (QES = corporate stock).
2. **Create a 401(k) plan for the new corp** — traditional 401(k) is typical, but **defined-benefit or
   profit-sharing plans are also permitted**. The plan must contain **special plan language allowing
   participants to purchase company stock**, so it needs a **TPA** familiar with these specialized plans.
3. **Roll existing retirement funds into the new plan** — a transfer between qualified plans: **no
   distribution → no taxable event → no penalty**.
4. **The plan buys C-corp stock (the QES transaction)** — using the rolled-in funds from step 3.
5. **Deploy the capital** — "purchase equipment, lease or purchase a building, pay for franchise fees, etc."

## Compliance obligations
- **Form 5500 — from day one (the key nuance).** "All business owners engaged in a ROBS transaction must
  file Form 5500." This **differs from ordinary one-participant plans**, which need not file until **$250,000**
  in assets — a ROBS plan files *regardless*, because it owns a **hard-to-value private-company-stock** asset.
  Filed with the **Department of Labor + IRS**.
- **Offer the plan to all eligible employees — current AND future** (federal coverage requirement).
- **Standard 401(k) plan administration** applies; a TPA is recommended.
- The company must remain a **bona fide, active operating company**.

## Failure modes (each exits or breaks ROBS)
1. **Not offering the plan to all eligible employees** — coverage violation.
2. **Failing to file Form 5500** — automatic compliance violation.
3. **Converting to an S corporation** (or otherwise ceasing to "report as a C Corporation to the federal
   government") — *"a foundational requirement of the ROBS strategy"*; conversion **exits ROBS entirely**.
4. **Using ROBS solely to access retirement funds tax-free** — the entity must be a real operating company,
   not a cash-extraction shell.

## Net new for the seat (folded into Dossier §3/§5 compliance)
- **Form 5500 files from day one** (no $250k grace) — the sharpest practical addition.
- **The C-corp must stay a C-corp** — an S-corp/LLC conversion is a discrete, fatal failure mode.
- **DB / profit-sharing plans** can serve as the ROBS plan, not only a 401(k); plan needs employer-stock
  language + a ROBS-fluent TPA.
