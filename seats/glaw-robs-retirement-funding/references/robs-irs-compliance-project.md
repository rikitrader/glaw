# IRS ROBS Compliance Project (PRIMARY — the IRS's own position)

> **Source:** IRS — "Rollovers as Business Start-Ups Compliance Project" —
> https://www.irs.gov/retirement-plans/rollovers-as-business-start-ups-compliance-project . Ingested 2026-06-20.
> This is the **authoritative IRS page** (primary, government source). The 2008 EP ROBS Guidelines memorandum
> (Michael Julianelle, Director EP Rulings & Agreements) is the underlying authority — saved at
> `forms/robs_guidelines_2008_memo.pdf`.

## IRS position on ROBS (quote)
ROBS is "an arrangement in which prospective business owners use their retirement funds to pay for new business
start-up costs." Per the IRS, ROBS is **"not considered an abusive tax avoidance transaction,"** but it is
**"questionable because [it] may solely benefit one individual"** — the rollover individual. Promoters
"aggressively market" these arrangements.

## What the IRS found (the Project's results — sober reality)
- **Most ROBS businesses "either failed or were on the road to failure."**
- High rates of **business and personal bankruptcy, liens, and corporate dissolution**.
- Individuals **lost both their retirement assets and their business**.
- Retirement savings were often **"depleted or 'lost'"** *before the business even began operating*.
- **"Large recurring promoter fees or legal issues were a factor"** in failures.
> Carry this into Dossier §1/§8: ROBS puts the client's *retirement* at risk to fund the business — the IRS's
> own data is that most fail. This belongs in the candid risk framing, not buried.

## Compliance requirements the IRS lists
| Requirement | IRS detail |
|---|---|
| **Annual return** | **Form 5500** or **5500-EZ** required. Many sponsors wrongly believed an exception applied. |
| **5500-EZ one-participant exception does NOT apply** | The <$250k one-participant exception fails for ROBS because **"the plan, through its company stock investments, rather than the individual, owns the trade or business."** |
| **Nondiscrimination / coverage** | Plan amendments that **prevent other employees from participating** (e.g., locking out new hires from buying stock) violate qualification requirements. |
| **Benefits, rights & features** | Amendments can create BRF violations. |
| **Determination letter limits** | A DL is issued on plan *terms*; it **does NOT protect** against incorrect administration or discriminatory *operation*. |
| **Prohibited transactions** | A PT by the sponsor can **disqualify the plan** → adverse tax consequences to sponsor + participants. |
| **Stock valuation** | A compliance-check area requiring documentation. |
| **Recordkeeping & information reporting** | Contact letters request documentation. |
| **Form 1099-R** | "Failure to issue" 1099-R for the rolled-over assets was flagged as a problem. |
| **Form 1120** | IRS checked whether sponsors filed the C-corp return. |

## EP ROBS Project (2009) — three goals
1. Define traits of **compliant vs noncompliant** ROBS plans.
2. **Identify and correct** noncompliant ROBS plans.
3. Build compliance strategies (EPCRS, Fix-It Guides, web resources, newsletters).

Compliance-check questions covered: plan status, contribution history, rollover/transfer asset info,
participant info, **stock valuation and purchases**, business info, and **reasons for any missing 5500/5500-EZ
or 1120 filings.**

## Forms named by the IRS (downloaded — see `forms/` + `forms/README.md`)
- **Form 5500 / 5500-EZ** — annual plan return (REQUIRED for ROBS; no one-participant exception).
- **Form 1120** — U.S. C-corporation income tax return.
- **Form 1099-R** — report the rollover; failure to issue is a flagged defect.
- (Determination-letter applications **Form 5300**, and plan-termination **Form 5310**, downloaded for the
  DL / wind-down legs of the workflow.)
