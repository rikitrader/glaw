# Worked Example — Founder Heading Into a $40M Exit

A full end-to-end pass through the 8-step workflow, showing how the levers stack. Figures are
illustrative; verify all current limits/rates per Step 1a. This is a teaching example, not advice
for a specific person — every number here would be modeled and confirmed with counsel + a CPA.

## The facts (intake — Steps 1–2)

- **Who:** 44-year-old founder, married, two kids. Sole founder of a SaaS C-corp ("Newco").
- **Stock:** founded NewCo 6 years ago; took **§83(b)** at incorporation; basis ~$50k; stock is
  **QSBS** (C-corp, was <$50M gross assets at issuance, pre-7/4/2025 stock → **old $10M / 5-yr /
  100% rule applies**). Held **>5 years** → QSBS clock satisfied.
- **Event:** strategic buyer offers **$40M** cash for the stock, closing in ~9 months.
- **Other:** lives in **California** (13.3% top rate). ~$600k W-2 salary from NewCo. Modest
  401(k). No trusts. Worried about (a) the tax on the $40M, (b) lawsuits/creditors afterward,
  (c) leaving wealth to the kids efficiently.
- **Guardrail gate (1d):** clean, legitimate operating business; no evasion flags → proceed. All
  structures below need real substance and to be done **before** the sale closes.

**Honest framing (Step 2):** the biggest wins here are **QSBS exclusion + QSBS stacking + state
move**, executed *before* closing. After closing, most of the leverage is gone — timing is
everything.

## The plan (Steps 3–7), in execution order

### 1. Lock the QSBS exclusion — and multiply it (Steps 3–4)
- Base **§1202**: up to **$10M** of gain excluded from federal tax (pre-7/4/2025 stock). On a $40M
  sale with ~$50k basis, that shelters the first $10M of the ~$39.95M gain.
- **QSBS stacking** — *before signing/closing*, gift tranches of NewCo stock to **non-grantor
  trusts** for the spouse and each child (and/or to the children directly). Each separate
  non-grantor taxpayer gets **its own $10M §1202 cap**. Four buckets (founder + 3 trusts) →
  potentially **~$40M excluded**, i.e., much/most of the gain federally tax-free.
  - Requires: real, completed gifts (uses lifetime gift exemption — see Step 5), trusts that are
    **non-grantor** (so each is a separate taxpayer for §1202), drafted and funded **before** the
    sale is "substantially certain" (avoid assignment-of-income), defensible valuations.
- Any gain above the stacked exclusions: see **§1045 rollover** into new QSBS as a fallback for
  the remainder.

### 2. Beat the 13.3% California tax — residency (Step 6)
- The federal QSBS exclusion **does not bind California** (CA doesn't conform to §1202) — so the
  state tax is a separate, large problem.
- Consider **establishing residency in a no-income-tax state** (TX/FL/NV/WA) **well before**
  closing — real domicile change (home, license, voter reg, days, family). 
- **Caution:** CA aggressively audits departing founders and taxes income **sourced** to CA / that
  accrued while resident; a move timed too close to closing invites a residency audit. This must be
  done early and for real, with counsel — it's the highest-risk lever here. Model CA's claim
  honestly; don't promise certainty.

### 3. Move appreciation out of the estate (Step 5)
- The gifts to the children's/spouse's trusts in Step 1 do **double duty**: they lock extra QSBS
  caps **and** remove that stock's value (and all future growth) from the founder's taxable
  estate, using the **lifetime gift/estate exemption** (use it before any scheduled reduction).
- For assets the founder keeps, consider a **GRAT** funded with pre-IPO/illiquid upside, or an
  **IDGT sale** to freeze estate value while the founder pays the trust's income tax (a further
  tax-free transfer to the kids).
- A **SLAT** for the spouse uses exemption now while keeping indirect family access.

### 4. Diversify a concentrated position without the gain (Step 5)
- If the founder wanted to keep some appreciated stock and diversify, a **Charitable Remainder
  Trust (CRT)** could sell inside the trust tax-deferred, pay an income stream, and yield a
  deduction — useful if charitable intent exists. (Here the sale is all-cash, so this mainly
  applies to any retained/rolled equity.)
- **Donate appreciated shares** to a **donor-advised fund** in the sale year for a large deduction
  at FMV + gain avoidance, if charitably inclined.

### 5. Shelter the ordinary income & build the retirement layer (Step 5)
- Pre-sale, max the **401(k)** + consider a **defined-benefit/cash-balance plan** at NewCo for the
  founder's final high-comp years (six-figure deductible contributions) — coordinate before the
  company changes hands.
- **Mega-backdoor Roth** if NewCo's plan allows, to move after-tax dollars into Roth.

### 6. Protect the post-sale wealth from lawsuits (Step 6)
- **Before** any claim exists (post-close, when no litigation is pending): umbrella + adequate
  liability insurance first.
- Hold investment assets in **LLCs by silo**; consider a **DAPT** (NV/SD/AK…) or, for serious
  deterrence, an offshore APT — **tax-neutral** grantor trusts that shield from *future* creditors
  after seasoning. Never fund these to dodge a known/pending claim (UVTA/FUFTA).
- The irrevocable gift trusts from Steps 1/3 are *already* creditor-protected — an overlap win.

### 7. Document everything (Step 7)
- Business-purpose memos, trust instruments, gift-tax returns (Form 709) reporting the stock
  gifts, contemporaneous valuations, residency-change evidence file, and §1202 qualification
  representations in the deal documents.

## Step 8 — what the founder hears

**Bottom line:** "If we act *before* the sale closes, we can likely make most of the $40M gain
**federally tax-free** (QSBS + stacking across family trusts), neutralize or sharply reduce the
**13.3% California** bite with a real, early residency move, and simultaneously push the wealth
**out of your estate** and **out of future creditors' reach** — using the same gifts. The window
closes at signing. After that, you're mostly paying full freight."

**Ranked moves:**

| Lever | Est. benefit | Complexity / risk | Substance required |
|---|---|---|---|
| QSBS base exclusion (§1202) | ~$10M gain federally tax-free | Low (already qualifies) | Hold + qualification docs |
| **QSBS stacking** via family non-grantor trusts | Up to ~$30M *more* excluded | High — must complete gifts pre-sale, valuations, non-grantor drafting | Real, completed gifts; trust substance |
| **CA → no-tax-state** residency | Up to ~13.3% of gain | High — residency-audit risk; must be early & genuine | Real domicile change |
| Estate freeze (same gifts + GRAT/IDGT/SLAT) | Removes growth from taxable estate | Medium–High | Irrevocable transfers |
| DB/cash-balance + Roth (final comp years) | Shelters ordinary income | Medium | Bona fide plan |
| Asset-protection trusts/LLCs (post-close) | Creditor deterrence | Medium | Before any claim; no fraudulent transfer |

**What we did NOT recommend:** offshore IP boxes (irrelevant + closed), any "structure" without
real gifts/substance (QSBS stacking fails if the gifts aren't complete and real — *assignment of
income* and economic-substance doctrine), and funding asset protection after a claim arises.

**Disclaimer:** illustrative and informational — not advice for a specific person. Every figure
must be modeled and every document drafted/reviewed by a licensed tax attorney + CPA, and the
residency and QSBS-stacking timing confirmed, **before** the transaction. Verify all current
limits/rates on IRS.gov.
