# Guardrails, Economic Substance & the Honest Reality

**Read this first. It governs every recommendation.** Verify current figures per SKILL.md Step 1a.

## The bright line: avoidance (legal) vs evasion (illegal)

- **Tax avoidance** — arranging affairs to minimize tax using structures and elections the law
  permits. **Legal and encouraged** (*Gregory v. Helvering*; *"anyone may arrange his affairs so
  that his taxes shall be as low as possible"* — Learned Hand).
- **Tax evasion** — concealing income, falsifying records, fabricating deductions, sham entities,
  backdating. **Felony** (§7201). We never help with this.

You help with avoidance **that has economic substance**. The difference between a respected
strategy and a felony is usually (a) real substance and (b) honest disclosure — not cleverness.

## Economic substance doctrine — §7701(o) (the rule that kills hollow structures)

A transaction is respected only if it passes a **two-part conjunctive test**:
1. It **changes the taxpayer's economic position** in a meaningful way (apart from tax effects), AND
2. The taxpayer has a **substantial non-tax business purpose** for it.

Fail either prong and the tax benefits are disallowed **plus** a **strict-liability penalty** of
**20%** (§6662(b)(6)) — **40%** if the transaction wasn't disclosed (§6662(i)). Strict liability
means *no reasonable-cause defense*. This is why every structure here must have a real business
purpose, real operations, and arm's-length terms.

## Transfer pricing — §482

Intercompany charges (royalties, management fees, intercompany sales) must be at **arm's length**
— what unrelated parties would pay. The IRS can reallocate income among related entities that
don't price at arm's length, and contemporaneous documentation is required (penalty exposure
under §6662(e)). Any "license your IP to your own holdco" plan lives or dies on a defensible
royalty rate and a real transfer of rights.

## Reportable / listed / abusive transactions

- Certain transactions must be **disclosed on Form 8886** (reportable transactions); failure
  carries its own penalties (§6707A).
- The IRS publishes the annual **"Dirty Dozen"** and lists abusive shelters (e.g., abusive
  micro-captive insurance, syndicated conservation easements, monetized installment sales,
  abusive trust schemes, Puerto Rico Act 60 arrangements *without bona-fide residence*). **Never
  recommend a pattern on these lists.** If a client asks about one, explain the exposure.

## The honest reality (do not oversell the "billionaire playbook")

1. **The Double Irish / Dutch Sandwich is dead.** Ireland closed it (fully by 2020); OECD BEPS,
   US GILTI/NCTI (~14% floor), and the **Pillar Two 15% global minimum tax** (MNE groups
   >€750M revenue) ended the era of near-zero offshore IP rates. Don't propose it.
2. **Offshore IP licensing needs real foreign substance and massive scale** — and still faces
   GILTI/NCTI, FDDEI, and Pillar Two. It is not a small-business move.
3. **"Buy-borrow-die" is real but conditional** — it needs highly appreciated assets, lender
   access, and tolerance for leverage; the magic is the **§1014 step-up at death**, which a
   future Congress could change. It defers, it doesn't erase, and it carries market/interest risk.
4. **Most of a real client's savings are mundane** — entity choice, retirement/deferral, QBI,
   depreciation, QSBS, equity-comp timing, charitable timing, residency. Lead with these; they're
   bulletproof and large. The exotic stuff is the last 10%, not the first.

## Documentation = the defense

Every structure needs, *contemporaneously*: a written **business-purpose memo**, executed
intercompany agreements at arm's-length, board/manager minutes, valuations where value is shifted,
and real operational substance (bank accounts, functions, people, decisions made at the entity).
A structure with no paper trail is a future audit loss.
