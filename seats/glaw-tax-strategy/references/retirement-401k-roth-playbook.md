# Retirement, 401(k) & Roth Playbook (the full toolkit)

The single biggest *legal, bulletproof* lever for most earners. Limits are **2026** (verify each
year on IRS.gov; they index). Authority: §401, §402, §408/§408A, §415, §72, §4975.

## 2026 contribution limits (verified)

| Account | 2026 limit |
|---|---|
| **401(k)/403(b)/457 elective deferral** | **$24,500** |
| Catch-up (age 50+) | **+$8,000** (→ $32,500) |
| Enhanced catch-up (ages **60–63**) | **+$11,250** (instead of $8,000) |
| **§415(c) total annual additions** (employee + employer + after-tax, per employer) | **$72,000** (+catch-up: $80,000, or $83,250 ages 60–63) |
| **Traditional/Roth IRA** | **$7,500** (+$1,100 catch-up) |
| Roth IRA phase-out | single $153k–168k; MFJ $242k–252k |

**SECURE 2.0 Roth catch-up rule (2026):** if prior-year wages with the employer exceeded
**$150,000**, catch-up contributions **must** be Roth (after-tax).

## The account ladder (order of operations for a high earner)

1. **401(k) to the match** — free money first.
2. **HSA** (if HDHP) — **triple tax-free** (§223): deduct in, grow tax-free, withdraw tax-free for
   medical; after 65 acts like an IRA. The most tax-efficient account in the code.
3. **Max 401(k) elective deferral** ($24,500 + catch-up).
4. **Backdoor Roth IRA** — non-deductible IRA → convert to Roth (§408A). Watch the **pro-rata
   rule** (§408(d)(2)): aggregate pre-tax IRA balances are taxed on conversion — roll them into a
   401(k) first to clear the way.
5. **Mega-backdoor Roth** — if the plan allows **after-tax** contributions + **in-plan Roth
   conversion** or in-service withdrawal, fill the §415(c) gap (up to $72k total minus deferrals
   and match) with after-tax dollars converted to Roth. Tens of $k/yr of Roth space.
6. **Roth conversions** in low-income years (gap years, early retirement) — fill low brackets.

## Self-employed / business-owner plans (bigger limits)

- **Solo 401(k)** — employee deferral + employer profit-sharing up to §415(c); add a spouse on
  payroll to double. Best for one-owner businesses.
- **SEP-IRA** — up to 25% of comp to the §415(c) cap; simple but no Roth/loan features.
- **Defined-benefit / cash-balance plan** — the heavy hitter: deductible contributions can reach
  **six figures/year** for older high-earning owners (§412/§404); pair with a 401(k). Requires an
  actuary and stable cash flow.
- Stack: cash-balance + safe-harbor 401(k) + profit-sharing can shelter $200k–$400k/yr for a
  50-something owner.

## Advanced moves

- **NUA — Net Unrealized Appreciation (§402(e)(4)):** if you hold **employer stock** in a 401(k),
  on a qualifying lump-sum distribution you pay **ordinary tax only on basis**, and the
  appreciation is taxed at **long-term capital-gain** rates when sold — a large rate arbitrage.
  Plan before rolling company stock to an IRA (which would forfeit NUA).
- **Self-directed IRA/Roth** — can hold private equity, startup shares (the Thiel move), real
  estate. **Danger zones:** prohibited transactions (§4975 — no self-dealing, no transactions with
  disqualified persons), **UBIT/UDFI** on leveraged or operating-business income, and **valuation**
  of private assets. Get a custodian + counsel.
- **Roth for founder/early equity** — putting *low-current-value, high-growth* shares in a Roth is
  the most powerful legal play (gains escape tax forever); price the shares defensibly.

## Access before 59½ (without the 10% penalty)

- **§72(t) SEPP** — substantially-equal periodic payments.
- **Rule of 55** — penalty-free 401(k) withdrawals if you separate from service at 55+.
- **Roth contribution basis** — always withdrawable tax/penalty-free; **Roth conversion ladder**
  (5-year seasoning) funds early retirement.
- **457(b)** (governmental/nonprofit) — no early-withdrawal penalty after separation.

## Distribution phase

- **RMDs** begin at **73** (75 for those born 1960+) under SECURE 2.0; **Roth IRAs have no RMDs**
  for the owner (and, post-SECURE 2.0, no RMDs on Roth 401(k)s either).
- **QCD** — after 70½, give up to the annual cap directly from an IRA to charity, excluded from
  income (counts toward RMD).
- **Inherited accounts** — most non-spouse beneficiaries must empty within **10 years**
  (SECURE Act); plan Roth conversions to spare heirs the tax.

> Headline: for almost everyone, **maxing the right accounts in the right order** beats every
> exotic structure on a risk-adjusted basis. Build this layer first.
