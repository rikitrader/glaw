# IP Licensing, Royalty Structures & Transfer Pricing (the "Meta/Apple" lens)

This is the most misunderstood and most abused area. Be honest about what survives. Verify
current rules per SKILL.md Step 1a.

## The classic move and why it's largely closed

The 2010s playbook (Apple, Google, Meta): park valuable **IP in a low-tax jurisdiction**, have
high-tax operating subsidiaries pay it large **royalties**, stripping profit out of high-tax
countries (the "Double Irish with a Dutch Sandwich"). **This era is over:**
- **Ireland closed the Double Irish** (grandfathering ended 2020).
- **US GILTI / now NCTI** taxes US multinationals' foreign intangible income at a **~14% floor**
  (OBBBA cut the §250 deduction to 40% and removed the QBAI exemption for tax years after
  12/31/2025) — so parking IP offshore no longer yields near-zero rates.
- **OECD Pillar Two** imposes a **15% global minimum effective rate** on MNE groups with revenue
  **>€750M**; even if the US hasn't enacted it, US groups with foreign operations are subject
  abroad and risk top-up tax.

**Bottom line:** aggressive offshore-IP-stripping is dead for all but the largest groups, and even
they now pay a ~14–15% floor. **Do not propose a hollow offshore IP box.**

## What still works — domestically, with substance

- **FDII / now FDDEI** — a US incentive: a US C-corp earning income from **serving foreign
  markets** gets a deduction lowering the effective rate to ~**14%**. This rewards keeping IP and
  jobs **in the US** while exporting — the legitimate inverse of offshoring. Verify post-OBBBA
  mechanics (QBAI removed, deduction 33.34%).
- **Domestic IP holdco** — a US **trademark/IP holding company** that owns the brand and licenses
  it to operating entities for an **arm's-length royalty**. Legitimate for liability isolation
  and centralized IP management; the income still lands in the US tax net, so the benefit is
  organizational + modest state-tax arbitrage (and some states attack "intangible holding
  company" royalty deductions — verify state addback rules).

## The two rules that govern everything here

1. **Arm's-length / §482** — the royalty (or management fee, or intercompany sale price) must be
   what **unrelated parties** would pay. Support it with a **transfer-pricing study / comparables**
   and contemporaneous documentation (§6662(e) penalty for mispricing). A 30% royalty with no
   comparables invites reallocation.
2. **Economic substance / §7701(o)** — the IP entity must actually **own the rights, bear risk,
   and perform functions** (people making decisions). A nameplate entity holding a logo is a
   strict-liability **20–40% penalty** waiting to happen (guardrails reference).

## How to advise a real client

- **SMB / single-country:** a domestic IP/brand holdco licensing to opcos can isolate liability
  and centralize IP, but expect **little federal** benefit (income stays in the US) and check
  **state royalty-addback** statutes before promising state savings. Be candid that it's mostly an
  asset-protection + governance play, not a tax silver bullet.
- **Genuine multinational with foreign operations:** model **FDDEI** (US-based, export-serving)
  vs foreign structuring under **GILTI/NCTI + Pillar Two**; insist on real foreign substance;
  bring in international tax counsel. Cross-ref CFC/Subpart F in state-and-offshore.
- **Always:** transfer-pricing study + business-purpose memo + real functions, or don't do it.
