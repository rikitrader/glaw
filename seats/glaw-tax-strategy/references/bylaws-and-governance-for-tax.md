# Bylaws, Charter & Operating-Agreement Provisions That Enable Tax Strategy

A structure only delivers its tax result if the **governing documents** authorize it and the
**paper trail** supports it. This is how you turn the code into reality — and how a "loophole"
becomes a defensible *position* (it's a position only with substance + documentation; see
guardrails). Have counsel draft; this is the checklist.

## Charter / Certificate of Incorporation (C-corp)
- **Single class of stock that qualifies for QSBS** — confirm the corp is a domestic C-corp,
  ≤$75M gross assets at issuance, active-business use; issue stock on a dated, documented
  original issuance to start the §1202 clock. Avoid redemptions that taint QSBS.
- **Authorized share classes** — common + preferred for investors; ensure founder common is
  issued early at low value (paired with **§83(b)**).
- **Blue-sky / 1202 representations** in stock-purchase agreements to evidence qualification.

## S-corp governance
- **S-election (Form 2553)** + shareholder consents; one class of stock (watch disproportionate
  distributions that bust the election).
- **Reasonable-compensation policy** — board resolution setting officer salary by a comp study;
  document the salary/distribution split (the #1 S-corp audit issue).
- **F-reorganization** — drop the S-corp under a new holding S-corp + QSub before a sale to enable
  a tax-efficient asset-deal treatment for buyers (a common M&A structuring move).

## LLC / partnership operating agreement
- **Special allocations** with **§704(b)** substantial-economic-effect language (capital-account
  maintenance, qualified-income-offset, DROs) so allocations of income/loss/depreciation hold up.
- **Profits-interest** grants to service providers (Rev. Proc. 93-27/2001-43) — equity comp with
  **no tax at grant** if structured right; vesting + §83(b) protective election.
- **Manager-managed** structure isolating control; **§754 election** language for basis step-ups
  on transfers/death.
- **Guaranteed payments vs distributive share** — define for SE-tax and timing planning.

## Holdco / Opco / management-company agreements
- **Intercompany services agreement** — management company provides defined services to opcos at
  an **arm's-length fee** (§482); list the actual functions, people, deliverables, pricing method.
- **IP license agreement** — IP holdco licenses brand/IP to opcos at a supportable **royalty**
  (transfer-pricing study attached).
- **Lease** — real-estate LLC leases premises to opco at market rent (appraisal/comps on file).
- **Board minutes** authorizing each arrangement, with the **business-purpose memo**.

## Accountable plan & fringe-benefit resolutions
- Adopt an **accountable plan** (Treas. Reg. §1.62-2) so the company reimburses home office,
  mileage, travel, cell **tax-free** to the owner/employees.
- Board resolutions for **§280A(g) Augusta** home rental (≤14 days, market rate, real meetings),
  **§119** lodging, and fringe-benefit plans (§125 cafeteria, HSA, education §127).

## Compensation & equity governance
- **Compensation committee** + plan documents for ISOs (§422), NSOs, RSUs, profits interests;
  **§409A**-compliant deferred-comp and valuation policy.
- **Dividend / distribution policy** — for C-corps, manage the accumulated-earnings (§531) and
  PHC (§541) traps; for passthroughs, tax-distribution provisions so owners can pay the tax.

## Documentation discipline (what makes it survive audit)
For **every** structure: a contemporaneous **business-purpose memo**, executed agreements at
arm's-length, board/manager minutes, valuations where value moves, separate bank accounts, and
real operational substance. Undocumented = a future loss under §7701(o)/§482. Turning a
"loophole" into an advantage is 10% choosing the provision and 90% papering it correctly.
