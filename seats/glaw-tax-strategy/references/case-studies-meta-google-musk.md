# Case Studies — Meta, Google/Apple, Musk, Thiel (what they actually did)

Sourced, honest breakdowns — including what's **closed** now. The lesson is the *mechanism*, not
a copy-paste. Verify current law per Step 1a.

## Google & Apple — the "Double Irish with a Dutch Sandwich" (CLOSED)

**What they did (2000s–2010s):** licensed valuable IP to subsidiaries in Ireland; an Irish
operating company paid huge **royalties** to a second Irish company tax-resident in a haven
(Bermuda), routed through a Dutch conduit to avoid withholding. Result: tens of billions of
foreign profit taxed at near-0%. Google alone reportedly moved ~$23B through a Dutch/Bermuda
structure in 2017.

**Why it's dead — do not replicate:**
- Ireland **closed** the Double Irish (no new users 2015; grandfathering ended **2020**).
- **US GILTI → NCTI** now taxes US parents' foreign intangible income at a **~14% floor**.
- **OECD Pillar Two** sets a **15% global minimum** ETR for groups >€750M.
- The legitimate descendant is **onshore**: keep IP in the US and use **FDDEI** (export
  incentive, ~14%) — see ip-licensing-and-transfer-pricing.

**Transferable lesson:** IP is mobile and valuable; locating it and pricing intercompany
royalties (**§482**) matters — but the arbitrage is gone, substance is mandatory, and this is a
multinational-scale move, not an SMB one.

## Meta — IP centralization + R&D + SBC

Meta's low effective rates historically came from (a) offshore IP/royalty structuring (now
curtailed as above), (b) massive **R&D expensing/credits (§41/§174)**, and (c) **stock-based
compensation** deductions — the company deducts the value employees recognize on vesting/exercise,
often a larger book-tax difference than cash comp. **Transferable lesson for a real company:** the
durable levers are **R&D credits** and **equity compensation deductions**, not offshore IP.

## Elon Musk — the personal-wealth playbook (the "Elon" model)

Verified facts:
- **No cash salary** — paid in **stock options**, so little ordinary income most years.
- **Lives off debt** — borrows against his Tesla/SpaceX holdings instead of selling (a **loan is
  not taxable income**) → the "buy-borrow-die" core.
- **Moved to Texas** (no state income tax) before major realizations.
- **2021:** his options (25.5M granted 2012) were **expiring in 2022**, forcing exercise. He
  turned ~$142.6M of strike cost into ~**$23.6B** of stock → ~**$23.5B** ordinary income, a
  ~**$11B** federal bill (~41% rate) — he called it the largest individual tax bill ever.

**The lessons, honestly:**
1. **Borrowing defers, expiration forces.** The strategy delays tax for years, but options expire
   and forced exercise creates a giant ordinary-income event — timing and AMT/withholding planning
   are everything (see personal-wealth-tactics).
2. **No-salary + borrow + no-tax-state + hold-don't-sell** is the repeatable individual pattern —
   for anyone with appreciated, borrowable assets. It **defers**, and the **§1014 step-up** is what
   ultimately erases the gain at death.
3. It is **not** zero forever — and it carries leverage/market risk.

## Peter Thiel — the $5B Roth IRA (the most extreme legal retirement play)

**What happened (ProPublica, IRS records):** in 1999 Thiel put **1.7M PayPal founder shares at
$0.001 = $1,700** into a **Roth IRA**; PayPal's sale made it ~$28.5M by 2002; he then invested Roth
funds into Facebook (2004), Palantir, SpaceX, etc. By 2019 the Roth held ~**$5B — all gains
permanently tax-free** (Roth withdrawals are tax-free).

**Transferable lessons (scaled down, legal):**
- Hold **high-growth, low-current-value** assets (founder shares, early startup equity) **inside a
  Roth** so all appreciation escapes tax — the single most powerful legal account.
- **Self-directed Roth** can hold private/startup equity (see retirement playbook), but beware
  **prohibited-transaction** (§4975) and **valuation** rules — Thiel's $0.001 valuation drew
  scrutiny; price founder shares defensibly.
- Congress is targeting "mega-IRAs" (proposed caps/forced distributions) — the door may narrow.

## The through-line

The rich don't use one magic trick; they stack: **own appreciating assets → hold inside
tax-advantaged wrappers (Roth, QSBS C-corp, trusts) → borrow instead of sell → locate in low-tax
states → step up at death.** Most of it is legal *because* there's real ownership and substance.
The offshore-IP shortcuts are gone; the ownership-timing-and-borrowing playbook remains.
