# Trusts — for Tax Advantage, Estate Transfer & Asset Protection

A trust is a tool, not a magic word. Each type does a *specific* job; using the wrong one (or
expecting a living trust to save income tax) is the common mistake. Verify current exemptions per
Step 1a; involve an estate attorney to draft.

## First distinction: revocable vs irrevocable

| | **Revocable living trust** | **Irrevocable trust** |
|---|---|---|
| Control | You keep full control / can revoke | You give up control |
| **Income tax** | **None** — ignored, taxed to you (grantor) | Varies (grantor vs non-grantor) |
| **Estate tax** | **No** reduction — assets still in your estate | Can **remove** assets from your estate |
| **Creditor protection** | **None** — reachable | Strong, if not self-settled (or a DAPT) |
| Probate | **Avoids probate** (its main benefit) + privacy + incapacity management | Also avoids probate |

> **Key honesty:** a **revocable living trust does NOT save income or estate tax and does NOT
> protect from creditors.** Its real value is **avoiding probate**, privacy, and seamless
> incapacity/death administration. Don't oversell it. (It does carry assets to a **§1014 step-up**
> at death like other owned property.)

## Estate-freeze / wealth-transfer trusts (move appreciation out of the estate)
- **GRAT (§2702)** — put appreciating assets in; take back an annuity; growth above the §7520
  hurdle passes to heirs **gift-tax-free**. Great with volatile/high-growth assets; "zeroed-out"
  GRATs minimize gift cost.
- **IDGT (intentionally defective grantor trust, §671–679)** — **sell** appreciating assets to the
  trust for a note; it's irrevocable for **estate** tax (freezes value) but a **grantor** trust for
  **income** tax, so *you* pay its income tax — a further **tax-free gift** to the heirs, and the
  sale isn't a taxable event (sale to your own grantor trust).
- **SLAT** — irrevocable trust for your spouse; uses your lifetime exemption now while the family
  retains indirect access via the spouse. Watch the reciprocal-trust doctrine if both spouses do it.
- **Dynasty trust (GST, §2631)** — multi-generational; in a no-perpetuities state (SD, NV) it
  shelters assets from estate/GST tax for generations **and** from descendants' creditors/divorces.
- **QPRT** — transfer a residence at a discounted gift value, keep living in it for a term.

## Charitable trusts (income + deduction + gain deferral)
- **CRT (§664)** — contribute appreciated assets; the trust **sells them tax-free**, pays you an
  income stream for life/term, you get a partial **deduction**, remainder to charity. Converts a
  concentrated low-basis position into diversified income without an upfront capital-gains hit.
- **CLAT** — pays charity first for a term, remainder to heirs at a reduced transfer-tax cost;
  powerful in low-interest-rate environments.

## Insurance & special-purpose trusts
- **ILIT** — owns life insurance outside your estate so the **death benefit (§101, income-tax-free)
  is also estate-tax-free**; provides estate liquidity to pay estate tax or equalize heirs.
- **Special-needs / spendthrift** trusts — protect beneficiaries and assets from their creditors.

## Asset-protection trusts (the protection job — see asset-protection.md)
- **DAPT** — self-settled spendthrift trust in a DAPT state (NV/SD/AK/DE/WY/OH/TN…): be a
  discretionary beneficiary yet shield assets from **future** creditors after seasoning.
- **Offshore APT** (Cook Islands/Nevis) — strongest deterrence; **tax-neutral** (grantor trust),
  FBAR/FATCA reporting; deterrence not tax savings.
- Both must be funded **before** any claim (no fraudulent transfer — UVTA/FUFTA).

## Choosing — match the trust to the goal

| Goal | Use |
|---|---|
| Avoid probate / privacy / incapacity | **Revocable living trust** |
| Remove appreciation from estate | **GRAT, IDGT, SLAT** |
| Multi-generational shelter | **Dynasty/GST trust** |
| Diversify low-basis stock + income | **CRT** |
| Estate-tax-free life insurance | **ILIT** |
| Shield from future lawsuits | **DAPT / offshore APT** |
| Pass home at a discount | **QPRT** |

## Guardrails
- "**Pure trust / constitutional trust / common-law business trust**" schemes that claim to erase
  income tax are **abusive** (IRS Dirty Dozen) — never recommend them.
- Irrevocable means **irrevocable** — model the loss of control before funding.
- Grantor-trust status, GST allocation, and basis (carryover gift basis vs §1014 step-up) interact;
  coordinate income, estate, and protection goals together. This is attorney-drafted work; the
  skill designs the plan and hands off for execution.
