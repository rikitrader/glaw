# State Residency, Puerto Rico & Cross-Border

Verify current rules per SKILL.md Step 1a. Cross-ref `tax-compliance` for FBAR/FATCA/streamlined.

## No-income-tax states (the "move to Texas" move)

Nine states levy no broad individual income tax: **AK, FL, NV, NH, SD, TN, TX, WA, WY** (WA does
tax some capital gains). Relocating before a **liquidity event** (IPO, business sale, big bonus)
can save the full state rate (e.g., CA up to ~13.3%).

**The catch — you must really move:**
- Establish **domicile** (the place you intend as permanent home): change driver's license, voter
  registration, primary residence, doctors, where family lives, days present.
- **Beware exit audits.** California and New York aggressively audit departing high earners and
  apply **source rules** — income *earned* while resident (e.g., stock that vested in CA) stays
  taxable there even after you leave. Timing the move **before** value accrues matters.
- **183-day** statutory-residency traps if you keep a home in the old state.

## Puerto Rico — Act 60 (genuine, but a high bar)

A US citizen who becomes a **bona fide PR resident** can get **~0%** PR tax on PR-source capital
gains and a low rate on PR-sourced business/export income (PR is outside the US federal income tax
net for bona-fide residents under §933). It's real — and heavily scrutinized:
- Requires the **bona-fide-residence tests**: physical presence (generally **183 days**), tax
  home in PR, and a **closer connection** to PR than the US.
- **Only gain that accrues *while* a PR resident** escapes — appreciation before the move is still
  US-taxable (anti-abuse rules allocate built-in gain).
- The IRS lists **abusive Act 60 arrangements** (residents in name only) among enforcement
  priorities — see guardrails. Bona-fide substance or don't do it.

## Cross-border / offshore (only with real substance)

- **CFC / Subpart F / GILTI(NCTI)** — US owners of controlled foreign corporations are currently
  taxed on much of the foreign income regardless of distribution; offshore deferral is mostly
  gone. ~14% floor on intangible income (see ip-licensing reference).
- **FBAR (FinCEN 114)** + **FATCA (Form 8938)** — disclosure is mandatory above thresholds;
  non-disclosure is the **criminal** line. Cross-ref `tax-compliance` for delinquent procedures.
- **Expatriation / §877A exit tax** — renouncing US citizenship or long-term green card triggers a
  mark-to-market **exit tax** for covered expatriates. A last-resort, high-stakes move.
- **Treaties** — can reduce withholding and resolve dual residency; analyze before structuring.

## How to advise

1. **State residency is the cleanest, highest-value cross-border-ish lever** for most people —
   real, legal, large. Time it before value accrues; document the move thoroughly.
2. **Puerto Rico** can be transformational for the right person (large prospective gains, willing
   to truly live there) — but only with bona-fide residence; flag the audit risk honestly.
3. **Offshore corporate structures** rarely help individuals/SMBs post-GILTI and add CFC + FBAR/
   FATCA compliance burden and criminal exposure if mishandled. Recommend only with genuine
   foreign operations, real substance, and international tax counsel.
