# Personal Wealth Tactics (the "Elon" lens) — equity, borrowing, charity, trusts

How individual wealth is built and held tax-efficiently. Verify current figures per Step 1a.

## Buy-borrow-die (the headline "billionaire" move)

The mechanism, honestly:
1. **Buy** appreciating assets (founder stock, real estate, index funds).
2. **Borrow** against them (margin / securities-backed line / portfolio loan) for living/liquidity
   instead of **selling** — a **loan is not income**, so no capital-gains tax on spending.
3. **Die** — heirs get a **§1014 stepped-up basis** to fair market value at death, so the lifetime
   appreciation **escapes income tax entirely**; the loans are repaid from the stepped-up estate.

Honest caveats: needs **highly appreciated assets** + **lender access** + tolerance for leverage
and interest cost; it **defers** income tax during life (doesn't erase until the step-up); the
estate may still owe **estate tax** above the exemption; and **§1014 is a policy target** Congress
could change. Real, but conditional — not free money.

## Equity compensation timing

- **ISOs** — no ordinary tax at exercise, but the spread is an **AMT** preference; exercise-and-
  hold strategy targets long-term cap gains + possible QSBS. Model AMT.
- **NSOs / RSUs** — ordinary income at exercise/vest; plan withholding and the sale year.
- **83(b) election** — for restricted stock, elect within **30 days** to be taxed on (tiny) grant
  value now so all appreciation is capital gain later. Critical for founders.
- **83(i)** — private-company employees may defer income on certain equity up to 5 years.
- **QSBS stacking** — see entity-and-holdco; combine with gifting to multiply the §1202 exclusion.

## Charitable structures (deduction + gain avoidance + control)

| Vehicle | Benefit |
|---|---|
| **Donate appreciated stock** | Deduct **FMV** and **avoid the capital gain** — beats donating cash |
| **Donor-Advised Fund (DAF)** | Big deduction now (bunch into a high-income year), grant out over time |
| **Charitable Remainder Trust (CRT)** | Sell appreciated assets **inside** the trust tax-deferred, get an income stream + partial deduction |
| **Charitable Lead Trust (CLAT)** | Front-loads charity; passes remainder to heirs at reduced transfer-tax cost |
| **Private foundation** | Family control + legacy; deduction limits lower, 1.39% excise + self-dealing rules |

## Estate-freeze trusts (move future appreciation out of the estate)

- **GRAT** — put appreciating assets in; get an annuity back; appreciation above the IRS §7520
  hurdle passes to heirs **gift-tax-free**. Great in low-rate / high-growth setups.
- **IDGT (intentionally defective grantor trust)** — **sell** appreciating assets to the trust for
  a note; grantor pays the income tax (a further tax-free gift to heirs), freezing estate value.
- **SLAT** — spousal lifetime access trust; use the lifetime exemption now while retaining
  indirect access via spouse.
- **Dynasty trust** — multi-generational, in a state with no rule against perpetuities (SD, NV) to
  shelter assets from estate tax for generations.

## Lifetime gift / estate exemption

Use the (historically high, indexed) **unified exemption** before any scheduled reduction — gift
appreciating assets out now to lock in today's exemption and shift future growth. Coordinate with
GST exemption for dynasty planning. Verify the current exemption amount.

## Realization timing (everyone)

- Hold > 1 year for **long-term** capital-gain rates.
- **Tax-loss harvesting** to offset gains; watch the **wash-sale** rule.
- Spread income / Roth conversions into **low-bracket years**; bunch deductions.
