# Criminal Statutes — Elements Library (Federal + Florida)

> Use to build the Phase-5 Proof Matrix. For each, list element → evidence → gap.
> This is an investigative checklist, not legal advice; verify current text before filing.

## FEDERAL

### 18 U.S.C. § 1343 — Wire Fraud
1. A scheme to defraud (to obtain money/property by materially false pretenses);
2. Intent to defraud (scienter);
3. Use of interstate wire (call, text, email, bank wire, WhatsApp across state lines)
   in furtherance of the scheme.
*Each wire = a separate count. WhatsApp messages across state lines qualify.*

### 18 U.S.C. § 1341 — Mail Fraud
Same as wire fraud, but use of the mails / private carrier (FedEx/UPS) to further the scheme.
*Mailing checks to a CA address to divert them = candidate predicate.*

### 18 U.S.C. § 1344 — Bank Fraud
1. Scheme to defraud a financial institution, OR to obtain bank-held funds by false pretenses;
2. Intent; 3. Target is a federally insured bank.
*Depositing checks not payable to the account holder, false endorsements, signature-card fraud.*

### 18 U.S.C. § 1957 — Money Laundering (spending criminally-derived funds)
1. Knowing monetary transaction; 2. In criminally derived property > $10,000;
3. Property derived from "specified unlawful activity" (fraud qualifies).

### 18 U.S.C. § 1956 — Money Laundering (concealment / promotion)
Financial transaction with proceeds of unlawful activity, knowing it is proceeds, AND
(a) intent to promote the unlawful activity, or (b) intent to **conceal** source/ownership/
control, or (c) to avoid reporting. *Layering through SPVs / same-address shells = concealment.*

### 18 U.S.C. § 371 — Conspiracy
1. Agreement between 2+ persons to commit a federal offense (or defraud the US);
2. Knowing/voluntary joinder; 3. An overt act in furtherance. *Co-conspirator statements admissible.*

### 18 U.S.C. § 1962 (RICO)
1. An "enterprise" (can be association-in-fact of entities/people);
2. Affecting interstate commerce; 3. Conducted through a **pattern** (2+ related predicate acts
   within 10 yrs); 4. Predicates: mail/wire/bank fraud, money laundering, etc.
*§1964(c) gives a CIVIL RICO claim with TREBLE damages + attorney's fees.*

### 18 U.S.C. § 513 / § 514, 18 U.S.C. § 1028/1028A — Forged securities / false IDs / aggravated ID theft
*Forged endorsements, forged DocuSign, fabricated documents.*

## FLORIDA

### Fla. Stat. § 817.034 — Florida Communications Fraud Act ("Scheme to Defraud")
Engaging in a **systematic, ongoing course of conduct** with intent to defraud, obtaining
property thereby. ≥$50,000 = 1st-degree felony. *Designed for multi-victim schemes.*

### Fla. Stat. § 812.014 — Theft / Grand Theft
Knowingly obtaining/using another's property with intent to deprive. Grand theft tiers by value
($750+, $5k+, $20k+, $100k+). *Diverted deposits/insurance checks.*

### Fla. Stat. § 772.11 — Civil Theft (the civil hook to 812.014)
Proof by **clear and convincing** evidence of a 812.014 violation → **TREBLE damages + fees**.
Requires a **pre-suit written demand** (60 days, for $200). *Often the strongest civil club.*

### Fla. Stat. § 817.234 — Insurance Fraud
False/fraudulent insurance claims, inflated claims, deductible-waiver schemes. *Roofing/insurance
"we keep what we don't do" emails are candidates.*

### Fla. Stat. Ch. 895 — Florida RICO
State analogue; "criminal enterprise" + pattern of "racketeering activity"; civil remedies +
asset forfeiture / injunction.

### Fla. Stat. § 831 — Forgery / Uttering forged instruments
Forged checks, endorsements, contracts, notarizations.

## SCIENTER (the usual battleground)
Intent is proven by: contemporaneous admissions, concealment, false statements, use of
nominees/shells, destruction of records, fleeing, paying insiders while insolvent, and
"consciousness of guilt" (e.g., warrant-avoidance, fabricating cover stories).
