# Civil Causes of Action — Elements Library (Florida-centric)

### Fraud / Fraudulent Misrepresentation
1. False statement of material fact; 2. Maker knew it was false (or reckless);
3. Intent that plaintiff rely; 4. Justifiable reliance; 5. Damages.
*"You will have funds any minute / It's out" + funds never arrived = candidate.*

### Fraudulent Inducement
Fraud that induces entry into a contract/venture. Same elements, pre-contract.

### Conversion
1. Plaintiff's ownership/right to specific property (incl. identifiable money);
2. Defendant's wrongful dominion/deprivation; 3. Demand & refusal (for money: a specific,
identifiable sum). *Diverted checks/deposits into a controlled account.*

### Civil Theft — Fla. Stat. § 772.11 (the heavy club)
Underlying § 812.014 theft proven by **clear & convincing** evidence + **felonious intent** →
**treble damages + attorney's fees**. **Requires 60-day pre-suit written demand for $200.**

### Breach of Fiduciary Duty
1. A fiduciary relationship (partners, joint venturers, those entrusted with funds/account control);
2. Breach; 3. Causation & damages. *Partner given sole BofA/escrow control who diverts = textbook.*

### Civil Conspiracy
1. Agreement between 2+; 2. To do an unlawful act (or lawful act by unlawful means);
3. Overt act; 4. Damages. *Ties co-actors together for joint & several liability.*

### Unjust Enrichment / Money Had & Received
1. Plaintiff conferred a benefit; 2. Defendant knew & retained it; 3. Inequitable to keep it.
*Backstop where no contract; reaches money that ended up in a shell.*

### FUFTA — Florida Uniform Fraudulent Transfer Act, Ch. 726
- **§726.105(1)(a):** transfer with **actual intent** to hinder/delay/defraud creditors.
- **§726.105(1)(b) / 726.106:** **constructive** fraud — transfer for less than reasonably
  equivalent value while insolvent / left undercapitalized.
- **Badges of fraud (§726.105(2)):** transfer to insider; debtor retained control; concealed;
  before/after being sued; substantially all assets; absconding; removed/concealed assets;
  value not reasonably equivalent; insolvent at/after transfer; transfer shortly before/after
  a big debt; transfer of essential assets to a lienor who transferred to an insider.
- **Remedies:** avoid the transfer, attach the asset, injunction, receiver, levy. **Reaches the
  transferee** (the shell/insider who received the money).

### Civil RICO — 18 U.S.C. §1964(c) / Fla. Stat. §772.104
Enterprise + pattern of predicate acts (fraud, etc.) causing injury → **treble damages + fees**.

### Accounting (equitable)
Where a fiduciary/partner controlled funds, plaintiff may compel a full **accounting** of every
dollar in/out of the controlled accounts — a powerful discovery-forcing remedy.

### Piercing the Corporate Veil / Alter Ego (FL)
1. The shareholder dominated the entity to the point it had no separate existence;
2. Used it for an improper/fraudulent purpose; 3. Causing injury. *Same-address SPVs, commingling,
no corporate formalities, undercapitalization → reach the individual personally.*

### Successor Liability / Single Business Enterprise
Mere continuation, de facto merger, or fraudulent purpose to escape debts → new entity liable
for old entity's obligations. *"Nationwide can't be attached / I'll form a new LLC" + re-skin to
Blackstone = candidate.*

## DAMAGES & REMEDIES TOOLKIT
Compensatory; treble (civil theft, RICO); punitive (fraud); pre-judgment interest; attorney's
fees (772.11, RICO); **pre-judgment asset freeze / writ of attachment / lis pendens / receiver**
(FUFTA); constructive trust over traceable funds; equitable accounting; rescission.
