# The IRS Fresh Start Initiative (in detail)

"Fresh Start" began in 2008 and was expanded ~2011–2012. It is the umbrella of **relaxed
collection rules** — not a standalone "forgiveness program." Verify all current figures on IRS.gov
per Step 1a; reuse `tax-compliance/references/collection-resolution.md` for the form-level mechanics.

## 1. Streamlined Installment Agreements (the centerpiece)
- Individuals owing **≤ $50,000** (assessed tax + penalty + interest) can get a streamlined IA up
  to **72 months** with **minimal financial disclosure** (no full 433 in many cases).
- Two tiers: **≤ $25,000** (individual / out-of-business / in-business income-tax-only) and
  **$25,001–$50,000** (individual or out-of-business sole prop) — the upper tier generally requires
  **direct debit or payroll deduction**.
- Doubled the prior **$25,000 / 60-month** limits. Apply via the Online Payment Agreement or
  **Form 9465**. While an IA is active, the failure-to-pay penalty drops to **0.25%/mo** *if the
  return was timely filed*.

## 2. Easier lien rules
- The IRS generally won't file a **Notice of Federal Tax Lien** below ~**$10,000** (raised under
  Fresh Start; discretionary).
- **Lien withdrawal** is available after entering a **direct-debit installment agreement** (and
  paying down under the threshold) — removes the public lien, helping credit.
- **Discharge** (free a specific asset to sell) and **subordination** (let a refinance close) are
  available with the right forms.

## 3. Expanded Offer in Compromise
- Fresh Start broadened the **allowable living-expense** standards and future-income lookback used
  in the **RCP** calc, making more offers viable.
- **OIC basics:** application fee **$205** (waived for low-income — AGI ≤ **250%** of the federal
  poverty level); **lump-sum** offers require **20% down** with the application; **periodic-payment**
  offers require ongoing payments during review. The **CSED is suspended** while the offer is
  pending. See `choose-the-path.md` for the RCP estimator.

## 4. Penalty relief
- **First-Time Abatement** (administrative waiver) first, then **reasonable cause** — reuse
  `tax-compliance/references/penalty-relief.md`. Abating penalties also abates the interest *on*
  those penalties.

## 5. Partial Payment Installment Agreement (PPIA)
Not always branded "Fresh Start," but key: if the taxpayer can pay *something* monthly but not
enough to clear the debt before the CSED, the IRS may accept payments that **won't full-pay** the
balance (the remainder expires at CSED). Requires a full financial statement (433-F) and periodic
review.

## What Fresh Start is NOT
- Not blanket forgiveness; not "pennies on the dollar" for everyone; not a way to skip filing.
- A taxpayer who can afford to pay will be expected to pay (via IA), not settle cheap.
