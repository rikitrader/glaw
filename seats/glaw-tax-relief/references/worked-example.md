# Worked Example — $38k Owed, One Unfiled Year, Wage-Levy Notice

End-to-end pass through the workflow. Illustrative; verify figures per Step 1a.

## Facts (Step 1)
- Owes ~**$38,000** for 2021–2023 (assessed); **2024 return not filed**. Just got an **LT11 (Final
  Notice of Intent to Levy)** dated 9 days ago. W-2 employee, ~$5,500/mo gross; rent + basics eat
  most of it; ~$1,200/mo disposable; **no real assets** (old car, $2k savings). Single.

## Step 1d — compliance gate
2024 is unfiled → **cannot** get any agreement until it's filed. **Route to `tax-compliance`** to
file 2024 now (and confirm 2021–2023 are real returns, not SFRs — replacing an SFR could lower the
$38k). Adjust 2026 withholding so a new balance doesn't accrue.

## Step 2 — triage the fire
The **LT11 = 21 days left** on the CDP window. **File Form 12153 now** → pauses the levy, preserves
Tax Court rights, and puts the case with Appeals where we propose a collection alternative. This is
the single most urgent action.

## Step 3 — pre-qualify (RCP)
```
Assets: car (below allowance) + $2,000 savings → net realizable equity ≈ $2,000
Future income: $1,200/mo disposable × 24 (periodic) ≈ $28,800   [× 12 lump-sum ≈ $14,400]
RCP ≈ $2,000 + $14,400 = ~$16,400 (lump-sum basis)
```
- **RCP (~$16.4k) < debt ($38k)** → an **OIC is plausible**, offer ≈ RCP. *But* with $1,200/mo
  disposable, the IRS may push a **streamlined IA** instead (debt ≤ $50k, payable over 72 mo at
  ~$528/mo) — and an IA is faster/more certain.
- Honest call: present **both** — streamlined IA (certain, ~$528/mo) vs OIC (~$16k, uncertain,
  months of review, 20% down). No "pennies on the dollar" promise.

## Step 4 — Fresh Start toolkit
- **Streamlined IA** (Fresh Start, ≤$50k/72mo) via direct debit → also enables **lien withdrawal**
  if a lien was filed.
- **First-Time Abatement** on the earliest year's penalties (if the prior 3 yrs were clean) — knocks
  down a chunk of the $38k.
- If the taxpayer prefers settlement and accepts the odds → prepare the **OIC** at ~$16.4k.

## Step 5 — build & file
- IA: Form 9465 / online, ~$528/mo direct debit; OR OIC: Form 656 + 433-A(OIC) (low-income →
  **$205 fee waived**), 20% down on a lump-sum offer; financials on Collection Financial Standards.
- Draft cover narratives from `tax-compliance/references/letter-templates.md`.

## Step 6 — what the taxpayer hears
**Bottom line:** "Two urgent moves: (1) we file your 2024 return this week, and (2) we file Form
12153 in the next few days to stop the wage levy. Then you choose: a **streamlined payment plan at
about $528/month** (certain, stops collection now) or an **Offer in Compromise around $16,000**
(possible because your collection potential is below the debt, but it's months of review, 20% down,
and not guaranteed). I'd also request **first-time penalty abatement** to cut the balance either
way. What I won't do is promise 'pennies on the dollar' — your real number is the ~$16k floor."

**Steps:** file 2024 (→tax-compliance) → Form 12153 (stop levy) → choose IA vs OIC → FTA → stay
compliant. **Flags:** CDP deadline ~21 days; CSED dates from the account transcript.

figures on IRS.gov.
