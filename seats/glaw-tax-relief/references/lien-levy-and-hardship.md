# Liens, Levies & Hardship — stopping and releasing collection

The urgent side of relief. Reuse `tax-compliance/references/statutes-and-rights.md` for the notice
sequence; this focuses on *stopping the bleeding*. Verify current rules per Step 1a.

## Levy vs lien (know the difference)
- **Lien** — a public *claim* against your property (Notice of Federal Tax Lien). Hurts credit;
  doesn't take anything yet.
- **Levy** — actual *seizure* (bank account one-time grab; **wage garnishment** is continuous).

## Stop a levy / garnishment (fastest relief)
- A levy is preceded by a **Final Notice of Intent to Levy** — **LT11 / CP90 / Letter 1058** —
  giving **30 days** to request a **Collection Due Process hearing (Form 12153)**, which **pauses
  the levy** and preserves Tax Court review. **This deadline is the priority.**
- **CP504** is an earlier "intent to levy (state refund)" notice — appeal via the **Collection
  Appeals Program (Form 9423)**; the real CDP notice comes next.
- **Already levied?** Request a release: entering an **installment agreement** or **CNC**, or
  showing the levy causes **economic hardship** (IRC §6343), generally gets a wage/bank levy
  released. The **Taxpayer Advocate Service (Form 911)** can expedite an imminent-hardship release.
- Wages: only the exempt amount is protected; the rest is continuously garnished until released —
  act fast.

## Release / manage a lien (Fresh Start lien rules)
- **Withdrawal** — removes the public NFTL; available after a **direct-debit installment agreement**
  (and paying under the ~$10k threshold). Best credit outcome.
- **Discharge** — frees a **specific asset** so you can sell it (e.g., your house), with proceeds to
  the IRS up to its interest.
- **Subordination** — lets a **refinance** close by moving the IRS behind the new lender (often
  nets the IRS more, so they grant it).
- A lien self-releases when the debt is paid or the **CSED** expires.

## Currently Not Collectible (hardship)
- For genuine hardship: paying anything would prevent basic living expenses. File **Form 433-F**
  showing income ≤ allowable Collection Financial Standards.
- Collection **pauses**, levies release — but **penalties/interest keep accruing**, refunds are
  **offset**, and a lien may still be filed. CNC can **run out the 10-year CSED** on the debt.
- The IRS reviews periodically and reactivates if income rises.

## CSED awareness (the clock that can save you)
Pull the **Account transcript** for the assessment date → estimate the **CSED** (10 yrs from
assessment; tolled by pending OIC/CDP/bankruptcy/time abroad). Sometimes the best "relief" is a
PPIA or CNC that simply outlasts the CSED. Flag any debt nearing expiration.
