# Construction Accounting and Financial Management

**Source:** Steven J. Peterson, MBA, PE — Weber State University. Pearson Prentice Hall, 2nd Edition (2009). ISBN: 978-0-13-501711-1.

## Structure

Five parts, 18 chapters + 8 appendices covering the complete financial management lifecycle for construction companies.

## Part I: Introduction to Construction Financial Management (Ch. 1)

- Financial management = use of a company's financial resources encompassing ALL decisions affecting financial health
- Construction is DIFFERENT from other industries: project-oriented, decentralized production, unique payment terms, heavy subcontractor use
- Financial manager responsibilities: accounting for resources, managing costs/profits, managing cash flows, choosing among financial alternatives

## Part II: Accounting for Financial Resources (Ch. 2-6)

### Ch. 2: Construction Accounting Systems
- **Cost reporting vs. cost control** — reporting tells what happened; control prevents cost overruns
- **General ledger** — master record of all financial transactions
- **Methods of accounting:**
  - Cash basis (record when cash changes hands)
  - Accrual basis (record when earned/incurred regardless of cash)
  - Percentage-of-completion (recognize revenue proportional to work done)
  - Completed-contract (recognize all revenue at job completion)
- **Balance sheet** components: Assets (current + fixed), Liabilities (current + long-term), Owner's Equity
- **Income statement** components: Revenues, Construction costs, Equipment costs, Overhead, Other income/expense, Income tax
- **Job cost ledger** — subsidiary ledger tracking costs per job by category (materials, labor, subs, equipment, other)
- **Equipment ledger** — tracks each piece of equipment: cost, depreciation, maintenance, utilization

### Ch. 3: Accounting Transactions
Key transaction types for construction:
- Invoice charged to job (with/without retention)
- Labor charged to job vs. general overhead
- Payroll taxes, benefits, vacation accruals
- Equipment depreciation, charge to jobs
- Billing clients, retention billing, payment receipt
- Purchase/sale of equipment, inventory
- Costs & profits in excess of billings (underbilling) vs. billings in excess of costs & profits (overbilling)

### Ch. 4: More Construction Accounting
- **Committed costs** — POs and subcontracts signed but not yet invoiced
- **Estimated cost at completion** = costs to date + committed costs + estimated remaining costs
- **Overbillings** = billed more than earned → liability (billings in excess of costs)
- **Underbillings** = earned more than billed → asset (costs in excess of billings)
- **Internal controls** — segregation of duties, authorization levels, physical safeguards, reconciliations

### Ch. 5: Depreciation
- **Straight-line:** (Cost − Salvage) ÷ Useful life
- **Sum-of-years-digits:** Accelerated, front-loaded
- **Declining balance:** Fixed % of book value each year (150% or 200%)
- **MACRS (Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System):**
  - 5-year property: automobiles, light trucks, computers
  - 7-year property: office furniture, construction equipment
  - 15-year property: land improvements, fencing
  - 27.5-year: residential rental
  - 39-year: nonresidential real property
- **Section 179 deduction:** Expense qualifying assets immediately (limits apply)
- **Book depreciation** (for financial statements) vs. **tax depreciation** (for IRS)

### Ch. 6: Analysis of Financial Statements
Key ratios for construction companies:
- **Quick ratio:** (Cash + AR) ÷ Current liabilities (target: >1.0)
- **Current ratio:** Current assets ÷ Current liabilities (target: >1.2)
- **Debt-to-equity:** Total liabilities ÷ Owner's equity (target: <2.0)
- **Collection period:** AR ÷ (Revenue ÷ 365) — measures AR speed
- **Average age of AP:** AP ÷ (COGS ÷ 365) — measures payment speed
- **Working capital turns:** Revenue ÷ Working capital
- **Gross profit margin:** Gross profit ÷ Revenue (construction: 15-35%)
- **General overhead ratio:** Overhead ÷ Revenue (target: <15%)
- **Profit margin:** Net income ÷ Revenue
- **Return on assets:** Net income ÷ Total assets
- **Return on equity:** Net income ÷ Owner's equity
- **Degree of fixed asset newness:** Net fixed assets ÷ Gross fixed assets

## Part III: Managing Costs and Profits (Ch. 7-11)

### Ch. 7: Managing Costs
- Monitor: material purchases, labor, subcontracts, equipment, other costs
- **Cost-loaded schedule** — assigns budgeted costs to each schedule activity
- **Schedule Performance Index (SPI):** Earned value ÷ Planned value (>1.0 = ahead)
- **Cost Performance Index (CPI):** Earned value ÷ Actual cost (>1.0 = under budget)
- **Target CPI:** should remain >1.0 throughout project
- **Project closeout audit** — compare actual to estimated, document lessons learned

### Ch. 8: Determining Labor Burden
Components of labor burden (add to base wage):
- Cash equivalents and allowances
- Payroll taxes (FICA 7.65%, FUTA 0.6%, SUTA varies)
- Unemployment insurance (state-specific)
- Workers' compensation insurance (roofing: high-risk class, 15-40% of payroll)
- General liability insurance (allocated per $100 of payroll)
- Health insurance, dental, vision
- Retirement contributions
- Union payments (dues, pension, training funds)
- Other benefits (tool allowance, vehicle, phone)
- **Total burden rate typically 30-60% above base wage for roofing**

### Ch. 9: Managing General Overhead Costs
- General overhead = costs NOT directly chargeable to specific jobs
- Budget categories: officer salaries, office rent, utilities, insurance, vehicle fleet, software, professional fees, marketing, depreciation, office supplies, travel, training
- **Types of costs:** Fixed (rent), variable (commissions), semi-variable (utilities)
- Overhead allocation methods: % of direct labor, % of revenue, per-job flat rate
- **Break-even analysis:** Fixed costs ÷ Contribution margin ratio

### Ch. 10: Setting Profit Margins for Bidding
- **Profit equation:** Profit = Revenue − Direct costs − Overhead
- **Contribution margin:** Revenue − Variable costs
- **Break-even volume:** Fixed costs ÷ Contribution margin ratio
- **Profit & overhead markup:** Applied on top of estimated direct costs
- Adjust financial mix between residential, commercial, service work

### Ch. 11: Profit Center Analysis
Analyze profitability by:
- **Crews** — revenue, costs, and margin per crew
- **Project managers** — portfolio profitability
- **Estimators** — close rate, margin accuracy, volume
- **Types of jobs** — residential, commercial, service
- **Customers** — revenue, margin, payment speed per customer
- **Equipment** — utilization rate, cost recovery per piece
- General overhead must be allocated to profit centers for true P&L
