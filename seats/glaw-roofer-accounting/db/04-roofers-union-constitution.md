# United Union of Roofers, Waterproofers and Allied Workers — Constitution & By-Laws

**Source:** Constitution and By-Laws of the United Union of Roofers, Waterproofers and Allied Workers. Amended and adopted at the 30th Convention, October 9-13, 2023, Las Vegas, Nevada. Affiliated with AFL-CIO and North America's Building Trades Unions.

## Financial Implications for Roofing Contractors

### Work Jurisdiction (Article II)
The Union claims jurisdiction over ALL roofing, dampproofing, waterproofing work including:

**Steep Roofers (Section 2):**
- All slate, tile, shingle roofing of any type
- Metal flashing on membranes
- Ice and water shields, underlayments
- Composite insulations bonded to roofing
- Solar/photovoltaic cell-type shingles
- Removal and replacement of all roofing materials

**Composition Roofers/Waterproofers (Section 3):**
- Built-up roofing systems (organic/inorganic felts)
- Bituminous waterproofing (above/below grade)
- IRMA (Inverted Roof Membrane Assembly)
- All flashings, ballast, compression seals
- Coal tar pitch and bitumen products
- Spray-applied foam (urethane, polyurethane, polyisocyanurate)

**Elastomeric/Thermoplastic Systems (Section 4):**
- PVC, EPDM, TPO, modified bitumens, KEE
- HDPE, CPE, CSPE, PIB, Neoprene
- All base/curb/counterflashings
- Insulation applied with above systems
- Photovoltaic cell membranes

**Building Envelope Systems (Section 5):**
- All materials and equipment for installation

### Financial Obligations to Union

**Per Capita Tax & Dues:**
- Members pay per capita tax set by Convention
- Late dues: must be paid by last day of 3rd month after due
- Arrears → suspension → loss of benefits

**Convention Expenses (Article V, Section 5):**
- Travel reimbursement: 2 × miles × $0.10 + $950
- Local unions fund additional delegate expenses

### Union Labor Accounting Implications for Contractors

| Cost Component | How to Account |
|----------------|---------------|
| Union wages (negotiated rate) | Direct labor → job cost |
| Fringe benefits (health, pension, annuity) | Labor burden → allocated to jobs |
| Union dues (employer-paid portion) | Labor burden |
| Training fund contributions | Labor burden |
| Supplemental unemployment fund | Labor burden |
| Vacation/holiday fund | Labor burden or direct accrual |
| Apprentice wages (lower scale) | Direct labor at apprentice rate |
| Overtime (union rules: often 1.5x after 8hrs/day) | Premium tracked separately |
| Show-up pay (guaranteed minimum) | Direct labor if job-related, overhead if not |
| Travel/subsistence pay | Per diem or labor burden |

### Prevailing Wage Requirements
- Union contractors on public (government) projects must pay prevailing wage (Davis-Bacon Act)
- Certified payroll reports required weekly
- Fringe must be paid either as cash or to bona fide plans
- **Non-compliance penalties:** debarment, back pay + liquidated damages

### Workers' Compensation Classification
- Roofers are classified in high-risk categories
- Typical WC rates: 15-40% of payroll (varies by state)
- Experience modification rate (EMR) adjusts base rate
- Safety programs directly reduce WC costs (target EMR <1.0)

### Impact on Bidding
Union contractors typically have:
- Higher labor burden (35-60% above base wage)
- More predictable labor costs (negotiated rates)
- Access to skilled labor pool (apprenticeship programs)
- Higher overhead allocation per hour
- Must account for union work rules in productivity estimates
