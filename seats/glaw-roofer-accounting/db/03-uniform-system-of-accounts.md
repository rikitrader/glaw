# Uniform System of Accounts (USOA) — Adapted for Construction

**Source:** Federal Transit Administration — Uniform System of Accounts (USOA). Final Rule January 15, 1993, updated annually. Adapted for construction/roofing context.

## Core Accounting Principles

### Accrual Method (Required)
- Expenditures recorded when liabilities are incurred, regardless of payment timing
- Revenue recorded when earned, regardless of cash receipt
- Events must have support before accrual

### Accounting Records Requirements
- Must allow ready analysis by prescribed accounts
- Must permit preparation of financial and operating data at fiscal year-end
- Clear audit trail to standardized categories

## Labor Distribution Accounting

### Classification of Labor
Three dimensions for every employee:
1. **Compensation category** (operator/field vs. other/office)
2. **Function** (where labor was spent — production, admin, sales, etc.)
3. **Service type** (residential roofing, commercial, solar, restoration)

### Cost Allocation Methods (in order of preference)
1. **Actual time recording** — each employee records hours by function (BEST for direct labor)
2. **Periodic time studies** — sample periods to determine allocation %
3. **Employee estimates** — end-of-period self-allocation (ONLY for indirect labor)

### For multi-service roofing companies:
- Direct labor (installers, crew leads) → allocate to specific job/service line
- Indirect labor (estimators serving multiple lines) → allocate by time study or revenue %
- Admin labor → allocate by headcount or revenue proportion

## Fringe Benefit Accounting

### Allocation of Employer Fringe Costs
Must be allocated to functions and service types using one of:
1. **Hours method:** fringe total × (function hours ÷ total hours)
2. **Dollar method:** fringe total × (function labor cost ÷ total labor cost)
3. **FTE method:** fringe total × (function FTEs ÷ total FTEs)

### Components to Track
- FICA employer share (7.65%)
- Federal unemployment (FUTA 0.6%)
- State unemployment (SUTA varies 0.5-6%+)
- Workers' compensation (roofing class: 15-40% of payroll)
- Health/dental/vision insurance
- Retirement contributions
- Paid time off accruals
- Union dues and training fund contributions

## Bond Accounting
- Bonds used for capital purchases, working capital, lease payments
- Two elements: principal and interest
- Interest → operating expense
- Principal → balance sheet (liability reduction)
- Accrual: prorate interest to reporting period

## Property and Depreciation

### Depreciation Treatment
- Must reflect economic and physical life of asset
- Based on: wear and tear, deterioration, inadequacy, obsolescence
- Accumulate in contra-asset accounts
- Book depreciation (GAAP) separate from tax depreciation (MACRS)

### Construction Equipment Categories
| Category | Examples | Tax Life (MACRS) | Book Life |
|----------|----------|-----------------|-----------|
| Light vehicles | Trucks <6,000 lbs | 5 years | 5-7 years |
| Heavy trucks | Trucks >6,000 lbs | 5 years | 7-10 years |
| Construction equipment | Lifts, generators, compressors | 7 years | 7-12 years |
| Office equipment | Computers, furniture | 5-7 years | 5-7 years |
| Buildings | Office, warehouse | 39 years | 30-40 years |
| Land improvements | Fencing, paving, drainage | 15 years | 15-20 years |

## Expense Transfers
Three types of reclassification:
1. **Function reclassification** — move cost between functions (e.g., admin → job)
2. **Expense reclassification** — move between expense categories
3. **Capitalization of non-operating costs** — move expense to asset (e.g., labor on self-constructed improvement)

## Lease Accounting

### Three Types
1. **True lease (operating):** expense lease payments as incurred
2. **Purchase lease (finance/capital):** capitalize the asset, record liability
3. **Related-party lease:** modified capitalization basis (use lessor's cost)

### Finance Lease Criteria (any one triggers capitalization)
- Transfer of ownership at end
- Bargain purchase option
- Lease term ≥ 75% of economic life
- PV of payments ≥ 90% of fair value

## Capital Grant / Capital Expenditure Accounting
- Capital costs = large, non-annual, depreciable expenditures
- Capitalize at cost (invoice − discount + freight + tax + install)
- Track by: type (vehicle, equipment, facility), function, and service line
- Replacement vs. fleet expansion vs. rehabilitation vs. major components
