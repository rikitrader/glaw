# SEC Form C Offering Statement — Regulation Crowdfunding Reference

**Source:** Check My Pet XP, Inc. Form C Offering Statement filed January 7, 2025. SEC Form C under Securities Act of 1933. Intermediary: PicMii Crowdfunding, LLC (CIK 0001817013, SEC 007-00246).

## Relevance to Roofing Companies

This document provides a template for understanding SEC Regulation CF (crowdfunding) offerings, applicable when roofing companies consider raising capital from non-accredited investors.

## Regulation CF Key Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Maximum annual raise | $5,000,000 |
| Minimum investment | $100 (issuer sets) |
| Investor limits (income/NW <$124K) | Greater of $2,500 or 5% of lesser of income/NW |
| Investor limits (income/NW ≥$124K) | 10% of lesser of income/NW, max $124,000 |
| Resale restriction | 1 year hold (exceptions: to issuer, accredited, family, IPO) |
| Required intermediary | SEC-registered funding portal or broker-dealer |
| Financial statements | Tax returns (<$124K raise), reviewed ($124K-$618K), audited (>$618K) |
| Ongoing reporting | Form C-AR annual report required |

## Offering Structure Elements

### Compensation to Intermediary
- Onboarding fee + % of raise + escrow reimbursement
- Typical: $2,500 + 4-7% of amount raised

### Escrow
- All funds held in escrow until target met + 21 days from filing
- If target not met by deadline → all funds returned, no securities issued

### Amount-Based Incentives (Bonus Shares Example)
- Tier 1: $1,500+ → 10% bonus shares
- Tier 2: $5,000+ → 15% bonus shares
- Tier 3: $10,000+ → 20% bonus shares
- Tier 4: $25,000+ → 25% bonus shares

## Financial Disclosure Requirements

For a Form C filing, the issuer must disclose:
- Total assets, cash, AR, short-term debt, long-term debt
- Revenue, COGS, taxes paid, net income
- Prior fiscal year comparatives
- Officers, directors, 20%+ shareholders
- Use of proceeds
- Risk factors (comprehensive)
- Business description
- Outstanding securities (all classes)

## Applicability to Roofing Companies

### When Reg CF Makes Sense
- Early-stage roofing technology company
- Community-based roofing company wanting customer-investors
- Raising <$5M for expansion without giving up control
- Marketing benefit (customers become investors become ambassadors)

### When Reg CF Does NOT Make Sense
- Established roofing company with bank relationships (cheaper capital)
- Company needing >$5M (use Reg D 506(c) instead)
- Company wanting no ongoing SEC reporting obligations
- Company with sophisticated investor base (Reg D more efficient)

### Alternative Exemptions for Roofing Companies

| Exemption | Best For | Max Raise | Investors |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Reg D 506(b) | PE/angel raise, no advertising | Unlimited | ≤35 non-accredited + unlimited accredited |
| Reg D 506(c) | Broad marketing, verified accredited | Unlimited | Accredited only |
| Reg CF | Community/customer raise | $5M | Anyone |
| Reg A+ Tier 2 | Mini-IPO, broad marketing | $75M | Anyone (with limits) |

## Certifications Required

Both CEO and Principal Financial Officer must certify:
1. Annual report reviewed
2. No untrue material statements
3. Financial statements fairly present financial condition
