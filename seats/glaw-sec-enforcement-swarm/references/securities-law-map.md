# Securities Law Map — Elements Each Seat Must Prove

A working map of the federal securities provisions the seats rely on, and the **elements**
each cause of action requires. This is an orientation aid, **not authority** — every cite
must be verified against primary law before it goes in a deliverable, and case holdings must
be confirmed (they evolve).

> Use the **element tables** to drive the Proof Matrix (Step 5 #5): one row per element,
> mark ✅ PROVEN / 🟡 PARTIAL / ❌ NO EVIDENCE with a source cite.

## Core anti-fraud — Exchange Act §10(b) & Rule 10b-5

Unlawful to employ a manipulative/deceptive device in connection with the purchase or sale
of a security. Elements (SEC enforcement action):

| # | Element | What proves it |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Material misrepresentation or omission (or a manipulative act) | The statement/omission + why a reasonable investor would care (*TSC v. Northway* materiality) |
| 2 | Scienter | Intent or recklessness — contemporaneous knowledge, motive, red flags ignored |
| 3 | In connection with purchase/sale of a security | The transaction nexus |
| 4 | Use of interstate commerce / means | Emails, wires, exchanges (usually trivially met) |

Note: in an **SEC** action there is **no reliance, loss-causation, or damages** element
(those are private-litigation 10b-5 elements) — but RED team will still attack materiality
and scienter hard. Rule 10b-5(a)/(c) reach **scheme liability** (conduct, not just statements).

## Securities Act §17(a) — fraud in the offer/sale

- **§17(a)(1)** requires scienter; **§17(a)(2) & (3)** can be met by **negligence**
  (*Aaron v. SEC*). Useful when scienter is thin.

## Securities Act §5 — unregistered offerings

| Element | Notes |
|---|---|
| Offer/sale of a security | *Howey* test for investment contracts (crypto/token matters) |
| Use of interstate means | — |
| No effective registration statement & no valid exemption | Reg D / Reg S / §4(a)(2) etc. — exemption is an affirmative defense |

## Reporting / books-and-records — Exchange Act §13 & §16

- **§13(a)** — accurate periodic reports (10-K/10-Q/8-K).
- **§13(b)(2)(A)** — keep accurate books and records; **§13(b)(2)(B)** — maintain adequate
  internal accounting controls (ICFR). Strict-ish; no scienter needed for the controls books-
  and-records charges, which is why they are common "fallback" charges.
- **§16(b)** — short-swing profit disgorgement by insiders; **§16(a)** reporting (Forms 3/4/5).

## Insider trading — §10(b) + case law

Two theories:
- **Classical** — corporate insider trades on MNPI, breaching a duty to shareholders.
- **Misappropriation** (*O'Hagan*) — trading on MNPI in breach of a duty owed to the source.
- **Tipping** (*Dirks*) — tipper breaches duty for a **personal benefit**; tippee liable if
  they knew/should have known of the breach. *Salman* (gift to relative = benefit); confirm
  current circuit law on "personal benefit" (*Newman* line) before relying.
- **Rule 10b5-1** plans — affirmative defense **only if** adopted in good faith, not while
  aware of MNPI; abuse patterns: no cooling-off, frequent amendments, single-trade plans.

## FCPA — Foreign Corrupt Practices Act

- **Anti-bribery §30A** — corruptly offer/pay anything of value to a **foreign official** to
  obtain/retain business. Elements: payment/offer · to foreign official · corrupt intent ·
  business purpose · use of interstate means (for domestic concerns). Third-party/intermediary
  payments count where there's knowledge (incl. willful blindness).
- **Books-and-records & internal controls §13(b)** — the workhorse civil charges; no proof of
  bribery intent required, only inaccurate books / weak controls. Often the only SEC count.

## Whistleblower — Dodd-Frank §21F (Exchange Act)

- **Eligibility:** original information, voluntarily provided, leading to >$1M in sanctions →
  **10–30% award**. Form **TCR** is the filing vehicle.
- **Anti-retaliation §21F(h)** — protects the whistleblower; *Digital Realty v. Somers*
  limits the anti-retaliation private remedy to those who report **to the SEC**.

## Market manipulation patterns (for seat 4)

| Pattern | Signature in the blotter |
|---|---|
| Pump-and-dump | Promotion + coordinated buying → price spike → insider distribution |
| Spoofing | Large non-bona-fide orders entered then cancelled to move price |
| Layering | Multiple price levels of spoof orders on one side |
| Wash trades | Matched buy/sell, same beneficial owner, no change in ownership |
| Marking the close | Trades at/near close to set a benchmark/NAV/option-pin |
| Matched orders | Pre-arranged trades between colluding accounts |

## Remedies (for seat 1)

- **Disgorgement** — *Liu v. SEC* (must be net of legitimate expenses, returned to victims);
  **NDAA 2021** extended SEC disgorgement SOL to **10 years for scienter-based** fraud, 5
  years otherwise. Confirm clock per claim.
- **Civil penalties** — tiered per Exchange Act §21(d)(3) / Securities Act §20(d).
- **Injunctions, officer-and-director bars, penny-stock bars, conduct-based bars.**
- **SOL** — 28 U.S.C. §2462 five-year default for penalties (*Gabelli*, *Kokesh*).

## Edge cases
- **Crypto/token** — run *Howey*; SEC v. Ripple split program/institutional sales. Highly
  unsettled; flag for counsel.
- **SPAC / projections** — PSLRA safe harbor's availability to SPACs is contested; do not
  assume protection.
- **Foreign issuer / cross-border** — *Morrison* transactional test limits §10(b) reach.
