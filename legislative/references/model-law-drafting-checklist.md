# Model Law Drafting Checklist

Use this checklist when `glaw-legislative` drafts bills, ordinances, rules, model
statutes, findings, or implementation schedules.

## Authority and Scope

1. Name the jurisdiction and enacting body.
2. Identify the enabling authority and source it with `SRC-####`.
3. State whether the instrument is a bill, ordinance, rule, resolution, charter
   amendment, procurement term, or policy.
4. Identify affected parties and regulated conduct.
5. Define the implementation owner and enforcement body.

## Required Drafting Blocks

| Block | Required Question |
|---|---|
| Title and purpose | What legal problem is being solved? |
| Findings | What record facts justify the measure? |
| Definitions | Which terms can trigger enforcement or liability? |
| Operative rule | What conduct is required, permitted, or prohibited? |
| Exceptions | Who is outside the rule and why? |
| Enforcement | Who enforces, using what process and remedy? |
| Due process | What notice, hearing, appeal, and record rights exist? |
| Fiscal impact | What cost, funding source, and reporting burden attach? |
| Implementation | Who must do what by when? |
| Preemption | Does higher law occupy or limit the field? |
| Severability | What survives if a provision is invalid? |
| Effective date | When does each obligation become active? |

## Drafting Controls

- Use one defined term for one concept.
- Keep obligations in operative sections, not findings.
- Avoid hidden delegation: name who decides, by what standard, and with what record.
- Avoid vague enforcement terms unless source law defines them.
- Put deadlines in a table and route them to `/glaw-docket`.
- For any penalty, state authority, process, appeal, and collection mechanism.
- For any data collection, route privacy and records issues to the owning seat.

## Mandatory Cross-Routes

- Constitutional burden or classification: `/glaw-constitutional`.
- Agency implementation, rulemaking, guidance, hearing, or record: `/glaw-admin-law`.
- Court remedy or review standard: `/glaw-judicial`.
- Tax, fee, budget, or financial statement effect: `/glaw-accounting` and tax seat.
- Regulated entity compliance program: `/glaw-compliance`.

## Adversarial Review

Attack the draft from these positions:

- Regulated entity: impossible or ambiguous compliance burden.
- Budget staff: unfunded mandate or unscored fiscal exposure.
- Agency implementer: missing authority, staff, data, or process.
- Civil-liberties counsel: overbreadth, vagueness, unequal treatment, or due-process gap.
- Reviewing court: severability, preemption, delegation, or arbitrary classifications.

## Sign-Off Condition

A legislative draft is not ready until it has operative text, findings, source
table, implementation table, fiscal-impact note, constitutional/admin-law review,
and unresolved-issue conditions.
