# Constitutional Scrutiny Tier Checklist

Use this checklist before any constitutional conclusion leaves `glaw-constitutional`.
It is a workpaper discipline, not a substitute for verified authority.

## Threshold Gates

1. **Government action**
   - Name the actor, delegated authority, challenged act, and source record.
   - If the actor is private, identify the state-action theory or mark the issue blocked.
   - Required evidence: `SRC-####` for the action and `SRC-####` for the actor's authority.

2. **Standing and justiciability**
   - Injury: concrete, particularized, actual or imminent.
   - Causation: trace the injury to the challenged act.
   - Redressability: identify the remedy and who can grant it.
   - Screen mootness, ripeness, political-question, abstention, immunity, and sovereign-capacity issues.

3. **Right, classification, or structural limit**
   - Identify the right or structural doctrine precisely.
   - Separate state action, facial challenge, as-applied challenge, overbreadth, vagueness, and preemption theories.
   - Assign the branch owner for overlapping issues: legislative text, agency record, or judicial posture.

## Tier Selection

Use the highest plausible tier until authority proves otherwise.

| Trigger | Default Tier | Required Workpaper |
|---|---|---|
| Fundamental right or suspect classification | Strict scrutiny | Compelling interest, narrow tailoring, less-restrictive alternatives |
| Important quasi-suspect classification | Intermediate scrutiny | Important interest, substantial relation, fit evidence |
| Ordinary economic or social regulation | Rational basis | Legitimate interest, rational nexus, no arbitrary carveouts |
| Speech content/viewpoint restriction | Strict scrutiny or categorical doctrine | Forum, speaker, burden, exceptions, tailoring |
| Time/place/manner speech regulation | Intermediate/path-specific | Content neutrality, alternative channels, narrow fit |
| Search/seizure | Amendment-specific reasonableness | Warrant, exception, suspicion level, scope, remedy |
| Due process property/liberty deprivation | Mathews or doctrine-specific | Interest, risk of error, safeguards, government burden |
| Federalism or separation of powers | Structural doctrine | Text, history, delegation, commandeering, removal, spending conditions |

## Required Analysis Blocks

Every memo must answer:

- What exact government act is challenged?
- What source proves the act?
- What doctrine supplies the rule?
- What facts trigger the tier?
- What government interest is asserted?
- What record evidence supports the interest?
- What nexus connects the act to the interest?
- What less-burdensome alternatives were considered?
- What remedy is legally available?
- What fact or authority would change the answer?

## Adversarial Attacks

Run these attacks before sign-off:

- **Civil-liberties challenger:** the burden is broader than the source record supports.
- **Government defense counsel:** the plaintiff lacks standing or asks for an unavailable remedy.
- **Skeptical judge:** the memo skipped a threshold doctrine.
- **Admin-record reviewer:** the record does not support the stated interest.
- **Outside critic:** the memo relies on policy preference instead of authority.

## Sign-Off Condition

The branch may sign off only when each conclusion has:

- a current `SRC-####` source,
- a named doctrine,
- a tier or reason no tier applies,
- a government-interest and fit analysis where required,
- an unresolved-risk table,
- and a citation-gate path for every legal proposition.
