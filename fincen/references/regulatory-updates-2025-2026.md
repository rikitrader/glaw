# FinCEN Regulatory-Change Ledger — 2025–2026

**Source of authority:** the user's subscribed *FinCEN Updates* email feed
(`fincenupdates@public.govdelivery.com`), each item cross-checked against the primary
release on **FinCEN.gov** / the **Federal Register**. This is the standing
regulatory-change ledger for the GLAW FinCEN cell. Every entry carries a date, the
release type, the substance, the primary authority, and a one-line **Compliance impact**
for a GLAW client.

> **Status discipline.** Many of the most consequential 2026 items are **PROPOSED rules
> (NPRMs)** with open comment periods and no settled effective date. An NPRM is *not law*.
> Where a precise Federal Register citation is not independently confirmed below, the rule
> is named accurately without a fabricated number. **Always verify current status
> (proposed vs. interim-final vs. final, comment deadline, effective date) on FinCEN.gov
> and federalregister.gov before relying on any entry.**

Legend for **Type**: NPRM (notice of proposed rulemaking) · Final · FAQ · Advisory ·
Alert · Notice · GTO (geographic targeting order) · Enforcement · Exemptive/Exceptive
Relief · OMB/PRA (paperwork-renewal comment request) · Report · Admin (organizational).

## (A) Rulemaking & program reform

### 2026-04-07 — FinCEN Proposes Rule to Fundamentally Reform FI AML/CFT Programs `NPRM`
- **Substance:** FinCEN proposes to overhaul the BSA AML/CFT **program rules** for all
  covered financial institutions, shifting from a checklist posture to **risk-based,
  "effective and reasonably designed"** programs. Implements the mandate of the
  **Anti-Money Laundering Act of 2020 (AMLA)** and the AMLA-directed requirement that
  Treasury issue national AML/CFT **Priorities** that institutions must incorporate.
- **Critical mechanics:** this NPRM **fully supersedes and withdraws** the **July 3, 2024**
  proposed program rule (89 Fed. Reg., "AML/CFT Program" NPRM). Treat the 2024 proposal as
  dead; the 2026 proposal is the live text.
- **Authority:** 31 U.S.C. 5318(h) (program requirement); AMLA-2020 §§ 6101, 6002, 6101(b)
  (effective-and-reasonably-designed standard, Priorities); 31 CFR Chapter X program
  sections (1020.210, 1022.210, 1023.210, etc.). Verify the Fed. Reg. NPRM number on
  federalregister.gov.
- **Compliance impact:** every covered client's written AML program will need to be
  re-anchored to a **documented risk assessment** that maps to the national Priorities;
  start the gap analysis now, but **do not rebuild to proposed text as if final**.

### 2026-02-24 — Nominations for the Bank Secrecy Act Advisory Group (BSAAG) `Admin`
- **Substance:** FinCEN invited nominations for membership on the **BSAAG** — the standing
  public-private advisory body (31 U.S.C. 5311 note / Annunzio-Wylie Act § 1564) through
  which industry shapes BSA policy.
- **Compliance impact:** informational; a channel for industry comment, not an obligation.

### 2026-04-16 — FinCEN Releases FY2025 Year in Review `Report`
- **Substance:** annual summary of FinCEN's enforcement, rulemaking, BOI, and analytic
  activity for fiscal year 2025.
- **Compliance impact:** use as a read on enforcement priorities and exam emphasis for the
  coming year; not itself binding.

### 2026-04-30 — RMSB Registration OMB Renewal — Comment Request `OMB/PRA`
- **Substance:** FinCEN requests comments on renewal of the OMB control number for the
  **Registration of Money Services Business (RMSB)** regulations and **FinCEN Form 107**.
- **Authority:** 31 CFR 1022.380 (MSB registration); Form 107.
- **Compliance impact:** MSB clients should confirm their Form 107 registration is current
  and renewed every two years; the renewal itself is a paperwork formality.

## (B) CDD / Beneficial Ownership

### 2026-02-13 — FinCEN Issues Exceptive Relief to Streamline CDD Requirements `Exceptive Relief`
- **Substance:** FinCEN grants **exceptive relief** reducing "burdensome and outdated"
  customer-due-diligence requirements. The relief modifies how covered institutions must
  satisfy CDD obligations (read with the CDD Rule).
- **Authority:** exceptive-relief authority under 31 U.S.C. 5318(a)(5)/(7); CDD Rule
  31 CFR 1010.230. Read the order text for exact scope.
- **Compliance impact:** institutions may be able to scale back certain CDD steps — but
  **only to the extent the order's terms allow**; confirm scope before changing onboarding.

### 2026-05-06 — CDD FAQs Consolidated & Updated to Align with the Exceptive Relief Order `FAQ`
- **Substance:** FinCEN consolidated and updated its **Customer Due Diligence FAQs** to
  conform to the Feb-13-2026 exceptive-relief order.
- **Authority:** interpretive of CDD Rule 31 CFR 1010.230 + the relief order.
- **Compliance impact:** the consolidated FAQ is the current interpretive baseline for CDD;
  prior CDD FAQ guidance is superseded where it conflicts.

> **CTA / Beneficial Ownership Information (BOI) reporting note.** The Corporate
> Transparency Act BOI reporting regime (31 U.S.C. 5336; 31 CFR 1010.380) is **separate**
> from the CDD Rule and has been in heavy flux (interim final rule limiting reporting to
> foreign reporting companies). **Verify the current BOI reporting scope and deadlines on
> FinCEN.gov before advising any entity on filing** — see `cdd-beneficial-ownership.md`.

## (C) SAR / CTR reporting

### 2025-10-09 — SAR FAQs (Four) to Clarify Suspicious-Activity-Reporting Requirements `FAQ`
- **Substance:** FinCEN, **jointly with the FRB, FDIC, NCUA, and OCC**, issued four FAQs
  clarifying SAR requirements. Stated goal: stop **needless expenditure of resources** on
  defensive/duplicative filings and on continuing-activity reviews.
- **Authority:** interpretive of SAR rules — 31 CFR 1020.320 (banks) and parallel sections
  (1022.320 MSBs, 1023.320 broker-dealers, etc.); 31 U.S.C. 5318(g).
- **Compliance impact:** institutions can rely on the FAQs to **right-size** SAR programs
  (e.g., continuing-activity review cadence); document the FAQ basis for any change.

## (D) Geographic Targeting Orders (GTOs)

### Southwest Border GTO — money-services-business cash-reporting order
- **2025-09-08 — Modified Southwest Border GTO** `GTO` — modified the SWB GTO terms.
- **2026-03-10 — Expanded Southwest Border GTO** `GTO` — expanded covered geography/scope.
- **2026-03-19 — FAQs on the Southwest Border GTO** `FAQ` — implementation guidance.
- **Substance:** a GTO lowering the **currency-transaction-report threshold** for **money
  services businesses** in specified Southwest-border ZIP codes (well below the $10,000 CTR
  threshold) to surface cartel/bulk-cash activity.
- **Authority:** GTO authority under 31 U.S.C. 5326; 31 CFR 1010.370.
- **Compliance impact:** MSBs in covered ZIPs must file CTRs at the **GTO's lower
  threshold** for the order's duration; confirm covered ZIPs and the exact threshold from
  the current order text (terms change with each modification).

### Minnesota (Hennepin / Ramsey) Fraud GTO
- **2026-01-09 — Secretary Bessent announces initiatives to combat fraud in Minnesota** `Notice`.
- **2026-01-13 — FAQs on the Minnesota Fraud GTO** `FAQ`.
- **2026-02-27 — Limited Exemptive Relief Order for FIs affected by the Minnesota GTO** `Exceptive Relief`.
- **2026-04-09 — Updated FAQs on the Minnesota (Hennepin/Ramsey) GTO** `FAQ`.
- **Substance:** a GTO directed at **money transmitters and banks** in **Hennepin and
  Ramsey Counties, Minnesota**, responding to large-scale public-program fraud; the Feb-27
  order grants limited exemptive relief to certain affected institutions.
- **Authority:** 31 U.S.C. 5326; 31 CFR 1010.370; exemptive relief under 5318(a).
- **Compliance impact:** covered money transmitters/banks must comply with the GTO's
  reporting/recordkeeping terms; affected institutions should read the exemptive-relief
  order to see whether they qualify for the carve-out.

### 2025-12-22 — Data-Driven Border Operation re: Potential Money Laundering `Notice`
- **Substance:** FinCEN announced a data-driven border operation targeting potential
  money-laundering corridors (paired with the SWB GTO program).
- **Compliance impact:** signals heightened scrutiny of border-region cash flows; context
  for SWB-GTO compliance.

## (E) Crypto / Stablecoin — GENIUS Act

### 2026-04-08 — Treasury Proposes Rule to Implement the GENIUS Act's Illicit-Finance Requirements `NPRM`
- **Substance:** Treasury/FinCEN proposes rules implementing the **GENIUS Act**'s
  anti-illicit-finance provisions for the **payment-stablecoin** regime.
- **Authority:** GENIUS Act (Guiding and Establishing National Innovation for U.S.
  Stablecoins Act of 2025); BSA authorities at 31 U.S.C. 5311 et seq. Verify Fed. Reg. no.
- **Compliance impact:** stablecoin issuers and intermediaries should map themselves to the
  proposed FI definition and begin scoping BSA program build-out (proposed, not final).

### 2026-06-18 — GENIUS Act Customer Identification Program (CIP) Joint NPRM `NPRM`
- **Substance:** FinCEN **jointly with the OCC, FRB, FDIC, and NCUA** proposes a rule
  treating **permitted payment stablecoin issuers** as **financial institutions under the
  BSA**, requiring each to maintain a **Customer Identification Program (CIP)**.
- **Authority:** **Fed. Reg. 2026-12460** (joint NPRM); GENIUS Act; BSA CIP authority
  31 U.S.C. 5318(l); CIP rules 31 CFR 1020.220 (analogous program to be applied to issuers).
- **Compliance impact:** a permitted payment stablecoin issuer must plan to stand up a
  **CIP** (identity collection/verification, recordkeeping, OFAC list comparison) as part
  of a full BSA program — **but the rule is proposed; comment, do not yet build to final.**

## (F) Enforcement actions

### 2026-03-06 — Historic $80,000,000 Penalty Against Canaccord Genuity LLC `Enforcement`
- **Substance:** FinCEN assessed an **$80M civil money penalty** against **Canaccord Genuity
  LLC** for **securities-fraud-related BSA violations** — among the largest BSA penalties
  against a broker-dealer.
- **Authority:** BSA program/SAR requirements for broker-dealers, 31 CFR 1023.210 / 1023.320;
  penalty authority 31 U.S.C. 5321.
- **Compliance impact:** broker-dealers — a defective AML program plus missed SARs on
  securities-fraud red flags is now an eight-figure exposure; pressure-test microcap /
  penny-stock surveillance.

### 2025-12-09 — $3,500,000 Penalty Against Paxful `Enforcement`
- **Substance:** FinCEN assessed a **$3.5M penalty** against **Paxful** (a peer-to-peer
  convertible-virtual-currency platform / MSB) for **facilitating suspicious activity
  involving illicit actors** — AML-program and SAR failures.
- **Authority:** MSB AML-program and SAR rules, 31 CFR 1022.210 / 1022.320; 31 U.S.C. 5321.
- **Compliance impact:** crypto MSBs are squarely in scope; a thin AML program and unfiled
  SARs on illicit-actor activity draws a penalty even at mid-size platform scale.

> See `enforcement-actions.md` for full facts/lessons and the related earlier actions
> surfaced in the corpus (Brink's $37M, Feb 2025; Asre $100k, Jan 2024).

## (G) International / FATF

### 2025-11-21 — FATF Identifies Jurisdictions with AML/CFT/CPF Deficiencies `Advisory`
- **Substance:** FinCEN relayed the **FATF** plenary update identifying jurisdictions with
  AML / countering-financing-of-terrorism / counter-proliferation-financing deficiencies —
  the FATF **"grey list"** (increased monitoring) and **"black list"** (call for action:
  Iran, DPRK).
- **Authority:** FATF Recommendations; incorporated via FinCEN advisory and the
  general-due-diligence expectations of 31 CFR Chapter X.
- **Compliance impact:** apply enhanced due diligence to transactions touching listed
  jurisdictions; refresh the country-risk matrix against the **current** FATF lists (they
  change at each plenary).

### 2025-09-05 — Guidance on Cross-Border Information Sharing `Advisory`
- **Substance:** FinCEN guidance to FIs on sharing information **across borders** consistent
  with BSA confidentiality constraints.
- **Compliance impact:** enables (within limits) cross-border AML information sharing inside
  a financial group; read alongside the SAR confidentiality rules before sharing.

### 2026-06-05 — Joint Advisory on Non-Work-Authorized Populations & Their Employers `Advisory`
- **Substance:** joint advisory on financial-system-integrity risks tied to
  non-work-authorized populations and the employers who pay them off-book.
- **Compliance impact:** adds payroll/employment-fraud red flags to transaction monitoring.

### 2026-05-11 — Notice on Human Trafficking Threat During the 2026 FIFA World Cup `Notice`
### 2026-06-11 — Spanish Translation of the 2026 FIFA World Cup HT Notice `Notice`
- **Substance:** notice on elevated **human-trafficking** financial activity around the
  **2026 FIFA World Cup** (with a Spanish-language version for broader institution reach).
- **Authority:** builds on FinCEN's human-trafficking advisories (red-flag typologies).
- **Compliance impact:** apply HT financial red flags (funnel accounts, third-party
  lodging/travel patterns) during the event window; tag World-Cup-host-region activity.

### 2026-05-11 — Alert to Stop Money Laundering by Iran's IRGC `Alert`
- **Substance:** FinCEN alert with red flags for laundering by the **Islamic Revolutionary
  Guard Corps (IRGC)** and its networks.
- **Authority:** read with OFAC's IRGC/Iran sanctions (the IRGC is designated); 31 CFR Ch. X
  SAR triggers.
- **Compliance impact:** screen for IRGC-linked typologies; an IRGC nexus is both a **SAR**
  trigger and an **OFAC** exposure — route to `/glaw-fincen-ofac`.

  (often crypto-denominated extortion payments).
- **Compliance impact:** add sextortion indicators to fraud/crypto monitoring.

### 2025-11-13 — Sinaloa Cartel: 10 Mexico-Based Gambling Establishments — Primary ML Concern `Notice`
- **Substance:** FinCEN found **10 Mexico-based gambling establishments** linked to the
  **Sinaloa Cartel** to be of **primary money-laundering concern**.

## (I) Whistleblower & Information Sharing

### 2026-02-13 — FinCEN Launches Whistleblower Tips Webpage `Notice`
- **Substance:** FinCEN launched a **webpage for whistleblower tips** on fraud, money
  laundering, and sanctions violations under the **AML Whistleblower Program**.
- **Authority:** **AMLA § 6314**, codified at **31 U.S.C. 5323** (as amended by the AML
  Whistleblower Improvement Act of 2022).
- **Compliance impact:** insiders now have a funded, anti-retaliation reporting channel —
  raises the stakes for internal AML controls and culpable-conduct exposure. See
  `whistleblower-program.md`.

### 2026-06-12 — Guidance to Help FIs Eliminate Fraud Through Information Sharing `Advisory`
- **Substance:** guidance encouraging FIs to use **information sharing** to detect/eliminate
  fraud.
- **Authority:** **314(b)** voluntary information-sharing safe harbor — 31 U.S.C. 5311 note;
  **31 CFR 1010.540**.
- **Compliance impact:** institutions can lean harder on 314(b) sharing for fraud (not just
  ML/TF); confirm 314(b) registration and the safe-harbor conditions before sharing.
