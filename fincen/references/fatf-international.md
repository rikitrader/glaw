# FATF & International AML/CFT

## A. FATF — the standard-setter
The **Financial Action Task Force** is the intergovernmental body that sets global AML/CFT/CPF
standards (the **FATF 40 Recommendations**). FinCEN relays FATF plenary outcomes to U.S.
financial institutions by advisory.

### The two FATF lists
- **"Black list" — High-Risk Jurisdictions Subject to a Call for Action.** As of recent
  plenaries: **Iran** and **DPRK (North Korea)** (countermeasures / enhanced scrutiny).
- **"Grey list" — Jurisdictions Under Increased Monitoring.** A rotating set of countries with
  strategic AML/CFT deficiencies that have committed to remediate. **Membership changes at each
  plenary** — never quote the grey list from memory.

### 2025-11-21 — FATF Identifies Jurisdictions with AML/CFT/CPF Deficiencies `Advisory`
- **Substance:** FinCEN's relay of the current FATF list update.
- **Compliance impact:** refresh the country-risk matrix against the **current** lists; apply
  **enhanced due diligence** to transactions touching listed jurisdictions; apply
  **countermeasures** for call-for-action jurisdictions (Iran, DPRK).
- **Authority:** FATF Recommendations; effectuated through FinCEN advisory and the
  general/correspondent due-diligence expectations of 31 CFR Chapter X (incl. 31 CFR 1010.610
  for foreign correspondent accounts).

> Corpus shows FATF advisories recur (2025-06-23, 2025-11-21, and earlier). **Always use the
> latest plenary's list** — verify on FinCEN.gov / fatf-gafi.org.

## B. Cross-border information sharing
### 2025-09-05 — Guidance on Cross-Border Information Sharing `Advisory`
- Lets FIs share AML information **across borders** within a group, subject to BSA
  confidentiality constraints. Read with the 314(b) framework (`sar-ctr-reporting.md`).

## C. Sanctions interplay (hand to OFAC seat)
- FATF call-for-action jurisdictions (Iran, DPRK) overlap heavily with **OFAC comprehensive
  programs**. A FATF-listed nexus is frequently **also** an OFAC exposure.
- The **2026-05-11 IRGC alert** is the live example: an Iran/IRGC nexus is simultaneously a
  **SAR** trigger and an **OFAC** sanctions issue. Route sanctions doctrine to
  `/glaw-fincen-ofac` and `/glaw-regulatory-aml`.

## D. Special-measures / primary-money-laundering-concern (31 U.S.C. 5318A)
FinCEN can name a jurisdiction, institution, or class of transactions a **primary money
laundering concern** and impose special measures. Live example: **2025-11-13**, ten Mexican
gambling establishments tied to the **Sinaloa Cartel** found to be of primary ML concern.
