# Customer Due Diligence & Beneficial Ownership

Two distinct regimes that are often conflated. Keep them separate.

## A. The CDD Rule (the "fifth pillar") — 31 CFR 1010.230
Applies to **covered financial institutions** (banks, broker-dealers, mutual funds, futures
commission merchants/introducing brokers). Effective since May 11, 2018.

### The four CDD prongs
1. **Identify and verify the identity of customers** (the CIP — 31 CFR 1020.220 et al.).
2. **Identify and verify the beneficial owners of legal-entity customers** — both:
   - **Ownership prong:** each individual owning **≥ 25%** of the equity; and
   - **Control prong:** at least **one individual with significant managerial control**.
3. **Understand the nature and purpose** of the customer relationship (to build a risk
   profile and a baseline of expected activity).
4. **Conduct ongoing monitoring** to identify/report suspicious activity and to keep customer
   information current.

### 2026 changes — read these on top of the rule
- **2026-02-13 — Exceptive Relief to Streamline CDD** (`regulatory-updates-2025-2026.md` § B):
  reduces certain "burdensome and outdated" CDD steps. Scope is defined by the order — do not
  over-read it.
- **2026-05-06 — Consolidated CDD FAQs aligned to the relief order:** now the current
  interpretive baseline; supersedes conflicting prior CDD FAQ guidance.
- **2025-06-27 (corpus, pre-window):** FinCEN permitted banks an **alternative collection
  method for TIN information** — a related CDD/CIP flexibility worth checking if a bank client
  asks about TIN collection.

> **Action note:** when advising on CDD, quote the **consolidated FAQ** and the
> **relief-order terms**, not the un-modified 2018 rule alone. Verify the current FAQ text on
> FinCEN.gov.

## B. The CTA / Beneficial Ownership Information (BOI) reporting regime — separate
**Do not confuse with the CDD Rule.** This is a **reporting obligation on entities
themselves**, not a diligence obligation on financial institutions.
- **Authority:** Corporate Transparency Act, AMLA-2020 § 6403 → **31 U.S.C. 5336**; rules at
  **31 CFR 1010.380**.
- **What it requires:** "reporting companies" file BOI (their beneficial owners and company
  applicants) with FinCEN.
- **Status — VOLATILE.** The BOI regime has been through litigation, an enforcement
  suspension, and an **interim final rule narrowing the scope** (broadly, limiting mandatory
  reporting to **foreign** reporting companies and exempting U.S.-formed entities). **The
  scope and deadlines have changed repeatedly.**
  - **Compliance impact:** **never state a current BOI filing obligation from memory.**
    Verify the live rule, the entity's status (domestic vs. foreign), and any deadline on
    FinCEN.gov / federalregister.gov before advising any entity to file or not file.
