# FinCEN Enforcement Actions — Lessons Ledger

Recent civil-money-penalty actions surfaced in the corpus, with the violation type, penalty,
and the operative lesson for a GLAW client. Penalty authority: **31 U.S.C. 5321** (civil),
**5322** (criminal, willful).

## 2026-03-06 — Canaccord Genuity LLC — **$80,000,000** (historic) `broker-dealer`
- **Facts:** securities-fraud-related BSA violations; FinCEN's framing tied AML-program and
  SAR failures to underlying securities fraud (microcap/penny-stock context typical of such
  cases).
- **Violation type:** AML-program deficiency + SAR failures (31 CFR 1023.210 / 1023.320).
- **Lesson:** for **broker-dealers**, a thin AML program plus missed SARs on securities-fraud
  red flags is now an **eight-figure** exposure. Pressure-test microcap deposit/liquidation
  surveillance, suspicious-trading SAR triggers, and independent testing.

## 2025-12-09 — Paxful — **$3,500,000** `crypto MSB`
- **Facts:** the peer-to-peer convertible-virtual-currency platform facilitated suspicious
  activity involving illicit actors; AML-program and SAR shortcomings.
- **Violation type:** MSB AML-program + SAR failures (31 CFR 1022.210 / 1022.320).
- **Lesson:** **crypto MSBs are fully in scope.** A mid-size platform with a weak AML program
  and unfiled SARs on illicit-actor activity draws a penalty. KYC, transaction monitoring, and
  OFAC screening are not optional for CVC money transmitters.

## 2025-02-06 — Brink's Global Services USA, Inc. — **$37,000,000** `cross-border cash`
- **Facts (corpus):** BSA violations tied to cross-border movement of currency / armored-cash
  logistics; reporting/program failures.
- **Lesson:** **cross-border bulk-cash carriers** are BSA-covered; cross-border currency
  reporting and an adequate program matter even outside traditional banking.

## 2024-01-31 — Gyanendra Kumar Asre — **$100,000** `individual liability`
- **Facts (corpus):** civil money penalty against an **individual** for BSA violations (a
  compliance/AML-responsible person at a small institution).
- **Lesson:** **individual accountability is real.** A named BSA/AML officer or responsible
  person can be personally penalized for program failures — not just the institution.

## Earlier corpus reference points (context)
- **2021-08-10 — BitMEX — $100M** (unregistered futures commission merchant; willful BSA
  violations) — crypto-adjacent, individual-and-entity exposure.
- **2020-10-19 — first bitcoin "mixer" penalized** — mixers are MSBs and BSA-covered.

## Cross-cutting lessons
1. **The program is the product.** Most penalties trace to a deficient or unimplemented AML
   program (the five pillars) plus **failure to file SARs** on activity the FI saw.
2. **Sector breadth.** Broker-dealers, crypto platforms, cash logistics, futures — all
   covered. "We're not a bank" is not a defense.
3. **Individuals are exposed.** The whistleblower program (`whistleblower-program.md`) makes
   internal reporting of these failures more likely.
