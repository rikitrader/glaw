## Geographic Targeting Orders — the cash side of trade-based value movement
Authority for all: 31 U.S.C. 5326; 31 CFR 1010.370.

### Southwest Border GTO (MSBs)
- **2025-09-08 — Modified** · **2026-03-10 — Expanded** · **2026-03-19 — FAQs**.
- Lowers the **CTR threshold for MSBs** in listed Southwest-border ZIP codes to surface
  cartel/bulk-cash flows. *Impact:* covered MSBs file CTRs at the **lower GTO threshold** —
  confirm covered ZIPs + threshold from the **current** order.

### Minnesota (Hennepin/Ramsey) Fraud GTO (money transmitters & banks)
- **2026-01-09 — Bessent initiatives** · **2026-01-13 — FAQs** · **2026-02-27 — Limited
  Exemptive Relief** · **2026-04-09 — Updated FAQs**.
- *Impact:* covered money transmitters/banks comply with the GTO terms; affected institutions
  read the exemptive-relief order for the carve-out.

## Analyst takeaways
1. TBML moves value by **lying about a trade** — over/under/multiple-invoicing, phantom
   shipments, mis-described goods. Reconcile invoice ↔ bill of lading ↔ customs declaration and
   price against the **real market**.
2. GTOs are the **cash-reporting** counterpart — structuring **around the lowered threshold** in
   border ZIPs is the tell.
3. Self-dealing **corporate networks** (buyer = seller = forwarder behind the curtain) are the
   structural red flag.
