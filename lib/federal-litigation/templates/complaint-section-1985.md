- § 1985(3) ELEMENTS: (1) a conspiracy; (2) for the purpose of depriving, directly or indirectly, any person or class of the equal protection of the laws or equal privileges and immunities; (3) an act in furtherance; (4) injury to person/property OR deprivation of a right/privilege of a U.S. citizen. (Griffin v. Breckenridge.)
- CLASS-BASED ANIMUS IS MANDATORY: the conspiracy must be motivated by "some racial, or perhaps otherwise class-based, invidiously discriminatory animus." Economic/commercial or political animus is generally insufficient. Define the protected class with care; circuits split on which non-racial classes qualify.
- PRIVATE-CONSPIRACY RIGHT LIMITATION: for purely private conspiracies, the deprived right must be one protected against private (not just state) interference — chiefly the right to interstate travel and Thirteenth-Amendment / § 1981/§ 1982 rights (United Bhd. of Carpenters v. Scott; Bray v. Alexandria Women's Health Clinic). Most equal-protection rights require state action.
- INTRACORPORATE CONSPIRACY DOCTRINE: a single entity acting through its own officers/employees generally cannot "conspire" with itself. Plead independent actors or an applicable exception (scope-of-employment / personal-stake); circuits split on its application to § 1985(3).
- NO INDEPENDENT RIGHTS: § 1985(3) creates no substantive rights; it is a remedy for conspiracy to violate rights secured elsewhere. Identify the underlying right.
- § 1986 IS DERIVATIVE: a § 1986 claim FAILS without a valid underlying § 1985 claim. § 1986 also carries an express ONE-YEAR statute of limitations ("within one year after the cause of action has accrued") — the shortest in the federal civil-rights scheme; calendar it immediately.
- § 1985 LIMITATIONS: borrows the forum state's personal-injury statute of limitations (varies by state); confirm and plead within it.
- STATE ACTION vs. COLOR OF LAW: if any Defendant is a state actor, consider whether the claim should also/instead be pleaded under 42 U.S.C. § 1983 (and whether qualified immunity applies to individual-capacity officials).
- STANDING / CONCRETE INJURY: allege a concrete, particularized, actual-or-imminent injury (TransUnion LLC v. Ramirez; Spokeo v. Robins) — a bare statutory or procedural violation without concrete harm will not confer Article III standing.
- DAMAGES: §§ 1985(3)/1986 do NOT provide statutory or treble damages of their own; punitive damages require malicious/reckless intent and are unavailable against governmental entities. Treble/statutory-damages language in the prayer must be tied to a separately pleaded statute that authorizes it.
