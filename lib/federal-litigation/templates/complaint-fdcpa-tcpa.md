============================== CAUSE-SPECIFIC GOTCHAS — VERIFY BEFORE FILING ==============================

FDCPA (15 U.S.C. § 1692):
- 1-YEAR statute of limitations from the violation: § 1692k(d). Each violative communication may be a separate accrual; do NOT assume continuing-violation tolling.
- Statutory damages CAP at $1,000 PER ACTION (not per violation) — Wright v. Finance Serv. of Norwalk. Multiple violations affect the § 1692k(b) "frequency and persistence" factor, not the cap.
- Standing post-Spokeo / TransUnion v. Ramirez (2021): plead CONCRETE injury-in-fact, not a bare procedural violation. Tie each violation to real-world harm.
- Confirm Defendant is a "debt collector," not a creditor collecting its own debt in its own name (creditors generally exempt). Watch the Henson v. Santander debt-buyer wrinkle.
- "Least sophisticated consumer" standard governs deception — cite the controlling circuit's formulation.
- § 1692g validation: 30-day dispute window; verify the 5-day notice timing and any overshadowing.

TCPA (47 U.S.C. § 227):
- ATDS definition NARROWED by Facebook v. Duguid (2021): equipment must use a random or sequential number generator to STORE or PRODUCE numbers. Plead facts supporting this (or rely on prerecorded/artificial-voice prong, which is independent of ATDS).
- 4-YEAR federal catch-all limitations period (28 U.S.C. § 1658). Confirm — no TCPA-specific SOL.
- "Prior express written consent" required for telemarketing to cell numbers; revocation can be by any reasonable means.
- DNC claim (§ 227(c)) requires MORE THAN ONE call in 12 months AND a residential subscriber registered ≥31 days. Cell used as residence may qualify — verify circuit treatment.
- Willfulness for trebling does NOT require knowledge the conduct was unlawful — only that the act was intentional (FCC interpretation); confirm circuit.
- Vicarious liability for third-party callers under agency principles (Dish Network/FCC ruling) — plead agency facts to reach the principal.
- Article III standing for bare TCPA texts: most circuits find a single unwanted text sufficient (Salcedo v. Hanna split in the 11th Cir. — VERIFY your circuit on a single-text theory).

GENERAL PLEADING:
- Federal-question jurisdiction confirmed for TCPA by Mims v. Arrow (2012); FDCPA is federal-question by statute.
- This is NOT a fraud claim, so Rule 9(b) particularity generally does not apply — but if a state UDAP/fraud count is added, Rule 9(b) WILL apply to that count.
- NOT a securities action: PSLRA discovery stay + Tellabs scienter do NOT apply.
- NOT a qui tam: FCA seal + § 3730(b)(2) disclosure do NOT apply.
- NOT an employment action: Title VII EEOC exhaustion + 90-day right-to-sue window do NOT apply.
- NOT a RICO action: RICO enterprise-distinctness (person ≠ enterprise) does NOT apply unless you add a RICO count.
- Class action? If pleading TCPA on a class basis, add Rule 23 allegations, numerosity from call records, and address the per-violation aggregate-damages exposure.
- Confirm diversity amount-in-controversy if relying on § 1332 — statutory-damages-only cases rarely clear $75k absent a class or large call volume; § 1331 is the safer hook.
- Check for an arbitration clause / class-action waiver in the underlying credit agreement before filing.
