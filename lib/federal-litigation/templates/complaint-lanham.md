• STANDING (Lexmark, 572 U.S. 118 (2014)): plaintiff must plead (1) zone-of-interests — an injury to a commercial interest in reputation or sales, NOT a mere consumer; and (2) proximate cause — economic/reputational injury flowing directly from deception of consumers. For false advertising, parties need not be direct competitors, but the injury must flow from the defendant's deception. Plead both prongs explicitly.

• PROTECTABILITY: Unregistered marks/trade dress require distinctiveness (inherent or secondary meaning). PRODUCT-DESIGN trade dress can NEVER be inherently distinctive and ALWAYS requires secondary meaning (Wal-Mart v. Samara, 529 U.S. 205 (2000)). PACKAGING trade dress can be inherently distinctive (Two Pesos v. Taco Cabana, 505 U.S. 763 (1992)).

• FUNCTIONALITY: For unregistered trade dress, plaintiff bears the burden to prove non-functionality, 15 U.S.C. § 1125(a)(3). A functional feature is not protectable (TrafFix v. Marketing Displays, 532 U.S. 23 (2001)). Plead non-functionality with particularity.
