CAUSE-SPECIFIC GOTCHAS — COPYRIGHT INFRINGEMENT (17 U.S.C. § 501) — verify each before filing:

1. REGISTRATION IS A PRECONDITION TO SUIT (Fourth Estate Pub. Benefit Corp. v. Wall-Street.com, 586 U.S. 296 (2019)): A § 411(a) civil action may be brought only AFTER the Copyright Office has REGISTERED (or, in the circuits' minority/refusal posture, acted on) the claim — the mere filing of an APPLICATION is NOT enough in most circuits. Confirm a Certificate of Registration has ISSUED (or a refusal under § 411(a) with notice to the Register). Do not file on an "application approach" pending application.
2. § 410(c) PRESUMPTION: Registration made within 5 years of first publication = prima facie evidence of validity. Plead the registration date relative to first publication.
3. STATUTORY DAMAGES & FEES ELIGIBILITY (17 U.S.C. § 412): NO statutory damages and NO attorneys' fees for (a) infringement of an UNPUBLISHED work commenced before registration, or (b) infringement of a published work commenced after first publication and before registration UNLESS registration was made within 3 months of first publication. Map each infringement-commencement date against the registration date BEFORE pleading § 504(c)/§ 505 relief; if barred, plead actual damages/profits under § 504(b) only.
4. § 504(c) ELECTION: Plaintiff elects statutory vs. actual damages any time before final judgment; statutory range is $750–$30,000 per work, up to $150,000 if willful, as low as $200 for innocent infringement. "Per work," not per act of infringement (Bryant v. Media Right; compilation = one work under § 504(c)(1)).
5. SUBSTANTIAL SIMILARITY: Plead access + copying of ORIGINAL PROTECTED EXPRESSION; idea/expression dichotomy (§ 102(b)) — ideas, facts, methods, scènes à faire, and merger-doctrine elements are unprotectable. Do not claim the unprotectable.
6. STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS: 3 years (17 U.S.C. § 507(b)); discovery rule applies in most circuits, but Warner Chappell Music v. Nealy (2024) confirms damages reach back to the full accrued period — verify your circuit's accrual rule and the Petrella separate-accrual framework for ongoing infringement.
7. DMCA § 1202 (CMI) is a SEPARATE claim with its own elements (knowing removal/alteration of copyright management information + intent/knowledge it would conceal/induce infringement) — plead as a separate count if pursued.
8. WORK MADE FOR HIRE / CHAIN OF TITLE: If ownership runs through assignment, § 204(a) requires a SIGNED WRITING. Plead the writing.
9. JURY RIGHT: Feltner v. Columbia Pictures Television, 523 U.S. 340 (1998) — Seventh Amendment right to a jury on statutory damages. Retain the jury demand for damages claims; omit only for purely equitable relief or non-Article-III fora (e.g., Court of Federal Claims / Tucker Act § 1491, where jury is unavailable and § 1498(b) governs copyright claims against the United States).
10. VENUE: Copyright actions have a SPECIAL venue statue — 28 U.S.C. § 1400(a) (where defendant "resides or may be found") — in addition to general § 1391. Plead both if helpful.
11. COPYRIGHT CLAIMS BOARD (CCB) alternative: small claims (≤ $30,000) may proceed before the CCB under the CASE Act — confirm the client did not opt into / is not bound by a CCB proceeding; CCB is opt-out for respondents.
