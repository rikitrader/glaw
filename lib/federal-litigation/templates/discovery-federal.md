# 0. RULE 26(a)(1) INITIAL DISCLOSURES (no request needed; within 14 days of the 26(f) conference)
(A) individuals likely to have discoverable information + the subjects; (B) documents/ESI the
disclosing party may use to support its claims/defenses; (C) a computation of each category of
damages + supporting documents; (D) any insurance agreement. Supplement under Rule 26(e).

# I. INTERROGATORIES (Rule 33 — no more than 25 including discrete subparts; answered under oath, 30 days)
1. Identify each person with knowledge of the facts alleged and the subject of their knowledge.
2. State the factual basis for each of your [claims/defenses], identifying supporting documents.
3. Identify every document and communication concerning [the transaction/event at issue].
4. Itemize the damages you claim, the method of calculation, and the supporting documents.
5. Identify each expert you may call, the subjects, and the substance + grounds of the opinions
   (Rule 26(a)(2)).
6. Identify each affirmative defense and every fact supporting it.
7. [Claim-specific: securities → the statements alleged false + who made them; §1983 → the policy/
   custom and final policymaker; FLSA → hours, pay, and the classification basis; antitrust → the
   relevant market and the agreement.]

# II. REQUESTS FOR PRODUCTION (Rule 34 — 30 days; produce as kept or labeled to the requests)
1. All documents and ESI concerning [the transaction/event/relationship at issue].
2. All communications between the parties (and with third parties) about the dispute, [date] to present.
3. All documents you may use to support a claim or defense (overlapping Rule 26(a)(1)).
4. All documents identified in or used to answer the interrogatories.
5. Organizational/contract/policy documents [the policy manual; the agreement; the registration
   statement; the plan documents].
6. ESI in native format with metadata for [custodians] [date range]; specify the form of production.
7. A privilege log (Rule 26(b)(5)) for anything withheld.

# III. REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION (Rule 36 — 30 days; deemed ADMITTED if not timely answered)
1. Admit the genuineness of each document attached as Exhibit [__].
2. Admit [the dispositive fact for each element — e.g., the statement was made; the contract exists;
   the payment was/was not made; the registration was not filed].
3. Admit you have no document supporting [the contested position].
4. Admit [no good-faith basis exists to dispute [the key fact]].

# IV. DEPOSITIONS (Rule 30 — ≤10 per side, 1 day of 7 hours each absent leave; Rule 30(b)(6) for entities)
- Notice each fact witness; for an organization, a **Rule 30(b)(6)** notice listing the matters for
  examination, requiring designation of one or more persons to testify on its behalf.
- Pair a deposition with a Rule 34 request (or a Rule 45 subpoena duces tecum for a non-party).
