# 1. MOTION TO DISMISS — Rule 12(b)(6) (failure to state a claim)
**Standard:** the complaint must contain enough facts to state a claim **plausible on its face**;
the court accepts well-pleaded *facts* as true but **not legal conclusions** (Twombly/Iqbal). A
two-step analysis: strip the conclusions, then ask whether the remaining facts plausibly entitle the
plaintiff to relief. Matters outside the pleadings convert it to Rule 56 (except documents integral to
/incorporated by the complaint + judicially noticeable facts). *Motion:* "Count [__] fails because the
complaint pleads no facts making [ELEMENT] plausible; [it is time-barred on its face / barred by
[immunity]]." De novo on appeal.

# 2. MOTION TO DISMISS — Rule 12(b)(1) (subject-matter jurisdiction) / Standing
**Standard:** no §1331 federal question or §1332 diversity, or no **Article III standing** (concrete,
particularized injury-in-fact; causation; redressability — Lujan/Spokeo/TransUnion: "no concrete harm,
no standing"). A **facial** attack takes the allegations as true; a **factual** attack lets the court
weigh evidence. Non-waivable; raised anytime (Rule 12(h)(3)).

# 3. MOTION TO DISMISS — Rule 9(b) / PSLRA (fraud particularity)
**Standard:** fraud/mistake must be pleaded with **particularity** (the who, what, when, where, how);
for securities fraud the **PSLRA** requires facts giving rise to a **strong inference of scienter** at
least as compelling as any opposing inference (Tellabs). *Pair with 12(b)(6).*

# 4. MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT — Rule 56 (Celotex)
**Standard:** judgment where there is **no genuine dispute of material fact** and the movant is
entitled to judgment as a matter of law. The movant may show **the absence of evidence** supporting an
essential element on which the nonmovant bears the burden (Celotex); the nonmovant must then point to
evidence on which a **reasonable jury** could find in its favor — a scintilla is not enough (Anderson;
Matsushita). State each material fact with a **record citation** (Rule 56(c)); local rules usually
require a separate statement of undisputed facts.

# 5. DAUBERT MOTION / MOTION IN LIMINE — FRE 702 (expert admissibility)
**Standard:** the expert must be **qualified**; the opinion must rest on **sufficient facts/data**, be
the product of **reliable principles and methods**, and reflect a **reliable application** to the facts,
and must help the trier (Daubert/Kumho Tire). The **2023 amendment** makes the proponent show each
prong by a **preponderance** and bars overstating conclusions beyond what the methods support. Attack
methodology, fit, and ipse dixit.

# 6. RULE 11 / § 1927 / INHERENT-POWER SANCTIONS
**Standard:** a filing must have evidentiary support and a non-frivolous legal basis; serve the motion
and allow the **21-day safe harbor** before filing (Rule 11(c)(2)). § 1927 reaches counsel who
**multiply the proceedings unreasonably and vexatiously**; the court's inherent power reaches bad-faith
conduct (Chambers). 

# 7. MOTION TO REMAND (28 U.S.C. § 1447) / the removal framework
**Standard:** removal (§ 1441) requires original federal jurisdiction; a **§ 1447(c) motion to remand**
for a non-jurisdictional defect must be filed **within 30 days** of removal (a jurisdictional defect may
be raised anytime). The removing defendant bears the burden; **doubts are resolved against removal**.
The **forum-defendant rule** (§ 1441(b)(2)) and the **one-year limit** on diversity removal apply.

# 8. MOTION TO COMPEL ARBITRATION + STAY (FAA, 9 U.S.C. §§ 3-4)
**Standard:** a valid written arbitration agreement covering the dispute → the court **compels
arbitration and stays** (Coinbase: an appeal of a denial stays the case). Severability/delegation
clauses (who decides arbitrability) per Rent-A-Center.

# 9. MOTION TO COMPEL DISCOVERY (Rule 37) / MOTION FOR PROTECTIVE ORDER (Rule 26(c))
**Standard:** after a good-faith **meet-and-confer** (Rule 37(a)(1)), compel answers/production that are
**relevant and proportional** (Rule 26(b)(1)); sanctions + fees for an unjustified failure. A protective
order issues for **good cause** to prevent annoyance, burden, or disclosure of trade secrets.
