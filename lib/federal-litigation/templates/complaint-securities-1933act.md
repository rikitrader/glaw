26. Plaintiff repeats and realleges ¶¶ 1, 4–25 above as if fully set forth herein. **For this Count, Plaintiff expressly excludes and does not incorporate any allegation sounding in fraud or scienter; this Count is based solely on strict liability and negligence.**

27. The Registration Statement, at the time it became effective, **contained untrue statements of material fact** and/or **omitted material facts required to be stated therein or necessary to make the statements therein not misleading**, as alleged in ¶¶ 18–21.

28. Each Defendant named in this Count is a statutorily enumerated defendant under **§11(a)**: **[ISSUER]** signed the Registration Statement (§11(a)(1)); the **Individual Defendants** signed it and/or were directors or named as about-to-become directors (§11(a)(1)–(2)); the **Underwriter Defendants** underwrote the Offering (§11(a)(5)); and the **Auditor/Expert Defendant** certified the expertised portion (§11(a)(4)).

29. Plaintiff purchased **[ISSUER] [SECURITY]** **traceable to** the Registration Statement (¶ 23) and is therefore within the class protected by §11.

30. **§11 imposes strict liability** on the issuer and **does not require** that Plaintiff prove reliance [except as provided in §11(a) where the purchase occurred after a 12-month earnings statement was issued], **scienter, or loss causation**; **negative causation** under **§11(e)** is an **affirmative defense** that Defendants bear the burden to prove.

31. The **Individual, Underwriter, and Expert Defendants** are liable unless they sustain the burden of proving the **statutory due-diligence defense under §11(b)** — i.e., that after reasonable investigation they had reasonable ground to believe, and did believe, the statements were true and not misleading. Plaintiff alleges that no such reasonable investigation was conducted, as shown by **[FACTS — red flags, ignored information, inadequate verification].** The issuer has **no due-diligence defense.**

32. As a direct result, Plaintiff suffered damages measured by **§11(e)** — the difference between the amount paid for the security (not exceeding the offering price) and (a) its value at the time of suit, or (b) the price at which it was disposed of in the market before suit, or (c) the price at which disposed of after suit but before judgment — in an amount to be proven at trial.
