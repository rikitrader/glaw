<!--
DTSA-SPECIFIC GOTCHAS — verify before filing:
- INTERSTATE-COMMERCE NEXUS IS JURISDICTIONAL: § 1836(b)(1) requires the trade secret be "related to a product or service used in, or intended for use in, interstate or foreign commerce." Plead it as a fact (¶21), not a conclusion.
- DTSA EFFECTIVE DATE (May 11, 2016): No DTSA claim for misappropriation that occurred entirely before that date. Allege a post-enactment act or a continuing/"new" use. Confirm circuit treatment of pre-/post-enactment acquisition vs. continued use.
- IDENTIFY THE TRADE SECRET WITH PARTICULARITY: courts dismiss vague "everything we know" descriptions; describe the category specifically without disclosing the secret itself. Consider filing under seal / a protective order; do NOT attach the secret as a public exhibit.
- REASONABLE SECRECY MEASURES are an element (§ 1839(3)(A)) — ¶15 must be concrete (NDAs, access controls), not boilerplate.
- ECONOMIC VALUE from secrecy is an element (§ 1839(3)(B)) — distinct from "valuable"; plead derivation of value FROM not being known.
- EX PARTE SEIZURE (§ 1836(b)(2)) is an extraordinary remedy with strict findings; pleading it requires a separate verified application + affidavit — not part of an ordinary complaint.
- INJUNCTION LIMITS (§ 1836(b)(3)(A)(i)): cannot "prevent a person from entering into an employment relationship" and any employment conditions must be based on evidence of threatened misappropriation, not merely what the person knows (inevitable-disclosure doctrine is NOT adopted by the DTSA and is barred in some states, e.g., California).
- EXEMPLARY DAMAGES (up to 2x) and ATTORNEYS' FEES require WILLFUL AND MALICIOUS misappropriation (§ 1836(b)(3)(C)–(D)) — plead supporting facts (¶25).
- IMMUNITY-NOTICE / FEE-RECOVERY TRAP (§ 1833(b)(3)): to recover exemplary damages OR attorneys' fees against an EMPLOYEE/CONTRACTOR, the employer must have given the whistleblower-immunity notice in the relevant agreement (or cross-referenced policy). Confirm the notice was given for agreements entered/updated after May 11, 2016, or the fee/exemplary remedy may be unavailable against that individual.
- STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS: 3 years from when misappropriation is discovered or should have been discovered (§ 1836(d)); a continuing misappropriation is a single claim.
- STATE-LAW PREEMPTION/DISPLACEMENT: most state UTSAs displace conflicting common-law claims over the same facts (conversion, unjust enrichment, etc.); the federal DTSA does NOT preempt state trade-secret law — but verify the chosen state's displacement scope before adding Count II or common-law counts.
- DIVERSITY (if invoked): plead each LLC member's citizenship; § 1332 amount > $75,000.
- VENUE/PERSONAL JURISDICTION: tie to where acquisition/use occurred; for entity defendants check § 1391(c)/(d).
- VERIFICATION: a DTSA complaint need not be verified, but any TRO/seizure application must be supported by a verified pleading or affidavit (Fed. R. Civ. P. 65 / § 1836(b)(2)).
