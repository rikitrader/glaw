CAUSE-SPECIFIC GOTCHAS — PATENT INFRINGEMENT (35 U.S.C. § 271):
- STANDING / OWNERSHIP: Plaintiff must hold "all substantial rights" (or join the patentee). A bare licensee lacks standing; an exclusive licensee may need to join the owner. Plead the chain of title; record assignments at USPTO.
- VENUE (§ 1400(b) is EXCLUSIVE): TC Heartland (2017) — a domestic corporation "resides" ONLY in its state of incorporation. Alternative venue requires acts of infringement + a "regular and established place of business" (In re Cray, Fed. Cir. 2017: physical, regular, and the defendant's place). The general venue statute does NOT save patent venue.
- PLEADING STANDARD: Form 18 was ABROGATED (eff. Dec. 1, 2015) — Twombly/Iqbal plausibility now governs. Tie the accused product to specific claim limitations; a bare recitation of § 271 is vulnerable to a 12(b)(6). Identify at least one asserted claim and map each limitation (literal or DOE).
- DOCTRINE OF EQUIVALENTS: watch prosecution-history estoppel (Festo) and the all-elements rule; DOE cannot vitiate a claim limitation.
- WILLFULNESS (Halo, 2016): plead PRE-SUIT knowledge + egregious/deliberate conduct. Filing-date knowledge alone generally will NOT support pre-suit willfulness; many courts hold the complaint itself cannot supply the knowledge for the very conduct it accuses (split authority). Enhanced damages are discretionary (Read factors).
- MARKING / DAMAGES START DATE (§ 287): if patentee/licensees make a patented article and fail to mark, pre-suit damages are barred until actual notice. No article made = no marking required (damages from issuance). For METHOD claims, § 287 marking generally does not apply.
- DAMAGES FLOOR: reasonable royalty is the statutory minimum (§ 284); lost profits require Panduit (demand, absence of acceptable noninfringing substitutes, capacity, profit). Apportion to the patented feature; watch the entire-market-value rule and Georgia-Pacific factors.
- INJUNCTION: eBay (2006) — no presumption of irreparable harm; prove all four equitable factors. NPEs frequently denied permanent injunctions.
- INTER PARTES REVIEW (IPR) RISK: defendant may petition PTAB; consider stay exposure and the 1-year § 315(b) IPR bar that runs from service of THIS complaint.
- § 101 / ELIGIBILITY: software/business-method claims face Alice 12(b)(6)/12(c) eligibility attacks before discovery.
- LACHES IS DEAD as a damages defense (SCA Hygiene v. First Quality, 2017); the 6-year § 286 damages limitation still caps recovery to 6 years before the complaint.
- PRE-SUIT DUE DILIGENCE / RULE 11: an adequate pre-filing investigation (claim construction + infringement read on the accused product) is required; baseless suits invite § 285 fees and Rule 11 sanctions against PLAINTIFF.
- FALSE MARKING / DECLARATORY-JUDGMENT COUNTERCLAIM exposure; expect invalidity (§§ 102/103/112) and inequitable-conduct defenses.
- § 1498 / GOVERNMENT INFRINGER: if the accused infringer is the U.S. or a contractor acting with Government authorization/consent, the EXCLUSIVE remedy is the Court of Federal Claims under § 1498(a) — NO injunction, NO jury, remedy is "reasonable and entire compensation." Do not sue the Government in district court.
- INVENTORSHIP / ASSIGNMENT DEFECTS: confirm each named inventor and a complete recorded assignment; a missing inventor or broken chain can defeat standing.
