## ARTICLE II — REGISTRATION RIGHTS
**2.1 Mandatory / Demand Registration.** On the Investors' request (after the IPO or per the threshold), the Company
files and keeps effective a registration statement under the Securities Act covering the Registrable Securities;
[demand + S-3 + piggyback] mechanics. **2.2 Allowed Delay.** The Company may suspend for a limited period for a
material development (blackout). **2.3 Expenses.** The Company pays registration expenses (ex-selling commissions).
**2.4 Company Obligations.** Prepare/file, respond to the SEC, deliver prospectuses, listing, blue-sky, keep
effective. **2.5 Due Diligence / Information.** Investor access for diligence subject to confidentiality. **2.6
Obligations of the Investors.** Furnish required information; comply with the plan of distribution; lock-up
cooperation. **2.7 Indemnification.** Company indemnifies Investors (and vice-versa for Investor-supplied info);
contribution fallback. **2.8 Applicability.** Article II applies for as long as the Investors hold Registrable
Securities (or until Rule 144 freely available).

## ARTICLE III — OTHER RIGHTS
**3.1 Right of First Offer (ROFO).** Before issuing New Securities, the Company first offers the Investors their
pro-rata share on the same terms (with an over-allotment). **3.2 Due Diligence in Subsequent Financings.**
**3.3 Preemptive Rights.** Pro-rata participation in New Securities (carve-outs = Excluded Securities: option-pool
grants, M&A, etc.); these contractual rights govern in lieu of any statutory preemptive rights under §607.0630,
Fla. Stat. **3.4 Additional Rights / MFN.** If the Company grants better rights to a later investor, the
Investors get them. **3.5 Voting.** Voting/drag/voting-agreement undertakings as agreed, enforceable as a
shareholders' agreement under §607.0731, Fla. Stat. **3.6 Transfers.** Transfer restrictions authorized by
§607.0627, Fla. Stat.; permitted transferees take subject to the Agreement; ROFR/co-sale cross-reference.
**3.7 Standstill.** The Investors will not, for [_] years, acquire >[__]% or launch a proxy/tender without Board
consent (with fall-aways).

**4.1 Investor Directors.** So long as the Sponsor Group holds ≥ the Board Threshold, it may designate [N] directors.
**4.2 Continuing Designation.** Re-nomination, vacancy fill, removal at the Investor's direction (consistent with
§§607.0806, 607.0808 and 607.0809, Fla. Stat.). **4.3 Committees.** Proportionate committee representation [as
permitted by law/exchange]. **4.4 Independence/Qualification.** **4.5 Voting Support.** The Company/others support
the designees' election. **4.6 Fees and Expenses.** Reimburse director expenses; same compensation as other
non-employee directors. **4.7 Board Observer.** Below the director threshold, a non-voting observer (subject to
confidentiality/privilege carve-outs). **4.8 D&O Indemnification.** Indemnification and advancement to the fullest
extent permitted by §§607.0850–607.0859, Fla. Stat. **4.9 Reporting Information.** **4.10 Directors and Officers

## ARTICLE V — CONSENT RIGHTS (PROTECTIVE PROVISIONS)
**5.1 Investor Approval.** So long as the threshold is met, the Company will not (without Investor consent): amend
the articles of incorporation/bylaws adverse to the Investors; create senior/pari securities; pay dividends;
redeem/repurchase; incur debt over [$_]; sell/encumber material assets or the company; change the authorized board
size; change the principal business; related-party transactions (subject to §607.0832, Fla. Stat.);
liquidate/dissolve; or other enumerated major actions.

the statutory inspection rights under §607.1602, Fla. Stat.
