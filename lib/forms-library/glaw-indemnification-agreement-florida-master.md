# INDEMNITY AGREEMENT

This Indemnity Agreement (this "**Agreement**"), dated as of [DATE], is made by and between [Company], Inc., a Florida corporation (the "**Company**"), and [INDEMNITEE NAME] (the "**Indemnitee**").

**RECITALS**

**A.** The Company is aware that competent and experienced persons are increasingly reluctant to serve as directors, officers, or agents of corporations unless they are protected by comprehensive liability insurance or indemnification, due to increased exposure to litigation costs and risks resulting from their service.

**B.** The statutes and judicial decisions regarding the duties of directors and officers are often difficult to apply, ambiguous, or conflicting, and therefore fail to provide adequate, reliable knowledge of legal risks to which such persons are exposed.

**C.** Plaintiffs often seek damages in such large amounts, and the costs of litigation may be so enormous (whether or not the case is meritorious), that the defense or settlement of such litigation is often beyond the personal resources of directors, officers, and other agents.

**D.** The Company believes it is unfair for its agents to assume the risk of large judgments and other expenses that may occur in cases in which the agent received no personal profit and was not culpable.

**E.** The indemnification provisions of the Florida Business Corporation Act, §607.0850 through §607.0859, Florida Statutes (collectively, the "**FBCA Indemnification Provisions**"), under which the Company is organized, empower the Company to indemnify its directors, officers, employees, and agents by agreement and to indemnify persons who serve at the request of the Company as directors, officers, employees, or agents of other enterprises, and expressly provide that the indemnification thereunder is not exclusive of any other rights to which a person seeking indemnification may be entitled.

**F.** The Company desires and has requested the Indemnitee to serve or continue to serve as an agent of the Company and/or one or more of its subsidiaries, free from undue concern for claims for damages arising out of or related to such services, and the Indemnitee is willing to do so provided the Indemnitee is furnished the indemnity provided for herein.

**AGREEMENT**

NOW, THEREFORE, the parties hereto, intending to be legally bound, hereby agree as follows:

**1. Definitions.** (a) **Agent.** "Agent" means any person who is or was a director, officer, employee, or other agent of the Company or a subsidiary of the Company; or is or was serving at the request of, for the convenience of, or to represent the interests of the Company or a subsidiary as a director, officer, employee, or agent of another enterprise; or was such with respect to a predecessor corporation. (b) **Expenses.** "Expenses" includes all out-of-pocket costs of any type whatsoever (including, without limitation, all attorneys' fees and related disbursements) actually and reasonably incurred by the Indemnitee in connection with the investigation, defense, or appeal of a proceeding or establishing or enforcing a right to indemnification; provided that "expenses" shall not include any judgments, fines, ERISA excise taxes or penalties, or amounts paid in settlement. (c) **Proceeding.** "Proceeding" means any threatened, pending, or completed action, suit, or other proceeding, whether civil, criminal, administrative, or investigative. (d) **Subsidiary.** "Subsidiary" means any corporation of which more than 50% of the outstanding voting securities is owned directly or indirectly by the Company, by the Company and one or more other subsidiaries, or by one or more other subsidiaries.

**2. Agreement to Serve.** The Indemnitee agrees to serve and/or continue to serve as an agent of the Company, at its will (or under separate agreement, if such agreement exists), in the capacity the Indemnitee currently serves, so long as duly appointed or elected and qualified in accordance with the applicable Bylaws or until the Indemnitee tenders a written resignation; provided that nothing in this Agreement is intended to create any right to continued employment.

**3. Liability Insurance.** (a) **Maintenance of D&O Insurance.** So long as the Indemnitee serves as an agent of the Company, and thereafter so long as the Indemnitee may be subject to any possible proceeding by reason of such service, the Company, subject to Section 3(c), shall promptly obtain and maintain in full force directors' and officers' liability insurance ("**D&O Insurance**") in reasonable amounts from established and reputable insurers. (b) **Rights and Benefits.** In all D&O Insurance policies, the Indemnitee shall be named as an insured so as to provide the Indemnitee the same rights and benefits as are accorded to the most favorably insured of the Company's directors (or officers or key employees, as applicable). (c) **Limitation.** The Company shall have no obligation to obtain or maintain D&O Insurance if it determines in good faith that such insurance is not reasonably available, the premium costs are disproportionate to the coverage, the coverage is limited by exclusions so as to provide insufficient benefit, or the Indemnitee is covered by similar insurance maintained by a subsidiary.

**4. Mandatory Indemnification.** Subject to Section 9, the Company shall indemnify the Indemnitee as follows: (a) **Third Party Actions.** If the Indemnitee is or is threatened to be made a party to any proceeding (other than an action by or in the right of the Company) by reason of being an agent of the Company, the Company shall indemnify the Indemnitee against any and all expenses and liabilities of any type whatsoever (including judgments, fines, ERISA excise taxes and penalties, and amounts paid in settlement) actually and reasonably incurred, provided the Indemnitee acted in good faith and in a manner reasonably believed to be in or not opposed to the best interests of the Company and its shareholders and, with respect to any criminal action, had no reasonable cause to believe the conduct was unlawful. (b) **Derivative Actions.** If the Indemnitee is or is threatened to be made a party to any proceeding by or in the right of the Company by reason of being an agent, the Company shall indemnify against all expenses actually and reasonably incurred, provided the Indemnitee acted in good faith and in a manner reasonably believed to be in or not opposed to the best interests of the Company; except that no indemnification shall be made for any claim as to which the Indemnitee is finally adjudged liable to the Company, unless and to the extent a court determines the Indemnitee is fairly and reasonably entitled to indemnity. (c) **Deceased Indemnitee.** If the Indemnitee becomes deceased before, during, or after the proceeding, the Company shall indemnify the Indemnitee's heirs, executors, and administrators to the extent the Indemnitee would have been entitled under Sections 4(a) or 4(b). (d) **Limitations.** The Company shall not be obligated to indemnify for amounts actually paid to or on behalf of the Indemnitee under a valid and collectible D&O Insurance policy or under a valid and enforceable indemnity clause, bylaw, or agreement.

**5. Partial Indemnification.** If the Indemnitee is entitled to indemnification for some but not all of any expenses or liabilities, the Company shall nevertheless indemnify the Indemnitee for the portion to which the Indemnitee is entitled.

**6. Mandatory Advancement of Expenses.** Subject to Section 9(a), the Company shall advance all expenses incurred by the Indemnitee in connection with any proceeding to which the Indemnitee is or is threatened to be made a party by reason of being an agent, upon the Indemnitee's undertaking to repay such amounts if it is ultimately determined the Indemnitee is not entitled to be indemnified. Advances shall be paid within [twenty (20)] days following delivery of a written request. If the Company fails to pay expenses as required, the Indemnitee may seek mandatory injunctive relief, and it shall not be a defense that the Indemnitee has an adequate remedy at law for damages.

**7. Notice and Other Indemnification Procedures.** (a) **Notice by Indemnitee.** Promptly after receiving notice of the commencement or threat of any proceeding, the Indemnitee shall, if indemnification may be sought, notify the Company. (b) **Notice by Company.** If the Company has D&O Insurance in effect, it shall give prompt notice to the insurers and take all necessary action to cause the insurers to pay amounts payable. (c) **Defense.** Where the Company is obligated to pay expenses, the Company may assume the defense with counsel approved by the Indemnitee, after which the Company will not be liable for fees of counsel subsequently incurred by the Indemnitee, provided that (i) the Indemnitee may employ separate counsel at the Indemnitee's expense; and (ii) the fees of the Indemnitee's counsel shall be at the Company's expense if (A) the Company previously authorized such employment, (B) the Indemnitee reasonably concludes there may be a conflict of interest, or (C) the Company has not in fact employed counsel to assume the defense.

**8. Determination of Right to Indemnification.** (a) **Successful Defense.** To the extent the Indemnitee has been successful on the merits or otherwise in defense of any proceeding, the Company shall indemnify the Indemnitee against all expenses actually and reasonably incurred. (b) **Other Situations.** Where Section 8(a) is inapplicable, the Company shall indemnify the Indemnitee unless, and except to the extent that, the Company proves by clear and convincing evidence in a forum listed in Section 8(c) that the Indemnitee has not met the applicable standard of conduct. (c) **Selection of Forum.** The Indemnitee may select the forum from among: (i) a quorum of the Board consisting of directors not parties to the proceeding; (ii) the shareholders of the Company; (iii) legal counsel selected by the Indemnitee and reasonably approved by the Board, by written opinion; or (iv) a panel of three arbitrators (one selected by each party and the third by the first two). (d) **Submission to Forum.** Within [thirty (30)] days after notice of the Indemnitee's choice of forum, the Company shall, at its own expense, submit its claim to the selected forum and act in the utmost good faith to assure the Indemnitee a complete opportunity to defend. (e) **Application to Court.** Notwithstanding any contrary determination, the Indemnitee may apply to a court of competent jurisdiction in [COUNTY] County, Florida, or any other court of competent jurisdiction to enforce the right to indemnification. (f) **Expenses Related to this Agreement.** The Company shall indemnify the Indemnitee against all expenses incurred in connection with any proceeding to interpret or enforce this Agreement, unless a court finds each of the Indemnitee's claims or defenses was frivolous or made in bad faith.

**9. Exceptions.** Notwithstanding anything to the contrary, the Company shall not be obligated to: (a) **Claims Initiated by Indemnitee.** Indemnify or advance expenses for proceedings initiated voluntarily by the Indemnitee (and not by way of defense), unless (i) required by law, (ii) the proceeding was authorized by the Board, (iii) the Company provides such indemnification in its sole discretion, or (iv) the proceeding is brought to establish or enforce a right to indemnification; (b) **Lack of Good Faith.** Indemnify for expenses incurred in a proceeding instituted by the Indemnitee to enforce or interpret this Agreement if a court determines each of the Indemnitee's material assertions was not made in good faith or was frivolous; or (c) **Unauthorized Settlements.** Indemnify for amounts paid in settlement unless the Company consents, which consent shall not be unreasonably withheld.

**10. Non-Exclusivity.** The indemnification and advancement rights set forth herein are not exclusive of any other rights the Indemnitee may have under law, including §607.0850 et seq., Florida Statutes, the Company's Articles of Incorporation or Bylaws, a vote of shareholders or disinterested directors, other agreements, or otherwise, and shall continue after the Indemnitee has ceased to be an agent and shall inure to the benefit of the Indemnitee's heirs, executors, and administrators.

**11. Enforcement.** Any right to indemnification or advances shall be enforceable in any court of competent jurisdiction if (i) the claim is denied in whole or in part, or (ii) no disposition is made within [ninety (90)] days of request. In such an action, the Indemnitee, if successful in whole or in part, shall also be paid the expense of prosecuting the claim. Neither the failure of the Company to have made a determination that indemnification is proper, nor an actual determination that it is improper, shall be a defense or create a presumption that the Indemnitee is not entitled.

**12. Subrogation.** Upon making any payment hereunder, the Company shall be subrogated to the extent of such payment to all rights of recovery of the Indemnitee under any insurance policy or other indemnity agreement, and the Indemnitee shall execute all documents and do all acts necessary to secure such rights.

**13. Survival of Rights.** (a) All obligations of the Company shall continue during the period the Indemnitee is an agent and thereafter so long as the Indemnitee may be subject to any possible proceeding by reason of such service. (b) The Company shall require any successor (by purchase, merger, consolidation, or otherwise) to all or substantially all of its business or assets to expressly assume and agree to perform this Agreement.

**14. Interpretation of Agreement.** The parties intend this Agreement to be interpreted and enforced so as to provide indemnification to the fullest extent permitted by law, including circumstances in which indemnification would otherwise be discretionary.

**15. Severability.** If any provision is held invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, (i) the remaining provisions shall not be affected or impaired, and (ii) to the fullest extent possible, such provision shall be construed so as to give effect to the intent manifested and to Section 14.

**16. Modification and Waiver.** No supplement, modification, or amendment shall be binding unless executed in writing by both parties. No waiver of any provision shall constitute a waiver of any other provision or a continuing waiver.

**17. Notice.** All notices shall be in writing and deemed duly given (i) if delivered by hand and receipted for by the addressee, or (ii) if mailed by certified or registered mail with postage prepaid, on the third business day after mailing. Addresses for notice are as shown on the signature page or as subsequently modified by written notice.

**18. Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed exclusively by and construed according to the laws of the State of Florida as applied to contracts between Florida residents entered into and to be performed entirely within Florida.
