## TEXAS BLUE-SKY EXEMPTION AND NSMIA PREEMPTION

**Texas securities-law framework.** The offer and sale of the Offered Shares in Texas is governed by the **Texas Securities Act, Chapter 4001 of the Texas Government Code (Title 12, Subtitle B)**, administered by the Texas State Securities Board. The Offered Shares are offered in reliance on the available Texas exemptions for limited and private offerings (including the secured-transaction/limited-offering exemptions under **Tex. Gov't Code §4005.xxx** [VERIFY current section] and Texas Administrative Code provisions implementing Regulation D offerings) and on the federal exemptions under **§4(a)(2)** of the Securities Act and **Rule 506** of Regulation D. The Company will not engage in general solicitation or advertising in Texas.

**NSMIA preemption note.** Securities sold under **Rule 506** of Regulation D are "**covered securities**" under **Section 18 of the Securities Act of 1933** (added by the National Securities Markets Improvement Act of 1996, "**NSMIA**"). Accordingly, Texas may not require registration or qualification of the Offered Shares; the State of Texas retains only its authority to require a **notice filing** (a copy of the Form D filed with the SEC, the related consent to service of process, and the applicable fee) and to bring enforcement actions for fraud. The Company will make any such Texas notice filing required of a Rule 506 issuer within the time prescribed.

**Community-property / spousal note.** Texas is a **community-property** state. Property acquired by a married person during marriage is presumptively community property in which the person's spouse holds an interest. A married Texas Investor acknowledges that (i) the Offered Shares may constitute community property, (ii) the funds tendered may be community funds, and (iii) where applicable the Investor will obtain, and deliver to the Company, the **written consent of the Investor's spouse** (in the form the Company may reasonably require) to this investment and to the representations, transfer restrictions, arbitration, jury-trial waiver, and other terms of the Securities Purchase Agreement, so that the Investor's spouse is bound to the same extent as the Investor with respect to any community-property interest in the Offered Shares.
