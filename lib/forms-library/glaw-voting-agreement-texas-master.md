# VOTING AGREEMENT OF [COMPANY], INC. (A TEXAS CORPORATION)

> GLAW MASTER FORM — Texas-law voting agreement (NVCA-style priced-round board-election + drag-along doc),
> authorized by and drafted to **Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC) §21.368 (voting agreements)** and
> **TBOC §21.369 (voting trusts)**. ATTORNEY WORK-PRODUCT; securities counsel must adapt. Not legal advice.

This **VOTING AGREEMENT** is dated as of [DATE], by and among **[COMPANY], INC.**, a Texas corporation (the
"Company"), the holders of Preferred Stock on Schedule A (the "Investors"), and the key holders/founders on
Schedule B (the "Key Holders"). The Company is a corporation organized under the Texas Business Organizations Code,
and its shares are held by shareholders. This Agreement is a written voting agreement among shareholders entered into
under **TBOC §21.368** and is specifically enforceable under that section; nothing herein limits the parties' ability
to instead deposit shares into a voting trust under **TBOC §21.369**.

## 1. VOTING — BOARD OF DIRECTORS
**1.1 Size of the Board.** Each party votes its shares to fix the Board at [N] directors.
**1.2 Board Composition.** Each party votes its shares so the Board comprises: (a) **[N] Investor Director(s)**
designated by [the lead investor / majority of the Preferred]; (b) **[N] Common/Founder Director(s)** designated by
[the Key Holders / the CEO]; (c) the **person serving as CEO**; and (d) [N] **mutually-agreed independent
director(s)** acceptable to the Board/Preferred.
**1.3 Failure to Designate.** A board seat for which no designee is named stays vacant until filled by the entitled party.
**1.4 Removal / Vacancies.** A designee is removed/replaced only at the direction of the party entitled to designate
that seat; the parties vote accordingly; vacancies filled by the same party.

## 2. DRAG-ALONG
On a Sale of the Company approved by [the Board, a majority of the Preferred (as-converted), and a majority of the
Common held by Key Holders], each party agrees to **vote for, not exercise dissenters'/appraisal rights against,
and take all reasonable actions to consummate** the sale (subject to standard conditions: pro-rata/same-form
consideration, several (not joint) liability capped at proceeds, customary reps only, no non-compete forced on minor
holders). Each party waives, to the fullest extent permitted by **TBOC Subchapter H (§§10.351–10.368, dissent and
appraisal rights)**, any right of dissent and appraisal with respect to such Sale, and grants the proxy in §3.2 to
effect this.

## 3. REMEDIES
**3.1 Covenants of the Company.** The Company will not record a transfer or take action inconsistent with this
Agreement, and will enforce its terms. **3.2 Irrevocable Proxy.** Each party grants an irrevocable proxy (coupled
with an interest, and accordingly irrevocable under **TBOC §21.369(d)** and **§21.368**) to vote its shares
consistently with §§1–2 if it fails to do so. **3.3 Specific Enforcement.** The parties agree damages are inadequate
and this Agreement is specifically enforceable, including as expressly provided in **TBOC §21.368(d)**. **3.4 Remedies
Cumulative.**

## 4. TERM
This Agreement terminates on the earliest of: (a) a Qualified IPO; (b) a Sale of the Company / Deemed Liquidation;
or (c) the written agreement of the requisite parties.
