# VOTING AGREEMENT OF [COMPANY], INC. (A FLORIDA CORPORATION)

This **VOTING AGREEMENT** (this "Agreement") is dated as of [DATE], by and among **[COMPANY], INC.**, a Florida corporation (the "Company"), the holders of the Company's Preferred Stock listed on **Schedule A** (the "Investors"), and the key holders and founders listed on **Schedule B** (the "Key Holders"). The Investors and the Key Holders are referred to collectively as the "Shareholders." This Agreement is entered into under the Florida Business Corporation Act, Chapter 607, Florida Statutes (the "FBCA"), including the provisions governing voting agreements among shareholders (§ 607.0731, Fla. Stat.).

## 1. VOTING — BOARD OF DIRECTORS
**1.1 Size of the Board.** Each Shareholder shall vote all shares of the Company's capital stock now or hereafter owned or controlled by such Shareholder (the "Shares"), and shall take all other necessary or desirable actions within its control (whether at a meeting of shareholders or by written consent under § 607.0704, Fla. Stat.), to fix and maintain the number of directors of the Board of Directors (the "Board") at [N] directors.
**1.2 Board Composition.** Each Shareholder shall vote its Shares so that the Board is comprised of: (a) **[N] Investor Director(s)** designated by [the lead Investor / the holders of a majority of the Preferred Stock, voting as a separate class on an as-converted basis]; (b) **[N] Common/Founder Director(s)** designated by [the Key Holders / the Company's Chief Executive Officer]; (c) the **person then serving as the Company's Chief Executive Officer**; and (d) [N] **independent director(s)** mutually agreed upon and acceptable to the Board and the holders of a majority of the Preferred Stock.
**1.3 Failure to Designate.** A Board seat for which the entitled party has not named a designee shall remain vacant until filled by that party, and shall not be filled by the remaining directors or any other party.
**1.4 Removal and Vacancies.** A director designated under § 1.2 shall be removed or replaced only at the written direction of the party then entitled to designate that seat, and each Shareholder shall vote its Shares accordingly. Any vacancy created by such removal, or otherwise arising as to a designated seat, shall be filled only by the party entitled to designate that seat.

## 2. DRAG-ALONG
On a Sale of the Company approved by [the Board, the holders of a majority of the Preferred Stock (on an as-converted basis), and the holders of a majority of the Common Stock held by the Key Holders] (collectively, the "Requisite Parties"), each Shareholder agrees to **vote all of its Shares in favor of, refrain from exercising any dissenters' or appraisal rights (under §§ 607.1301–607.1340, Fla. Stat.) against, and take all reasonable actions to consummate** such Sale of the Company. Each Shareholder's obligation is subject to customary conditions, including that: consideration is allocated pro rata and paid in the same form to holders of the same class or series; any indemnification liability is several (not joint) and capped at the proceeds actually received by such Shareholder; only customary representations and warranties are required; and no non-competition or similar restrictive covenant is imposed on a Shareholder that is not also an employee, officer, or service provider of the Company. Each Shareholder grants the irrevocable proxy set forth in § 3.2 to effect this Section 2.

## 3. REMEDIES
**3.1 Covenants of the Company.** The Company shall not record any transfer of Shares, or take any action, inconsistent with this Agreement, and shall enforce its terms. **3.2 Irrevocable Proxy.** Each Shareholder hereby grants to [the Secretary of the Company / the designee of the Requisite Parties] an irrevocable proxy, coupled with an interest sufficient in law to support an irrevocable proxy under § 607.0722(5), Fla. Stat., to vote such Shareholder's Shares in a manner consistent with §§ 1 and 2 if such Shareholder fails to do so. **3.3 Specific Enforcement.** Each party agrees that monetary damages would be inadequate and that this Agreement shall be specifically enforceable, and consents to the entry of an order for specific performance or injunctive relief without the necessity of posting a bond. **3.4 Remedies Cumulative.** All remedies under this Agreement are cumulative and in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity.

## 4. TERM
This Agreement shall terminate, and be of no further force or effect, upon the earliest to occur of: (a) a Qualified IPO; (b) a Sale of the Company or other Deemed Liquidation Event; or (c) the written agreement of the Requisite Parties.
