# RETAIL FUND PROSPECTUS — DRAFTING-GUIDE EXEMPLAR

> **Reference guide, not a template or a copy.** Captures the structure of a retail closed-end fund prospectus with placeholders; it is not a reproduction of any source document.

This guide walks the standard outline of a continuously-offered, non-traded retail closed-end fund prospectus (a Form N-2 registrant under the Investment Company Act of 1940, often built as a feeder in a master-feeder structure). For each major section it gives (1) the heading, (2) what the drafter must put there, (3) the defined terms / data fields to fill (as `[BRACKETS]`), and (4) at most one short illustrative quoted phrase to anchor tone. Replace every bracket; verify every cross-reference; confirm every figure against the audited financials.

---

## COVER PAGE & LEGENDS

**What it covers.** The face page identifies the fund, its registration status, objective, share class offered, offering mechanics, and price/proceeds; the back of the cover stacks the mandatory legends (risk warning, SEC non-approval, no-unauthorized-representations, electronic-delivery notice, SAI incorporation-by-reference).

**Fields to fill.** **Fund name** = `[FUND NAME]`; **organization** = `[DE/MD] statutory trust` registered under the **"1940 Act"** as a `[closed-end / non-diversified] management investment company`; **structure** = `["feeder" in a "master-feeder"]` if applicable, naming the **"Master Fund"**; **share class** = `[CLASS NAME] Shares` (the **"Shares"**), par value `$[PAR]`; **continuous offering at NAV**; **Securities Act registration amount** = `$[AMOUNT] in Shares`; **price-to-public table** = `[At Current NAV]` / `[Proceeds to Fund]` with footnoted sales load `[None / X%]`; **Distributor** = `[DISTRIBUTOR L.P.]` (best-efforts); **Investment Manager** = `[MANAGER L.L.C.]` (the **"Investment Manager"**); **Transfer Agent / Custodian** = `[AGENT]`; **prospectus date** = `[DATE]`; **investor-eligibility gate** = `["Qualified Investors" / accredited / qualified clients]`; **minimum initial / subsequent investment** = `$[MIN] / $[MIN]`.

**Required legends to include verbatim-equivalent.** high-degree-of-risk warning; SEC/state non-approval ("criminal offense" line); not-FDIC-insured; no-secondary-market / non-transferable / not-redeemable; "not legal, tax, or financial advice — consult your own advisers"; SAI incorporated by reference; escrow of subscription funds pending closing.

> Illustrative: *"Investing in the Fund's Shares involves a high degree of risk. You could lose some or all of your investment."*

## TABLE OF CONTENTS

**What it covers.** A single-page TOC listing each numbered section with page references, followed by the parallel TOC of the **"SAI"** (Statement of Additional Information). Order is conventional: Prospectus Summary → Summary of Fees and Expenses → Financial Highlights → The Fund → Investment Objective and Strategies → Leverage → Risk Factors → Conflicts of Interest → Management of the Fund → Net Asset Value → Distributions → Repurchases and Transfers of Shares → Description of Shares → Subscription for Shares → Tax Considerations → Certain ERISA Considerations → Custodian/Transfer Agent → SAI table of contents.

**Fields to fill.** `[SECTION] ........ [PAGE]` for each row.

## PROSPECTUS SUMMARY

**What it covers.** A run-in-label digest of every substantive section, formatted as a two-column "term → 1–3 paragraph description" list. Each entry must mirror, in compressed form, the full section later in the document and end with a `See "[Full Section]"` cross-reference. This is the most heavily-read part of the document.

**Sub-entries (each a run-in bold label) and their fields.**
- **The Fund** — organization, 1940-Act status, master-feeder role. `[FUND NAME]`, `[STRUCTURE]`.
- **Investment Objective** — one sentence + the no-assurance disclaimer. `[OBJECTIVE]`.
- **Investment Strategies** — the 80% policy and asset categories. `["80% of Managed Assets in [ASSET CLASS]"]`; define **"Managed Assets"** = `[net assets + leverage for investment purposes]`.
- **Investment Manager** — identity, affiliation, AUM. `[MANAGER]`, `[PARENT]`, `$[AUM]`.
- **Management Fee / Incentive Fee** — rate, accrual basis, waiver. `[X.XX%] of Managed Assets`; **"Incentive Fee"** = `[15%] of [Net Capital Appreciation]` over a `[loss carryforward]` hurdle.
- **Shareholder Eligibility** — the **"Qualified Investors"** definition and net-worth/AUM tests. `$[1M] under management or $[2.1M] net worth`.
- **Distributions; Automatic Dividend Reinvestment Plan** — frequency, the **"Plan"**, opt-out mechanics.
- **Fund Expenses / Master Fund Expenses** — what each entity bears; the **"Specified Expenses"** definition and expense cap.
- **Repurchases** — closed-end illiquidity, tender-offer mechanics, **"Early Withdrawal Fee"**, **"Tender Valuation Date"**.
- **Transferability of Shares** — consent requirement.
- **Valuations** — monthly NAV by the **"Administrator"**.
- **Allocation of Investment Opportunities** — affiliate allocation policy; **"Other [Sponsor] Vehicles"**.
- **Summary of Tax Matters** — **"RIC"** election + (if feeder) master treated as partnership.
- **Reports / Exculpation, Indemnification / Administrator / Distributor / Transfer Agent / Custodian / Independent Registered Public Accounting Firm / Fiscal Year** — name each service provider and the governing agreement.

> Illustrative: *"The Fund expects to generally pay distributions on the Shares quarterly in amounts representing substantially all of its net investment income, if any."*

## THE FUND / INVESTMENT OBJECTIVE & STRATEGIES

**What it covers.** Expands the cover-page objective into the full investment program: the objective statement, the **Investment Strategy** (the 80%-policy with the full enumerated asset list), the feeder→master flow-through, portfolio composition, derivatives usage and caps, and credit-quality latitude.

**Fields to fill.** **Objective** = `[seek long-term total return / current income / capital appreciation by investing in [ASSET FOCUS]]`; **80% policy** naming each instrument category `[CMBS, mortgages, mezzanine, RMBS, CLO/CDO equity, REITs, ...]`; **derivatives cap** = `[up to 30% of Managed Assets]` listing instrument types `[total return swaps, credit default swaps, interest rate swaps, FX contracts, futures, options]`; **credit-quality** = `[any credit quality / below-investment-grade "high yield"]`; cross-reference each instrument to its risk factor.

> Illustrative: *"Under normal circumstances, at least 80% of the [Master Fund's] Managed Assets are invested in [asset class]."*

## LEVERAGE

**What it covers.** The fund's authority and limits for incurring leverage, the forms used, the senior-security analysis under the 1940 Act, the preferred-share authority (even if dormant), and the effect of leverage on fees, NAV volatility, and distributions. Often paired with a Reverse Repurchase Agreements detail block and a collateral table.

**Fields to fill.** **"Borrowings"** / **"Effective Leverage"** definitions; aggregate limit = `[33⅓% of total assets]` after giving effect; preferred-share authority = `[up to 50% of total assets]`; coverage/segregation mechanics; the SEC proposed-rule caveat on reverse repos; **Reverse Repurchase Agreement / "MRA"** mechanics; face value of open agreements `$[AMOUNT]` and weighted-average rate `[X.XX%]`.

> Illustrative: *"Leverage creates risk for the common shareholders, including the likelihood of greater volatility of NAV and distributions."*

## CONFLICTS OF INTEREST

**What it covers.** Discloses every material conflict arising from the manager's affiliations: allocation of investment opportunities among **"Other [Sponsor] Vehicles"**, co-investment limits under Section 17 and any SEC exemptive order, cross-trades, principal transactions, fee-driven incentives, and access to non-public information.

**Fields to fill.** names of overlapping vehicles `[VEHICLE A, B, C]`; the allocation methodology ("fair and equitable in sole discretion"); **exemptive order** reference; Section 17 limitations.

---

## MANAGEMENT OF THE FUND / ADVISORY (MANAGEMENT) FEE

**What it covers.** Identifies the **"Investment Manager"** and key personnel, summarizes the **Investment Management Agreement(s)** (fund-level and master-level if feeder), states the **Management Fee** rate and accrual mechanics, the **Incentive Fee** formula with a worked example, the **Expense Limitation Undertaking** with recapture terms, the Board of Trustees and the **"Administrator"**, and indemnification.

**Fields to fill.** **Management Fee** = `[X.XX%] of Managed Assets`, accrued `[monthly]`, paid `[quarterly] in arrears`; master-feeder single-fee mechanic; any temporary waiver to `[X.XX%]` through `[DATE]`; **Incentive Fee** = `[15%]` of **"Net Capital Appreciation"** for each **"Fiscal Period"** over the **loss carryforward account** and allocated Management Fee, with a share-repurchase crystallization trigger; **expense cap** = `[0.35%]` with `[3-year]` recapture; the **"Specified Expenses"** definition.

> Illustrative: *"The Incentive Fee is determined as of the end of the fiscal year in an amount equal to [15%] of the amount by which the Master Fund's Net Capital Appreciation … exceeds the balance of the loss carryforward account."*

## NET ASSET VALUE (DETERMINATION OF NAV AND MANAGED ASSETS)

**What it covers.** How and when NAV is computed: NAV = assets − liabilities (including accrued fees) as of each valuation date; calculated by the **"Administrator"** `[monthly]` per Board-approved policies; valuation hierarchy (market quotations from independent pricing sources; fair value for illiquid positions); feeder NAV depends on the master's portfolio value.

**Fields to fill.** valuation frequency = `[end of each calendar month]`; pricing sources `[pricing vendors, broker-dealers]`; fair-value committee/process; definition of **"Managed Assets"**.

> Illustrative: *"The NAV of the Fund … will be equivalent to its assets less its liabilities, including accrued fees and expenses, as of any date of determination."*

---

## DISTRIBUTIONS / AUTOMATIC DIVIDEND REINVESTMENT PLAN (DRIP)

**What it covers.** Distribution policy (frequency, source, Board discretion, no minimum, not guaranteed) and the mechanics of the **"Plan"** (default reinvestment at NAV unless the shareholder opts out, no sales load on reinvestment, issuance at next valuation-date NAV, suspension right, and the tax consequence that reinvested distributions are still taxable income).

**Fields to fill.** distribution frequency = `[quarterly]`; sources `[net investment income; annual net realized capital gains]`; opt-out method `[subscription documents / written notice to Administrator]`; reinvestment price `[NAV on next valuation date]`; Board factors `[earnings, cash flow, capital needs, Delaware law]`.

> Illustrative: *"Unless a shareholder is ineligible or otherwise elects, all distributions (including capital gain dividends) are automatically reinvested by the Fund in additional Shares."*

---

## PLAN OF DISTRIBUTION / SUBSCRIPTION FOR SHARES

**What it covers.** Combines **The Offering** (continuous offering at NAV, monthly/periodic closings, escrow of funds pending closing, minimums), the **"Qualified Investors"** eligibility standard, the subscription-document process, the **Distributor's** best-efforts role and selling-agent arrangements, and any additional compensation paid by the manager out of its own assets.

**Fields to fill.** offering = `[continuous, at then-NAV]`; closing cadence `[monthly]`; minimum initial / subsequent = `$[MIN] / $[MIN]`; **Distributor** = `[NAME L.P.]`; eligibility test = `[qualified clients per Rule 205-3 / accredited investors / qualified purchasers]`; escrow agent and account terms.

> Illustrative: *"Institutional Class II Shares are being offered only to 'Qualified Investors' … on a continuous basis."*

---

## REPURCHASES AND TRANSFERS OF SHARES (LIQUIDITY)

**What it covers.** The sole liquidity mechanism for a non-traded closed-end fund: discretionary issuer tender offers. Cover the closed-end no-redemption baseline, the Board's discretion and the factors it weighs, the typical offer size, the tender-offer timeline, partial/pro-rata fills, the **"Early Withdrawal Fee"**, holdback of a portion pending audit, forced/compulsory repurchase, and the consent requirement for transfers.

**Fields to fill.** repurchase frequency target = `[quarterly, effective 3/31, 6/30, 9/30, 12/31]`; offer size = `[10–25% of net assets]`; **"Tender Valuation Date"**; offer window opens `[~95 days prior]`, stays open `[~30 days]`; initial payment = `[95% of unaudited NAV]`, balance post-audit; **Early Withdrawal Fee** = `[2%]` within `[12 months]`, FIFO; forced-redemption authority; transfer-consent (sole discretion of the Fund).

> Illustrative: *"Shareholders in a closed-end fund do not have the right to redeem their Shares on a daily basis … liquidity is available only through limited tender offers."*

## DESCRIPTION OF SHARES

**What it covers.** The terms of the Shares under the Declaration of Trust: classes and series, par value, voting rights, no preemptive rights, the multi-class fee differences, and any preferred-share authority.

**Fields to fill.** class(es) `[CLASS NAMES]`; par value `$[PAR]`; voting/quorum terms; the **"Fund Declaration of Trust"** reference and date.

---

## TAX CONSIDERATIONS

**What it covers.** U.S. federal income-tax treatment: the **"RIC"** qualification under Subchapter M (diversification, 90%-gross-income, and distribution requirements), the consequence of failing to qualify, taxation of distributions to shareholders (ordinary vs. capital gain dividends), reinvested-distribution taxation, excise tax, and — for a feeder — the master fund's treatment as a **partnership** with flow-through allocations. Note that it is a summary and investors should consult their own advisers.

**Fields to fill.** RIC requirements `[diversification / 90% gross income / distribution]`; master entity classification = `[partnership]`; backup withholding; non-U.S. holder treatment; cross-reference SAI for detail.

> Illustrative: *"The Fund has qualified to be treated as a regulated investment company (a 'RIC') under Subchapter M of the Code, which generally relieves the Fund of any liability for federal income tax to the extent its earnings are distributed to shareholders."*

---

## CERTAIN ERISA CONSIDERATIONS

**What it covers.** Whether and how plan investors subject to Title I of **"ERISA"** or Section 4975 of the Code may invest, the plan-asset analysis (whether fund assets are treated as "plan assets"), fiduciary considerations, and prohibited-transaction caveats.

**Fields to fill.** plan-asset conclusion = `[the fund's assets will not be treated as "plan assets"]` (state the basis); fiduciary-prudence reminder; consult-own-counsel statement; cross-reference the **Qualified Investors** eligibility for tax-exempt entities.

---

## CUSTODIAN / TRANSFER AGENT / ADMINISTRATOR / OTHER SERVICE PROVIDERS

**What it covers.** Names and roles of the **"Custodian"** (and its authority to appoint sub-custodians), the **"Transfer Agent"**, the **"Administrator"** (books-and-records, accounting, clerical), the **Distributor**, and the **independent registered public accounting firm**, each tied to its governing agreement.

**Fields to fill.** Custodian = `[BANK] (the "Custodian")`; Transfer Agent = `[AGENT]`; Administrator = `[BANK] (the "Administrator")` under each **"Administration Agreement"**; Auditor = `[FIRM LLP]`; sub-custodian authority.

## SAI (INCORPORATED BY REFERENCE)

**What it covers.** A pointer to the **Statement of Additional Information**, incorporated by reference in its entirety, listing its table of contents (additional detail on investment policies, trustees/officers, the management agreements, brokerage, tax, and the audited financial statements). Provide how to obtain it free of charge.

**Fields to fill.** SAI date `[DATE]`; request contact `[TRANSFER AGENT address / phone]`; SEC EDGAR pointer.
