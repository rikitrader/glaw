## 12. CONSTRUCTION ANTI-INDEMNITY CARVE-OUT (Tex. Ins. Code Ch. 151) — READ IF THE MATTER IS CONSTRUCTION

> **CONSTRUCTION FLAG (load-bearing):** Texas has a **Construction Anti-Indemnity Act**, codified at **Tex. Ins.
> Code §§ 151.001–.151 (Subch. C)** (the "TAIA"; effective Jan. 1, 2012). *(Caution: this Act is in the
> **Insurance Code**, NOT the Business & Commerce Code; a citation to "Tex. Bus. & Com. Code Ch. 151" is
> incorrect.)* If the Matter is a "construction contract" or an agreement collateral to or affecting one:
>
> - **§ 151.102** renders **VOID and unenforceable** any provision that requires the indemnitor to indemnify,
>   hold harmless, or defend a party against a claim caused by the **negligence or fault, breach of statute, or
>   breach of contract of the indemnitee** (or its agents/employees or third parties under the indemnitee's
>   control). The express-negligence and conspicuousness language in § 4 **CANNOT cure this** — the prohibition
>   is absolute.
> - **§ 151.104** voids any provision requiring **additional-insured coverage** (or any endorsement) to the
>   extent it provides coverage that would be prohibited for an indemnity under the Act.
> - **§ 151.103** preserves an exception for claims for **bodily injury or death of the indemnitor's (or its
>   agent's or subcontractor's) employee.**
> - **§ 151.105** lists exclusions (e.g., independent breach-of-contract/warranty claims, loan and financing
>   documents, surety general agreements of indemnity, workers'-compensation benefits).
>
> **If the Matter is construction, DELETE § 4's own-negligence shift, cap indemnity at the indemnitor's own
> negligence/fault, and have construction counsel conform the insurance provisions.** The following carve-out
> is included as a backstop:

Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, if and to the extent this Agreement constitutes or affects
a "construction contract" governed by Tex. Ins. Code Ch. 151, Subch. C, no Releasor shall be required to
indemnify, hold harmless, or defend any Indemnitee against, and no release shall extend to, any liability, loss,
or claim caused by the negligence, fault, or breach of the Indemnitee, its agent, employee, or any third party
under the Indemnitee's control or supervision, and no provision of this Agreement shall be construed to require
additional-insured coverage prohibited by § 151.104. Any provision to the contrary is void to that extent under
§ 151.102 and shall be reformed accordingly.

> **AUTHORITIES RELIED UPON (pin-cited):**
> - *Ethyl Corp. v. Daniel Constr. Co.*, 725 S.W.2d 705, 707–08 (Tex. 1987) — express negligence rule: intent to
>   indemnify an indemnitee for its OWN negligence must be expressed in specific terms within the four corners
>   of the contract.
> - *Dresser Indus., Inc. v. Page Petroleum, Inc.*, 853 S.W.2d 505, 508–11 (Tex. 1993) — fair notice doctrine;
>   the conspicuousness requirement (and express negligence rule) apply to BOTH indemnities and releases that
>   shift the indemnitee's own negligence; conspicuousness requires text that attracts a reasonable person's
>   attention (e.g., ALL CAPS, contrasting type or color).
> - *Storage & Processors, Inc. v. Reyes*, 134 S.W.3d 190, 192 (Tex. 2004) — conspicuousness and express
>   negligence are questions of law for the court.
> - Tex. Ins. Code §§ 151.001–.151, Subch. C (Texas Construction Anti-Indemnity Act): § 151.102 (void
>   indemnity for indemnitee's own negligence/fault in construction contracts); § 151.103 (employee bodily-injury
>   exception); § 151.104 (void additional-insured coverage); § 151.105 (exclusions). Eff. Jan. 1, 2012.
