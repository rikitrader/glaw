# CLOSED-END FUND (FORM N-2) — DRAFTING-GUIDE EXEMPLAR

> **Reference guide, not a template or a copy.** Structure of a Form N-2 registration statement; federal (1940 Act); not state-variable.

---

## HEADER NOTE — JURISDICTION & SCOPE

Form **N-2** is the SEC registration form for **closed-end management investment companies** (including interval funds, tender-offer funds, business development company-adjacent vehicles, and many private/registered hybrid funds) registering under the **Investment Company Act of 1940** ("**1940 Act**") and, where shares are publicly offered, the **Securities Act of 1933** ("**1933 Act**"). Authority: 1940 Act §8(b); 1933 Act registration via Form N-2; rules at 17 CFR 274.11a-1 and Item requirements in the form instructions.

**No state variants.** This is a **federal** filing. For a registered investment company, **state blue-sky merit and registration requirements are preempted** — registered fund shares are "covered securities" under **National Securities Markets Improvement Act of 1996 (NSMIA)**, §18 of the 1933 Act (15 U.S.C. §77r). States retain only **notice-filing + fee** authority. There is therefore **no DE / FL / TX state variant** of this guide; the document is the same instrument nationwide.

**Two filing modes** (the form is the same, the cover differs):
- **Publicly offered** closed-end fund → registered under **both** the 1940 Act and 1933 Act; the Part A document is a **prospectus**.
- **Privately offered** fund relying on 1933 Act §4(a)(2) / Reg D (e.g., a Reg D private equity access fund) → registered under the **1940 Act only**; the Part A document is a **Confidential Private Placement Memorandum (PPM)** but tracks the same N-2 items. (The fetched exemplar — *Princeton Private Investment Fund*, File No. 811-2300, POS AMI — is this private mode: *"interests in the Registrant are not being registered under the [1933 Act] … since such interests will be issued solely in private placement transactions."*)

**Three parts:**
- **Part A** — the Prospectus / PPM (Items 1–14).
- **Part B** — the Statement of Additional Information / SAI (Items 15–24).
- **Part C** — Other Information & Exhibits (Items 25–34).

---

## COVER / FACING SHEET (Form N-2 facing page)

What goes here: the EDGAR facing sheet identifying the filing type and the registrant. States the Acts under which registration is sought and the amendment number; lists the registrant's exact name, principal-office address, telephone, agent for service, and counsel.

Key `[BRACKET]` fields:
- `INVESTMENT COMPANY ACT FILE NO. 811-[NNNNN]`
- `1933 ACT FILE NO. 333-[NNNNNN]` (omit / mark N/A if private-only)
- Boxes: ☒ Registration Statement under the 1940 Act ☐/☒ under the 1933 Act; ☐ Pre-Effective Amendment No. `[N]`; ☐ Post-Effective Amendment No. `[N]`; ☒ Amendment No. `[N]` to the 1940 Act registration
- `[EXACT NAME OF REGISTRANT]`, `[STREET, CITY, STATE ZIP]`, `[TELEPHONE]`
- Name & address of **agent for service** `[NAME, TITLE, ADVISER ENTITY]` + `COPY TO: [OUTSIDE COUNSEL, FIRM, ADDRESS]`
- Approximate date of proposed public offering: `[DATE / "As soon as practicable after the effective date"]`

Private-mode legend (verbatim category, paraphrased): a bold statement that the interests are **not** registered under the 1933 Act, are sold only to **"accredited investors" within the meaning of Regulation D**, and that the document "does not constitute an offer to sell."

## PART A — THE PROSPECTUS / PPM

### ITEM 1 — Outside Front and Back Cover Page

What goes here: the marketing/identification cover — fund name, securities offered, a one-paragraph description of the offering and the fund type, and the mandatory legends.

Key `[BRACKET]` fields:
- `[FUND NAME]`; securities offered: `[CLASS A / CLASS I shares of beneficial interest]`; `[CONTINUOUS / FIXED]` offering
- `[MINIMUM INITIAL INVESTMENT $___]`; offering price `[at NAV / $___ per share]`
- Closed-end structure statement; if an interval fund: `"The Fund is a non-diversified, closed-end management investment company that continuously offers its shares."`
- SEC legend (paraphrase): *"These securities have not been approved or disapproved by the Securities and Exchange Commission … any representation to the contrary is a criminal offense."*
- Back cover: table of contents reference, dealer-prospectus-delivery legend, transfer agent / distributor contacts.

Illustrative phrase: *"Investing in the Fund's shares involves a high degree of risk. See 'Types of Investments and Related Risk Factors.'"*

### ITEM 2 — Cover Pages; Other Offering Information

What goes here: any additional offering mechanics not on the front cover — pricing frequency, sales-load summary, distribution arrangements, exchange listing (or statement that shares are **not listed and have limited liquidity**).

Key fields: `[SALES LOAD up to __%]`, `[NO PUBLIC MARKET / listed on [EXCHANGE] under symbol [TICKER]]`, `[NAV struck [daily/weekly/monthly]]`.

### ITEM 3 — Fee Table and Synopsis (Summary of Fund Expenses)

What goes here: the standardized **fee table** required by Item 3, showing shareholder transaction expenses and annual expenses as a percentage of net assets, followed by the **expense example**. This is the single most format-controlled item in the form.

#### FEE TABLE — STRUCTURE (figures shown as `[BRACKET]`, not real numbers)

```
SHAREHOLDER TRANSACTION EXPENSES                                Class A      Class I
  Maximum Sales Load (as a % of offering price)                  [__]%        None
  Maximum Early Withdrawal / Contingent Deferred Sales Charge    [__]%        [__]%
  Dividend Reinvestment Plan Fees                                [None/$_]    [None/$_]

ANNUAL EXPENSES (as a percentage of net assets attributable to shares)
  Management Fee                                                  [__]%        [__]%
  Incentive Fee / Performance Fee (if any)                        [__]%        [__]%
  Distribution and/or Service (12b-1) Fees                        [__]%        None
  Interest Payments on Borrowed Funds (leverage cost)             [__]%        [__]%
  Acquired Fund Fees and Expenses (AFFE)                          [__]%        [__]%
  Other Expenses                                                  [__]%        [__]%
                                                                 ------       ------
  Total Annual Expenses                                           [__]%        [__]%
  (Less) Fee Waiver / Expense Reimbursement                      ([__])%      ([__])%
                                                                 ------       ------
  Total Annual Expenses After Waiver                              [__]%        [__]%
```

> Footnotes (required): describe the management-fee base (e.g., *"[__]% of the Fund's [net assets / managed assets including leverage], accrued [daily] and paid [monthly]"*), the AFFE methodology (underlying private-fund / acquired-fund costs), and the term and recoupment provisions of any **expense limitation agreement** (`[adviser may recoup waived amounts within [3] years if expenses remain below the cap]`).

#### EXPENSE EXAMPLE (required immediately after the table)

```
You would pay the following expenses on a $1,000 investment, assuming a [5]% annual return:
                          1 Year     3 Years    5 Years    10 Years
  Class A                 $[__]      $[__]      $[__]      $[__]
  Class I                 $[__]      $[__]      $[__]      $[__]
```

Synopsis: a brief plain-English overview of the fund, its objective, the adviser, and the principal risks, keyed to fuller discussion later. Illustrative phrase: *"The example should not be considered a representation of future expenses, and actual expenses may be greater or less than those shown."*

### ITEM 4 — Financial Highlights

What goes here: per-share operating performance and ratios for each of the last `[10]` fiscal years (or life of fund if shorter), audited. For a fund with no operations yet: a statement that no financial highlights are presented because the fund has not commenced operations.

#### FINANCIAL HIGHLIGHTS — STRUCTURE (per share, `[BRACKET]` figures)

```
PER SHARE OPERATING PERFORMANCE                         FY[YYYY]   FY[YYYY]   FY[YYYY]
  Net asset value, beginning of period                  $[__]      $[__]      $[__]
  Net investment income (loss)                           [__]       [__]       [__]
  Net realized and unrealized gain (loss)                [__]       [__]       [__]
    Total from investment operations                     [__]       [__]       [__]
  Distributions (from net investment income)            ([__])     ([__])     ([__])
  Distributions (from realized gains / return of capital)([__])    ([__])     ([__])
  Net asset value, end of period                        $[__]      $[__]      $[__]
  TOTAL RETURN                                           [__]%      [__]%      [__]%

RATIOS / SUPPLEMENTAL DATA
  Net assets, end of period (000s)                      $[__]      $[__]      $[__]
  Ratio of expenses to average net assets                [__]%      [__]%      [__]%
  Ratio of net investment income to average net assets   [__]%      [__]%      [__]%
  Portfolio turnover rate                                [__]%      [__]%      [__]%
```

### ITEM 5 — Plan of Distribution

What goes here: how shares are sold — the distributor/underwriter, sales loads, distribution/service-fee plan, dealer arrangements, and (private mode) the §4(a)(2)/Reg D private-placement mechanics and investor-eligibility gating.

Key fields: `[PRINCIPAL UNDERWRITER / DISTRIBUTOR NAME]`; `[continuous offering at NAV]`; `[selling agents receive up to __%]`; `[12b-1 plan: __% of average daily net assets]`; private mode: `[interests offered only to "accredited investors" / "qualified clients" / "qualified purchasers" via subscription agreement]`.

Illustrative phrase: *"The Fund engages in a continuous offering of shares and may sell shares on each [business day] at the net asset value per share."*

### ITEM 6 — Selling Shareholders

What goes here: usually **"Not applicable."** Required only if existing holders are reselling registered shares; if so, a table of selling holders and amounts.

Key field: `[Not applicable / table: name, shares owned before, shares offered, shares after]`.

### ITEM 7 — Use of Proceeds

What goes here: how the fund will deploy net offering proceeds, the expected investment timeline, and any temporary-investment / ramp-up period.

Key fields: `[net proceeds invested in accordance with the investment objective within approximately [__] months]`; `[pending investment, proceeds held in [cash / money-market / short-term instruments]]`; deduction of `[organizational and offering expenses]`.

Illustrative phrase: *"There can be no assurance that the Fund will be able to invest the proceeds within the anticipated timeframe."*

### ITEM 8 — General Description of the Registrant ("The Fund" / "Structure")

What goes here: the fund's organization (entity type, jurisdiction, date), its classification (diversified vs. non-diversified, closed-end, interval/tender-offer), share classes, and key structural features.

Key fields: `[FUND] is a [Delaware statutory trust / Maryland corporation] organized on [DATE]`; `[non-]diversified, closed-end management investment company`; `[interval fund making periodic repurchase offers / tender-offer fund]`; `[Class A and Class I shares]`.

Illustrative phrase: *"The Fund is a [non-diversified], closed-end management investment company organized as a [Delaware statutory trust]."*

### ITEM 8 (cont.) — Investment Objective and Strategies / Policies

What goes here: the fund's investment objective, principal strategies, asset-class targets and allocation ranges, fundamental policies (those changeable only by shareholder vote), leverage and concentration policies, and the universe of permitted investments.

Key fields:
- `[FUND] seeks [long-term capital appreciation / current income / total return]`
- Allocation table — strategy and geography ranges, e.g. `Buyout [40–100]%, Growth Capital [0–60]%, Special Situations [0–40]%, Other [0–20]%`; `North America [20–100]%, Europe [0–40]%, Asia [0–40]%`
- `[fundamental policy: the Fund will not invest more than [25]% of assets in any one industry]`; `[leverage up to [33⅓]% of total assets per §18]`

Illustrative phrase: *"Over time, the allocation ranges and commitment strategy may be adjusted based on the Advisor's analysis of the [private equity] market."*

### ITEM 8 (cont.) — Risk Factors (Types of Investments and Related Risk Factors)

What goes here: a detailed, plain-English enumeration of principal risks of investing in the fund — market, liquidity/illiquidity (closed-end structure), valuation, leverage, concentration, manager/key-person, underlying-fund (for funds-of-funds), conflicts of interest, and tax risks.

Key fields (risk-heading list): `[Limited Liquidity / Closed-End Structure Risk]`, `[Valuation Risk]`, `[Investment Fund / Underlying Fund Risk]`, `[Leverage Risk]`, `[Non-Diversification Risk]`, `[Conflicts of Interest]`, `[Tax Risk — RIC qualification]`, `[No Operating History]`.

Illustrative phrase: *"An investment in the Fund involves a high degree of risk, including the risk that the entire amount invested may be lost."*

### ITEM 9 — Management

What goes here: the investment adviser (and any sub-adviser), the advisory agreement and fee, the portfolio managers, the board of trustees/directors and their oversight role, the administrator, custodian, transfer agent, distributor, and a description of material conflicts of interest.

Key fields: `[ADVISER, LLC]` registered under the Advisers Act; `[advisory fee = __% of [net/managed] assets]`; `[SUB-ADVISER]`; `[portfolio managers: NAMES, tenure]`; `[Board: __ trustees, __ independent]`; `[ADMINISTRATOR]`, `[CUSTODIAN]`, `[TRANSFER AGENT]`, `[DISTRIBUTOR]`; `[Adviser also manages other accounts → allocation conflicts]`.

Illustrative phrase: *"The Advisor intends to concentrate investments in funds sponsored by established Investment Fund Managers."*

### ITEM 10 — Capital Stock, Long-Term Debt, and Other Securities

What goes here: a description of the fund's shares (classes, voting, dividend and liquidation rights, redemption/repurchase features), any senior securities / preferred / debt or borrowings, and the tax treatment of the fund and its distributions (RIC status).

Key fields: `[shares of beneficial interest, no par / $__ par]`; `[Class A vs Class I — load and fee differences]`; `[one vote per share / dollar-weighted]`; `[senior securities: credit facility up to $__ / preferred shares]`; **Distribution Policy / DRIP** — `[distributions paid [quarterly]; managed distribution policy at [__]% annualized]`, `[Dividend Reinvestment Plan: distributions automatically reinvested at NAV unless cash elected]`; **Taxes** — `[Fund intends to qualify as a regulated investment company (RIC) under Subchapter M of the Internal Revenue Code]`, `[distributions taxed as ordinary income / capital gain / return of capital]`.

Illustrative phrase: *"The Fund intends to elect to be treated, and to qualify each year, as a regulated investment company under the Code."*

### ITEM 11 — Defaults and Arrears on Senior Securities

What goes here: usually **"Not applicable."** Required only if the fund has senior securities in default or dividend arrears.

Key field: `[Not applicable / description of default amount and duration]`.

### ITEM 12 — Legal Proceedings

What goes here: any material pending legal proceedings against the fund or its adviser; otherwise a statement that there are none.

Key field: `[Neither the Fund nor the Adviser is currently a party to any material legal proceedings.]`

### ITEM 13 — Table of Contents of the Statement of Additional Information

What goes here: a cross-reference list of the headings contained in Part B (the SAI), so investors can request it.

Key field: `[list of SAI headings + statement that the SAI is incorporated by reference and available free on request]`.

## PART B — STATEMENT OF ADDITIONAL INFORMATION (SAI)

The SAI elaborates on Part A in detail and is incorporated by reference into the prospectus. It is **not** a marketing document; it is dense disclosure.

### ITEM 14 — Cover Page and Table of Contents (SAI)

What goes here: SAI cover identifying the fund and the related prospectus by date; statement that the SAI is not a prospectus and must be read with it; TOC.

Key field: `[Statement of Additional Information dated [DATE], relating to the Prospectus dated [DATE]]`.

### ITEM 15 — General Information and History

What goes here: fund history, name changes, prior reorganizations.

### ITEM 16 — Investment Objectives and Policies

What goes here: the **full** statement of fundamental and non-fundamental investment policies, detailed descriptions of every permitted instrument and technique, and portfolio-turnover discussion. Far more granular than Item 8.

Key fields: `[fundamental policies (1)–(N), changeable only by majority-of-outstanding-voting-securities vote]`; `[investment restrictions: borrowing, lending, underwriting, real estate, commodities, concentration]`.

### ITEM 17 — Management

What goes here: detailed information on trustees/directors and officers — bios, other directorships, share ownership in the fund, board committee structure, leadership structure, risk-oversight role, and trustee compensation table.

Key fields: `[Trustee table: name, age, position, term, principal occupation, # of portfolios overseen, other directorships]`; `[Compensation table: aggregate compensation from Fund and Fund Complex]`; `[ownership ranges: none / $1–$10,000 / over $100,000]`.

### ITEM 18 — Control Persons and Principal Holders of Securities

What goes here: persons owning ≥5% of any class, and any control persons (>25%).

Key field: `[5% holders table: name, class, # shares, % of class]`.

### ITEM 19 — Investment Advisory and Other Services

What goes here: full description of the advisory agreement (term, termination, fees paid in last 3 years), sub-advisory, administration, custody, transfer-agency, principal-underwriting, and independent-accountant arrangements, and codes of ethics.

Key fields: `[advisory fees paid: FY[YYYY] $___ , [YYYY] $___ , [YYYY] $___]`; `[independent registered public accounting firm: NAME]`; `[Rule 17j-1 code of ethics]`.

### ITEM 20 — Portfolio Managers

What goes here: for each portfolio manager — other accounts managed (and related conflicts), compensation structure, and ownership of fund shares.

Key fields: `[other accounts table: # of RICs / pooled vehicles / other accounts and assets, with performance-fee subset]`; `[compensation: base + bonus tied to [pre-tax performance]]`.

### ITEM 21 — Brokerage Allocation and Other Practices

What goes here: brokerage-selection practices, soft dollars (§28(e)), and aggregate brokerage commissions paid.

Key field: `[brokerage commissions: FY[YYYY] $___]` or `[the Fund invests primarily in privately offered funds and pays no brokerage commissions]`.

### ITEM 22 — Tax Status

What goes here: detailed RIC qualification requirements (Subchapter M asset/income/distribution tests), consequences of failure, excise tax, and tax treatment of specific instruments and of shareholders.

Key fields: `[90% gross-income test]`, `[asset diversification test]`, `[distribution requirement — 90% of investment company taxable income]`, `[4% excise tax]`.

### ITEM 23 — Financial Statements

What goes here: audited financial statements (or incorporation by reference to the annual report), and the auditor's report; or a seed-capital balance sheet for a fund not yet operating.

Key field: `[audited financial statements for the fiscal year ended [DATE] / seed balance sheet as of [DATE]]`.

What goes here: organizational chart or list of controlled / affiliated entities; often **"None"** for a standalone fund.

### ITEM 28 — Number of Holders of Securities

What goes here: a table of the number of record holders per class as of a recent date.

```
  Title of Class            Number of Record Holders (as of [DATE])
  Class A shares            [__]

```

### ITEM 29 — Indemnification

What goes here: description of indemnification of trustees/officers under the governing documents and the 1940 Act §17(h) limits, plus the SEC's undertaking re: indemnification of §17 liabilities.

Key field: `[indemnification per Declaration of Trust Article __, subject to §17(h)/(i) of the 1940 Act]`.

### ITEM 30 — Business and Other Connections of Investment Adviser

What goes here: the adviser's other business activities and the other businesses of its directors/officers (cross-references Form ADV).

### ITEM 31 — Location of Accounts and Records

What goes here: where the §31(a) books and records are kept (custodian, administrator, adviser, transfer agent addresses).

### ITEM 32 — Management Services

What goes here: any management-related service contracts not discussed elsewhere; usually **"Not applicable."**

### ITEM 33 — Undertakings

What goes here: the registrant's standard undertakings, including the continuous/delayed-offering Rule 415 undertakings, the indemnification undertaking, and (for closed-end funds) repurchase/tender undertakings where applicable.

Key fields: `[undertaking to file a post-effective amendment]`; `[undertaking re: §17 indemnification — submit to a court the question of whether indemnification is against public policy]`.

### SIGNATURES

What goes here: the signature page signed by the registrant, its principal executive officer, principal financial/accounting officer, and a majority of the trustees/directors (or attorney-in-fact under powers of attorney), with date.

Key fields: `[Pursuant to the requirements of the [1940 Act / and 1933 Act], the Registrant has duly caused this Registration Statement to be signed … on [DATE]]`; `[/s/ NAME, Title]` for each signatory.

## DRAFTING NOTES & CROSS-REFERENCES

- **Diversified vs. non-diversified** drives Item 8 classification and risk language; non-diversified funds carry a mandatory concentration-risk disclosure.
- **Interval fund?** Add Rule 23c-3 periodic-repurchase disclosure (repurchase offer schedule, repurchase request deadline, repurchase pricing date) under Item 8/Item 10 and a fundamental policy exhibit.
- **Leverage:** §18(a) asset-coverage limits (300% for debt senior securities) must be disclosed wherever leverage is described; leverage cost appears as a line in the Item 3 fee table.
- **RIC tax:** keep Item 10 (summary) and Item 22 (detail) consistent; the "intends to qualify as a RIC under Subchapter M" language must match.
- **AFFE:** funds-of-funds must include Acquired Fund Fees and Expenses in the fee table — frequently the largest line for private-equity access funds.
- **Private mode (1940-Act-only):** the cover legend, Item 5 (Reg D/§4(a)(2) mechanics + accredited-investor gating), and the "Confidential PPM" caption replace the public-offering prospectus framing; the item structure is otherwise unchanged.
- **Cross-references** between Part A summary disclosures and the fuller Part B (SAI) treatments must be exact — Item 13 (Part A) lists the SAI TOC; Item 14 (Part B) is that TOC.
