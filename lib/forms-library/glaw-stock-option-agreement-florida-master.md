# EXHIBIT A TO STOCK OPTION GRANT NOTICE
## STOCK OPTION AGREEMENT

Pursuant to the Grant Notice to which this Agreement is attached, [Company], Inc. (the "Company") has granted to
Participant an Option under the Plan to purchase the number of Shares indicated in the Grant Notice.

### ARTICLE 1. GENERAL
**1.1 Defined Terms.** Capitalized terms not defined herein have the meanings in the Plan and the Grant Notice.
**1.2 Incorporation of Terms of Plan.** The Option is subject to the Plan, incorporated by reference. In any
inconsistency between the Plan and this Agreement, the Plan controls.

### ARTICLE 2. GRANT OF OPTION
**2.1 Grant of Option.** In consideration of Participant's past and/or continued Service and other good and valuable
consideration, effective as of the Grant Date, the Company irrevocably grants the Option to purchase any part or all
of the aggregate Shares in the Grant Notice, on the terms of the Plan and this Agreement and subject to the Plan's
adjustment provisions. Unless designated a Nonqualified Stock Option in the Grant Notice, the Option is an Incentive
Stock Option to the maximum extent permitted by law.
**2.2 Exercise Price.** As set forth in the Grant Notice, without commission; provided the exercise price per Share
shall **not be less than 100% of the Fair Market Value of a Share on the Grant Date** (and **110%** if an ISO and
Participant is a Greater Than 10% Stockholder on the Grant Date).
**2.3 Corporate Authority; Adequacy of Consideration (FBCA §607.0621).** The Company represents that (a) the Shares
issuable on exercise have been duly authorized by the Company's board of directors and reserved for issuance under
the Plan; and (b) consistent with **§607.0621(2)–(3), Fla. Stat.**, the board of directors has determined that the
consideration to be received for the Shares — comprising the exercise price together with Participant's past and/or
continued Service rendered to the Company (which is permitted consideration for shares under **§607.0621(2)**, which
includes "any tangible or intangible property or benefit to the corporation, including cash, promissory notes,
services performed, [and] contracts for services to be performed") — is adequate. Under **§607.0621(3), Fla. Stat.**,
the board's determination of the adequacy of consideration for the issuance of shares is **conclusive** insofar as it
relates to whether the Shares are validly issued, fully paid, and nonassessable, provided the determination was made
in good faith. Upon issuance against payment of the exercise price and satisfaction of the conditions herein, the
Shares will be validly issued, fully paid, and nonassessable. **No preemptive rights apply:** under **§607.0630, Fla.
Stat.**, shareholders of a Florida corporation have **no statutory preemptive rights** unless the articles of
incorporation expressly grant them; this Option and the Shares are not subject to preemptive rights except to the
extent, if any, the Company's articles of incorporation so provide.

### ARTICLE 3. PERIOD OF EXERCISABILITY
**3.1 Commencement of Exercisability.** (a) Subject to Sections 3.1, 3.2, 3.3, 5.11 and 5.17, the Option vests and
becomes exercisable in the amounts and at the times in the Grant Notice. (b) No unvested portion becomes exercisable
after Participant's Termination of Service, except as the Administrator or a written agreement provides. (c) On a
Change in Control, the Option is treated per the Plan's CIC sections.
**3.2 Duration of Exercisability.** Installments are cumulative; each vested installment remains exercisable until it
becomes unexercisable under Section 3.3.
**3.3 Expiration of Option.** The Option may not be exercised after the first of: (a) the Expiration Date (≤10 years
from Grant Date); (b) for an ISO to a Greater Than 10% Stockholder, 5 years from the Grant Date; (c) 3 months after
Termination of Service (unless by death, Disability, or Cause); (d) 1 year after Termination by death or Disability;
or (e) Termination for Cause.
**3.4 Special Tax Consequences (§422(d)).** To the extent the aggregate Fair Market Value (at grant) of Shares for
which ISOs (including this Option) are first exercisable in any calendar year exceeds **$100,000**, the excess is a
Nonqualified Stock Option, applied in grant order per §422(d). An ISO exercised more than 3 months after Termination
(other than death/Disability) is taxed as an NSO.
**3.5 Tax Indemnity.** (a) Participant indemnifies the Company, any Subsidiary, and the employing company for any
Tax-Related Items from the grant/exercise of, or benefit from, the Option, the acquisition of Shares, or their
disposal. (b) The Option cannot be exercised until Participant satisfies all Tax-Related-Item arrangements the
Company requires; the Company need not issue Shares until then. (c) The Company makes no representations on tax
treatment and may be required to withhold in more than one jurisdiction.

### ARTICLE 4. EXERCISE OF OPTION
**4.1 Person Eligible to Exercise.** During Participant's lifetime, only Participant may exercise (unless disposed
of under a DRO); after death, the personal representative or those entitled under the will/descent may exercise
before the Option becomes unexercisable under 3.3.
**4.2 Partial Exercise.** Any exercisable portion may be exercised in whole or part before it becomes unexercisable;
no fractional Shares.
**4.3 Manner of Exercise.** Exercise by delivery to the Secretary (or designated administrator; including electronic
delivery) of: (a) an exercise notice in the Administrator's form, signed; (b) full payment for the Shares plus any
Tax-Related Items, per Section 4.4; (c) any representations/documents required to evidence compliance with the
Securities Act, Exchange Act, or other Applicable Law; and (d) if exercised by someone other than Participant, proof
of right to exercise. The Company may specify all conditions of the manner of exercise (which may vary by country).
**4.4 Method of Payment.** At Participant's election, by any of, or a combination of: (a) cash or check;
(b) with Administrator consent, surrender of Shares held long enough to avoid adverse accounting, valued at FMV; or
(c) other legal consideration acceptable to the Administrator, including a **broker-assisted cashless** market sell
order directing the broker to pay the Company sufficient net proceeds to cover the exercise price. Each form of
consideration described above constitutes **eligible consideration for shares under §607.0621(2), Fla. Stat.**
**4.5 Conditions to Issuance.** Shares may be authorized-but-unissued or reacquired, fully paid and nonassessable;
the Company need not issue until all legal/listing/withholding conditions are met.
**4.6 Rights as Stockholder.** Neither Participant nor any beneficiary has rights as a stockholder until the Shares
are issued (and, if certificated, delivered).

### ARTICLE 5. OTHER PROVISIONS
**5.1 Administration.** The Administrator has full authority to administer/interpret the Plan and this Agreement;
its decisions are final and binding.
**5.2 Whole Shares.** The Option may only be exercised for whole Shares.
**5.3 Transferability.** Subject to Section 5.14, the Option may not be sold, pledged, assigned, or transferred
except by will or the laws of descent (or as the Plan permits / under a DRO); it is exercisable during Participant's
life only by Participant.
**5.4 Tax Consultation.** Participant should consult their own tax advisors; the Company makes no tax representations.
**5.5 Binding Agreement.** Binding on the parties and their heirs, successors, and permitted assigns.
**5.6 Adjustments Upon Specified Events.** Shares and exercise price adjust per the Plan on the events the Plan
specifies (splits, recaps, reorganizations, etc.).
**5.7 Notices.** In writing to the addresses on the Grant Notice (or as updated); deemed given per the Plan.
**5.8 Titles.** Headings are for convenience only.
**5.9 Governing Law.** **Florida**, without regard to conflict-of-laws. This Agreement and the Option are governed by,
and construed in accordance with, the laws of the State of Florida, including the **Florida Business Corporation Act,
Ch. 607, Fla. Stat.**
**5.10 Conformity to Applicable Law.** Construed to be in compliance with all Applicable Law; invalid provisions are
limited/severed to comply.
**5.11 Amendment, Suspension and Termination.** The Plan and this Agreement may be amended/suspended/terminated per
the Plan; material impairment of Participant's rights requires consent.
**5.12 Successors and Assigns.** The Company may assign its rights; binding on successors.
**5.13 Notification of Disposition.** For an ISO, Participant must notify the Company of any disposition of Shares
within the §422 holding periods (2 years from grant / 1 year from exercise).
**5.14 Limitations Applicable to Section 16 Persons.** For Section 16 persons, the Option and Agreement are subject
to any additional limitations under Rule 16b-3 needed for the exemption.
**5.15 Not a Contract of Service Relationship.** Nothing confers a right to continued Service or interferes with the
Company's right to terminate.
**5.16 Entire Agreement.** The Plan, Grant Notice, and this Agreement are the entire agreement on the subject and
supersede prior understandings.
**5.17 Section 409A.** The Option is intended to be exempt from or comply with §409A and is construed accordingly;
the Company may amend to maintain exemption/compliance but is not liable for any §409A tax.
**5.18 Limitation on Participant's Rights.** Participation confers only the contractual rights herein; no trust or
funded benefit is created; Participant is a general unsecured creditor with respect to the Option.
**5.19 Securities Law Exemption (Federal Rule 701 / Florida Ch. 517).** The Option and the Shares are offered and
sold in reliance on exemptions from registration. At the federal level, the Company intends to rely on **Rule 701**
under the Securities Act of 1933 (compensatory benefit plan exemption) or, where applicable, **Regulation D**. At the
Florida level, offers and sales to Participant are intended to be exempt under **Ch. 517, Fla. Stat. (Florida
Securities and Investor Protection Act)**: securities sold in a federally **Rule 701-covered** compensatory
transaction are treated as exempt/covered, and the Company may alternatively rely on the **§517.061, Fla. Stat.**
exemption (including, as applicable, the limited-offering and no-public-solicitation provisions thereof) for offers
and sales of the Shares. No registration statement has been filed with the Florida Office of Financial Regulation,
and the Shares are "restricted securities" that may not be resold absent registration or an available exemption.
Participant acquires the Shares for investment and not with a view to distribution.
