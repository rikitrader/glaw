**9. TEXAS USURY SAVINGS CLAUSE — MAXIMUM LAWFUL AMOUNT & SPREADING.**
**(a) Texas usury framework.** The parties intend strict compliance with the Texas usury statutes, including **Tex.
Finance Code Chapters 302 and 303** (maximum rates of interest, including the "weekly ceiling" computed under
Ch. 303) and **Tex. Finance Code Chapter 305** (usury penalties — a creditor charging usurious interest may be
liable for three times the excess interest plus, in some cases, forfeiture of principal). The "**Maximum Lawful
Amount**" means the maximum amount of interest the Holder may contract for, charge, take, reserve, or receive on the
Principal under applicable Texas law (Ch. 302/303) and any controlling federal law, in effect from time to time.
**(b) Savings clause.** The parties do not intend to contract for, charge, take, reserve, or receive interest in
excess of the Maximum Lawful Amount. If interest in excess of the Maximum Lawful Amount is contracted for, charged,
taken, reserved, or received for any reason: (i) the provision is automatically reformed and the rate reduced so the
interest payable is the Maximum Lawful Amount; (ii) any excess actually received shall be **credited to Principal**
(or, if Principal has been paid in full, refunded to the Company); and (iii) the Holder is conclusively deemed to
have had **no intent** to charge or receive usurious interest, any such excess being the result of inadvertence or
bona fide error notwithstanding the exercise of due care.
**(c) Spreading provision.** To the extent permitted by Texas law (as contemplated by **Tex. Finance Code
§306.004(c)** and Texas case law), **all interest at any time contracted for, charged, taken, reserved, or received
in connection with this Note shall be amortized, prorated, allocated, and spread, in equal parts, throughout the
full stated term of this Note** (including any extensions or renewals) until payment in full, so that the actual
rate of interest on account of the indebtedness does not exceed the Maximum Lawful Amount. If this Note is paid,
prepaid, or accelerated (in whole or in part) before stated maturity, any unearned interest shall be canceled, and
any excess applied or refunded under (b).
**(d) Conversion not interest.** The conversion discount, valuation-cap benefit, change-of-control premium, and any
equity received on conversion are bargained-for consideration for investment risk and are **not** "interest" within
the meaning of the Texas Finance Code; to the extent ever so characterized, they are subject to the savings and
spreading provisions above.
**(e) Survival & control.** This Section survives repayment, conversion, acceleration, and termination, governs the
entire indebtedness, and controls over any conflicting provision of this Note or any related document.
