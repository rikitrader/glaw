As a condition of, and in consideration of, my [employment / consulting / service] with **[COMPANY], INC.** (with
its parents/subsidiaries/affiliates, the "Company Group"), I agree:

**1. RELATIONSHIP / AT-WILL.** This Agreement applies to my entire period of service and survives its end. In
Texas, employment is **at-will** unless a separate written agreement says otherwise; nothing here alters that.

**2. CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION.** (a) I will hold the Company Group's Confidential Information in strict confidence,
use it only for the Company's benefit, and not disclose it. (b) "Confidential Information" = non-public technical,
business, financial, customer, and product information, and includes "trade secrets" as defined in the Texas Uniform
Trade Secrets Act, **Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code §134A.002**. (c) **Third-party / former-employer information:** I
will not use or disclose any third party's confidential information or trade secrets. (d) **Defend Trade Secrets Act
notice (18 U.S.C. §1833(b)):** immunity for disclosures to government/in a sealed court filing for reporting a
suspected legal violation. Nothing in this Agreement prohibits protected whistleblower disclosures to a government
agency.

**3. CONSIDERATION — EXCHANGE OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION (Tex. Bus. & Com. Code §15.50(a)).** The Company Group
agrees to provide me, and I agree to receive, **access to its Confidential Information and trade secrets** during my
service, and [where applicable: an award of **[stock/options/RSUs]** and/or **specialized training**]. This exchange
is the **otherwise enforceable agreement** to which the covenants in §6 are **ancillary** at the time they are made,
giving rise to the Company's interest in restraining competition. I acknowledge that the promise to provide
Confidential Information is a **non-illusory** promise and that Confidential Information will in fact be provided.

**4. OWNERSHIP OF INVENTIONS.** (a) **Prior Inventions:** listed on **Exhibit A** are excluded; if none, I represent
there are none. (b) **Assignment of Inventions:** I assign to the Company all Inventions conceived/developed during
my service that relate to the Company's business or result from Company work/resources. (c) **Work Made for Hire:**
copyrightable works are "works made for hire"; to the extent not, I assign them. (d) **Moral Rights:** I waive any
moral rights to the fullest extent permitted. (e) **State-law carve-out:** Texas has **no** employee-invention
assignment-limiting statute (unlike Cal. Lab. Code §2870); the assignment in (b) is enforced as written, limited
only by its express scope. If this person also performs work in a statute state (e.g., CA, IL, WA, NC, MN, DE, NJ,
NV, UT), counsel must attach the corresponding Exhibit B notice. (f) **Further assurances + Power of Attorney:** I
will assist in obtaining/enforcing IP and appoint the Company my attorney-in-fact to execute related documents.

**5. RETURN OF COMPANY PROPERTY.** On termination (or on request) I will return all Company property, documents, and
devices, and delete Company data from personal systems.

**6. RESTRICTIVE COVENANTS (Tex. Bus. & Com. Code §15.50).** The following covenants are **ancillary to** the
otherwise enforceable agreement in §3 and contain limitations as to **time, geographic area, and scope of activity**
that are **reasonable** and do not impose a greater restraint than necessary to protect the Company Group's goodwill
and business interest (its Confidential Information and trade secrets):

&nbsp;&nbsp;(a) **Non-competition.** During service and for **[__] months** after, within **[GEOGRAPHIC AREA / the
territory in which I worked or had responsibility]**, I will not engage in or provide to a competitor services of
the type I performed for the Company Group in **[LINE OF BUSINESS / SCOPE OF ACTIVITY]**.

&nbsp;&nbsp;(b) **Non-solicitation of customers.** During service and for **[__] months** after, I will not solicit
or service the Company Group's existing or prospective customers or clients with whom I had material contact or
about whom I obtained Confidential Information. (Texas analyzes employee/customer non-solicitation covenants as
covenants not to compete under §15.50.)

&nbsp;&nbsp;(c) **Non-solicitation of personnel.** During service and for **[__] months** after, I will not solicit,
recruit, or hire away any employee, contractor, or consultant of the Company Group.

&nbsp;&nbsp;(d) **Non-disparagement.** I will not make false or disparaging statements about the Company Group, its
products, officers, or directors; this does not restrict truthful testimony, protected disclosures, or statements to
a government agency.

**7. REFORMATION (Tex. Bus. & Com. Code §15.51).** If any covenant in §6 is found to contain limitations as to time,
geographic area, or scope of activity that are not reasonable and impose a greater restraint than necessary, the
court **shall reform** the covenant to the extent necessary to make those limitations reasonable and enforce it as
reformed. I acknowledge that, where the primary purpose of the agreement is to obligate me to render personal
services and the Company seeks enforcement **after** the term, the Company's **damages, attorneys' fees, and costs**
incurred before the date the covenant is reformed may be **limited** by §15.51(c) if reformation is required;
accordingly, the Company expects to seek **injunctive relief** as its principal remedy.

**8. PREEMPTION (Tex. Bus. & Com. Code §15.52).** The criteria for enforceability of the covenants in §6 are
governed exclusively by §§15.50–15.52, which **preempt** any other common-law rule or procedure on the subject.

**9. REMEDIES.** Breach causes irreparable harm; the Company is entitled to **injunctive/equitable relief** plus
other remedies, without posting bond where permitted, subject to the §15.51 reformation limits above.

binds successors/heirs; severability/reformation per §15.51; **governing law: Texas (without regard to conflicts
