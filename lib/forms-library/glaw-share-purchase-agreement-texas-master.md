## TEXAS STATUTORY MAPPING (replaces the Delaware DGCL references)
- **Mergers / fundamental business combinations:** TBOC **§10.001 et seq.** (Chapter 10) — mechanics of mergers,
  conversions, and interest exchanges (the Texas analog to DGCL §251 et seq.). Relevant if the deal is restructured
  from a stock purchase to a statutory merger/squeeze-out.
- **Appraisal / dissenters' rights:** TBOC **§10.354–§10.368** (the rights of dissenting owners and the appraisal
  procedure; analog to DGCL §262). A pure stock purchase from a willing seller generally does not trigger these, but
  they govern any back-end merger or minority squeeze-out.
- **Business-combination (anti-takeover) statute:** TBOC **§21.601–§21.611** (the Texas analog to DGCL §203 — the
  three-year moratorium on business combinations with an "affiliated shareholder"). **FLAG:** by its own terms the
  Texas statute applies only to an **"issuing public corporation"** (broadly, a Texas corporation with 100+ record
  shareholders whose voting shares are registered/qualified under the federal securities laws or listed on a national
  exchange — see §21.602–§21.603). **A privately held Texas target is typically NOT an "issuing public corporation"
  and is therefore exempt from §21.601 et seq.** Confirm the target's shareholder count and registration status; do
  not import a §203-style covenant unless the target actually qualifies.
