## 1. CORPORATE AUTHORITY; AUTHORIZATION & RESERVATION OF SHARES
**1.1 Rights and options (Fla. Stat. §607.0624).** The Company's board of directors has, under **Fla. Stat. §607.0624**, the power to create and issue these warrants as **rights or options** entitling the holders to purchase shares of the Company, on the terms fixed by the board and set out in this Agreement. The board has duly authorized the creation, issuance, and terms of the Warrants and the issuance of the shares issuable on exercise (the "Warrant Shares").
**1.2 Authorized shares (Fla. Stat. §607.0602).** The Company's articles of incorporation authorize a number of shares of the class issuable on exercise sufficient to cover all outstanding Warrants on a fully-diluted basis; classes and series are established consistently with **Fla. Stat. §607.0601–§607.0602**.
**1.3 Reservation (Fla. Stat. §607.0621).** The Company shall at all times **reserve and keep available**, free of preemptive rights and other liens, out of its authorized but unissued shares, the full number of Warrant Shares. Upon exercise and payment (or net exercise), the Warrant Shares will be **validly issued, fully paid, and nonassessable** in accordance with **Fla. Stat. §607.0621** (consideration for shares).
**1.4 No preemptive rights (Fla. Stat. §607.0630).** Under **Fla. Stat. §607.0630**, shareholders have **no preemptive rights** to acquire the Company's unissued shares **except to the extent the articles of incorporation expressly provide**; the issuance of the Warrants and the Warrant Shares is therefore free of statutory preemptive rights unless and to the extent the articles grant them, in which case such rights have been waived or satisfied.
