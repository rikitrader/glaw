**1. Authority to Amend.** This Agreement may be amended only with the consent of the Company, the Escrow Agent, and a
majority of the public interest-holders voting as a class.

**2. Non-transferable.** While escrowed, no Escrowed Interest may be sold, pledged, assigned, hypothecated, or
transferred without consent of the Company and a class vote of the public interest-holders; any permitted transferee
must sign an escrow agreement identical to this one.

**3. Voting rights.** Escrowed Interests vote like other outstanding interests of the same class.

**4. Dividend / distribution participation.** Escrowed Interests participate in dividends and distributions on the same
basis as other interests of the same class, subject to **Section 5**.

**5. Distribution on dissolution.** Escrowed Interests are subordinated. The Promoters waive any liquidating
distribution until the public holders receive **≥ $[__]** per unit, after which distributions are made pro rata. This
Section applies on any merger, sale, consolidation, or other reorganization and adjusts for splits, recapitalizations,
and reclassifications.

**6. Reclassification.** Any interests or property issued, reclassified, or distributed in respect of Escrowed Interests
are themselves deposited into and become Escrowed Interests under this Agreement.

**7. Release conditions.** The Escrow Agent shall release Escrowed Interests upon the satisfaction of any one of the
following, evidenced by a written opinion of the Company's counsel that the condition has been met: **(A)** per-unit net

**(C)** an established trading market for **90** consecutive days at **> $[__]** per unit; or **(D)** the Promoters pay
**$[__]** per unit to the Company.

**8. Duration / termination.** This Agreement terminates **five (5) years** from the date hereof. If no release
condition under **Section 7** has been satisfied by that date, the Escrowed Interests are returned to the Company for
cancellation.

**9. Inspection of records.** The Escrow Agent shall keep accurate records of the Escrowed Interests and permit the
Company and any public interest-holder to inspect them on reasonable notice.

**10. Additional requirements.** The parties shall execute such further instruments and take such further actions as are
reasonably necessary to carry out this Agreement, including any documentation required by the Company's transfer agent.

**11. Escrow Agent's lien.** The Escrow Agent has a lien on, and may set off against, the Escrowed Interests and any
funds held hereunder for its unpaid fees and indemnified expenses under **Sections 13 and 14**.

**12. Controversy; interpleader (Texas).** If any dispute arises among the parties, or between any party and a third
person, as to the Escrowed Interests or any funds held hereunder, or if the Escrow Agent in good faith is in doubt as to
what action it should take, the Escrow Agent may, at its option:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(a) refuse to comply with any claim or demand and retain the Escrowed Interests until it
receives (i) a final, non-appealable order of a court of competent jurisdiction or (ii) a written instruction signed by
all parties to the dispute; and/or

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b) **unconditionally tender the Escrowed Interests and any disputed funds into the registry of,
and commence or be named in an action of interpleader in, a district court of [COUNTY] County, Texas (or the Texas
Business Court for the [__] Division if jurisdictional thresholds are met), pursuant to Rule 43, Texas Rules of Civil
Procedure.** Persons having claims against the Escrow Agent (as plaintiff-stakeholder) may be joined as defendants and
required to interplead when their claims expose the Escrow Agent to double or multiple liability; a defendant-stakeholder
may obtain the same relief by crossclaim or counterclaim. Tex. R. Civ. P. 43. A disinterested stakeholder who has not
unreasonably delayed and has unconditionally tendered the stake into the court's registry is entitled to be discharged
from liability to the rival claimants and to recover its reasonable attorneys' fees and costs out of the interpleaded
fund.

ministerial and limited to those expressly set out in this Agreement; no implied duties or fiduciary obligations shall

enforceable if conspicuous and within the fair-notice doctrine; however, a release or exculpation for the Escrow
Agent's own **gross negligence, intentional torts, or fraud is unenforceable as against Texas public policy** and is
**not** waived here.

## SCHEDULE 1 — TEXAS LAW PROVISIONS

**(i) Governing law; forum.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Texas, without regard to its
conflict-of-laws principles. The parties irrevocably submit to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the district
courts of **[COUNTY] County, Texas** (or, where its jurisdiction applies, the **Texas Business Court, [__] Division**),
and the U.S. District Court for the **[__]** District of Texas.

**(ii) Escrow-agent duties / standard of care.** Ministerial only; liability limited to gross negligence, bad faith, or
willful misconduct (Section 13). Texas enforces limitations of liability under the fair-notice/conspicuousness doctrine,
but exculpation for gross negligence, intentional torts, or fraud is unenforceable as against public policy.

**(iii) Interpleader / stakeholder remedy.** **Tex. R. Civ. P. 43** (Interpleader). A disinterested stakeholder who
(1) faces or reasonably anticipates rival claims, (2) has not unreasonably delayed, and (3) unconditionally tenders the
stake into the court's registry is discharged and may recover its attorneys' fees and costs out of the fund.

**(iv) Money-services caveat.** **CAUTION — Texas is broader than most states.** General escrow services can constitute
**money transmission** under the **Texas Money Services Act, Ch. 151, Tex. Fin. Code**, administered by the Texas
Department of Banking. The Act excludes an attorney or title company that, in connection with a **real-property
transaction**, receives and disburses currency or issues an escrow/trust-fund check only on behalf of a party
(§ 151.003). A securities-escrow agent that is not a bank/trust company and does not fit that narrow exclusion may need
a money-transmission license; counsel must confirm before engagement.

**(v) Unclaimed property / escheat.** Released property unclaimed by an owner is governed by **Title 6, Tex. Prop. Code
(Unclaimed Property)** and escheats to the **Texas Comptroller of Public Accounts**. Stock or other intangible
ownership interest in a business association is **presumed abandoned three (3) years** after the earliest of (a) the
first unclaimed sum payable on the interest or (b) a returned-undeliverable communication; the period stops on any
owner act of ownership or communication. **§ 72.101, Tex. Prop. Code.** Property held by financial institutions is
addressed in **Ch. 73**, and reporting, delivery, and the claims process in **Ch. 74**; the holder must report and
deliver by **November 1** for property held on the preceding **June 30**. **§ 74.101, Tex. Prop. Code.**
