**1. Authority to Amend.** This Agreement may be amended only with the consent of the Company, the Escrow Agent, and a

**2. Non-transferable.** While escrowed, no Escrowed Interest may be sold, pledged, assigned, hypothecated, or
transferred without consent of the Company and a class vote of the public interest-holders; any permitted transferee
must sign an escrow agreement identical to this one.

**3. Voting rights.** Escrowed Interests vote like other outstanding interests of the same class.

**4. Dividend / distribution participation.** Escrowed Interests participate in dividends and distributions on the same
basis as other interests of the same class, subject to **Section 5**.

**5. Distribution on dissolution.** Escrowed Interests are subordinated. The Promoters waive any liquidating
distribution until the public holders receive **≥ $[__]** per unit, after which distributions are made pro rata. This

**6. Reclassification.** Any interests or property issued, reclassified, or distributed in respect of Escrowed Interests
are themselves deposited into and become Escrowed Interests under this Agreement.

**7. Release conditions.** The Escrow Agent shall release Escrowed Interests upon the satisfaction of any one of the

**(C)** an established trading market for **90** consecutive days at **> $[__]** per unit; or **(D)** the Promoters pay
**$[__]** per unit to the Company.

**8. Duration / termination.** This Agreement terminates **five (5) years** from the date hereof. If no release
condition under **Section 7** has been satisfied by that date, the Escrowed Interests are returned to the Company for
cancellation.

**9. Inspection of records.** The Escrow Agent shall keep accurate records of the Escrowed Interests and permit the
Company and any public interest-holder to inspect them on reasonable notice.

**10. Additional requirements.** The parties shall execute such further instruments and take such further actions as are
reasonably necessary to carry out this Agreement, including any documentation required by the Company's transfer agent.

**11. Escrow Agent's lien.** The Escrow Agent has a lien on, and may set off against, the Escrowed Interests and any
funds held hereunder for its unpaid fees and indemnified expenses under **Sections 13 and 14**.

**12. Controversy; interpleader (Florida).** If any dispute arises among the parties, or between any party and a third

what action it should take, the Escrow Agent may, at its option:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(a) refuse to comply with any claim or demand and retain the Escrowed Interests until it

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b) **deposit the Escrowed Interests and any disputed funds into the registry of, and commence or
be named in an action of interpleader in, the Circuit Court in and for [COUNTY] County, Florida, pursuant to Rule
1.240, Florida Rules of Civil Procedure.** Persons having claims against the Escrow Agent (as plaintiff-stakeholder) may
be joined as defendants and required to interplead when their claims expose the Escrow Agent to double or multiple
liability; a defendant-stakeholder may obtain the same relief by crossclaim or counterclaim. Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.240.
Upon depositing the Escrowed Interests and disputed funds into the court registry, the Escrow Agent is discharged from
all further liability with respect to the deposited property, and its reasonable attorneys' fees and costs of the
interpleader shall be paid out of the deposited property.

## SCHEDULE 1 — FLORIDA LAW PROVISIONS

**(i) Governing law; forum.** This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Florida, without regard to its
conflict-of-laws principles. The parties irrevocably submit to the exclusive jurisdiction and venue of the Circuit
Court in and for **[COUNTY] County, Florida**, and the U.S. District Court for the **[Middle/Southern/Northern]**
District of Florida.

**(ii) Escrow-agent duties / standard of care.** Ministerial only; liability limited to gross negligence, bad faith, or
willful misconduct (Section 13). Florida enforces such limitations except that exculpation for gross negligence,
intentional torts, or fraud is void as against public policy.

**(iii) Interpleader / stakeholder remedy.** **Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.240** (Interpleader). An innocent stakeholder who
tenders the disputed property into the court registry is discharged and may recover its fees and costs out of the fund.

**(iv) Money-services caveat.** A bona fide escrow incidental to this securities transaction is **not** the business of
money transmission. The **Money Transmitters' Code, Ch. 560, Fla. Stat.**, regulates money services businesses but
exempts banks, trust companies, and other depository institutions (§ 560.104). If the Escrow Agent will hold and
disburse cash for unrelated parties as a standalone business, counsel must confirm no money-services-business license
is required under Ch. 560.

**(v) Unclaimed property / escheat.** Released property unclaimed by an owner is governed by the **Florida Disposition
of Unclaimed Property Act, Ch. 717, Fla. Stat.** Stock or other equity interest in a business association is
**presumed unclaimed three (3) years** after the earliest of (a) the most recent unclaimed dividend, split, or
distribution, or (b) a returned-undeliverable communication. **§ 717.1101, Fla. Stat.** Other intangible property held
in the ordinary course is presumed unclaimed after **five (5) years**. **§ 717.102, Fla. Stat.** The holder must report
and remit to the Florida Department of Financial Services by **April 30** each year. **§ 717.117, Fla. Stat.**
