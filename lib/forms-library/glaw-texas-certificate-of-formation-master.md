The undersigned, acting as organizer of [Company], Inc., a Texas for-profit corporation, under the Texas Business
Organizations Code, executes this Certificate of Formation. The required contents of TBOC §3.005 and the
for-profit-corporation supplements of TBOC §3.007 are set out below. The text is:

**ARTICLE I — ENTITY NAME / TYPE.** The name of the filing entity is [Company], Inc. The entity is a **for-profit
corporation**. The name contains the organizational designator "Incorporated" in its abbreviated form "Inc.", as
required by TBOC §§5.054 and 5.056 (a for-profit corporation's name must contain "company", "corporation",
"incorporated", "limited", or an abbreviation thereof).

**ARTICLE II — PURPOSE.** The Corporation is organized to transact any and all lawful business for which a for-profit
corporation may be organized under the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC §§2.001, 2.003, 3.005(a)(3)).

**ARTICLE III — REGISTERED AGENT / REGISTERED OFFICE.** The name of the initial registered agent is [REGISTERED
AGENT], who is [an individual resident of Texas / a domestic or registered foreign entity authorized to serve], and
the business address of the registered office in Texas is [TX REGISTERED OFFICE] (a Texas street address — not a P.O.
box), as required by TBOC §§3.005(a)(4) and 5.201. The registered office is the registered agent's business office.
The registered agent's written consent to serve, as required by **TBOC §5.201(b)**, appears in the consent block
below (or is maintained by the Corporation on Form 401-A).

**ARTICLE IV — AUTHORIZED STOCK.** The Corporation is authorized to issue [____] shares: [____] **Common Stock**
($[0.0001] par) and [____] **Preferred Stock** ($[0.0001] par), as required by TBOC §§3.007(a) and 21.151.
**[OPTIONAL dual-class:** Common split into Class A (1 vote) and Class B (10 votes), Class B convertible to Class A
1:1 and on transfer — include only if a founder-control/dual-class structure is intended; permitted under TBOC
§21.155.]

**ARTICLE V — PREFERRED STOCK (BLANK CHECK).** The Board of Directors is expressly authorized, by resolution and a
**statement of resolution / certificate of amendment establishing one or more series** (as permitted by **TBOC
§§21.155 and 21.156**), to issue Preferred Stock in series and to establish and fix the designations, preferences,
limitations, and relative rights of each series (including dividends, liquidation, conversion, voting, redemption,
and anti-dilution), and to increase or decrease the number of shares of any such series, without further shareholder
action. [This is the board authority that a Series A series designation exercises.]

**ARTICLE VI — COMMON STOCK.** Voting (1 vote/share unless dual-class), dividend, and liquidation rights, subject to
the Preferred and to TBOC §21.153.

**ARTICLE VII — [PROTECTIVE / SERIES VOTING].** Any class or series votes required by the Texas Business
Organizations Code or by a board resolution establishing a series (TBOC §§21.156, 21.364).

**ARTICLE VIII — EXISTENCE.** Perpetual, unless a period of duration is stated here (TBOC §3.003).

**ARTICLE IX — BALLOTS.** Elections of directors need not be by written ballot unless the bylaws so provide.

**ARTICLE X — DIRECTORS.** The Corporation will have a board of directors. The number of directors constituting the
initial board is [____], and the names and addresses of the persons who will serve as initial directors until the
first annual meeting of shareholders or until their successors are elected and qualified are [INITIAL DIRECTORS], as
required by TBOC §§3.007(b)(2) and 21.407. Thereafter the number of directors is fixed by or under the bylaws;
[classified/staggered board OPTIONAL post-IPO under TBOC §21.408]; vacancies are filled by the Board; removal is
[with/without cause] as TBOC §21.409 permits.

**ARTICLE XI — BYLAWS.** The Board is expressly authorized to adopt, amend, or repeal the bylaws (concurrent with
shareholders), as permitted by TBOC §21.057.

**ARTICLE XII — LIMITATION OF DIRECTOR LIABILITY.** To the fullest extent permitted by the Texas Business
Organizations Code, a director of the Corporation is **not liable** to the Corporation or its shareholders for
monetary damages for an act or omission in the director's capacity as a director, except to the extent that **TBOC
§7.001** does not permit such limitation. No amendment or repeal of this Article eliminates or reduces the effect of
this limitation for any act or omission occurring before the amendment or repeal. (Note: TBOC §7.001 carves out
breach of the duty of loyalty, acts not in good faith / intentional misconduct / knowing violation of law, transactions
from which the director received an improper benefit, and acts for which liability is expressly provided by statute;
the limitation cannot reach those.)

**ARTICLE XIII — INDEMNIFICATION.** The Corporation **shall indemnify**, and may advance reasonable expenses to, its
directors and officers (and may indemnify employees and agents) to the fullest extent permitted or required by the
Texas Business Organizations Code, including **TBOC §§8.051–8.105** (Chapter 8, Subchapters B and C), and may purchase
and maintain insurance under TBOC §8.151. Any amendment or repeal of this Article is prospective only and does not
affect rights existing at the time of conduct.

**ARTICLE XIV — SHAREHOLDER ACTION / MEETINGS.** Meetings may be held in or outside Texas; [action by written consent
permitted while private (TBOC §21.365) / consent in lieu of meeting modified or prohibited after a public offering];
special-meeting and forum-selection (a state or federal court located in Texas) provisions as drafted.

**ARTICLE XV — ORGANIZER.** The name and address of the organizer is [ORGANIZER], as required by TBOC §§3.004 and
3.005(a)(7). The organizer need not be a director, officer, or shareholder.

**ARTICLE XVI — SUPPLEMENTAL PROVISIONS.** [Any additional provisions consistent with law that the Corporation elects

(TBOC §21.361), restrictions on transfer, or other governance terms. Strike if none.]

**ARTICLE XVII — EFFECTIVENESS.** This Certificate of Formation takes effect when filed by the Texas Secretary of
State, **[OR** takes effect on [EFFECTIVE DATE] / upon the occurrence of [FUTURE EVENT] — a delayed effectiveness not
later than the 90th day after the date of signing, as permitted by **TBOC §§4.052–4.055**; include only if delayed
effectiveness is intended.]

by the Texas Business Organizations Code (TBOC §§21.351–21.364, 3.051–3.060), subject to any Preferred protective
provisions.
