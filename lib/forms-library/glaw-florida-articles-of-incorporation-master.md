The undersigned, acting as incorporator of [Company], Inc., a Florida corporation, under the Florida Business
Corporation Act (Chapter 607, Florida Statutes), adopts the following Articles of Incorporation. The text is:

**ARTICLE I — NAME.** The name of the Corporation is [Company], Inc. (The name contains the word "Incorporated"
in its abbreviated form "Inc." as required by §607.0401, Fla. Stat.; the corporate-designator requirement is
satisfied where the name includes "Corporation", "Incorporated", "Company", or an abbreviation thereof.)

**ARTICLE II — PRINCIPAL OFFICE / MAILING ADDRESS.** The street address of the principal office of the
Corporation is [PRINCIPAL OFFICE], and its mailing address (if different) is [MAILING ADDRESS], as required by
§607.0202, Fla. Stat.

**ARTICLE III — REGISTERED AGENT / REGISTERED OFFICE.** The name of the initial registered agent is
[REGISTERED AGENT], and the street address of the registered office in Florida is [FL STREET ADDRESS] (a
Florida street address — not a P.O. box), as required by §607.0501, Fla. Stat. The registered agent's
acceptance appears in the acceptance block below.

**ARTICLE IV — PURPOSE.** Any lawful act or activity for which a corporation may be organized under the Florida
Business Corporation Act (Chapter 607, Florida Statutes).

**ARTICLE V — AUTHORIZED STOCK.** The Corporation is authorized to issue [____] shares: [____] **Common Stock**
($[0.0001] par) and [____] **Preferred Stock** ($[0.0001] par). **[OPTIONAL dual-class:** Common split into
Class A (1 vote) and Class B (10 votes), Class B convertible to Class A 1:1 and on transfer — include only if a
founder-control/dual-class structure is intended.]

**ARTICLE VI — PREFERRED STOCK (BLANK CHECK).** The Board of Directors is authorized, by resolution and
**Articles of Amendment establishing the terms of a series** (as permitted by §607.0602, Fla. Stat.), to issue
Preferred Stock in series and to fix the designations, powers, preferences, and rights (dividends, liquidation,
conversion, voting, redemption, anti-dilution) and any limitations without further shareholder action. [This is
the authority the Series A series designation exercises.]

**ARTICLE VII — COMMON STOCK.** Voting (1 vote/share unless dual-class), dividend, and liquidation rights,
subject to the Preferred.

**ARTICLE VIII — [PROTECTIVE / SERIES VOTING].** Any class/series votes required by the Florida Business
Corporation Act or by Articles of Amendment establishing a series.

**ARTICLE IX — EXISTENCE.** Perpetual.

**ARTICLE X — BALLOTS.** Elections of directors need not be by written ballot unless the bylaws so provide.

**ARTICLE XI — BOARD.** The number of directors is fixed by/under the bylaws; [classified/staggered board
OPTIONAL post-IPO]; vacancies filled by the Board; removal [with/without cause] as the Florida Business
Corporation Act permits (§607.0808, Fla. Stat.).

**ARTICLE XII — BYLAWS.** The Board is expressly authorized to adopt, amend, or repeal the bylaws (concurrent
with shareholders), as permitted by §607.1020, Fla. Stat.

**ARTICLE XIII — LIMITATION OF LIABILITY & INDEMNIFICATION.** Directors (and officers, to the extent permitted
by §607.0831, Fla. Stat.) are **not liable** for monetary damages to the fullest extent permitted by the
Florida Business Corporation Act; the Corporation **indemnifies** and may advance expenses to directors/officers
to the fullest extent permitted by §§607.0850–607.0859, Fla. Stat.; amendments are prospective only.

**ARTICLE XIV — SHAREHOLDER ACTION / MEETINGS.** Meetings may be held in or outside Florida; [action by written
consent permitted while private / prohibited after a public offering]; special-meeting and forum-selection (a
state or federal court located in Florida) provisions as drafted.

**ARTICLE XV — INCORPORATOR.** The name and address of the incorporator is [INCORPORATOR], as required by
§607.0202, Fla. Stat.

**ARTICLE XVI — EFFECTIVE DATE.** These Articles are effective upon filing with the Florida Department of State,
**[OR** effective on [EFFECTIVE DATE] — a date not earlier than filing and not more than 90 days after filing,
as permitted by §607.0123, Fla. Stat.; include only if a delayed effective date is intended.]

**ARTICLE XVII — AMENDMENT.** The Corporation reserves the right to amend these Articles per the Florida Business
Corporation Act (§§607.1001–607.1009, Fla. Stat.); subject to any Preferred protective provisions.
