**9. FLORIDA USURY SAVINGS CLAUSE — MAXIMUM LAWFUL RATE.**
**(a) Florida usury framework.** The parties intend strict compliance with **Fla. Stat. §687.02–§687.03** (civil
usury). Under §687.03, interest exceeding **18% per annum is usurious on a loan of $500,000 or less**, and interest
exceeding **25% per annum is usurious on a loan exceeding $500,000**; civil usury exposure includes forfeiture of
interest and, for willful violations, of double the interest. **Fla. Stat. §687.071** makes it **criminal usury**
to willfully charge interest exceeding **25% per annum** (a misdemeanor / third-degree felony), and interest
exceeding **45% per annum** is a higher-degree criminal offense that may render the obligation **unenforceable**.
The "**Maximum Lawful Rate**" means the highest rate of interest the Holder may lawfully contract for, charge, take,
reserve, or receive from the Company under Florida law (and any controlling federal law) for a loan of this
Principal amount, determined as of the date of calculation.
**(b) Savings clause.** Notwithstanding anything to the contrary, the aggregate interest and all charges in the
nature of interest contracted for, charged, taken, reserved, or received under this Note shall not exceed the
Maximum Lawful Rate. If any provision would obligate the Company to pay interest in excess of the Maximum Lawful
Rate, (i) the rate is automatically reduced to the Maximum Lawful Rate; (ii) any excess previously received shall,
at the Holder's option, be applied to reduce Principal (and, once Principal is paid in full, refunded to the
Company); and (iii) the Holder shall be conclusively deemed to have had **no intent** to charge or collect interest
in excess of the Maximum Lawful Rate, any such excess being the result of inadvertence or bona fide error.
**(c) Conversion not interest.** The conversion discount, valuation-cap benefit, change-of-control premium, and any
equity received on conversion are bargained-for consideration for investment risk and are **not** "interest" or a
charge in the nature of interest within the meaning of Fla. Stat. Ch. 687; to the extent ever so characterized, they
are subject to the savings clause in (b).
**(d) Survival.** This Section survives repayment, conversion, acceleration, and termination, and controls over any
conflicting provision.
