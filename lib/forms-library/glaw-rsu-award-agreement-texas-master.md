**1. GRANT OF RSU.** The Company grants Participant a Restricted Stock Unit Award for the number of Shares of Common
Stock in the Award Record, subject to this Agreement and the Plan (incorporated in full; the Plan controls in any
conflict). An "outstanding" RSU is one granted but not yet a Vested RSU; outstanding RSUs are held for Participant
until vested unless terminated/forfeited.

**1A. CORPORATE AUTHORITY; ADEQUACY OF CONSIDERATION (TBOC §§21.160–21.161).** The Company represents that (a) the
Shares issuable on settlement of Vested RSUs have been duly authorized by the Company's board of directors and
reserved for issuance under the Plan; and (b) consistent with **TBOC §21.160**, the board of directors has determined
that the consideration to be received for those Shares — comprising Participant's past and/or continued Service to
the Company, which is permitted consideration for shares under **§21.160(a)** (covering "any tangible or intangible
benefit to the corporation or other property of any kind or nature, including cash, a promissory note, services
performed, [or] a contract for services to be performed") — is adequate. Under **TBOC §21.161(a)–(b)**, in the
absence of fraud in the transaction, the board's determination of the adequacy of consideration for the issuance of
shares is **conclusive**, and shares issued for that consideration are validly issued, fully paid, and
nonassessable. Upon issuance in settlement of Vested RSUs and satisfaction of the conditions herein, the Shares will
be validly issued, fully paid, and nonassessable. **No preemptive rights apply:** under **TBOC §21.203**, a
shareholder of a Texas for-profit corporation does **not** have a preemptive right to acquire additional shares
unless the certificate of formation expressly provides otherwise; the RSUs and the underlying Shares are not subject
to preemptive rights except to the extent, if any, the Company's certificate of formation so provides.

**2. VESTING.** A Participant benefits only if an RSU vests. **2.1** An RSU becomes a "**Vested RSU**" on the first
date both the Service-Based Requirement (2.2) and the Liquidity Event Requirement (2.3) are satisfied. **2.2
Service-Based Requirement:** 25% on the 12-month anniversary of the Vesting Commencement Date, then 1/36 monthly,
fully satisfied on the 4th anniversary; if a Liquidity Event occurs before Termination of Service, deemed fully
satisfied. Vesting ceases on Termination of Service. **2.3 Liquidity Event Requirement:** satisfied on a
"**Liquidity Event**" before Termination = the first of (1) a Change in Control; (2) expiration of an IPO lock-up;
(3) sale of a [brand/subsidiary or ≥10%-owned entity]; or (4) one day before the 10th anniversary of the Date of
Award. **2.4** Forfeiture if Participant joins a competitor post-termination. **2.5/2.6** On voluntary termination,
or involuntary termination by death/Disability/Cause, all RSUs forfeit without payment; on other involuntary
termination, the Company may (sole discretion) let service-met RSUs remain outstanding until a Liquidity Event
(clause (1) or (4) only). **2.7** Defines "**Cause**," "**Change in Control**," "**Disability**," "**IPO**" [conform to Plan].

**3. SETTLEMENT.** Vested RSUs are settled by issuance of one Share per Vested RSU (or cash equivalent) as soon as
practicable after vesting, within the period required to be **exempt from / compliant with Code §409A** (short-term
deferral or a fixed §409A-compliant schedule). No fractional Shares.

**4. NO OBLIGATION TO EMPLOY.** Nothing confers a right to continued Service or limits the Company's right to terminate.

**5. TAXES.** Participant is responsible for all Tax-Related Items; settlement is conditioned on satisfaction of
withholding (cash, share withholding/sell-to-cover, or other Plan-permitted method). The Company makes no tax
representation and may withhold in more than one jurisdiction.

**6. COMPLIANCE WITH LAWS AND REGULATIONS.** No Shares issued unless registered/exempt and in compliance with the
Securities Act, Exchange Act, and other Applicable Law; issuance may be deferred to comply. The RSUs and the
underlying Shares are offered and issued in reliance on exemptions from registration. At the federal level, the
Company intends to rely on **Rule 701** under the Securities Act of 1933 (compensatory benefit plan exemption) or
§4(a)(2) / **Regulation D**, as applicable. At the Texas level, offers and issuances to Participant are intended to be
exempt under the **Texas Securities Act, Ch. 4001, Tex. Gov't Code (Title 12)**, including, as applicable, the
exemptions administered by the Texas State Securities Board for compensatory equity and limited offerings, and
consistent with the federal **Rule 701-covered** treatment of the transaction. No registration statement has been
filed with the Texas State Securities Board; the Shares are "restricted securities" that may not be resold absent
registration or an available exemption; and Participant acquires the Shares for investment and not with a view to
distribution.

**7. NONTRANSFERABILITY OF RSUs.** RSUs may not be sold, pledged, assigned, or transferred except by will/descent
