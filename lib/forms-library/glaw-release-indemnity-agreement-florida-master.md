## 12. CONSTRUCTION / DESIGN-PROFESSIONAL ANTI-INDEMNITY CARVE-OUT (Fla. Stat. §§ 725.06, 725.08) — READ IF CONSTRUCTION

> **CONSTRUCTION FLAG (load-bearing):** Florida limits indemnity in construction and design-professional
> contracts. If the Matter is or affects such a contract:
>
> - **Fla. Stat. § 725.06** — An indemnification provision in a construction contract that purports to indemnify
>   the indemnitee for damages caused **at least in part by the indemnitee's own fault** is **VOID and
>   unenforceable UNLESS** the contract contains a **monetary limitation** on indemnification that bears a
>   **reasonable commercial relationship** to the contract and is part of the project specifications or bid
>   documents. For indemnification of an **owner** of real property by a party in privity, the monetary limitation
>   **shall not be less than $1 million per occurrence** unless the parties otherwise agree. **Specific monetary
>   consideration is required** for the indemnity to be enforceable. The "clear and unequivocal" language in § 4
>   does NOT cure a § 725.06 defect — the monetary cap and consideration are independent statutory prerequisites.
> - **Fla. Stat. § 725.08** — In a **design-professional** services contract, indemnity is limited to liability
>   caused by the **negligence, recklessness, or intentionally wrongful conduct of the design professional** and
>   persons it employs; broader indemnity (and, for **public-agency** contracts, any provision requiring the
>   design professional to defend/indemnify the agency beyond that scope) is **void as against public policy.**
>
> **If the Matter is construction or design-professional work, ADD a § 725.06-compliant monetary limitation with
> specific consideration (≥ $1M per occurrence for owner indemnitees), NARROW § 4 to the indemnitor's own
> negligence/fault for design professionals, and have construction counsel conform the provisions.** The following
> carve-out is included as a backstop:

Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, if and to the extent this Agreement constitutes or affects
a "construction contract" within Fla. Stat. § 725.06 or a design-professional services contract within § 725.08:
(a) the Releasors' total indemnification obligation for any portion of damages caused by the Indemnitees' own
fault is limited to $**[MONETARY LIMITATION — not less than $1,000,000 per occurrence for an owner indemnitee]**,
which limitation bears a reasonable commercial relationship to the contract and is part of the project
specifications/bid documents, in consideration of $**[SPECIFIC CONSIDERATION]**; and (b) where the Releasor is a
design professional, no indemnity shall extend beyond liability caused by the Releasor's own negligence,
recklessness, or intentionally wrongful conduct, and no broader obligation prohibited by § 725.08 shall be
enforceable. Any provision to the contrary is void to that extent and shall be reformed accordingly.

> **AUTHORITIES RELIED UPON (pin-cited):**
> - *Univ. Plaza Shopping Ctr., Inc. v. Stewart*, 272 So. 2d 507, 511 (Fla. 1973) — an indemnity contract will
>   not indemnify the indemnitee against its OWN negligence absent clear and unequivocal terms expressing that
>   intent. *(The District Court of Appeal opinion below is reported at Univ. Plaza Shopping Ctr. v. Stewart,
>   239 So. 2d 112 (Fla. 2d DCA 1970); the controlling FLORIDA SUPREME COURT decision is 272 So. 2d 507.)*
> - *Sanislo v. Give Kids the World, Inc.*, 157 So. 3d 256, 260–61 (Fla. 2015) — exculpatory clauses are
>   enforceable where the intent to relieve a party of its own negligence is clear and the wording unambiguous;
>   the magic word "negligence" is not strictly required, but explicit language is the safest course.
> - Fla. Stat. § 725.06 — construction contracts; indemnity for indemnitee's own fault void unless a monetary
>   limitation reasonably related to the contract (≥ $1M/occurrence for an owner) and specific consideration are
>   provided.
> - Fla. Stat. § 725.08 — design-professional contracts; indemnity limited to the design professional's own
>   negligence/recklessness/intentional conduct; broader (and public-agency) provisions void as against public
>   policy.
