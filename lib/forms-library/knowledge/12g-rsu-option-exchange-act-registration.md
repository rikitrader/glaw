# KNOWLEDGE WIKI — §12(g) EXCHANGE ACT REGISTRATION & THE OPTION/RSU EXEMPTIONS

> GLAW firm knowledge. Source: Hinshaw & Culbertson alert "SEC Exempts Restricted Stock Units From Exchange Act
> Registration" (Feb 2012) + statutory updates. Educational; confirm current rules with securities counsel. [VERIFY
> current thresholds.] Not legal advice.

## The problem it solves
A private company can accumulate so many option/RSU holders that it is forced to **register a class of equity under
the Securities Exchange Act of 1934** — triggering full public-company reporting — even though it has not gone public.

## The rule (§12(g) / Rule 12g-1)
- **§12(g)** requires a company to register a class of equity if, at fiscal year-end, it has **total assets >
  $10 million** AND that class is **held of record by ≥ the holder threshold**.
- **Holder threshold (current, post-JOBS Act 2012):** **2,000 holders of record, OR 500 holders who are NOT
  accredited investors.** (The Hinshaw alert's "500 persons" was the *pre-2012* threshold.)

## The exemptions / exclusions (how startups avoid it)
1. **Rule 12h-1(a)** — exempts interests in certain employee plans, but **NOT** stock options or RSUs by themselves.
2. **Rule 12h-1(f)** — exempts **compensatory employee stock OPTIONS** (and the holders of those options) from
   §12(g), subject to conditions (transfer restrictions; provision of risk/financial information). Adopted with
   **SEC Release No. 34-56887 (Dec. 7, 2007)**. Covers options, **not the shares** issued on exercise.
3. **RSUs** — were not covered by the 2007 option exemption; the SEC granted **no-action relief** to individual
   companies (e.g., **Facebook, Twitter, Zynga**) and took the position that compensatory RSUs are treated
   analogously. (This is the "SEC exempts RSUs" development the Hinshaw alert reports.)
4. **JOBS Act §12(g)(5) / Rule 12g5-1(a)(8) (the modern fix)** — **persons who received the securities under an
   employee compensation plan in an exempt transaction are EXCLUDED from the holder-of-record count.** This is the
   key reason a private startup granting options/RSUs to employees generally does **not** trip §12(g) today.

## Practical takeaway for a venture-backed C-corp (e.g., Example Holdings)
- Granting options/RSUs broadly to **employees** is generally safe from §12(g) registration because (a) Rule
  12h-1(f) exempts compensatory options and (b) the JOBS Act excludes employee-comp-plan recipients from the count.
- **Watch the count of NON-employee holders** (investors, ex-employees who exercised, secondary buyers) against the
  2,000 / 500-non-accredited thresholds + the $10M-assets test.
- Keep the equity plan + award agreements within the exemption conditions (transfer restrictions, information
  rights). Cross-ref: GLAW masters — Equity Incentive Plan, Stock Option Agreement, RSU Award Agreement.
