# Non-GAAP Measures, the PSLRA Safe Harbor & 10b5-1 / Insider-Policy Disclosure

This file covers three judgment areas: **non-GAAP** financial measures (Reg G / Item 10(e)), the
**forward-looking-statement safe harbor** (PSLRA), and the disclosure of **insider-trading policies
and Rule 10b5-1 trading plans**.

## Non-GAAP financial measures — Regulation G & S-K Item 10(e)

A **non-GAAP measure** is a numerical measure that adjusts the most directly comparable **GAAP**
measure (e.g., adjusted EBITDA, adjusted earnings, free cash flow). Two rules govern:

- **Regulation G** (17 C.F.R. Part 244) — applies to **any public disclosure** of a non-GAAP
  measure (press release, call, website): the measure must be **reconciled** to the most directly
  comparable GAAP measure, and the disclosure must **not be misleading**.
- **Reg S-K Item 10(e)** — applies to non-GAAP measures **in SEC filings**: it adds requirements,
  including that the comparable **GAAP measure be presented with equal or greater prominence**, the
  reconciliation be included, and management explain **why** the measure is useful. It prohibits
  certain adjustments (e.g., labeling a normal, recurring cash operating expense as "non-recurring").

**Tells (per SEC C&DIs on non-GAAP):** GAAP measure buried below or smaller than the non-GAAP
measure (prominence); a non-GAAP measure that excludes normal recurring costs; a "tailored"
accounting principle (e.g., recognizing revenue differently than GAAP); inconsistent period-to-
period adjustments that flatter the trend; per-share non-GAAP liquidity measures. The SEC's
non-GAAP **C&DIs** are the operative interpretive guidance — cite them and verify the current text.

## The PSLRA forward-looking-statement safe harbor

The **Private Securities Litigation Reform Act of 1995** added a statutory safe harbor
(Securities Act § 27A / Exchange Act § 21E) for **forward-looking statements** (projections,
plans, objectives, assumptions). A forward-looking statement is **not actionable** if **either**:

- It is **identified as forward-looking** and accompanied by **meaningful cautionary statements**
  identifying important factors that could cause actual results to differ materially; **or**
- The plaintiff fails to prove it was made with **actual knowledge** of falsity.

Limits this seat must respect:

- The safe harbor **excludes** certain contexts — e.g., statements in connection with an **IPO**,
  a **tender offer**, a **going-private** transaction, and by certain issuers (penny-stock, blank-
  check) — and does **not** protect statements of **present or historical fact**.
- The cautionary language must be **substantive and tailored**, not generic boilerplate, and must
  address the **risk that actually materialized** (the judicial **bespeaks-caution** doctrine
  aligns). A purely **historical or present** statement dressed up as forward-looking is **not**
  protected.

**Discipline:** before crediting the safe harbor, confirm the statement is genuinely
**forward-looking**, the cautionary language is **meaningful and tailored**, and the context is
**not excluded**.

## Insider-trading policies & Rule 10b5-1 plan disclosure

The SEC adopted rules requiring issuers to **disclose** their insider-trading policies and to
disclose **director/officer Rule 10b5-1 trading arrangements**:

- **Rule 10b5-1** provides an **affirmative defense** to insider-trading liability for trades made
  under a **pre-established written plan** adopted in good faith **while not aware of MNPI**. The SEC
  **amended** Rule 10b5-1 to add conditions — notably **cooling-off periods** before trading can
  begin, a **good-faith** operation requirement, **certifications** by directors/officers, and
  limits on **overlapping** plans and **single-trade** plans. Confirm the current conditions and
  cooling-off lengths on sec.gov.
- **Disclosure rules** (Reg S-K) require issuers to disclose whether they have **adopted insider-
  trading policies and procedures** (and to file the policy), and to disclose the **adoption,
  modification, or termination** of director/officer **10b5-1 (and certain non-10b5-1) trading
  arrangements**, with specified terms (in periodic reports). Equity-grant timing relative to MNPI
  releases is also addressed.

**Tells:** a 10b5-1 plan adopted **without** a cooling-off period or while the insider plausibly
held MNPI; well-timed trades clustered around disclosures; a missing or unfiled insider-trading
policy; undisclosed plan adoptions/modifications.

## How this seat applies it

1. **Non-GAAP:** test each measure for **reconciliation, equal-or-greater GAAP prominence, useful-
   purpose explanation, and prohibited adjustments** (Reg G / Item 10(e) / C&DIs).
2. **PSLRA:** before crediting the safe harbor, verify the statement is forward-looking, the
   cautionary language is **tailored** and addresses the realized risk, and the context is not
   excluded.
3. **10b5-1 / policy:** confirm the issuer's **policy disclosure/filing** and any director/officer
   **plan-adoption/modification** disclosures; flag plans missing the **cooling-off** and good-faith
   conditions; route trading patterns to `/glaw-sec` (insider-trading lens).
4. Grade and pin each finding; an unsupported read is a **lead, not a finding**.
