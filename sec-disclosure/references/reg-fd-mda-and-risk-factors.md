# Reg FD, MD&A (Item 303) & Risk Factors (Item 105)

Three of the most enforcement-active disclosure regimes: **Regulation FD** (no selective
disclosure), **MD&A** (the narrative that most often hides a known trend), and **risk factors** (the
hypothetical risk that had already materialized). This file states each standard and the tells this
seat looks for.

## Regulation FD — Fair Disclosure

**Regulation FD** (17 C.F.R. §§ 243.100–243.103) prohibits **selective disclosure** of material
non-public information (MNPI):

- When an issuer (or a person acting on its behalf) discloses MNPI to **securities-market
  professionals** (analysts, institutional investors) or to **holders likely to trade on it**, it
  must make **public disclosure** of that information — **simultaneously** for an *intentional*
  selective disclosure, or **promptly** for a *non-intentional* one (promptly being within 24 hours
  or before the next market open, whichever is later — confirm the current rule).
- **Public disclosure** is satisfied by a Form 8-K or another method **reasonably designed** to
  reach the public broadly (e.g., a widely accessible webcast/press release). The SEC has issued
  guidance on **social media** as a Reg FD channel (a company may use a social-media channel if it
  has alerted investors that it will).
- **Exclusions** — disclosures to persons owing a **duty of trust/confidence** (e.g., under an NDA),
  to temporary insiders (counsel, bankers, accountants), and in connection with most registered
  offerings.
- **Liability** — Reg FD is enforced by the **SEC** (it is **not** a basis for private 10b-5
  liability and a Reg FD violation alone is not fraud), but a selective disclosure that also
  involves **trading** implicates insider-trading law (route to `/glaw-sec`).

**Tells:** an analyst note or institutional briefing that contains numbers/guidance not yet public;
an earnings-call answer that goes beyond the released materials; a one-on-one meeting followed by
unusual trading.

## MD&A — Reg S-K Item 303

MD&A is the issuer's own narrative of its results, liquidity, and prospects — and the single most
common home for an **omitted known trend**. Item 303 requires discussion of:

- **Results of operations** — material changes and the **reasons** for them (price vs. volume,
  one-time vs. recurring).
- **Liquidity and capital resources** — sources/uses of cash, material commitments, and ability to
  meet obligations.
- **Known trends, demands, commitments, events, and uncertainties** that are **reasonably likely**
  to have a **material** effect on financial condition or results. The SEC's interpretive guidance
  (the 1989 and 2003 MD&A releases) sets a **two-part test**: (1) is the known trend/uncertainty
  **reasonably likely** to come to fruition? If management **cannot** conclude it is *not*
  reasonably likely, then (2) it must be disclosed unless management determines a **material effect**
  is **not reasonably likely**. The "known trend" omission is the recurring MD&A enforcement theory.
- **Critical accounting estimates** — the estimates requiring the most subjective judgment, and the
  sensitivity of reported results to them.

**Tells:** a deteriorating metric visible in the numbers but absent from the narrative; a known
customer loss, pricing pressure, covenant proximity, or liquidity squeeze that the MD&A omits;
results "explained" without the real driver.

## Risk Factors — Reg S-K Item 105

Item 105 requires a discussion of the **material factors** that make an investment speculative or
risky, organized under relevant headings (with a concise summary if the section exceeds the length
threshold). The disclosure defects this seat hunts:

- **Hypothetical-vs-realized.** A risk framed as something that **"could" or "may"** happen when it
  **has already happened** is misleading — the leading risk-factor enforcement theory (a "risk" that
  a known event will occur, disclosed as a mere possibility).
- **Boilerplate.** Generic risks that "apply to any company" with no tailoring to the issuer's
  actual exposure; risk factors that do not match the real risks visible elsewhere in the record.
- **Inconsistency.** Risk factors that contradict the optimism of the MD&A, the press release, or
  guidance — the mismatch is the finding.

## How this seat applies it

1. **Reg FD:** map every selective communication to market professionals/holders against
   contemporaneous public disclosure; flag MNPI shared without simultaneous/prompt public release;
   route any trading overlay to `/glaw-sec`.
2. **MD&A:** read the **numbers first**, then the narrative; flag any **known trend/uncertainty**
   the numbers reveal that Item 303's two-part test required and the narrative buried.
3. **Risk factors:** flag **realized-as-hypothetical** risks, boilerplate, and risk/MD&A
   inconsistency.
4. Pin each finding to the filing/item/line and grade it; **judgment** calls feed
   `/glaw-sec-enforcement`. Materiality is argued from the **total mix**.
