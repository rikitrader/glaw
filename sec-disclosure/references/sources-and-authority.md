# Sources & Authority Index — SEC Corporate Disclosure Seat

This KB rests on **primary authority**: the **Securities Act of 1933**, the **Securities Exchange
Act of 1934**, the SEC **regulations and rules** (Reg FD, Reg G, Reg S-K), and the controlling
**Supreme Court** materiality and disclosure cases. SEC **C&DIs** and **SABs** are persuasive staff
guidance, not law. Practice guides inform literacy but are never the operative rule. Every figure
defers to `tax-legal-shared/current-figures.md` or the current SEC rule.

## Case law (real holdings only)

- ***TSC Industries, Inc. v. Northway, Inc.***, 426 U.S. 438 (1976) — materiality = substantial
  likelihood a reasonable investor finds it important; the **total-mix** standard. | materiality-and-duty-to-disclose.md
- ***Basic Inc. v. Levinson***, 485 U.S. 224 (1988) — adopts *TSC* for Rule 10b-5; **probability ×
  magnitude** for contingent events; fraud-on-the-market presumption. | materiality-and-duty-to-disclose.md
- ***Matrixx Initiatives, Inc. v. Siracusano***, 563 U.S. 27 (2011) — no bright-line statistical-
  significance test for materiality. | materiality-and-duty-to-disclose.md
- ***Omnicare, Inc. v. Laborers Dist. Council***, 575 U.S. 175 (2015) — when a **statement of
  opinion** is misleading; the half-truth/omission principle for opinions. | materiality-and-duty-to-disclose.md
- ***Janus Capital Group, Inc. v. First Derivative Traders***, 564 U.S. 135 (2011) — who "makes" a
  statement for Rule 10b-5 (the "maker" = the person with ultimate authority). | (attribution)
- ***Lorenzo v. SEC***, 587 U.S. 71 (2019) — scheme liability can reach dissemination of false
  statements one did not "make." | (scheme liability)

> Cite a case only for what it actually holds; verify the specific holding in play.
