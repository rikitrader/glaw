# OFAC Sanctions — Regulatory-Change Slice (2025–2026)

The sanctions / FATF / evasion slice of the FinCEN Updates corpus. Full ledger:
**`../fincen/references/regulatory-updates-2025-2026.md`**; FATF/sanctions interplay:
`../fincen/references/fatf-international.md`. Verify all list status on FinCEN.gov / OFAC.

> Scope note: OFAC sanctions are administered by Treasury's OFAC, not FinCEN — but the FinCEN
> Updates feed carries the alerts/advisories where the **BSA and sanctions worlds intersect**.
> This seat reads those. OFAC list status itself must be confirmed on OFAC's SDN/Consolidated
> lists (sanctions.ofac.treas.gov).

## Sanctions-nexus alerts
- **2026-05-11 — Alert to Stop ML by Iran's IRGC** `Alert`. Red flags for **IRGC** laundering
  networks. The IRGC is OFAC-designated, so an IRGC nexus is **both** a **SAR** trigger and an
  **OFAC blocking** issue. Read with OFAC's Iran/IRGC programs.
  - *Impact:* screen for IRGC-linked typologies, proxies, and front companies; an apparent hit
    is a **potential hit** until identity is confirmed.

## FATF — drives enhanced due diligence / countermeasures
- **2025-11-21 — FATF Identifies AML/CFT/CPF-Deficient Jurisdictions** `Advisory`. Black list
  (call for action: **Iran, DPRK**) + grey list (increased monitoring, rotating).
  - *Impact:* apply **EDD** to listed-jurisdiction exposure and **countermeasures** for
    call-for-action jurisdictions; FATF call-for-action countries overlap heavily with OFAC
    comprehensive programs. Refresh the country-risk matrix against the **current** plenary list.

## Primary-money-laundering-concern / special measures
- **2025-11-13 — Sinaloa Cartel: 10 Mexico-based gambling establishments = primary ML
  concern** `Notice` (31 U.S.C. 5318A). Treat named entities as high-risk/avoid; possible
  special-measures restrictions. Cartel exposure frequently carries OFAC designations too.

## Cross-border sharing
- **2025-09-05 — Cross-border information-sharing guidance** `Advisory` — relevant to screening
  a multinational counterparty set within BSA confidentiality limits.

## Analyst takeaways
1. **50% rule:** an unlisted entity is **blocked** if blocked persons own ≥ 50% in aggregate —
   build the ownership chain, don't just name-screen.
2. **Fuzzy match = potential hit**, not a finding, until identity is resolved.
3. **Screening is advisory only** — OFAC licensing and voluntary self-disclosure are
   **counsel's call**; route doctrine to `/glaw-regulatory-aml`.
