# Administrative Law Record Review Checklist

Use this checklist for agency rules, guidance, adjudications, licensing,
enforcement actions, hearings, and judicial review of agency conduct.

## Action Classification

Classify the agency action before analysis:

- Legislative rule.
- Interpretive rule.
- Policy statement or guidance.
- Adjudication or order.
- Licensing or permit decision.
- Enforcement action.
- Inaction or delayed action.
- Informal letter, FAQ, manual, or other nonbinding communication.

## Authority and Procedure

| Gate | Required Workpaper |
|---|---|
| Agency authority | Enabling statute, delegation, limits, and `SRC-####` source |
| Action type | Rule/adjudication/guidance/enforcement classification |
| Procedure | Notice, comment, hearing, record, service, or publication path |
| Finality | Whether the action is final or reviewable |
| Exhaustion | Required administrative steps and deadlines |
| Record | Index of materials considered by the agency |
| Reasoned explanation | Problem, evidence, alternatives, response to material comments |
| Reliance interests | Who relied on prior policy and how the agency addressed it |
| Retroactivity | Whether the action reaches past conduct |
| Remedy | Vacatur, remand, injunction, stay, reopening, or petition path |

## Arbitrary-and-Capricious Workpaper

Ask:

1. Did the agency identify the problem?
2. Did it consider relevant factors?
3. Did it ignore important aspects of the problem?
4. Did it explain departures from prior policy?
5. Did it address serious reliance interests?
6. Did it respond to material comments?
7. Did the record support the factual predicates?
8. Did it choose a remedy within authority?

## Loper Bright / Statutory Interpretation Discipline

Do not assume judicial deference. Build the statutory analysis directly:

- Text.
- Structure.
- Definitions.
- Context.
- Delegation language.
- Limits and exceptions.
- Related provisions.
- Remedy provisions.
- Rule of lenity or constitutional avoidance if implicated.

## Sign-Off Condition

Admin-law output is not ready unless it has an action classification, authority
source, procedure map, record index, finality/exhaustion analysis, remedy path,
and adversarial attacks from agency counsel, regulated-party counsel, public
interest challenger, hearing officer, and reviewing court.
