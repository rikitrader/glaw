# Bench Memo Standards Guide

Use this guide when `glaw-judicial` prepares bench memos, model opinions,
standards-of-review maps, findings, conclusions, or adjudication simulations.

## Neutrality Rules

- Treat every disputed fact as disputed unless a record source resolves it.
- Separate record facts, procedural facts, party arguments, law, and analysis.
- Do not convert a party assertion into a finding.
- Do not state a model disposition as a binding order.
- Identify the court, tribunal, or decision maker that would have authority.

## Record Lock

Before analysis, create a record table:

| Item | Source | Offered For | Disputed? | Admissibility/Use Issue |
|---|---|---|---|---|
|  |  |  |  |  |

If a fact is important and unsupported, mark it `record gap` and route it back to
the owning matter seat. Every fact used in the model must cite a `SRC-####`
record source or remain disputed.

## Standards of Review

| Posture | Standard Workpaper |
|---|---|
| Motion to dismiss | Pleading standard, elements, attached/incorporated records |
| Summary judgment | Material facts, burden, admissible evidence, reasonable inferences |
| Bench trial/findings | Burden of proof, credibility limits, Rule or local equivalent |
| Agency review | Record scope, finality, exhaustion, statutory standard |
| Appeal | Preservation, standard, harmless error, remedy |
| Injunction/stay | Merits, irreparable harm, equities, public interest, bond |

## Analysis Blocks

Every memo must include:

1. Question presented.
2. Short answer.
3. Procedural posture.
4. Jurisdiction and authority.
5. Standard of review and burden.
6. Record facts with sources.
7. Party arguments.
8. Governing law with citation-gate status.
9. Application.
10. Disposition options and remedy.
11. Appealability or review path.
12. Red flags and human sign-off condition.

## Adversarial Attacks

- Appellant: preservation, standard, unsupported fact, wrong remedy.
- Appellee: harmless error, alternate ground, waiver.
- Trial judge: record management and factual support.
- Appellate panel: scope of review and institutional limits.
- Public-records reviewer: transparency and nonbinding model labeling.
- Ethics counsel: unauthorized adjudication or misleading order language.

## Sign-Off Condition

The branch may sign off only when the model output labels itself as nonbinding,
states the decision maker, ties facts to record sources, ties law to verified
authority, and identifies unresolved record gaps.
