## Filing Checklist

### With the District Court Clerk
- [ ] Notice of Appeal (this document)
- [ ] Filing fee ($605 as of 2025) or IFP motion
- [ ] Representation statement (if counsel has changed)

### Deadline Calculations (FRAP 4)

| Scenario | Deadline | Rule |
|----------|----------|------|
| Standard civil appeal | 30 days after judgment | FRAP 4(a)(1)(A) |
| U.S. is a party | 60 days after judgment | FRAP 4(a)(1)(B) |
| After post-judgment motion (50(b), 52(b), 59, 60) | 30 days after order on motion | FRAP 4(a)(4) |
| Cross-appeal | 14 days after first notice of appeal | FRAP 4(a)(3) |

### Tolling Motions (FRAP 4(a)(4))
The appeal deadline is tolled (restarted) by a timely:
- Rule 50(b) — Renewed JMOL
- Rule 52(b) — Amended findings
- Rule 59 — New trial or alter/amend judgment
- Rule 60 — Relief from judgment (if filed within 28 days)

### Jurisdictional — Cannot Be Extended
- The 30/60-day deadline is **jurisdictional** — *Bowles v. Russell*, 551 U.S. 205 (2007)
- District court may grant 30-day extension for "excusable neglect" (FRAP 4(a)(5)) if motion filed within 30 days after deadline
- No extension beyond that — appeal is lost

### Interlocutory Appeals (28 U.S.C. § 1292)
| Type | Basis | Procedure |
|------|-------|-----------|
| Injunction orders | § 1292(a)(1) | File notice of appeal |
| Certified question | § 1292(b) | District court certifies, then petition 11th Circuit within 10 days |
| Collateral order | Cohen doctrine | File notice of appeal — argue jurisdiction on appeal |
| Mandamus | 28 U.S.C. § 1651 | Petition filed directly in 11th Circuit |
