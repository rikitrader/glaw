# Federal Rules of Evidence (FRE) - Quick Reference

## Article I - General Provisions

### Rule 103 - Rulings on Evidence
- Preserve error by timely objection or offer of proof
- Plain error: court may take notice even without objection

### Rule 104 - Preliminary Questions
**(a)** Judge decides preliminary questions of admissibility
**(b)** Relevance conditioned on fact: jury decides if sufficient evidence

### Rule 106 - Remainder of Writings (Rule of Completeness)
- If party introduces part of writing, adverse party may require introduction of any other part that ought in fairness to be considered at the same time

## Article II - Judicial Notice (Rule 201)
- Court may judicially notice facts not subject to reasonable dispute
- Must be generally known or capable of accurate determination from reliable sources

## Article III - Presumptions (Rule 301)
- In civil cases, presumption imposes burden of producing evidence to rebut
- Does not shift burden of persuasion

## Article IV - Relevance

### Rule 401 - Test for Relevant Evidence
Evidence is relevant if: (a) it has any tendency to make a fact more or less probable; AND (b) the fact is of consequence in determining the action

### Rule 402 - General Admissibility
Relevant evidence is admissible unless excluded by Constitution, statute, or rules. Irrelevant evidence is not admissible.

### Rule 403 - Excluding Relevant Evidence
Court MAY exclude if probative value is **substantially outweighed** by:
- Unfair prejudice, confusing the issues, misleading the jury
- Undue delay, wasting time, needlessly presenting cumulative evidence

### Rule 404 - Character Evidence
**(a)** Character evidence NOT admissible to prove conforming conduct EXCEPT:
- (a)(2)(A) Defendant in criminal case may offer pertinent trait
- (a)(2)(B) Victim's character in certain criminal cases

**(b)** Crimes, Wrongs, or Other Acts:
- NOT admissible to prove character/conforming conduct
- MAY be admissible for: Motive, Opportunity, Intent, Preparation, Plan, Knowledge, Identity, Absence of mistake, Lack of accident (**MIMIC**)

### Rule 406 - Habit/Routine Practice
Evidence of habit or routine practice admissible to prove conforming conduct

### Rule 407 - Subsequent Remedial Measures
NOT admissible to prove negligence, culpable conduct, product defect, or need for warning
MAY be admissible for: ownership, control, feasibility (if controverted), impeachment

### Rule 408 - Compromise Offers and Negotiations
NOT admissible to prove/disprove validity or amount of disputed claim
EXCEPTION: Proving bias/prejudice, negating undue delay, proving obstruction

### Rule 409 - Offers to Pay Medical Expenses
NOT admissible to prove liability (but accompanying factual admissions ARE admissible)

### Rule 410 - Plea Bargain Statements
Withdrawn guilty pleas, nolo pleas, and related statements NOT admissible

### Rule 411 - Liability Insurance
NOT admissible to prove negligence/wrongful action
MAY be admissible for: agency, ownership, control, bias/prejudice

## Article V - Privileges (Rule 501)
- Federal common law governs privilege in federal question cases
- State privilege law governs in diversity cases
- Common privileges: Attorney-client, Work product, Spousal, Physician-patient

## Article VI - Witnesses

### Rule 601 - Competency
Every person is competent to be a witness (unless rules provide otherwise)

### Rule 602 - Need for Personal Knowledge
Witness must have personal knowledge of the matter

### Rule 607 - Who May Impeach
Any party, including the party who called the witness

### Rule 608 - Character for Truthfulness
**(a)** Opinion/reputation evidence of truthfulness (only after character attacked)
**(b)** Specific instances of conduct on cross-examination only

### Rule 609 - Impeachment by Criminal Conviction
**(a)(1)** Crimes punishable by >1 year: admissible subject to 403 balancing (civil); defendant in criminal: probative must outweigh prejudice
**(a)(2)** Crimes involving dishonesty: automatically admissible regardless of punishment
**(b)** Time limit: 10 years (with exceptions for probative value)

### Rule 611 - Mode and Order of Examining Witnesses
**(a)** Court controls mode/order
**(b)** Cross-examination limited to scope of direct + credibility
**(c)** Leading questions: generally not on direct; allowed on cross

### Rule 613 - Prior Statements of Witnesses
Must give witness opportunity to explain or deny (need not be before extrinsic evidence)

## Article VII - Opinions and Expert Testimony

### Rule 701 - Lay Opinion
Admissible if: (a) rationally based on perception; (b) helpful to understanding; (c) not based on scientific/technical knowledge within Rule 702

### Rule 702 - Expert Testimony (Daubert Standard)
Expert may testify if:
1. Sufficient facts or data
2. Reliable principles and methods
3. Reliably applied principles to facts of case
4. Testimony will help trier of fact

**Daubert Factors:**
- Testing, peer review, error rate, general acceptance, standards
- 11th Circuit: applies Daubert rigorously

### Rule 703 - Bases of Expert Opinion
May rely on facts/data not admissible if experts in field would reasonably rely on such data

### Rule 705 - Disclosing Facts/Data
Expert may state opinion without first disclosing underlying facts (but subject to cross)

## Article VIII - Hearsay

### Rule 801 - Definitions
**(a)** Statement: oral/written assertion or nonverbal conduct intended as assertion
**(c)** Hearsay: out-of-court statement offered to prove truth of matter asserted

**(d) NOT Hearsay (Exclusions):**
1. **(d)(1)(A)** Prior inconsistent statement under oath at proceeding
2. **(d)(1)(B)** Prior consistent statement to rebut charge of fabrication
3. **(d)(1)(C)** Statement of identification
4. **(d)(2)** Opposing party's statement (admission): by party, adopted, authorized, agent/employee, co-conspirator

### Rule 802 - The Rule Against Hearsay
Hearsay is not admissible unless exception applies

### Rule 803 - Exceptions (Availability Immaterial)
1. Present sense impression
2. Excited utterance
3. Then-existing mental/emotional/physical condition
4. Medical diagnosis/treatment
5. Recorded recollection
6. Records of regularly conducted activity (business records)
7. Absence of business record entry
8. Public records
9-23. [Various additional exceptions]

### Rule 804 - Exceptions (Declarant Unavailable)
**(a)** Unavailable: privilege, refusal, lack of memory, death/illness, absence
**(b) Exceptions:**
1. Former testimony
2. Dying declaration
3. Statement against interest
4. Statement of personal/family history
5. [Forfeiture by wrongdoing]

### Rule 805 - Hearsay Within Hearsay
Admissible if each part falls within exception

### Rule 807 - Residual Exception
Statement not covered by 803/804 if: sufficient guarantees of trustworthiness, offered as evidence of material fact, more probative than other evidence, interests of justice served, notice given

## Article IX - Authentication

### Rule 901 - Authenticating Evidence
**(a)** Must produce evidence sufficient to support finding that item is what proponent claims
**(b) Examples:** Testimony, nonexpert handwriting, comparison by expert, distinctive characteristics, voice identification, phone conversations, public records, process/system, methods provided by statute/rule

### Rule 902 - Self-Authenticating Evidence
Domestic public documents under seal, certified copies, official publications, newspapers, trade inscriptions, acknowledged documents, commercial paper, certified domestic/foreign records, certified electronic records

## Article X - Contents of Writings (Best Evidence Rule)

### Rule 1002 - Requirement of Original
To prove content of writing/recording/photograph: original required

### Rule 1003 - Admissibility of Duplicates
Duplicate admissible unless genuine question of authenticity or unfair to admit

### Rule 1004 - Admissibility of Other Evidence of Content
Original not required if: lost/destroyed (not in bad faith), unobtainable, in possession of opponent, collateral matter
