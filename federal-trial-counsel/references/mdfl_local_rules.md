# U.S. District Court, Middle District of Florida - Local Rules Quick Reference

## Filing and Service

### Local Rule 1.01 - Scope
These rules supplement the Federal Rules of Civil Procedure and apply to all civil actions.

### Local Rule 1.02 - Divisions
- Tampa Division
- Jacksonville Division
- Orlando Division
- Fort Myers Division
- Ocala Division

### Local Rule 1.05 - Filing
- CM/ECF mandatory for attorneys
- Pro se may file conventionally with Clerk's permission
- All documents must comply with CM/ECF Administrative Procedures

### Local Rule 1.06 - Signatures
- Electronic signatures: /s/ [attorney name]
- Attorney must maintain an original signed copy for 1 year

## Pleadings

### Local Rule 3.01 - Motions
**(a) Generally:** All motions must include a memorandum of law
**(b) Response:** Due **21 days** after service of motion
**(c) Reply:** Due **7 days** after service of response
**(g) Meet and Confer:** Required before filing discovery motions. Certification of good faith conference MANDATORY.

**Word/Page Limits:**
- Motions: 25 pages
- Responses: 25 pages
- Replies: 10 pages
- (Court leave required for excess)

### Local Rule 3.02 - Requests for Oral Argument
- Not automatic - must request in motion or response
- Court decides whether to grant

### Local Rule 3.03 - Emergency Motions
- Must be truly emergent
- File separately with "EMERGENCY" in title
- Must explain why emergency exists

### Local Rule 3.04 - Motions to Compel
- Must comply with meet and confer (3.01(g))
- Must include certification of conference
- Must describe specific discovery requested and basis for objection

### Local Rule 3.05 - Discovery Motions
- Motions regarding discovery must comply with 3.01(g)
- Must append relevant discovery requests and responses

## Case Management

### Local Rule 3.05 - Case Management and Scheduling Order (CMSO)
- Entered after Rule 26(f) conference
- Typically includes:
  - Discovery cutoff
  - Expert designation deadline
  - Dispositive motions deadline
  - Mediation deadline
  - Trial period

### Local Rule 3.06 - Pretrial Statement
- Filed at least **5 days** before final pretrial conference
- Must include: stipulated facts, contested facts, witness lists, exhibit lists, legal issues, estimated trial time

### Local Rule 3.07 - Continuances
- Disfavored; must show good cause
- Agreement of parties alone insufficient
- Must show diligence in preparation

## Discovery

### Local Rule 3.01(g) - Good Faith Conference
**MANDATORY before filing any discovery motion:**
- Must confer in person or by telephone (email/letter insufficient)
- Must make good faith effort to resolve
- Certification must identify: date, time, duration of conference; persons participating; issues resolved and unresolved

### Local Rule 3.04 - Discovery Limitations
- Initial disclosures: per FRCP 26
- Interrogatories: 25 (per FRCP)
- Depositions: 10 per side (per FRCP)
- RFPs: no local limit (reasonableness applies)

### Expert Disclosures (per CMSO)
- Plaintiff's experts: per CMSO deadline
- Defendant's experts: per CMSO deadline (typically 30 days after plaintiff)
- Rebuttal experts: per CMSO deadline (typically 30 days after defendant)

## Motions Practice

### Motion to Dismiss (12(b)(6)) Timeline
1. Motion filed (with memorandum)
2. Response due: 21 days
3. Reply due: 7 days after response
4. Typical disposition: 30-90 days after fully briefed

### Summary Judgment Timeline
1. Motion + Statement of Undisputed Material Facts
2. Response + Counter-Statement of Facts: 21 days
3. Reply: 7 days
4. Typical disposition: 60-120 days after fully briefed

### Local Rule 56.1 - Statement of Material Facts
- Moving party: file separate statement of undisputed material facts
- Each fact numbered, with specific citation to record
- Opposing party: respond to each numbered fact (admit/deny with citation)
- Failure to controvert = deemed admitted for summary judgment purposes

## Trial

### Local Rule 5.01 - Trial Scheduling
- Trial set by CMSO or separate order
- Trial ready date: per CMSO
- Parties must be ready on date set

### Local Rule 5.02 - Trial Briefs
- Due per court's pretrial order (typically 7-14 days before trial)
- Should address key legal issues, motions in limine, voir dire

### Local Rule 5.03 - Jury Instructions
- Proposed instructions due per court's order
- Must cite controlling authority
- Must identify objections to opposing party's proposed instructions

### Local Rule 5.04 - Exhibit Lists
- Exchange exhibit lists per pretrial order
- Include: exhibit number, description, offering party, objections

## Mediation

### Local Rule 9.01 - Mediation Required
- Mediation required in most civil cases
- Must be completed by CMSO deadline
- Parties with settlement authority must attend

### Local Rule 9.02 - Mediation Procedures
- Mediator selected from court's certified list or by agreement
- Each party files mediation position paper (confidential to mediator)
- Good faith participation required

### Local Rule 9.04 - Mediation Report
- Mediator files report (impasse or settled) within 7 days
- No substantive content disclosed

## Pro Se Litigants

### Local Rule 1.05(b) - Pro Se Filing
- May file conventionally
- Must provide current address and phone number
- Failure to maintain current address may result in dismissal

### Local Rule 4.02 - Special Requirements
- Pro se complaint screened under 28 U.S.C. 1915 if IFP
- Liberal construction of pro se pleadings (Haines v. Kerner)

## Magistrate Judge Practice

### Local Rule 6.01 - Consent
- Parties may consent to magistrate judge for all proceedings
- Must be voluntary; no adverse consequences for declining

### Local Rule 6.02 - Referrals
- Dispositive motions: Report and Recommendation
- Non-dispositive motions: decided directly
- Objections to R&R: **14 days** (de novo review by district judge)

## Key M.D. Fla. Judicial Preferences

**General Practices:**
- Judges maintain individual practice standards on their webpages
- Check individual judge's page for: page limits, formatting, scheduling
- CM/ECF Technical Failures: must notify clerk promptly
- Sealed documents: must comply with Local Rule 1.09 (limited to trade secrets, etc.)
