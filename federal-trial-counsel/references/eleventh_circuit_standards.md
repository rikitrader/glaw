# Eleventh Circuit Court of Appeals - Standards of Review

## Standards of Review Summary

| Standard | Deference | Applies To |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| De novo | None | Questions of law, constitutional issues, statutory interpretation |
| Clearly erroneous | High | Findings of fact (bench trial) |
| Abuse of discretion | High | Procedural rulings, evidentiary rulings, sanctions |
| Plain error | Very high | Unpreserved errors |
| Substantial evidence | High | Administrative agency fact-finding |

---

## De Novo Review

**Definition:** The appellate court reviews the issue fresh, giving no deference to the trial court's conclusion.

**Applied to:**
- Motions to dismiss under Rule 12(b)(6) - *Chaparro v. Carnival Corp., 693 F.3d 1333 (11th Cir. 2012)*
- Summary judgment under Rule 56 - *Tolan v. Cotton, 572 U.S. 650 (2014)*
- Questions of law - *Salomon v. Adderley Indus., 960 F.2d 1095 (11th Cir. 1992)*
- Statutory interpretation - *Auer v. Robbins, 519 U.S. 452 (1997)*
- Constitutional questions - *Crawford v. Washington, 541 U.S. 36 (2004)*
- Qualified immunity (legal question) - *Saucier v. Katz, 533 U.S. 194 (2001)*
- Subject matter jurisdiction - *Underwriters at Lloyd's v. Osting-Schwinn, 613 F.3d 1079 (11th Cir. 2010)*
- Jury instructions (legal correctness) - *Palmer v. Bd. of Regents, 208 F.3d 969 (11th Cir. 2000)*
- Sufficiency of evidence (JMOL) - *Reeves v. Sanderson Plumbing, 530 U.S. 133 (2000)*

**Key 11th Circuit Notes:**
- On 12(b)(6): Accept all well-pleaded facts as true, draw all inferences in plaintiff's favor
- On summary judgment: View evidence in light most favorable to nonmoving party
- On QI: "Clearly established" requires precedent with "sufficiently similar facts" (*Mercado v. City of Orlando, 407 F.3d 1152 (11th Cir. 2005)*)

## Clearly Erroneous

**Definition:** Finding of fact is "clearly erroneous" when, although there is evidence to support it, the reviewing court is left with the definite and firm conviction that a mistake has been committed. *Anderson v. City of Bessemer City, 470 U.S. 564 (1985)*

**Applied to:**
- Findings of fact after bench trial - *In re Optical Technologies, 246 F.3d 1332 (11th Cir. 2001)*
- Factual findings underlying preliminary injunctions
- Mixed questions of law and fact (factual component)
- Class certification factual findings

**Key 11th Circuit Notes:**
- "Where there are two permissible views of the evidence, the factfinder's choice between them cannot be clearly erroneous"
- Credibility determinations receive special deference
- Must identify specific findings challenged

---

## Abuse of Discretion

**Definition:** Discretionary ruling reversed only if the court made a clear error of judgment, or applied the wrong legal standard, or based ruling on clearly erroneous factual findings.

**Applied to:**
- Evidentiary rulings (admission/exclusion) - *Frazier v. Aetna Life Ins. Co., 545 F.3d 1282 (11th Cir. 2008)*
- Discovery rulings - *Josendis v. Wall to Wall Residence Repairs, 662 F.3d 1292 (11th Cir. 2011)*
- Sanctions (Rule 11, inherent power) - *Peer v. Lewis, 606 F.3d 1306 (11th Cir. 2010)*
- Class certification decisions - *Klay v. Humana, 382 F.3d 1241 (11th Cir. 2004)*
- Injunctive relief - *Palmer v. Braun, 376 F.3d 1254 (11th Cir. 2004)*
- Amendment of pleadings - *Foman v. Davis, 371 U.S. 178 (1962)*
- Attorneys' fees awards - *Norman v. Housing Auth., 836 F.2d 1292 (11th Cir. 1988)*
- Recusal decisions - *United States v. Patti, 337 F.3d 1317 (11th Cir. 2003)*
- Daubert/expert testimony rulings - *Rink v. Cheminova, 400 F.3d 1286 (11th Cir. 2005)*
- New trial motions - *Lipphardt v. Durango Steakhouse, 267 F.3d 1183 (11th Cir. 2001)*

**Key 11th Circuit Notes:**
- Harmless error analysis applies to evidentiary rulings (must show ruling affected substantial rights)
- Discovery sanctions: must consider lesser sanctions before case-dispositive sanctions
- Fee awards: reviewed for mathematical error or application of wrong legal standard (*Loranger v. Stierheim, 10 F.3d 776 (11th Cir. 1994)*)

---

## Plain Error

**Definition:** Applied when objection not preserved below. Must show: (1) error; (2) that is plain; (3) affecting substantial rights; (4) seriously affecting fairness, integrity, or public reputation of judicial proceedings. *United States v. Olano, 507 U.S. 725 (1993)*

**Applied to:**
- Unpreserved jury instruction errors
- Unpreserved evidentiary errors
- Unpreserved constitutional violations (in some circumstances)

**Key 11th Circuit Notes:**
- "Plain" means clear or obvious under current law
- Burden on appellant to show all four prongs
- Applied sparingly; strong showing required

---

## Substantial Evidence

**Definition:** "Such relevant evidence as a reasonable mind might accept as adequate to support a conclusion." *Richardson v. Perales, 402 U.S. 389 (1971)*

**Applied to:**
- Social Security disability determinations
- NLRB findings
- APA agency fact-finding under 5 U.S.C. 706(2)(E)
- Other administrative agency findings

## Special Standards in the 11th Circuit

### Qualified Immunity
- Two-pronged analysis: (1) Was constitutional right violated? (2) Was right clearly established?
- "Clearly established" in 11th Circuit: requires binding precedent from Supreme Court, 11th Circuit, or highest state court with materially similar facts
- Court may address prongs in either order (*Pearson v. Callahan, 555 U.S. 223 (2009)*)
- Factual disputes: deny QI on summary judgment if plaintiff's version of facts shows violation of clearly established right

### Section 1983 - Eleventh Circuit Specifics
- Municipal liability (Monell): single incident may suffice only if final policymaker authorized it
- Deliberate indifference: requires actual knowledge of risk and failure to act
- Supervisory liability: requires personal participation, direction, or actual knowledge and acquiescence

### Title VII - Eleventh Circuit Framework
- McDonnell Douglas burden-shifting:
  1. Prima facie case (plaintiff)
  2. Legitimate nondiscriminatory reason (defendant)
  3. Pretext (plaintiff)
- "Convincing mosaic" of circumstantial evidence may defeat summary judgment (*Smith v. Lockheed-Martin Corp., 644 F.3d 1321 (11th Cir. 2011)*)
- Comparators must be "similarly situated in all material respects" (*Lewis v. City of Union City, 918 F.3d 1213 (11th Cir. 2019) (en banc)*)

### Excessive Force - Graham v. Connor Factors
11th Circuit applies:
1. Severity of the crime at issue
2. Whether the suspect poses an immediate threat to safety of officers or others
3. Whether the suspect is actively resisting or attempting to flee
- "Judged from the perspective of a reasonable officer on the scene"
- 11th Circuit allows consideration of events leading up to use of force

### Preliminary Injunction
Four-factor test reviewed for abuse of discretion:
1. Substantial likelihood of success on the merits
2. Irreparable injury absent injunction
3. Threatened injury outweighs harm to opposing party
4. Injunction would not disserve public interest

### Damages
- Compensatory damages: reviewed for sufficiency of evidence
- Punitive damages: reviewed for excessiveness under *BMW v. Gore* guideposts
- Remittitur: reviewed for abuse of discretion
- Additur: not permitted in jury cases (7th Amendment)

## Preservation Requirements

| Issue | Preservation Method | Failure Consequence |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Evidentiary | Timely objection + specific ground | Plain error review |
| Jury instruction | Request + objection after charge | Plain error review |
| Sufficiency of evidence | Rule 50(a) motion at close | Cannot renew under 50(b) |
| Misconduct | Timely objection + motion for mistrial | Waived |
| Sentencing | Objection at sentencing | Plain error review |

## Key 11th Circuit Procedural Rules

### FRAP (Federal Rules of Appellate Procedure) + 11th Cir. Rules

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| FRAP 4(a)(1) | Notice of appeal: 30 days from judgment |
| FRAP 4(a)(5) | Extension: 30 days for excusable neglect |
| FRAP 28(a) | Brief format requirements |
| 11th Cir. R. 28-1 | Certificate of interested persons required |
| 11th Cir. R. 31-1 | Brief due dates: appellant 40 days, appellee 30 days, reply 21 days |
| 11th Cir. R. 35-1 | Rehearing en banc: 14 days from panel opinion |
| 11th Cir. IOP | Oral argument: court's discretion; most cases decided on briefs |

### Brief Length Limits (11th Circuit)
- Appellant's opening brief: 13,000 words
- Appellee's response: 13,000 words
- Reply brief: 6,500 words
- Cross-appeal response/reply: 13,000/6,500 words

### Appendix Requirements
- Jointly compiled appendix preferred
- Must include: judgment/order appealed, relevant docket entries, relevant portions of record
