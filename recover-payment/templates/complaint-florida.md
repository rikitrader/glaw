# COMPLAINT

Plaintiff, [PLAINTIFF], sues Defendant(s), [DEFENDANT], and alleges:

## GENERAL ALLEGATIONS

1. This is an action for damages [that exceeds $[AMOUNT], exclusive of interest,
costs, and attorney's fees] [and to foreclose a construction lien on real property].

2. Plaintiff is [a Florida resident / a [STATE] [entity]] [doing business in
[COUNTY] County]. Defendant is [a Florida resident / a [STATE] [entity]] subject to
the jurisdiction of this Court.

3. Venue is proper in [COUNTY] County because [the cause of action accrued here / the
contract was to be performed here / the property is located here / Defendant
resides here].

4. All conditions precedent to this action have occurred, have been performed, or
have been waived.

5. [Narrative of the deal: what was agreed, what Plaintiff did, what Defendant
accepted/benefited from, what was invoiced, what remains unpaid — $[PRINCIPAL] plus
interest.]

## COUNT I — BREACH OF CONTRACT
*(plead where a contract exists)*

6. Plaintiff realleges paragraphs 1–5.

7. Plaintiff and Defendant entered into [a written contract, a copy of which is
attached as Exhibit "A" / an oral agreement] under which Defendant agreed to pay
Plaintiff $[AMOUNT] for [the work/goods].

8. Plaintiff performed all of its obligations under the contract.

9. Defendant breached the contract by failing to pay the amount due.

10. Plaintiff has been damaged in the amount of $[PRINCIPAL], plus prejudgment
interest.

**WHEREFORE** Plaintiff demands judgment against Defendant for damages, prejudgment
interest, court costs, [attorney's fees pursuant to [the contract / § 57.105 /
§ 713.29]], and such further relief as the Court deems just.

## COUNT II — QUANTUM MERUIT *(pleaded in the alternative to Count I)*

11. Plaintiff realleges paragraphs 1–5.

12. This count is pleaded in the alternative to Count I, in the event the Court
determines no enforceable express contract governs the parties' dealings.

13. Plaintiff provided [services/labor/materials] to Defendant.

14. Defendant knew of and accepted, used, or enjoyed the benefit of those services
under circumstances in which both parties reasonably understood Plaintiff expected to
be paid.

15. The reasonable value of the services is $[AMOUNT], which Defendant has failed to
pay, and it would be inequitable for Defendant to retain the benefit without paying.

**WHEREFORE** Plaintiff demands judgment for the reasonable value of its services,
prejudgment interest, costs, and such further relief as the Court deems just.

## COUNT III — UNJUST ENRICHMENT *(pleaded in the alternative)*

16. Plaintiff realleges paragraphs 1–5.

17. This count is pleaded in the alternative to Count I.

18. Plaintiff conferred a benefit on Defendant, namely [the work/goods/funds].

19. Defendant had knowledge of the benefit and voluntarily accepted and retained it.

20. It would be inequitable for Defendant to retain the benefit without paying its
value of $[AMOUNT].

**WHEREFORE** Plaintiff demands judgment for the value of the benefit conferred,
prejudgment interest, costs, and such further relief as the Court deems just.

## COUNT IV — ACCOUNT STATED *(plead where invoices/statements were rendered)*

21. Plaintiff realleges paragraphs 1–5.

22. Before this action Plaintiff and Defendant had business transactions and agreed
on the resulting balance of $[AMOUNT].

23. Plaintiff rendered a statement of the account to Defendant, copies of which are
attached as Exhibit "[__]", and Defendant did not object [within a reasonable time],
thereby assenting to its correctness.

24. Defendant has failed to pay the balance.

**WHEREFORE** Plaintiff demands judgment for $[AMOUNT], prejudgment interest, costs,
and such further relief as the Court deems just.

## COUNT V — FORECLOSURE OF CONSTRUCTION LIEN *(construction matters only — Ch. 713)*

25. Plaintiff realleges paragraphs 1–5.

26. Plaintiff [served a Notice to Owner on [DATE] and] recorded a Claim of Lien in
Official Records Book [__], Page [__], [COUNTY] County, on [DATE], a copy of which is
attached as Exhibit "[__]", against the real property described as: [LEGAL
DESCRIPTION / ADDRESS].

27. The lien secures the unpaid balance of $[AMOUNT] for improvements made to the
property. All Chapter 713 notice and timing requirements have been satisfied.

**WHEREFORE** Plaintiff demands judgment foreclosing its lien, sale of the property
to satisfy the amount due, prejudgment interest, costs, and attorney's fees pursuant
to § 713.29, Florida Statutes, and such further relief as the Court deems just.
