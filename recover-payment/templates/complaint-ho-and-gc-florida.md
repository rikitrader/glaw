## CLAIMS AGAINST THE GENERAL CONTRACTOR  (strong on EVERY property, both buckets)

### COUNT I — BREACH OF CONTRACT (vs. [GC])
Realleges ¶¶1–7. [GC] agreed to pay Plaintiff $[AMOUNT] under [the subcontract,
Ex. "A" / an oral agreement]; Plaintiff performed; [GC] failed to pay; Plaintiff is
damaged in $[UNPAID] plus interest.
**WHEREFORE** damages, prejudgment interest, costs[, fees per contract].

### COUNT II — MISAPPLICATION OF CONSTRUCTION FUNDS (vs. [GC] and [PRINCIPAL])
*(Fla. Stat. § 713.345 — when the GC took job funds and didn't pay down the cost of
the improvement; supports INDIVIDUAL liability of the person who diverted them.)*
Realleges ¶¶1–7. [GC] received $[AMOUNT] for the improvement to the Property and
knowingly applied it to a use other than paying for that improvement, leaving
Plaintiff unpaid. [PRINCIPAL] personally directed the diversion.
**WHEREFORE** damages against [GC] and [PRINCIPAL], jointly and severally, interest,
costs, and such relief as is just. *(Diversion over the statutory threshold is also a
criminal offense — preserve for referral.)*

### COUNT III — CIVIL THEFT (vs. [GC] and [PRINCIPAL])
*(Fla. Stat. §§ 772.11, 812.014 — TREBLE damages + attorney's fees; requires a
written 30-day pre-suit demand, attached as Ex. "[__]".)*
Realleges ¶¶1–7. Defendants knowingly obtained or used Plaintiff's funds/property
with felonious intent to deprive Plaintiff of them.
**WHEREFORE** treble damages, attorney's fees and costs under § 772.11.

### COUNT IV — QUANTUM MERUIT / UNJUST ENRICHMENT (vs. [GC], in the alternative to I)
Realleges ¶¶1–7. Pleaded in the alternative if no enforceable subcontract is found.
Plaintiff conferred a benefit [GC] accepted and unjustly retained without paying its
reasonable value of $[AMOUNT].
**WHEREFORE** the reasonable value, interest, costs.

### COUNT V — FORECLOSURE OF CONSTRUCTION LIEN (vs. [OWNER]) — *both buckets, IF lien preserved*
*(Fla. Stat. Ch. 713 — attaches to the Property even where the Owner paid the GC,
subject to the Owner's "proper payments" defense, §§ 713.06/713.10.)*
Realleges ¶¶1–7. Plaintiff [served a Notice to Owner on [DATE] and] recorded a Claim
of Lien at O.R. Book [__], Page [__], on [DATE] (Ex. "[__]") for $[UNPAID]. All
Ch. 713 notice/timing requirements were met.
**WHEREFORE** judgment foreclosing the lien, sale of the Property, interest, costs,
and attorney's fees under **§ 713.29**.
> ⚠ If NTO/lien deadlines lapsed, DELETE this count — the lien remedy is gone; rely on
> the GC counts (and, in Bucket B, Count VI).

### COUNT VI — UNJUST ENRICHMENT (vs. [OWNER]) — *BUCKET B ONLY (owner has not fully paid)*
*(Lead authority — read & verify before citing: Commerce P'ship 8098 Ltd. P'ship v.
Equity Contracting Co., 695 So. 2d 383 (Fla. 4th DCA 1997); see also Maloney v. Therm
Alum Indus. Corp., 636 So. 2d 767 (Fla. 4th DCA 1994).)*
Realleges ¶¶1–7. Plaintiff conferred a benefit on [OWNER] (labor/materials in the
Property). [OWNER] knew of and retained the benefit and has **not paid** its value;
because [OWNER] has not paid [GC] in full for Plaintiff's work, it would be
inequitable for [OWNER] to retain it without paying $[OWNER_BALANCE].
**WHEREFORE** judgment for the value retained, interest, costs.
