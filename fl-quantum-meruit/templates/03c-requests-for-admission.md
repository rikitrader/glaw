# PLAINTIFF'S FIRST REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION TO DEFENDANT

Pursuant to **Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.370**, Plaintiff requests that Defendant admit or deny each of
the following within **30 days** of service [**45 days** if served with the summons]. Under Rule
1.370(a), **each matter is ADMITTED unless a written answer or objection is served within the
time allowed**; a denial must fairly meet the substance of the request, and a party may not give
lack of information as a reason for failure to admit/deny unless it has made reasonable inquiry.

## REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION
1. Admit that Defendant requested or authorized Plaintiff to perform the Work described in the
   Complaint, paragraph 5.
2. Admit that Plaintiff performed the Work between [START DATE] and [END DATE].
3. Admit that Defendant accepted and used the Work.
4. Admit that the reasonable value of the Work is $[VALUE].
5. Admit that Defendant has paid Plaintiff $[PAID] for the Work.
6. Admit that $[OUTSTANDING] remains unpaid for the Work.
7. Admit that Defendant received the invoice(s) attached as Exhibit [__] and did not object to
   them in writing within [30] days of receipt.
8. Admit that no enforceable written contract governs the Work. [Use only if pleading QM/UE; omit
   if a contract count is also pleaded.]
9. Admit that Defendant has no documents evidencing payment in full for the Work.
10. Admit that the Work conformed to industry standards and Defendant identified no written
    deficiency before this suit.
11. [If sub-vs-owner:] Admit that as of [DATE] Defendant had not paid the general contractor
    [GC NAME] in full for the Work. [The "owner paid the GC" point is dispositive on UE — pin it.]
11A. [If sub-vs-GC:] Admit that the subcontract does not expressly condition Defendant's
     obligation to pay Plaintiff on Defendant first receiving payment from the owner. [Forces the
     pay-if-paid-vs-pay-when-paid question; ambiguity is construed as pay-when-paid — DEC Electric.]
12. Admit the genuineness of each document attached as Exhibit [__] (Rule 1.370(a) authentication).
13. Admit that Defendant has no good-faith basis to dispute the amount of $[OUTSTANDING].
