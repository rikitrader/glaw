# PLAINTIFF'S FIRST SET OF INTERROGATORIES TO DEFENDANT

Pursuant to **Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.340**, Plaintiff requests that Defendant answer the following
interrogatories separately, fully, in writing, and **under oath** within **30 days** of service
[**45 days** if served with the summons]. These interrogatories, including subparts, do not
exceed **30**. They are continuing under Rule 1.280(f).

## INTERROGATORIES
1. State the name, title, address, and telephone number of each person answering or assisting
   in answering these interrogatories.
2. Identify every person with knowledge of the Work, the parties' dealings, or this dispute, and
   state the subject of each person's knowledge.
3. Describe in detail Defendant's understanding of the agreement or request under which Plaintiff
   performed the Work, including who authorized it, when, and on what terms.
4. State whether Defendant contends a written or oral contract governs the Work, and if so,
   identify every document and communication constituting or evidencing it.
5. Itemize every payment Defendant contends it made for the Work: the date, amount, method, payee,
   and the document evidencing each payment.
6. State the amount, if any, Defendant contends remains owed for the Work, and explain the basis
   for any difference from the amount Plaintiff claims ($[OUTSTANDING]).
7. Describe every deficiency, defect, or non-conformity Defendant contends exists in the Work,
   stating for each: the location, the date discovered, who discovered it, and the cost to cure.
8. State whether Defendant accepted, used, occupied, sold, or leased the property or improvement
   to which the Work relates, and describe how and when.
9. [If sub-vs-owner:] State whether Defendant paid the general contractor [GC NAME] for the Work,
   and if so, the date, amount, and the document evidencing each such payment.
9A. [If sub-vs-GC:] State whether Defendant contends the subcontract conditions payment to
    Plaintiff on Defendant first being paid by the owner ("pay-if-paid"); if so, quote the exact
    contract language and state whether the owner has paid Defendant for the Work (DEC Electric).
10. Identify each affirmative defense Defendant asserts and state every fact supporting it.
11. Identify every expert Defendant may call, the subject matter, and the substance of the
    opinions and their grounds (Rule 1.280(b)).
12. Identify every document Defendant may introduce at trial or in opposition to summary judgment.
13. State whether Defendant has been a party to any other action involving non-payment for
    construction, labor, or services in the past [5] years, and identify each such action.
14. State the factual basis for Defendant's denial, if any, of paragraphs [6, 7, and 14] of the
    Complaint.
