# I. REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION (Rule 1.350)
1. All documents concerning [the transaction/property/instrument at issue].
2. All communications between the parties (letters, email, text/SMS, messaging apps) about the dispute.
3. All title/ownership documents: [deeds, certificates of title, bills of sale, security agreements,
   recorded instruments] for [the property].
4. All documents evidencing Defendant's claim or interest, and the basis for it.
5. All documents evidencing payments, demands, notices, or defaults between the parties.
6. All contracts, leases, or agreements governing [the property/relationship].
7. All documents Defendant will rely on at trial or in opposition to summary judgment.
8. All documents identified in or used to answer the interrogatories.
9. [Action-specific: replevin → location/condition records of the property; eviction → ledger +
   notice receipts; quiet title/partition → chain-of-title + survey + lien records; lien →
   perfection records (NTO, claim of lien); garnishment → the garnishee's account records.]
10. Privilege log (Rule 1.280(b)(6)) for anything withheld.

# II. INTERROGATORIES (Rule 1.340 — count subparts, ≤30; answered under oath)
1. Identify the person(s) answering and everyone who assisted.
2. Identify every person with knowledge of the facts and the subject of their knowledge.
3. State Defendant's version of the events and the basis for any denial of the complaint.
4. State the basis for Defendant's claim/interest/defense to [the property/debt/instrument].
5. Itemize every payment, demand, or notice between the parties (date, amount, method, document).
6. Identify every document Defendant may introduce at trial or in opposition to summary judgment.
7. Identify each affirmative defense and every fact supporting it.
8. Identify each expert (subject, opinions, grounds — Rule 1.280(b)).
9. [Action-specific: replevin → the property's location, condition, and who holds it; ejectment/quiet
   title → Defendant's chain of title; eviction → rent paid + into-registry position; garnishment →
   what the garnishee owes/holds for the debtor.]
10. State whether Defendant has been a party to a similar action in the past [5] years; identify each.

# III. REQUESTS FOR ADMISSION (Rule 1.370 — admitted if not timely answered)
1. Admit the genuineness of each document attached as Exhibit [__].
2. Admit Plaintiff holds [title/right to possession/the interest] in [the property].
3. Admit Defendant [detains the property / occupies the premises / claims the adverse interest].
4. Admit Defendant has no [lease / written authorization / valid instrument] supporting its position.
5. Admit the amount [due / of the property's value] is $[AMOUNT].
6. Admit [the statutory notice/demand] was received on [DATE] and not cured within [N] days.
7. Admit Defendant has no good-faith basis to dispute [the dispositive fact].
8. [Action-specific admissions mirroring the elements of the routed cause of action.]
