# PATH A — SISTER-STATE JUDGMENT (UEFJA, §§ 55.501-55.509)
**Step 1 — Record.** File with the clerk of the [COUNTY] County Circuit Court an **authenticated copy**
of the [STATE] judgment entered [DATE] in [COURT], Case No. [____], in favor of [CREDITOR] against
[DEBTOR] for $[AMOUNT] (§ 55.503).
**Step 2 — Affidavit (§ 55.505).** File an affidavit stating the name and last known address of the
judgment debtor and the judgment creditor.
**Step 3 — Notice of Recording (§ 55.505).** The clerk mails, and the creditor also mails, notice of
recording to the debtor at [ADDRESS]. **No execution issues for 30 days** after recording/notice.
**Step 4 — Stay / contest.** The debtor has 30 days to move to stay (e.g., an appeal pending in the
rendering state, or grounds to reopen). Absent a stay, the recorded judgment is enforceable as a
Florida judgment.

```

[CREDITOR/affiant] states: (1) the attached is an authenticated copy of the [STATE] judgment;
(2) the judgment debtor is [NAME], last known address [____]; (3) the creditor is [NAME], address
[____]; (4) $[UNSATISFIED] remains unpaid. Sworn [DATE]. ____________ [AFFIANT] / Notary.
```

# PATH B — FOREIGN-COUNTRY MONEY JUDGMENT (recognition action, §§ 55.601-55.607)
File a **complaint to recognize and enforce a foreign-country money judgment**: plead the rendering
country/court, the judgment, finality, and that none of the **mandatory or discretionary grounds for
nonrecognition** apply (lack of impartial tribunal / personal or subject-matter jurisdiction / due
process / fraud / public policy — § 55.605). Attach an authenticated, translated copy. The recognized
judgment is then enforceable like any Florida judgment.

# AFTER DOMESTICATION — ENFORCE
Record a judgment lien (§ 55.10 / JL filing §§ 55.201-55.241); issue a **writ of execution** (Ch. 56,
see `enforcement-writ-execution.md`); **garnish** (Ch. 77, see `enforcement-garnishment.md`); or open
**proceedings supplementary** (§ 56.29, see `enforcement-proceedings-supplementary.md`).
