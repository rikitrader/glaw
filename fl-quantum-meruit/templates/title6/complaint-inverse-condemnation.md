# COMPLAINT FOR INVERSE CONDEMNATION

Plaintiff [OWNER] sues Defendant [ENTITY] and alleges:
1. This is an action for inverse condemnation under Art. X, § 6, Fla. Const. and the Fifth and
   Fourteenth Amendments, within the jurisdiction of this Court.
2. Plaintiff owns the real property at [ADDRESS], legally described as [LEGAL DESCRIPTION] (Exhibit A).
3. Defendant is a [state/county/municipal] entity with the power of eminent domain.
4. **The taking:** Defendant [physically invaded / permanently flooded / denied all economically
   beneficial use of / substantially and unreasonably deprived Plaintiff of] the Property by [the
   ACTION — e.g., the drainage project / the regulation / the road project] beginning [DATE], without
   instituting eminent-domain proceedings or paying compensation.
5. Defendant's action has caused a taking of [all / a substantial part of] the Property's value;
   Plaintiff has been damaged in the amount of the full compensation due, $[AMOUNT].

**WHEREFORE** Plaintiff demands: (a) a determination that a taking occurred; (b) **full compensation**
(and, where a partial taking, severance damages); (c) statutory **attorney's fees and costs** as in
an eminent-domain proceeding (§ 73.092 / § 73.131); and such further relief as is just.

## [ALTERNATIVE] BERT HARRIS ACT CLAIM (§ 70.001) — if a regulation inordinately burdens the Property
**Presuit (jurisdictional):** at least **150 days** before suit, Plaintiff must present a **written
claim** with a bona fide appraisal to the governmental entity (§ 70.001(4)), which then makes a
settlement/ripeness offer. Plead the existing use or vested rights, the regulation, and the inordinate
burden. **WHEREFORE** Plaintiff seeks compensation for the inordinate burden + fees per § 70.001.
