# COMPLAINT FOR DECLARATORY JUDGMENT

Plaintiff sues Defendant and alleges:
1. This is an action for declaratory relief under **Ch. 86, Fla. Stat.**, within the jurisdiction of
   this Court.
2. The parties' rights arise under [the INSTRUMENT — e.g., the [contract / deed / insurance policy /
   promissory note / lease / statute] dated [DATE]], a copy of which is Exhibit A.
3. **An actual, bona fide, present controversy** exists between the parties: Plaintiff contends
   [PLAINTIFF'S POSITION on the meaning/validity/application], while Defendant contends [DEFENDANT'S
   POSITION]. [State the concrete dispute — e.g., whether the [clause] requires X; whether the [note]
   is enforceable; whether coverage applies.]
4. Plaintiff has a present, practical need for a declaration because [Plaintiff must act/refrain and
   faces [liability/loss/forfeiture] absent a determination]. The parties have a real and adverse
   interest, and the declaration sought is not advisory.
5. Plaintiff is in doubt as to its [rights / status / obligations] under the Instrument and is entitled
   to have them declared.

**WHEREFORE** Plaintiff requests a judgment: (a) declaring the rights, status, and legal relations of
the parties under the Instrument, specifically that [DECLARATION SOUGHT]; (b) granting supplemental
relief under § 86.051 [including [injunctive relief / damages]]; (c) costs and attorney's fees where
authorized (§ 86.081 / the Instrument); and such further relief as is just.
