# SHARED CAPTION (prepend to every count below)
```
IN THE CIRCUIT COURT OF THE [Nth] JUDICIAL CIRCUIT IN AND FOR [COUNTY] COUNTY, FLORIDA
[PLAINTIFF], Plaintiff, v. [DEFENDANT], Defendant.        CASE NO.: __________
```
General allegations every complaint repeats: (1) jurisdiction/amount in controversy; (2) parties +
residency/entity status; (3) venue (Ch. 47 — where the cause accrued / property lies / defendant
resides); (4) conditions precedent performed or waived. **Long-arm** over a nonresident: plead a
§ 48.193 act.

---

## 1. REPLEVIN — recover personal property (Ch. 78, § 78.01)
**Elements:** (a) plaintiff's right to possession of the described property; (b) the property is
wrongfully detained by defendant; (c) it was not taken for a tax/fine or under a judgment; (d) its value.
**Count:** "Plaintiff is entitled to possession of [PROPERTY, described with specificity + value
$[VALUE]]. Defendant wrongfully detains it at [LOCATION]. The property has not been taken for any
tax, assessment, or fine, nor under any execution/judgment against Plaintiff."
**Relief:** possession; damages for wrongful detention; costs. **Prejudgment writ:** § 78.067 (order
to show cause) or § 78.068 (without notice — bond = 2× value). **Gotcha:** describe the property so the
sheriff can identify and seize it.

## 2. EJECTMENT — recover real property with legal title (Ch. 66, § 66.021)
**Elements:** (a) plaintiff holds legal title and the right to possession; (b) defendant is in
wrongful possession; (c) legal description of the land; (d) damages/mesne profits for detention.
**Count:** "Plaintiff owns and is entitled to possession of [LEGAL DESCRIPTION]. Defendant is in
possession and wrongfully withholds it. Plaintiff is damaged in the rental/use value of $[AMOUNT]."
**Relief:** possession + mesne profits + costs. **Use ejectment (not eviction)** when there is no
landlord-tenant relationship but defendant claims a possessory/title interest.

## 3. UNLAWFUL DETAINER — possession from a non-tenant occupant (Ch. 82, § 82.04)
**Elements:** (a) plaintiff's right to possession; (b) defendant entered/remained without a tenancy
and without consent (e.g., holdover guest, ousted relative); (c) summary procedure (Ch. 51) applies.
**Relief:** possession (expedited); § 82.091 **double damages** for forcible/unlawful detainer.
**Gotcha:** if any landlord-tenant relationship exists, this is an eviction (Ch. 83), not unlawful detainer.

## 4. EVICTION — landlord/tenant (Ch. 83; residential § 83.56/83.59)
**Elements (nonpayment):** (a) lease/tenancy; (b) rent default; (c) **statutory notice served**
(residential: 3-day notice for rent, § 83.56(3), excluding weekends/holidays; 7/15-day for
noncompliance); (d) tenant remains in possession.
**Relief:** possession + unpaid rent + damages. **Gotchas:** the 3-day notice must state the exact
rent due and the deadline; tenant's § 83.60(2) defense requires rent **into the court registry**;
nonresidential tenancies use §§ 83.20-83.232.

## 5. QUIET TITLE — remove a cloud (Ch. 65, § 65.011/65.061)
**Elements:** (a) plaintiff's title/interest; (b) a cloud/adverse claim (identify the instrument or
claim attacked); (c) the adverse claim's invalidity.
**Count:** "Plaintiff holds [fee simple/interest] in [LEGAL DESCRIPTION] by [SOURCE — deed recorded
at [BOOK/PAGE]]. Defendant claims an adverse interest by [INSTRUMENT], which is void/invalid because
[REASON]. The cloud should be removed." **Relief:** decree quieting title; cancel the instrument.

## 6. PARTITION — divide co-owned realty (Ch. 64, § 64.011-64.061)
**Elements:** (a) plaintiff is a cotenant holding an undivided interest; (b) legal description;
(c) names + shares of all cotenants and lienholders. **Relief:** partition in kind, or sale + division
of proceeds (§ 64.061); costs/fees apportioned. **Gotcha:** join every cotenant and recorded lienholder.

## 7. DECLARATORY JUDGMENT (Ch. 86, § 86.011)
**Elements (Martinez/May factors):** (a) a bona fide, present, practical need for the declaration;
(b) a present controversy over a state of facts; (c) plaintiff's right/privilege depends on those
facts; (d) an adverse interest in defendant; (e) relief is not merely advisory.
**Count:** "An actual controversy exists between the parties as to [the meaning/validity of
[CONTRACT/DEED/STATUTE/POLICY]]. Plaintiff is in doubt about [RIGHT] and needs a declaration to
[act/avoid liability]." **Relief:** declaration of rights + supplemental relief (§ 86.051) + fees
where authorized (§ 86.081). Often pleaded **with** injunction or breach counts.

## 8. INJUNCTION / NUISANCE (Ch. 60, § 60.05)
**Elements:** (a) a clear legal right; (b) irreparable harm with no adequate remedy at law;
(c) likelihood of success; (d) balance of harms/public interest. **Temporary injunction** (§ 60.07)
requires a **bond**. **Nuisance** (§ 60.05): abate a public/private nuisance. **Relief:** TRO,
temporary and permanent injunction; abatement.

## 9. REESTABLISH A LOST INSTRUMENT (Ch. 71, § 71.011)
**Elements:** (a) the document (e.g., **lost promissory note / deed**) existed and its terms;
(b) it was lost/destroyed; (c) diligent but unsuccessful search; (d) entitlement to reestablishment.
**Relief:** decree reestablishing the instrument's contents. **Pairs with** a foreclosure or
enforcement count where the original note is lost (and the UCC § 673.3091 lost-note affidavit).

## 10. DISHONORED CHECK — treble damages (Ch. 68, § 68.065)
**Elements:** (a) a check/draft was dishonored; (b) the § 68.065 **written demand** was served and
**30 days** elapsed without payment. **Relief:** the check amount + **treble damages** (statutory cap),
bank fees, court costs, and attorney's fees. **Gotcha:** the statutory demand letter is a condition.

## 11. ENFORCE / FORECLOSE A STATUTORY LIEN (Ch. 85, § 85.011)
**Elements:** (a) a valid, perfected statutory lien (e.g., construction lien Ch. 713; repair;
warehouse; landlord's lien); (b) the secured debt is unpaid. **Relief:** foreclosure/sale of the lien
property in equity or at law; deficiency where allowed. **Construction-lien gotchas:** Notice to
Owner (§ 713.06), claim of lien recorded within 90 days, **1 year to foreclose** (§ 713.22).

## 12. ATTACHMENT — prejudgment security (Ch. 76, § 76.04) [ancillary remedy]
**Grounds (debt due):** defendant is removing/disposing of/secreting property to defraud creditors,
is a nonresident, etc. **Requires:** verified complaint/affidavit + **attachment bond** (§ 76.12).
**Relief:** writ directing the sheriff to seize defendant's property to secure the claim.

## 13. GARNISHMENT — reach third-party-held assets (Ch. 77) [ancillary remedy]
**Postjudgment (§ 77.01):** a money judgment + a garnishee holding the debtor's funds/property or
owing the debtor. **Prejudgment (§ 77.031):** limited grounds + bond. **Continuing wage garnishment:**
§ 77.0305. **Mandatory:** serve the § 77.041 **head-of-family exemption** notice. **Relief:** writ;
garnishee answers; judgment against garnishee.

## 14. PROCEEDINGS SUPPLEMENTARY — reach hidden/transferred assets (Ch. 56, § 56.29) [post-judgment]
**Use:** after an unsatisfied judgment, implead third parties holding the debtor's assets and **unwind
fraudulent transfers** (pairs with FUFTA Ch. 726). **Relief:** order applying the assets to the judgment.

## 15. DOMESTICATE A FOREIGN JUDGMENT (Ch. 55) [enforcement]
**Sister-state:** record under the **UEFJA** (§§ 55.501-55.509) + notice. **Foreign-country money
judgment:** recognition action (§§ 55.601-55.607). **Then** execute (Ch. 56) / garnish (Ch. 77).

## 16. CONTEST A TAX ASSESSMENT (Ch. 72, § 72.011) · EMINENT DOMAIN / INVERSE CONDEMNATION (Ch. 73)
72.011 — elect circuit-court action; pay or bond the contested tax. Ch. 73 — condemnation by the
authority; **inverse condemnation** by the owner when a taking occurs without a proceeding.

## EXTRAORDINARY WRITS (Ch. 79 habeas · Ch. 80 quo warranto · Ch. 81 prohibition)
Quo warranto — challenge a person's right to a public office/franchise. Prohibition — bar a lower
tribunal acting in excess of jurisdiction. Habeas — unlawful detention.
