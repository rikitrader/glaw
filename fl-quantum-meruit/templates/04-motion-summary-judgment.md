# PLAINTIFF'S MOTION FOR FINAL SUMMARY JUDGMENT AND INCORPORATED MEMORANDUM OF LAW

Plaintiff, [PLAINTIFF], moves under Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510 for final summary judgment
against Defendant on Count [I — Quantum Meruit] / [II — Unjust Enrichment], and states:

## I. STANDARD
Summary judgment is warranted where there is no genuine dispute as to any material fact
and the movant is entitled to judgment as a matter of law. Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510(a). The
Florida standard mirrors the federal standard. *In re Amends. to Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.510*,
317 So. 3d 72 (Fla. 2021); *Celotex Corp. v. Catrett*, 477 U.S. 317 (1986). **[verify]**

1. Plaintiff provided [WORK/SERVICES] to Defendant at Defendant's request. *[Ex. __,
   Pl. Aff. ¶__]*
2. Defendant accepted, used, and benefited from the work. *[Ex. __; Def. Adm. No. __]*
3. The reasonable value of the work is $[VALUE]; Defendant paid $[PAID]; $[OUTSTANDING]
   remains unpaid. *[Ex. __ invoices; Pl. Aff. ¶__]*
4. Defendant has not paid the balance and gave no written rejection of the services.
   *[Def. Adm. Nos. __]*

## III. ARGUMENT
The elements of quantum meruit / unjust enrichment are established as a matter of law:
(a) Plaintiff conferred a benefit / rendered services; (b) Defendant knew of and
accepted/retained the benefit; (c) Plaintiff reasonably expected payment; and (d)
Defendant has not paid the reasonable value, which it would be inequitable to retain.
The supporting affidavit and Defendant's admissions leave no genuine dispute of material
fact. [Where invoices were sent and undisputed, the amount is conclusively established.]
