# SHARED CAPTION
```
IN THE [CIRCUIT/COUNTY] COURT OF THE [Nth] JUDICIAL CIRCUIT IN AND FOR [COUNTY] COUNTY, FLORIDA
[PLAINTIFF], Plaintiff, v. [DEFENDANT], Defendant.        CASE NO.: __________
```
General allegations every complaint repeats: jurisdiction/amount; parties + residency; venue (Ch. 47);
long-arm (§ 48.193) for a nonresident; conditions precedent performed or waived.

## CONTRACT & QUASI-CONTRACT
**Breach of Contract** (SOL 5 yr written / 4 oral) — (1) a valid contract; (2) material breach;
(3) damages. *Count:* "The parties entered [the contract] (Ex. A). Plaintiff performed [or was
excused]. Defendant materially breached by [ACT]. Plaintiff was damaged $[AMT]." *Relief:* damages,
interest, [fees if contractual], costs.
**Account Stated** (4) — prior dealings; an agreed balance; a promise (often implied by retention
without objection) to pay. *Pair with* Open Account / Breach of Contract in the alternative.
**Open Account** (4) — a running account of transactions; goods/services delivered; unpaid balance
(verify by affidavit). **Money Lent** (4) — money delivered as a loan; non-repayment.
**Breach of Promissory Note** (5) — plaintiff holds the note (Ex. A); execution; default; amount due.
*Gotcha:* lost note → § 673.3091 + reestablishment (Ch. 71 template).
**Good Faith & Fair Dealing** (5) — an existing contract + a discretionary term exercised in bad
faith. *Cannot stand alone* — anchor to an express term.
**Express/Implied Warranty** (4, UCC §§ 672.313-.315) — the affirmation/merchantability/fitness;
breach; **notice**; damages. *Gotcha:* disclaimers (§ 672.316) + privity for economic loss.

## EQUITY & RESTITUTION
**Unjust Enrichment / Quantum Meruit** (4) — benefit conferred + knowledge + acceptance + inequity
to retain without paying. *Barred* by an express contract governing the subject (*Commerce*). See QM
Module 1 for the gates (§ 489.128, owner-paid-GC, pay-if-paid *DEC Electric*).
**Promissory Estoppel** (4) — a clear definite promise; expected reliance; detrimental reliance;
injustice avoidable only by enforcement. **Rescission** (4) — contract + a ground (fraud/mistake/
failure of consideration) + prompt repudiation + restore status quo + inadequate legal remedy.
**Specific Performance** (5) — a definite contract (usually real estate); plaintiff ready/able;
money damages inadequate; clean hands. **Constructive/Resulting Trust** — see `glaw-fl-cause show`.
**Equitable Accounting** (4) — fiduciary relationship or complex dealings + inadequate legal remedy.

## FRAUD & MISREPRESENTATION
**Fraud / Fraudulent Misrepresentation** (4 from discovery) — (1) a false statement of material fact;
(2) scienter; (3) intent to induce reliance; (4) justifiable reliance; (5) damages. *Plead with
particularity (Rule 1.120(b)).* **Fraudulent Inducement** — same, inducing the contract; the
**independent-tort exception** survives the economic-loss rule (*HTP v. Lineas Aereas*).
**Negligent Misrepresentation** (4) — false material fact made without reasonable care + intent to
induce + justifiable reliance + damages. **Constructive Fraud** — abuse of a confidential
relationship (no intent needed). **Fraudulent Nondisclosure (real estate)** — *Johnson v. Davis*:
seller's knowledge of a material, non-observable defect + failure to disclose.

## BUSINESS TORTS
**Breach of Fiduciary Duty** (4) — a fiduciary duty; breach; damages proximately caused. *+ Aiding
& Abetting:* an underlying breach + knowledge + substantial assistance. **Tortious Interference**
(4) — (with a **contract**) an existing contract + knowledge + intentional & unjustified procurement
of breach + damages; (with an **advantageous business relationship**) a relationship with legal
rights + knowledge + intentional unjustified interference + damages. *Defense:* a party/its agent
cannot interfere with its own contract; competition privilege. **Civil Conspiracy** (4) — agreement +
unlawful act/means + overt act + damages (needs an underlying tort, except 'peculiar coercive power').
**Breach of Joint Venture** (4) — community of interest + joint control + sharing of profits AND
losses + breach.

## INTENTIONAL TORTS
**Conversion** (4) — plaintiff's ownership/possession of specific property + defendant's wrongful
dominion + (money: kept intact/identifiable) + demand & refusal + damages. *Mere debt ≠ conversion.*
**Civil Theft (§ 772.11)** (5) — § 812.014 theft elements (felonious intent) by **clear and
convincing** evidence + the **30-day pre-suit demand** (see `demand-civil-theft.md`) → treble + fees.
**Assault / Battery / False Imprisonment / IIED** — see `glaw-fl-cause show`. **Defamation**
(2 yr, single-publication) — false statement of fact + publication + fault + damages (or per se);
**media defendant → § 770.01 5-day presuit notice**. **Trespass** (4) — possession + unauthorized
entry + damages. **Malicious Prosecution / Abuse of Process** (4) — see `glaw-fl-cause show`.

## NEGLIGENCE & STRICT LIABILITY
**Negligence** (4) — duty + breach + causation (actual + proximate) + damages; comparative fault
§ 768.81. Variants: premises/fall-down (§ 768.0755 notice), motor vehicle (PIP threshold),
negligent **entrustment/retention/supervision/security** (§ 768.0706 safe harbor). **Professional/
Legal Malpractice** (2 yr) — duty + breach of the standard of care + causation (case-within-a-case).
**Strict Products Liability** (4; repose § 95.031) — manufacture/sale + defect (design/manufacturing/
warning) unreasonably dangerous + existed at sale + causation. **Wrongful Death** (2 yr) — §§ 768.16-
.26; bring through the personal representative for the statutory survivors.

## STATUTORY
**FDUTPA (§ 501.201)** (4) — a deceptive/unfair practice in trade or commerce + causation + actual
damages (+ fees, declaratory/injunctive). **FCCPA (§ 559.72)** (2) — a consumer debt + a prohibited
collection practice (+ actual/statutory/punitive + fees). **Usurious Transaction (Ch. 687)** — a loan
+ absolute repayment obligation + rate > max (18% / 25% > $500k) + corrupt intent → forfeiture of
interest (criminal > 25%). *MCA defense:* "true sale of receivables, not a loan." **Worthless Check
(§ 68.065)** — see Title VI. **Misleading Advertisement (§ 817.41)**; **Building Code (§ 553.84)**.

## DECLARATORY / INJUNCTIVE / PROPERTY
**Declaratory Judgment** (Ch. 86), **Injunction/TRO** (Ch. 60 / Rule 1.610), **Quiet Title** (Ch. 65),
**Replevin** (Ch. 78), **Ejectment** (Ch. 66), **Partition** (Ch. 64), **Slander of Title** (4) —
see `../title6/` for the dedicated templates.
