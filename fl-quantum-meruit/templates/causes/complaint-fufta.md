# COMPLAINT TO AVOID FRAUDULENT TRANSFER (Ch. 726, Fla. Stat.)

Plaintiff [CREDITOR] sues Defendants [DEBTOR] (the "Debtor") and [TRANSFEREE] (the "Transferee") and alleges:

## JURISDICTION, VENUE, PARTIES
1. This is an action to avoid a fraudulent transfer under the Florida Uniform Fraudulent Transfer Act,
   Ch. 726, Fla. Stat., and for related relief; the amount in controversy exceeds [$50,000], exclusive
   of interest, costs, and fees. Venue is proper in [COUNTY] because [the transfer occurred / the
   property is located / a defendant resides there].
2. Plaintiff is a **creditor** of the Debtor: Plaintiff holds a **claim** (§ 726.102) — [a judgment
   entered [DATE] for $[___] / an unpaid debt of $[___] arising from [contract/obligation]] — that
   arose **[before / after]** the transfer described below.
3. The Debtor [is/was] [entity/individual] who owed Plaintiff the claim. The Transferee is
   [entity/individual] who received the asset.

## THE TRANSFER
4. On or about [DATE], the Debtor transferred to the Transferee [DESCRIBE THE ASSET — real property at
   [ADDRESS] / $[___] / the membership interest in [ENTITY] / the receivable] (the "Transfer").
5. [Describe the consideration, if any, the Debtor received for the Transfer — $[___] / none / an
   antecedent debt of $[___].]

## COUNT I — ACTUAL-INTENT FRAUDULENT TRANSFER (§ 726.105(1)(a))
6. Plaintiff realleges ¶¶ 1-5.
7. The Debtor made the Transfer **with actual intent to hinder, delay, or defraud** Plaintiff, as shown
   by the following **badges of fraud (§ 726.105(2))** — plead each that the facts support:
   - **(a) insider:** the Transferee is an insider of the Debtor because [relationship/control].
   - **(b) retained control:** after the Transfer the Debtor [continued to use/possess/control the asset].
   - **(c) concealment:** the Transfer was [concealed / not recorded / back-dated].
   - **(d) suit threatened/pending:** before the Transfer the Debtor had been [sued by / threatened with
     suit by] [Plaintiff/creditor] in [case/notice].
   - **(e) substantially all assets:** the Transfer was of substantially all the Debtor's assets.
   - **(h) not reasonably equivalent value:** the Debtor received [no / $[___] = far less than the
     $[___] value of] consideration.
   - **(i) insolvency:** the Debtor was insolvent when, or became insolvent shortly after, the Transfer.
   - **(j) timing:** the Transfer occurred shortly [before/after] a substantial debt to Plaintiff was incurred.
   - [(b)(c)(f)(g)(k) as applicable.]
8. The Transfer is **voidable** as to Plaintiff under § 726.105(1)(a).

## COUNT II — CONSTRUCTIVE FRAUDULENT TRANSFER (§ 726.105(1)(b) / § 726.106(1))
9. Plaintiff realleges ¶¶ 1-5.
10. The Debtor made the Transfer **without receiving a reasonably equivalent value**, AND
    [§ 726.105(1)(b): the Debtor's remaining assets were unreasonably small for its business / it
    intended to incur debts beyond its ability to pay] **— OR —** [§ 726.106(1): Plaintiff's claim
    arose before the Transfer and the Debtor was **insolvent** at that time or became insolvent as a
    result of the Transfer]. **No proof of intent is required.**
11. The Transfer is voidable under § 726.105(1)(b) / § 726.106(1).

## RELIEF (§ 726.108)
**WHEREFORE** Plaintiff demands:
(a) **Avoidance** of the Transfer to the extent necessary to satisfy Plaintiff's claim (§ 726.108(1)(a));
(b) an **attachment** or other provisional remedy against the asset or other property of the Transferee
   (§ 726.108(1)(b)); (c) an **injunction** against further disposition of the asset (§ 726.108(1)(c)(1));
(d) appointment of a **receiver** (§ 726.108(1)(c)(2)); (e) **levy/execution** on the asset or its
proceeds (if Plaintiff holds a judgment) (§ 726.108(2)); (f) money judgment against the Transferee up to
the value of the asset or the amount of the claim, whichever is less (§ 726.109(2)); and (g) costs,
[fees,] and any further relief the Court deems just.
