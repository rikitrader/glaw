# 1. MOTION TO DISMISS (Rule 1.140(b))
**Standard:** tests only the **four corners** of the complaint; all well-pleaded facts taken as true,
all reasonable inferences for the plaintiff; **no evidence considered**. De novo on appeal.
*Motion:* "The [Count __] fails to state a cause of action because [the complaint does not allege
[ELEMENT] / the claim is barred on its face by [the SOL / an affirmative defense apparent on the
face / the express-contract bar]]. WHEREFORE Defendant moves to dismiss [with prejudice]."
*Common grounds:* failure to state a claim (b)(6); lack of jurisdiction (b)(1)-(2); improper venue;
failure to plead fraud with particularity (Rule 1.120(b)); the economic-loss rule; SOL on the face.

# 2. MOTION FOR SUMMARY JUDGMENT (Rule 1.510 — 2021 federal standard)
**Standard (post-2021):** summary judgment is granted where there is **no genuine dispute of material
fact** and the movant is entitled to judgment as a matter of law (*Celotex/Anderson/Matsushita*). The
movant may show **the absence of evidence** to support the non-movant's case; the non-movant must come
forward with evidence on which a jury could reasonably find in its favor — a "scintilla" is not enough.
**The old Florida 'slightest doubt' standard is abrogated.** Serve at least **40 days** before the
hearing; the response is due **20 days** before. *Motion:* state each material fact as undisputed with
a **record citation**, then apply the law element-by-element.

# 3. MOTION FOR DIRECTED VERDICT (Rule 1.480) — at trial, after the opponent rests
**Standard:** no legally sufficient evidentiary basis for a reasonable jury to find for the non-movant;
the evidence and all inferences are viewed in the light most favorable to the non-movant. Reserve the
motion to preserve a post-verdict renewal.

# 4. MOTION FOR JUDGMENT NOTWITHSTANDING THE VERDICT / RENEWED DV (Rule 1.480(b))
**Standard:** same as directed verdict, made within **15 days** after the verdict; granted only if no
reasonable jury could have reached the verdict on the evidence. Often paired with a motion for new trial.

# 5. MOTION FOR NEW TRIAL (Rule 1.530)
**Standard:** the verdict is **against the manifest weight of the evidence**, or a material trial error
(improper argument, evidentiary error, inadequate/excessive damages) affected the result. Served within
**15 days** after the verdict. Reviewed for **abuse of discretion**.

# 6. DAUBERT — EXPERT ADMISSIBILITY (§ 90.702)
**Standard:** the witness is **qualified**; the testimony is based on **sufficient facts/data**, is the
product of **reliable principles and methods**, and those methods are **reliably applied** to the facts;
and it will **assist the trier**. Florida codified **Daubert** in § 90.702 (2019). *Motion in limine:*
attack methodology, fit, and ipse dixit.

# 7. PUNITIVE-DAMAGES PROFFER (§ 768.72)
**Gate:** punitive damages may **not be pleaded** until the court finds, on a **reasonable evidentiary
proffer**, a basis for **intentional misconduct or gross negligence** (clear and convincing at trial).
Employer/principal liability requires § 768.72(3). **Cap:** § 768.73 — the greater of **3× compensatory
or $500,000** (with exceptions). *Motion for leave to amend to add a claim for punitive damages* +
the evidentiary proffer (record evidence, not allegations).

# 8. STANDARDS ON APPEAL
- **De novo** — pure law: MTD, MSJ, contract construction, jurisdiction. No deference.
- **Abuse of discretion** — discretionary rulings: new trial, evidentiary calls, continuances,
  amendments. Reversed only if arbitrary/unreasonable.
- **Competent substantial evidence** — factual findings: affirmed if supported by evidence a
  reasonable mind would accept as adequate.
