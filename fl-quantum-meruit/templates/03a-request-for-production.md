# PLAINTIFF'S FIRST REQUEST FOR PRODUCTION TO DEFENDANT

Pursuant to **Fla. R. Civ. P. 1.350**, Plaintiff, [PLAINTIFF], requests that Defendant,
[DEFENDANT], produce the following documents and tangible things for inspection and copying
within **30 days** of service [**45 days** if served with the summons], at [FIRM ADDRESS] or
by electronic delivery to [EMAIL].

## DEFINITIONS & INSTRUCTIONS
1. "Document" includes all writings, emails, text/SMS and messaging-app records, photographs,
   invoices, estimates, change orders, ledgers, and electronically stored information (ESI),
   produced as kept in the usual course of business or labeled to correspond to these requests.
2. "The Work" means the labor, materials, and services described in the Complaint, paragraph 5.
3. "Communication" means any transmission between the parties or with any third party concerning
   the Work, payment, or this dispute.
4. If any document is withheld on a claim of privilege, serve a **privilege log** (Rule
   1.280(b)(6)) identifying each item, its date, author, recipients, and the basis.
5. These requests are continuing under Rule 1.280(f); supplement as documents are located.

## DOCUMENTS REQUESTED
1. All documents evidencing the agreement, request, or authorization for Plaintiff to perform
   the Work, including any [written contract, proposal, estimate, purchase order, or texts/emails].
2. All Communications between Plaintiff and Defendant concerning the Work, scope, schedule,
   pricing, or payment, from [START DATE] to the present.
3. All invoices, statements, or bills Plaintiff sent Defendant, and all of Defendant's responses
   or objections to them.
4. All documents evidencing payments Defendant made to Plaintiff or to any third party for the
   Work (checks, wires, ACH records, lien releases, payment-application records).
5. All documents reflecting Defendant's acceptance, use, or benefit of the Work (photographs,
   inspection or punch-list records, certificates of occupancy, sale/lease of the improved
   property).
6. All change orders, back-charges, or claimed deficiencies in the Work, and all documents
   supporting any such claim.
7. [If sub-vs-owner:] All documents showing whether and when Defendant paid the general
   contractor [GC NAME] for the Work, including the GC's payment applications and lien waivers.
7A. [If sub-vs-GC:] The complete subcontract between Plaintiff and Defendant and every document
    constituting or amending its payment terms — in particular any "pay-if-paid" / "pay-when-paid"
    or contingent-payment provision (DEC Electric) — and all documents showing whether the owner
    paid Defendant for the Work.
8. All contracts between Defendant and any other contractor or supplier for the same project.
9. Defendant's [contractor license / business registration] records for the relevant period.
10. All documents Defendant intends to rely on at trial or in opposition to summary judgment.
11. All documents identified in, or used to answer, Plaintiff's Interrogatories.
12. [Project file, daily logs, and accounting records for the project at [PROPERTY ADDRESS].]
