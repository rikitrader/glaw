# Federal Rules of Civil Procedure (FRCP) - Quick Reference

## Pleading Rules

### Rule 7 - Pleadings Allowed
- Complaint, Answer, Answer to Counterclaim, Answer to Crossclaim
- Third-Party Complaint, Third-Party Answer
- Reply to Answer (only if court orders)

### Rule 8 - General Rules of Pleading
**(a) Claim for Relief must contain:**
1. Short and plain statement of jurisdiction
2. Short and plain statement showing entitlement to relief
3. Demand for relief sought

**(b) Defenses and Denials:**
- Respond to each allegation: admit, deny, or state lack of knowledge
- General denial permitted only if party intends to deny all allegations

**(c) Affirmative Defenses (must be raised or waived):**
- Accord and satisfaction, arbitration and award, assumption of risk
- Contributory negligence, duress, estoppel, failure of consideration
- Fraud, illegality, injury by fellow servant, laches, license
- Payment, release, res judicata, statute of frauds, statute of limitations, waiver

**(d) Pleading to Be Concise and Direct:**
- Each allegation must be simple, concise, and direct
- Alternative and inconsistent statements permitted

### Rule 9 - Pleading Special Matters
**(a) Capacity/Authority:** Must be specifically denied
**(b) Fraud/Mistake:** State with PARTICULARITY (who, what, when, where, how)
- Malice, intent, knowledge, other conditions of mind: may be alleged generally
**(g) Special Damages:** Must be specifically stated

### Rule 10 - Form of Pleadings
- Caption with court name, title, file number, Rule 7(a) designation
- Numbered paragraphs, each limited to single set of circumstances
- Separate counts for separate claims

### Rule 11 - Signing Pleadings; Sanctions
- Every pleading signed by attorney or unrepresented party
- Certifications: (1) not for improper purpose; (2) legal contentions warranted; (3) factual contentions have evidentiary support; (4) denials warranted
- Safe harbor: 21-day opportunity to withdraw before sanctions motion filed
- Sanctions: what suffices to deter repetition

## Pre-Answer Motions

### Rule 12 - Defenses and Objections
**(b) How to Present Defenses - Motion to Dismiss:**
1. 12(b)(1) - Lack of subject-matter jurisdiction
2. 12(b)(2) - Lack of personal jurisdiction
3. 12(b)(3) - Improper venue
4. 12(b)(4) - Insufficient process
5. 12(b)(5) - Insufficient service of process
6. 12(b)(6) - Failure to state a claim upon which relief can be granted
7. 12(b)(7) - Failure to join a required party under Rule 19

**Consolidation requirement:** 12(b)(2)-(5) must be raised in first responsive pleading or pre-answer motion, or waived.
**Never waived:** 12(b)(1) and 12(b)(6) can be raised at any time.

**(c) Motion for Judgment on the Pleadings:** After pleadings closed, before trial
**(e) Motion for More Definite Statement:** Pleading so vague party cannot reasonably prepare response
**(f) Motion to Strike:** Redundant, immaterial, impertinent, or scandalous matter

**Timing:**
- Answer due: 21 days after service (60 days if waiver of service)
- 12(b) motion: Must be filed before responsive pleading
- If 12(b) motion denied: Answer due 14 days after notice of court's action

### Rule 15 - Amended and Supplemental Pleadings
**(a)(1) Amendment as matter of course:** Within 21 days of serving, or 21 days after responsive pleading/12(b) motion served
**(a)(2) Other amendments:** Only with opposing party's consent or court leave (freely given when justice requires)
**(b) Amendment during/after trial:** Conform to evidence
**(c) Relation back:** Amendment relates back if same transaction/occurrence, or party knew/should have known

## Discovery

### Rule 26 - Duty of Disclosure; General Provisions
**(a)(1) Initial Disclosures (within 14 days of Rule 26(f) conference):**
- Names/contact of individuals likely to have discoverable information
- Copy/description of documents/ESI/tangible things in possession
- Computation of damages claimed
- Insurance agreements

**(a)(2) Expert Disclosures:** At least 90 days before trial
- Written report if retained or specially employed

**(b)(1) Discovery Scope:** Any nonprivileged matter relevant to any party's claim or defense, proportional to the needs of the case

**(b)(3) Trial Preparation: Work Product:**
- Attorney mental impressions, conclusions, opinions, legal theories: virtually absolute protection
- Other work product: discoverable only upon showing substantial need and inability to obtain equivalent

**(f) Conference of Parties:** Required before discovery begins
**(g) Certification:** Every disclosure/request/response certified as consistent with rules

### Rule 30 - Depositions by Oral Examination
- 10 depositions per side (without stipulation or court order)
- 1 day of 7 hours per deposition
- 30 days' notice (reasonable written notice)

### Rule 33 - Interrogatories
- No more than 25 (including subparts) without stipulation or court order
- 30 days to respond

### Rule 34 - Requests for Production
- 30 days to respond
- Must produce as kept in ordinary course or organize/label to correspond

### Rule 36 - Requests for Admission
- 30 days to respond
- Failure to respond = deemed admitted

### Rule 37 - Failure to Make Disclosures or Cooperate in Discovery
- Motion to compel (requires meet and confer certification)
- Sanctions for failure to comply: expenses, adverse inference, default, contempt

## Dispositive Motions

### Rule 56 - Summary Judgment
- May be filed at any time until 30 days after close of discovery
- Standard: No genuine dispute of material fact; movant entitled to judgment as matter of law
- Must cite particular parts of record
- Opposing party cannot rest on mere allegations

## Trial

### Rule 50 - Judgment as a Matter of Law (JMOL)
**(a) Before case submitted to jury:** If reasonable jury would not have legally sufficient basis to find for that party
**(b) Renewed JMOL:** Must be filed within 28 days after entry of judgment
- Prerequisite: Must have moved under (a) before case submitted to jury

### Rule 59 - New Trial; Altering or Amending Judgment
- Must be filed within 28 days after entry of judgment
- Grounds: error of law, excessive/inadequate damages, newly discovered evidence, misconduct

### Rule 60 - Relief from Judgment
**(b) Grounds:** Mistake, newly discovered evidence, fraud, void judgment, satisfaction, any other reason justifying relief
- Filed within reasonable time (1 year for grounds 1-3)
