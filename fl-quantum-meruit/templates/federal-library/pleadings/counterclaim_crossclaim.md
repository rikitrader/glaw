## Counterclaim / Crossclaim Rules

### Compulsory vs. Permissive Counterclaims

| Type | Rule | Test | Waived if Not Filed? |
|------|------|------|---------------------|
| Compulsory | 13(a) | Same transaction or occurrence | **Yes** — waived |
| Permissive | 13(b) | Any claim against opposing party | No |

### "Same Transaction or Occurrence" Test
The Eleventh Circuit uses the **logical relationship test**: claims are compulsory if they "arise out of the same aggregate of operative facts." *Plant v. Blazer Fin. Servs., Inc.*, 598 F.2d 1357, 1361 (5th Cir. 1979).

### Crossclaim Requirements (Rule 13(g))
- Must be against a co-party (co-defendant or co-plaintiff)
- Must arise from same transaction/occurrence as original action OR a counterclaim
- Always permissive — never compulsory (no waiver)

### Reply to Counterclaim
- Plaintiff must serve a reply (answer) to the counterclaim within **21 days** — Rule 12(a)(1)(B)
- If counterclaim is labeled "compulsory," failure to respond = default risk

### Jurisdiction
- Compulsory counterclaims: supplemental jurisdiction under § 1367 (almost always)
- Permissive counterclaims: must have independent jurisdictional basis
- Crossclaims: supplemental jurisdiction under § 1367
