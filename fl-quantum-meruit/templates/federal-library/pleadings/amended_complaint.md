# AMENDED COMPLAINT TEMPLATE

## When to Use
- **As of right** (Rule 15(a)(1)): within 21 days of serving original complaint, or 21 days after service of a responsive pleading or Rule 12 motion (whichever is earlier)
- **By leave of court** (Rule 15(a)(2)): after the as-of-right window closes — requires motion to amend or written consent of opposing party
- **By court order** after dismissal without prejudice

## Critical Drafting Rules

### Amended Complaint Must Be Self-Contained
- An amended complaint **supersedes** the original — *Dresdner Bank AG v. M/V Olympia Voyager*, 463 F.3d 1210, 1215 (11th Cir. 2006)
- Include ALL allegations, not just changes — court will not read original
- Cannot incorporate original complaint by reference

### As-of-Right vs. Leave to Amend

| Method | Rule | Deadline | Court Approval |
|--------|------|----------|----------------|
| As of right | 15(a)(1)(A) | 21 days after serving original | No |
| As of right after 12(b) motion | 15(a)(1)(B) | 21 days after service of motion/response | No |
| By leave of court | 15(a)(2) | After as-of-right window | Yes (file motion to amend) |
| By stipulation | 15(a)(2) | Any time | Written consent of opposing party |

### Relation Back (Rule 15(c))
- New claims relate back to original filing date if they arise out of the **same conduct, transaction, or occurrence**
- New parties relate back only if: (i) same transaction, (ii) new party received notice within Rule 4(m) period, (iii) new party knew or should have known the action would have been brought against them
- Critical for statute of limitations issues

### Redline / Comparison Copy
- Not required by FRCP but strongly recommended by many judges
- File the clean amended complaint AND a redline showing changes
- Some MDFL judges require this in individual practice preferences
