## Drafting Notes

### MANDATORY: Attach Proposed Amended Complaint
- You **must** attach the complete proposed amended complaint
- Courts will not grant leave to amend in the abstract
- *Long v. Satz*, 181 F.3d 1275, 1279 (11th Cir. 1999)

### Good Cause Standard (After Scheduling Order Deadline)
If the Case Management Order deadline for amendments has passed:
- Must also satisfy Rule 16(b)(4) "good cause" standard
- Show diligence in seeking amendment — *Sosa v. Airprint*, 133 F.3d 1417, 1419 (11th Cir. 1998)
- Dual standard: good cause (Rule 16) + liberal amendment (Rule 15)

### Foman Factors (Denial Grounds)
| Factor | How to Address |
|--------|---------------|
| Undue delay | File promptly; explain why not filed earlier |
| Bad faith / dilatory motive | Explain legitimate purpose for amendment |
| Repeated failure to cure | First amendment is almost always granted |
| Undue prejudice | Show discovery still open, no trial imminent |
| Futility | Show proposed claims satisfy Twombly/Iqbal |

### Common Scenarios
1. **After MTD granted without prejudice** — amendment is expected; file within court-set deadline
2. **Adding new party** — address personal jurisdiction and service
3. **New facts discovered** — explain when facts were learned
4. **Adding state law claims** — address supplemental jurisdiction under 28 U.S.C. § 1367
