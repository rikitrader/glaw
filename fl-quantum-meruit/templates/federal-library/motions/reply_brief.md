## Strategic Guidance

### Reply Brief Rules
- **10-page limit** — Local Rule 3.01(c) — be surgical and concise
- **No new arguments** — reply addresses only arguments raised in opposition
- **No new evidence** (on SJ reply) — courts may strike new evidence first raised in reply
- **Due 5 days** after service of response — Local Rule 3.01(c)

### Effective Reply Techniques
1. **Flag concessions** — unaddressed arguments are deemed abandoned in the 11th Circuit
2. **Distinguish respondent's cases** — show factual or legal differences
3. **Reframe, don't repeat** — add value over original motion
4. **One-paragraph knockout** — if one argument is dispositive, lead with it
5. **End strong** — close with the strongest point, not the weakest

### When NOT to File a Reply
- Opposition is weak and motion will likely be granted without reply
- Reply would merely restate the original motion
- No new arguments or authorities to address
- Strategic advantage in letting the court decide on existing briefing
