                             LEGAL STANDARD

     6. Under Rule 55(b)(2), when a party against whom default has been
entered fails to plead or otherwise defend, the court may enter default
judgment. The court must first satisfy itself that it has jurisdiction
over the subject matter and the defendant.

     7. Upon entry of default, the well-pleaded factual allegations of
the complaint are deemed admitted, except those relating to the amount
of damages. Nishimatsu Const. Co. v. Houston Nat'l Bank, 515 F.2d
1200, 1206 (5th Cir. 1975).

     8. "Even when a default judgment is warranted based on a party's
failure to defend, the allegations in the complaint with respect to
the amount of the damages are not deemed true. The district court must
instead conduct an inquiry in order to ascertain the amount of
damages with reasonable certainty." Adolph Coors Co. v. Movement
Against Racism & the Klan, 777 F.2d 1538, 1543-44 (11th Cir. 1985)
(internal citations omitted).

## Two-Step Default Process

| Step | Rule | Filed With | Requirements |
|------|------|------------|--------------|
| 1. Clerk's entry of default | 55(a) | Clerk | Affidavit showing failure to plead/defend |
| 2. Default judgment | 55(b)(2) | Court | Motion + evidence of damages |

## SCRA Compliance (Servicemembers Civil Relief Act)
- Before default judgment, must file affidavit stating whether defendant is in military service
- Use Department of Defense Manpower Data Center to verify: https://scra.dmdc.osd.mil
- 50 U.S.C. § 3931(b)(1) — mandatory requirement

## Damages Evidence
- Court cannot accept damage amount on faith — must conduct inquiry
- Options: (1) evidentiary hearing, (2) detailed affidavits and documentation, (3) referral to magistrate judge
- Attach: medical records, invoices, pay stubs, tax returns, expert reports
