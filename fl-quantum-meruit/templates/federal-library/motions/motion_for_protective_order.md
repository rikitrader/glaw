## Types of Protective Orders (Rule 26(c)(1))

| Type | Rule | Purpose |
|------|------|---------|
| (A) Forbid disclosure/discovery | 26(c)(1)(A) | Block entirely |
| (B) Specify terms/conditions | 26(c)(1)(B) | Limit scope |
| (C) Alternate discovery method | 26(c)(1)(C) | Written vs. oral |
| (D) Limit scope of discovery | 26(c)(1)(D) | Narrow topics |
| (E) Designate persons present | 26(c)(1)(E) | Limit attendance |
| (F) Seal deposition | 26(c)(1)(F) | Confidentiality |
| (G) Trade secret protection | 26(c)(1)(G) | Special handling |
| (H) Simultaneous filing | 26(c)(1)(H) | Sealed envelopes |

## Apex Deposition Doctrine
- High-ranking corporate officers generally should not be deposed unless:
  (1) They have unique firsthand knowledge, AND
  (2) Other less burdensome discovery has been exhausted
- *Salter v. Upjohn Co.*, 593 F.2d 649 (5th Cir. 1979)
