# Florida Title XLI — Verified Operative Excerpts

## CH. 726 — FLORIDA UNIFORM FRAUDULENT TRANSFER ACT (FUFTA)

### § 726.105 — Transfers fraudulent as to present AND future creditors [VERIFIED]
A transfer is fraudulent as to a creditor whose claim arose before or after the transfer if made:
> **(1)(a) — ACTUAL INTENT:** "With actual intent to hinder, delay, or defraud any creditor of the debtor"; OR
> **(1)(b) — CONSTRUCTIVE:** "Without receiving a reasonably equivalent value in exchange for the
> transfer or obligation, and the debtor: 1. Was engaged or was about to engage in a business or a
> transaction for which the remaining assets of the debtor were unreasonably small in relation to the
> business or transaction; or 2. Intended to incur, or believed or reasonably should have believed
> that he or she would incur, debts beyond his or her ability to pay as they became due."

**§ 726.105(2) — the BADGES OF FRAUD (factors for actual intent):**
(a) the transfer was to an **insider**; (b) the debtor **retained possession or control** after the
transfer; (c) the transfer/obligation was **disclosed or concealed**; (d) before the transfer the
debtor **had been sued or threatened with suit**; (e) the transfer was of **substantially all** the
debtor's assets; (f) the debtor **absconded**; (g) the debtor **removed or concealed** assets; (h)
the value of consideration was **not reasonably equivalent**; (i) the debtor was **insolvent** or
became insolvent shortly after; (j) the transfer occurred **shortly before or after a substantial
debt** was incurred; (k) the debtor transferred **essential business assets to a lienor** who
transferred them to an insider.

### § 726.106 — Transfers fraudulent as to PRESENT creditors (constructive) [VERIFIED summary]
Fraudulent as to a creditor whose claim arose **before** the transfer if the debtor made it **without
receiving reasonably equivalent value AND was insolvent at that time or became insolvent as a result**
(§ 726.106(1)); or a transfer to an **insider** for an antecedent debt while insolvent and the insider
had reasonable cause to believe the debtor was insolvent (§ 726.106(2)).

### § 726.108 — Remedies of creditors [VERIFIED]
> (1) **Avoidance** of the transfer "to the extent necessary to satisfy the creditor's claim";
> (2) **Attachment** or other provisional remedy against the asset transferred or other property of
> the transferee; (3) an **injunction** against further disposition by the debtor or a transferee;
> (4) appointment of a **receiver** to take charge of the asset; (5) if the creditor has a judgment,
> **levy execution** on the asset or its proceeds; and any other relief the circumstances require.

### § 726.109 — Defenses, liability, and protection of transferee [VERIFIED]
> (1) **GOOD-FAITH-FOR-VALUE DEFENSE:** "A transfer or obligation is not voidable under s. 726.105(1)(a)
> against a person who took in **good faith and for a reasonably equivalent value** or against any
> subsequent transferee or obligee."
> (2) Recovery against the first transferee (or the person for whose benefit the transfer was made), or
> a non-good-faith subsequent transferee, is "the value of the asset transferred … or the amount
> necessary to satisfy the creditor's claim, whichever is less."
> (4) A good-faith transferee, to the extent of the value given, gets a **lien/right to retain** the
> interest, **enforcement** of the obligation, or a **reduction** in the judgment.

### § 726.110 — Extinguishment of cause of action (STATUTE OF LIMITATIONS) [VERIFIED]
> **Actual intent (§ 726.105(1)(a)):** "within **4 years** after the transfer was made … or, if later,
> within **1 year after the transfer … was or could reasonably have been discovered**."
> **Constructive (§ 726.105(1)(b) and § 726.106(1)):** "within **4 years** after the transfer."
> **Insider antecedent-debt (§ 726.106(2)):** "within **1 year** after the transfer."

## CH. 725 — UNENFORCEABLE CONTRACTS (STATUTE OF FRAUDS)

### § 725.01 — Promise to pay another's debt, etc. [VERIFIED summary]
No action on: a special promise of an executor to answer damages out of his own estate; **a special
promise to answer for the debt, default, or miscarriage of another** (a **guaranty**); an agreement in
consideration of marriage; **a contract for the sale of land** or any interest therein; or any
agreement **not to be performed within 1 year** — UNLESS the agreement (or a memorandum) is **in
writing and signed** by the party to be charged. (Cross-ref the defense "Statute of Frauds.")

## CH. 727 — ASSIGNMENT FOR THE BENEFIT OF CREDITORS (ABC) [VERIFIED summary]
A non-bankruptcy, court-supervised liquidation: the debtor (assignor) conveys all assets to a neutral
**assignee** who liquidates and distributes to creditors. § 727.104 (the assignment + petition filed in
circuit court); § 727.105 (no separate creditor proceedings against the assets — the ABC is the
exclusive forum); § 727.108 (claims filed with the assignee, bar date); § 727.109 (the court's powers);
§ 727.110-.111 (priorities + distribution, roughly tracking the Bankruptcy Code). An ABC can itself be
**attacked as a fraudulent transfer** if it is a sham to defeat a particular creditor.
