## CONSTRUCTION LIEN (Ch. 713) — the three deadlines

### § 713.06 — Notice to Owner (lienor NOT in privity): **45 days** [VERIFIED]
> The notice must be served “before commencing, or not later than **45 days after commencing**, to
> furnish his or her labor, services, or materials.” Served on the owner (and, for sub-subs/materialmen
> to a sub, on the contractor and the subcontractor where known, and any person designated in the
> Notice of Commencement). “**The failure to serve the notice, or to timely serve it, is a complete
> defense to enforcement of a lien by any person.**”
> Source: leg.state.fl.us …/0713/Sections/0713.06.html

### § 713.08 — Claim of Lien: record within **90 days** of final furnishing [VERIFIED]
> “The claim of lien may be recorded at any time during the progress of the work or thereafter but
> **not later than 90 days after the final furnishing** of the labor or services or materials by the
> lienor.” The claim of lien “shall be served on the owner”; failure to serve (per § 713.18) before
> recording or **within 15 days after recording** renders the lien voidable to the extent the
> failure/delay is shown to be prejudicial.
> Source: …/0713/Sections/0713.08.html

### § 713.22 — Duration of Lien: **1 year** to foreclose; Notice of Contest → **60 days** [VERIFIED]
> “A lien … does not continue for a longer period than **1 year after the claim of lien has been
> recorded** … unless within that time an action to enforce the lien is commenced in a court of
> competent jurisdiction.” On an owner's **Notice of Contest of Lien** (§ 713.22(2)), “the time within
> which you may file suit to enforce your lien is limited to **60 days** from the date of service of
> this notice,” and a lienor who fails to sue within 60 days has its lien “**extinguished
> automatically**.” [§ 713.21(4) summons-to-show-cause → 20 days — VERIFY verbatim.]
> Source: …/0713/Sections/0713.22.html

## PROCEEDINGS SUPPLEMENTARY (Ch. 56)

### § 56.29 — Proceedings supplementary [VERIFIED, summarized from the live text]
Commence by a **motion + affidavit** identifying the issuing court, case number, and the unsatisfied
amount, and stating that an execution is valid and outstanding. The court issues a **Notice to Appear**
to any person holding the debtor's property or owing the debtor, directing a responsive affidavit “by a
date certain, which date shall not be less than **7 business days** from the date of service,” describing
the property “with reasonable particularity” and advising of discovery and jury-trial rights. The court
may order the debtor's non-exempt property (or any property/debt/obligation) levied and applied to the
judgment, enter a **money judgment against transferees**, and award costs and attorney's fees.
> Source: …/0056/Sections/0056.29.html
