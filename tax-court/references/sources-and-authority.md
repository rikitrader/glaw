## Tax Court Rules of Practice and Procedure

- **Rule 34** — content of the petition (assignments of error; unraised issue deemed conceded).
- **Rule 36 / 37** — answer and reply.
- **Rule 41 / 60** — amendments; proper parties.
- **Rule 70–76** — formal discovery (interrogatories, document requests, depositions).
- **Rule 91** — stipulation of facts (required); Rule 91(f) motion to compel.
- **Rule 142(a)** — burden of proof (taxpayer; Commissioner on new matter/increased deficiency).
- **Rule 143** — evidence (FRE apply in regular cases); Rule 143(g) expert-report-as-direct.
- **Rule 151** — post-trial briefs.
- **Rule 155** — computation under the court's opinion.
- **Rule 171–172** — small-tax-case procedure and removal of the S election.

## Case law (real holdings only)

- ***Welch v. Helvering***, 290 U.S. 111 (1933) — Commissioner's determination presumed correct;
  taxpayer bears the burden.
- ***Branerton Corp. v. Commissioner***, 61 T.C. 691 (1974) — informal discovery must be attempted
  before formal discovery.
- ***Golsen v. Commissioner***, 54 T.C. 742 (1970) — Tax Court follows the Court of Appeals to
  which the case is appealable (venue-of-appeal precedent rule).
- ***Boechler, P.C. v. Commissioner***, 142 S. Ct. 1493 (2022) — the §6330(d)(1) CDP 30-day filing
  deadline is **non-jurisdictional and subject to equitable tolling** (note: this addresses CDP,
  **not** the §6213 deficiency 90-day clock — treat the deficiency clock as hard).
- ***Flora v. United States***, 362 U.S. 145 (1960) — full-payment rule for the district-court
  refund forum (the contrast that makes the Tax Court a prepayment forum).
- ***Cohan v. Commissioner***, 39 F.2d 540 (2d Cir. 1930) — estimate where records are lost
  (subject to §274(d) strict-substantiation limits) — relevant to substantiation hazards.
